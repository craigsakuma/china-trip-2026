# 20 Unique Things to Do in Hong Kong That Aren't in Your Guidebook

**Source:** Yoga, Wine & Travel (yogawinetravel.com), travel blog focused on off-beaten-path experiences. Published 2023, updated 2025.

---

## Quick Facts

- Hong Kong has hundreds of islands, most uninhabited — easy escapes from the urban core
- 75% of Hong Kong is green space with hiking trails accessible by public transit
- The city's British colonial heritage shows up in afternoon tea traditions, double-decker trams, and English signage
- Street art scenes thrive in Central/Soho, Sham Shui Po, Wong Chuk Hang, and Wan Chai
- Chinese White Dolphins (pink dolphins) still swim off Lantau — fewer than 50 remain

## Off-the-Beaten-Path Experiences

### 1. Victoria Peak Gardens (山頂花園)
- **What:** Hidden viewpoint above the commercial Peak Tower
- **Access:** From Peak Tower, walk left 10–15 minutes until barely-visible stairs appear; climb the hill
- **Reward:** Nearly 360-degree panoramic views — typically experienced completely alone
- **Why go:** Peaceful alternative to the crowded, commercialized Peak Tower observation deck

### 2. Lugard Road Trail (盧吉道)
- **What:** Leisurely 90-minute shaded loop walk next to Peak Tower
- **Route:** About midway, take the right fork at a small park for clifftop views
- **Reward:** "Unparalleled" unobstructed panoramic views of the harbor, superior to anything from Peak Tower
- **Bonus:** Dog-friendly, less crowded than main peak attractions

### 3. Aqua Luna Sunset Cruise (張保仔號)
- **What:** Traditional Chinese junk boat with iconic red sails on Victoria Harbour
- **Cost:** HK$220 (~US$28) for sunset cocktail cruise including one alcoholic drink
- **Discount:** 10–15% off when booking online
- **Departure:** Both harbor sides (Central and TST piers)

### 4. Cheung Chau Island (長洲)
- **What:** Car-free island with pirate heritage and fishing village charm
- **Access:** Ferry from Central Pier 5 — 30 min (fast ferry) or 60 min (slow/scenic)
- **Highlights:** Bicycles as main transport, seafood restaurants, local temple
- **Special event:** Annual Bun Festival — "one of the craziest (and tastiest) festivals in the world"

### 5. Sham Shui Po (深水埗) — Kowloon
- **What:** Colorful working-class Kowloon neighborhood offering stark contrast to Central's glitz
- **Highlights:** Each street specializes in different goods (electronics, fabrics, toys, buttons)
- **Food:** Michelin-listed Tim Ho Wan, Kung Wo Tofu, Kwan Kee desserts
- **Street art:** Okuda San Miguel's "Rainbow Thief" mural on Man Fung Building
- **Best time:** Afternoons and early evenings for street photography

### 6. PMQ / Police Married Quarters (元創方) — Central/Soho
- **What:** Heritage site converted to creative hub with ~100 design galleries and shops
- **Focus:** Homegrown Hong Kong designers — unique gifts and souvenirs
- **Opened:** 2014 after 14-year vacancy
- **Events:** Weekend pop-up exhibits and workshops
- **Cost:** Free entry
- **MTR:** Central Station

### 7. Tai Kwun Arts & Heritage Centre (大館) — Central
- **What:** Mid-1800s complex formerly serving as police station, prison, and magistracy
- **Reopened:** May 2018 after extensive renovation
- **Highlights:** Art and history exhibits, restaurants, bars including cocktail spot Dragonfly and speakeasy 001
- **Cost:** Free entry; allow 1–2 hours
- **MTR:** Central Station

### 8. Sheung Luk Waterfall, Tai Long Wan (上碌瀑布)
- **What:** Swimming-friendly cascading freshwater pools in Hong Kong's countryside
- **Best time:** May–November on weekdays for solitude
- **Access route:** MTR to Choi Hung Station → taxi to Sai Wan Pavilion (西灣亭) → 45-minute hike to Sai Wan Beach → buy boat ticket (pre-book — spots fill quickly) → 10–15 minute woodland walk with hill climbing
- **Important:** Wear comfortable walking shoes; not suitable for flip-flops

### 9. Self-Guided Street Art Tours
- **Central/Soho:** Graham Street through Hollywood Road to Sheung Wan
- **Wan Chai:** Queen's Road East toward Morrison Hill Swimming Pool
- **Wong Chuk Hang:** Warehouse district with emerging gallery scene
- **Sham Shui Po:** Markets and murals
- **Resource:** HKWalls.org for guided tour info and location maps

### 10. Egg Waffles / Eggettes (雞蛋仔)
- **What:** Hong Kong's signature street snack since the 1950s
- **Origin:** Created from leftover egg, flour, sugar, and milk
- **Modern flavors:** Orange chocolate, pandan, pineapple, meatfloss, sesame
- **Where:** Nearly every street corner food store; Mong Kok and Causeway Bay have the highest concentration
- **Cost:** HK$15–30

### 11. Pink Dolphin Watching — Lantau
- **What:** Chinese White Dolphins (中華白海豚) appear light pink; fewer than 50 remain in HK waters
- **Where:** Off Lantau Island coast
- **Recommended operator:** Hong Kong Dolphinwatch — ethical, responsible focus
- **Why go:** Rare wildlife experience; potential baby dolphin sightings; conservation awareness

### 12. High Tea at Mandarin Oriental (Clipper Lounge)
- **What:** Colonial-era afternoon tea tradition
- **Signature:** Raisin scones with rose jam and clotted cream
- **Location:** Original Mandarin Oriental Central — tell taxi "Gau Mun Wah" (舊文華)
- **Advantage:** Tables bookable (unlike Peninsula Hotel which often has long waits)
- **Reservation:** Recommended

### 13. The Mills (南豐紗廠) — Tsuen Wan
- **What:** Heritage textile factory complex, revitalized and reopened early 2019
- **Features:** Restored original staircases, signage, window frames repurposed as benches
- **Highlights:** Retail shops, restaurants, coffee venues, vintage-inspired street art
- **Vibe:** Quieter than Central locations; industrial-chic atmosphere
- **MTR:** Tsuen Wan Station

### 14. Wong Chuk Hang Art District (黃竹坑)
- **What:** Emerging warehouse district on south Hong Kong Island
- **Highlights:** Contemporary galleries, street art, creative studios
- **Why go:** Represents Hong Kong's grassroots art scene away from commercial galleries
- **MTR:** Wong Chuk Hang Station (South Island Line)

### 15. Tai Hang Neighbourhood (大坑)
- **What:** Peaceful enclave with local village atmosphere within urban Hong Kong
- **Highlights:** Umbrella decorations create Instagram-worthy photos; boutique restaurants and cafes
- **Annual event:** Fire Dragon Dance during Mid-Autumn Festival (September/October)
- **Stay:** Little Tai Hang boutique hotel
- **MTR:** Tin Hau Station, short walk

### 16. Star Ferry (天星小輪) at Sunset
- **What:** Iconic red-and-white ferries crossing Victoria Harbour since 1888
- **Cost:** Under HK$3
- **Best time:** Sunset for golden-hour harbor photography
- **Opinion:** "One of the most romantic experiences in Hong Kong"

### 17. Sai Kung Seaside Town (西貢)
- **What:** Coastal escape with fishing villages, beaches, water sports
- **Highlights:** Seafood restaurants along the waterfront, boat trips to hidden beaches
- **Nearby:** Sharp Island (橋咀洲) — tombolo (natural land bridge) appears at low tide
- **Access:** Minibus from Diamond Hill or Choi Hung MTR

## Island Escapes Beyond Lantau

### Cheung Chau (長洲)
Car-free pirate island with bicycle transport, annual Bun Festival, fishing culture. 30–60 min ferry from Central.

### Lamma Island (南丫島)
Expat-popular island with seafood restaurants, hiking trails, beaches. Ferry from Central until midnight. Camping available.

### Peng Chau (坪洲)
Tiny island (1 km²) — "flat island" with zero tourist infrastructure, authentic fishing village life. 30 min ferry from Central.

### Sharp Island (橋咀洲)
Off Sai Kung — crystal-clear waters, natural tombolo visible at low tide, snorkeling. Boat from Sai Kung waterfront.

## Practical Tips

- **Octopus Card:** Buy at airport or any MTR station; works on all transit, 7-Eleven, supermarkets
- **Taxis:** Start under HK$27 (~US$4); most drivers require cash
- **Best seasons:** March–June and September–November for comfortable weather
- **Street art route:** Central → Sheung Wan → Sham Shui Po for a full-day art walk
- **Island day trips:** Plan ferry schedules in advance; last ferries can be early (10–11pm)
- **Dolphin tours:** Book Hong Kong Dolphinwatch in advance; sightings not guaranteed but common
- **Waterfall trip:** Pre-book boat tickets for Sheung Luk; arrive early on weekdays
- **High tea:** Book Mandarin Oriental's Clipper Lounge in advance; walk-ins possible but risky
- **Photography etiquette:** Ask before photographing at residential buildings; street scenes are fair game
- **Hidden gem strategy:** Combine PMQ + Tai Kwun + Man Mo Temple + street art for a full Central/Sheung Wan walking day
