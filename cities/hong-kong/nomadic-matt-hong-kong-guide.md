# Hong Kong Travel Guide

**Source:** Nomadic Matt (nomadicmatt.com), one of the most popular travel blogs, budget-focused, regularly updated. Updated November 13, 2025.

---

## Overview

One of the most densely populated places on earth: 6,300 people per square kilometer. Three main regions: Hong Kong Island, Kowloon, and the New Territories, plus 260+ additional islands. Author calls it one of his favorite cities in the world, especially for food. Recommend at least 3 days.

---

## Top Attractions

### Temples
Many traditional temples throughout the city. Notable: Miu Fat Buddhist Monastery, Lo Pan Temple (author's two favorites), Sha Tin Che Kung Temple, Man Mo Temple (文武廟), Yuen Yuen Institute, Ten Thousand Buddhas Monastery (萬佛寺). Rich cultural history, very beautiful.

### Star Ferry (天星小輪)
Best way to cross harbor between Kowloon and Hong Kong Island. Operating 130+ years, moves 700,000+ people daily. Great activity in itself -- do it at night when city lights are aglow. Cost: 4-5 HKD weekdays, 5-6 HKD weekends/holidays. Runs 6:30am-11:30pm daily.

### Ngong Ping 360 (昂坪360)
Cable car stretching 5.7 km from Tung Chung across bay to Lantau Island. Panoramic views of airport, harbor, city, and mountains. Ride lasts ~25 minutes. At the top: Po Lin Monastery (寶蓮禪寺, founded 1906) and Tian Tan Buddha (天壇大佛, 34m bronze statue). Lantau Island somewhat touristy but the ride, views, and monastery are worth it. Book tickets online in advance.

### Peak Tram (山頂纜車)
Operating since 1888 (renovated multiple times). Takes you to top of the Peak, Hong Kong Island's largest mountain. Spectacular 180-degree views of Victoria Harbour skyscrapers, Kowloon, and surrounding hills. Best view of the city. Many hiking trails around the mountain (some lasting hours). Can walk down or take bus from other trailheads.

### Street Markets
Best places for atmosphere, vibe, and food. Top markets: Ladies Market (女人街), Temple Street Night Market (廟街夜市), Stanley Market. Hong Kong has 15,000+ restaurants -- consider a food tour to navigate markets.

## Other Things to See & Do

### Sheung Wan (上環)
Reflects old Hong Kong. High-end apartments tower over small stores and traditional markets. Less developed with modern buildings than other areas. A glimpse into the past, cool to walk around.

### Tsim Sha Tsui Waterfront (尖沙咀海濱)
Breathtaking skyline view of Hong Kong Island. Avenue of Stars (Hong Kong's Walk of Fame), Bruce Lee statue. Nearby: Hong Kong Museum of Art (est. 1962, first public art museum, 17,000+ items including Chinese ceramics, terra cotta, calligraphy, contemporary art) and Hong Kong Space Museum (opened 1980, egg-shaped building, astronomy exhibits, digital planetarium, Omnimax theatre, very interactive -- good for kids).

### Jungle Trekking / Geo Areas
Outside the city center: 8 different "geo-areas" across Sai Kung Volcanic Rock Region and Northeast New Territories Sedimentary Rock Region. A side of the city few people see.

### Dragon's Back Trail
Easily accessible from city. ~4 hours, moderately difficult. Bring lots of water. Good way to spend a day outside the hustle.

### Lan Kwai Fong (蘭桂坊) — Nightlife
Main nightlife and party area. Tons of bars, clubs, shisha, cheap drinks. Packed any night of the week. Recommended: 001, Solas, Dragon I, 6 Degrees.

### Disneyland Hong Kong
On Lantau Island, 20 min by car or ~45 min public transport. Opened 2005. Dozens of attractions including spinning teacups and Jungle Cruise. Good for families.

### Kowloon Park (九龍公園)
13 hectares (33 acres). Swimming pool, fitness center, duck ponds, Chinese garden, aviary, Hong Kong Heritage Discovery Centre. Great for people-watching and escaping heat.

### Tung Shoi Street / Ladies Street (通菜街/女人街)
Open-air bazaar in central Mong Kok (旺角). One of busiest places in Hong Kong. Shops and cheap goods. Remember to bargain.

### Mai Po Natural Area (米埔)
Mangrove forest and muddy swampland. 430+ bird species documented. Protected area -- access only via WWF guided tours (Fri-Sun, 9:30am start, last tour 2:30pm). Oct-Apr: watch annual bird migrations.

### Junk Boat / Duk Ling
Only one traditional junk boat left: the Duk Ling (鴨靈號). Built 1950s as fishing boat, refurbished in 1980s for tourists. Classic harbor cruise.

### Hong Kong Heritage Museum
Author's favorite museum. Showcases city's history and love of art. Opened 2000. Large exhibit about New Territories, opera house for performances. Located near Sha Tin Park and Shing Mun River (surrounding area interesting too).

### Hong Kong Museum of History
Closed and renovated after Chinese government takeover during COVID. New exhibits focus on China bringing "order" and security measures. History section somewhat sanitized but gives good overview of city's history and importance to global trade. Big exhibit on Chinese immigration to US.

### Cantonese Opera — Yau Ma Tei Theatre
For deeper cultural experience. Also: Broadway Cinematheque for indie films and Korean Film Festival.

## Transportation

### MTR / Public Transit
Fast, safe, reliable. Tourist day pass: 75 HKD/day (35 HKD kids) -- covers metro, tram, light rail. Individual tickets: 5-58 HKD based on distance. Runs 6am-1am daily. Day pass best if traveling a lot or long distances.

### Octopus Card (八達通)
Contactless payment card used by 99% of residents. Works on all public transport (MTR, light rail, buses, ferries, trams), convenience stores, restaurants, shops, tourist attractions. Functions as pre-paid debit card. Get one -- saves huge amounts of time.

### Star Ferry
4-5 HKD weekdays, 5-6 HKD weekends. Runs 6:30am-11:30pm.

### Airport Express
Departs every 10 minutes. 105-120 HKD one-way. ~25 min journey.

### Taxis / Uber
Taxis start ~29 HKD, add ~8 HKD per km. Uber cheaper than taxis and recommended over taxis. Skip taxis generally -- traffic makes bills add up.

### To/From Shenzhen (深圳)
Bus: ~75 HKD one-way, ~1 hour. Train: 75-130 HKD one-way, ~30 min.

### Car Rental
Not recommended. World-class public transport makes it unnecessary; driving conditions cramped and busy.

## Safety

Crime rate relatively low. Safe for solo travelers and solo female travelers.

**Key concerns:**
- **Pickpockets:** Most common in crowds and on public transport. Bag snatching is the most common crime but still rare
- **Scams:** Fake monks offering trinkets/"blessings" -- politely decline (real Buddhist monks don't hawk goods). Overcharging taxi drivers
- **Politics:** Since 2020, Chinese government exerts much more control, installed own leaders, cracked down on pro-democracy movements. HK citizens more open about politics than mainlanders but still better to be careful what you say
- **Travel insurance:** Strongly recommended

## When to Visit

Subtropical climate. Best time: November-March (less heat and humidity).

| Season | Conditions | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Nov-Mar | Mild, pleasant | Best time to visit. Nov-Dec good for accommodation deals. Tourism picks up after New Year |
| Summer | Up to 31°C (88°F), severe humidity | Not recommended. Typhoon threat |
| Chinese New Year | Busy, expensive | Book accommodation and tours well in advance. Chaos and fun atmosphere can be worth it |

## Money-Saving Tips

1. **Eat at buffets** -- all-you-can-eat under 110 HKD (noodles, dim sum, dumplings)
2. **Stay off Hong Kong Island** -- Kowloon much cheaper; or try Causeway Bay if you must be on the island
3. **Shop at markets** -- up to 50% cheaper than grocery stores for produce
4. **Chungking Mansion** -- cheapest accommodation (not the nicest but cheapest)
5. **Couchsurfing** -- free accommodation via locals/expats
6. **Transit day pass** -- 75 HKD adult, 35 HKD child; beats per-ride fares if traveling extensively
7. **Skip taxis** -- traffic makes bills add up; public transport is affordable and reliable
8. **Get an Octopus Card** -- contactless payment for transport and shops, massive time saver

## Practical Tips

- Minimum 3 days recommended
- Octopus Card is essential -- works everywhere, saves time
- Markets for groceries (much cheaper than stores)
- Check for festivals/Chinese New Year dates and book early
- Star Ferry at night for city lights
- Get to Ngong Ping 360 early to avoid crowds
- Peak Tram has multiple hiking trails at the top -- bring water
- Dragon's Back trail for nature escape (~4 hours)
- Mai Po bird reserve requires WWF tour booking (Fri-Sun only)
- Travel insurance strongly recommended
- Be careful discussing politics, especially post-2020 changes
