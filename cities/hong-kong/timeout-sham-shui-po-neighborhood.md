# The Ultimate Neighbourhood Guide to Sham Shui Po (深水埗)

**Source:** Tatum Ancheta, Time Out Hong Kong (timeout.com/hong-kong), leading Hong Kong city guide. Published Dec 22, 2023.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Kowloon, just north of Boundary Street — one of Hong Kong's oldest and densest districts
- **MTR:** Sham Shui Po Station (Tsuen Wan Line) drops you right in the center
- **Character:** Traditional working-class neighborhood transforming into creative hub while retaining old-school charm
- **Best for:** Street food, fabric/textile shopping, vintage finds, electronics bargains, street art, local culture
- **Time needed:** Half-day minimum; full day to explore properly
- **Vibe:** "Instead of glitzy, glass-panelled skyscrapers, you'll find buildings that hide a rich history behind their humble facades"

## Where to Eat

### Michelin-Recommended & Famous

#### Kung Wo Tofu Factory (公和荳品廠) — Pei Ho Street
- **Legacy:** 60+ years old
- **Signature:** Tofu puffs, deep-fried tofu with fish paste, fresh tofu pudding (豆腐花), hot soya milk
- **Why go:** Possibly Hong Kong's best traditional tofu; Michelin Bib Gourmand

#### Tim Ho Wan (添好運) — Sham Shui Po branch
- **Distinction:** One of the world's cheapest Michelin-starred restaurants
- **Signature:** Baked char siu bun (酥皮叉燒包)
- **Price:** Under HK$100 per person

#### Lau Sum Kee (劉森記) — Noodle shop
- **Legacy:** Third-generation family business
- **Method:** Traditional bamboo pole kneading for noodles — a dying art
- **Why go:** Some of Hong Kong's most authentic handmade noodles

#### Hon Fat Noodle Shop
- **Known for:** Thick-cut pork liver noodles — a Sham Shui Po specialty

### Traditional & Street Food

#### Oi Man Sang — Dai pai dong
- **Style:** Open-air dining (大排檔) with al fresco seating
- **Signature:** Razor clams in black bean sauce, garlic steamed prawns
- **Vibe:** Boisterous, authentic, local

#### Flying Eagle — Chinese steakhouse
- **Operating since:** 1977
- **Style:** Family-run Chinese-style steakhouse — rare surviving example of a uniquely Hong Kong dining format

#### Hop Yik Tai (合益泰) — Cheung fun specialist
- **Specialty:** Fresh cheung fun (腸粉) — silky rice noodle rolls with various fillings

#### Kwan Kee Store (坤記) — Dessert
- **Specialty:** Homemade put chai ko (缽仔糕) — steamed rice pudding in small bowls

#### Sun Heung Yuen — Cha chaan teng
- **Specialty:** Beef and egg sandwiches — classic Hong Kong comfort food

#### San Lung Cake Shop — Bakery
- **Specialty:** Black sesame cakes and century egg pastries

#### Block 18 Doggie's Noodle
- **Specialty:** Glutinous rice noodles

#### Tomato C Hing
- **Specialty:** Flavourful tomato soup bases for udon, noodles, and hotpot

### Modern & International

#### Munakataya — Japanese
- **Specialty:** Towering sashimi bowls

#### The Park by Years — Vegan cafe
- **Menu:** Spicy Thai Tom Yum spaghetti, avocado orange pumpkin salmon salad, Impossible burger, Japanese kushiyaki

#### Pei Ho Counterparts — Cha chaan teng with a mission
- **Style:** Hong Kong diner staples
- **Story:** Owner Ming Gor distributes free lunchboxes to elderly and those in need — eating here supports the community

## Shopping — Specialist Streets

Sham Shui Po is "one of Hong Kong's best-kept fashion secrets" — international designers (Versace, Calvin Klein) come here seeking textile inspiration.

### Pang Jai / Tung Chau Street (棚仔 / 通州街) — Textiles
- Designer fabric haven with textile and fashion accessories at near-wholesale prices

### Ki Lung Street (基隆街) — "Button Street"
- Buttons, zippers, clasps, fabrics, embroidery accessories — everything for sewing and tailoring

### Yu Chau Street (汝州街) — "Bead Street"
- Beads, sequins, and sewing supplies in every conceivable variety

### Nam Cheong Street (南昌街) — "Ribbon Street"
- Lace and ribbons in all shapes, sizes, and colors

### Tai Nan Street (大南街) — "Leather Street"
- **Luen Cheong Leather Hong Kong:** Established 1948; locally-made lamb and cowhides
- **Alri Star Leather Factory:** Leathers, crafting tools, beginner workshops
- **The Lederer:** European and American leather hides; tools, threads, dyes

### Fuk Wing Street (福榮街) — "Toy Street"
- LEGO sets, action figures, seasonal decorations — nostalgia paradise

### Apliu Street (鴨寮街) — Electronics flea market
- Electronics, fishing gear, photography equipment, vintage home decor
- Well-known for bargain electronics and secondhand finds

### Cheung Sha Wan Fashion Road (長沙灣道) — Fashion wholesale
- 200+ wholesale fashion shops with bargain pricing

### Golden Computer Centre and Arcade — Tech hub
- Computers, components, gaming accessories — Hong Kong's tech shopping mecca

## Arts, Culture & History

### Jockey Club Creative Arts Centre (JCCAC, 賽馬會創意藝術中心)
Former factory converted to arts hub. Exhibition spaces, theatre, craft shop, tea house. Studios for up-and-coming and established artists.

### Form Society
Community space founded by sculptor Wong Tin-yan. Exhibitions, artist talks, workshops, pop-up events.

### Parallel Space
Independent art gallery with cultural classes and artist talks.

### Man Fung Building Mural — Tai Nan Street
"Rainbow Thief" — multicolored geometric mural by Madrid artist Okuda San Miguel (2016), created for the HK Walls street art festival. One of Hong Kong's most photographed murals.

## Historic Buildings & Temples

### 170 Yee Kuk Street — 1920s tenement
Chinese tenement building with original verandah — rare surviving example of 1920s Hong Kong architecture.

### Nam Cheong Pawn Shop — Grade II historic building
Features upside-down bat holding coin emblem — a traditional Chinese symbol of good fortune (蝠 sounds like 福, "fortune").

### Lung Hing Tong (est. 1931)
Green and red design with protruding dragon head, cranes, and deer on roof — traditional Chinese decorative elements.

### Ex-Sham Shui Po Service Reservoir — Mission Hill
Grade I historic structure with large granite piers and red brick arches resembling European and Roman engineering. Recently opened to public — Hong Kong's newest heritage attraction.

### Kwan Tai Temple (關帝廟) — Grade II, built 1891
Dedicated to god of war and righteousness. Kwan Tai's birthday celebration on the 24th day of the sixth lunar month.

### Sam Tai Tsz Temple (三太子廟) — Grade II
Built by Hakka immigrants honoring their patron deity after the 1898 plague.

### Pak Tai Temple (北帝廟) — Grade III, 1920
Built by fishermen honoring the Emperor of the North.

### Lei Cheng Uk Han Tomb Museum (李鄭屋漢墓博物館)
Eastern Han dynasty tomb (AD 25–220) discovered during construction. Exhibition hall displaying pottery and bronze wares. Free entry.

## Where to Stay

### YHA Mei Ho House Youth Hostel (美荷樓)
- **History:** Historic Mark I H-shaped resettlement block (1954); 129 rooms
- **Status:** Grade II listed building
- **Feature:** Heritage museum with tours documenting Hong Kong's public housing history
- **Best for:** Budget travelers wanting to stay in living history

### Wontonmeen
- **Vibe:** Frequented by tourists and artists
- **Story:** Converted dormitory into recording studio during COVID-19
- **Downstairs:** Runners' Foods cafe serves the homeless community

## Outdoor

### Garden Hill (嘉頓山)
Short, steep hike from Sham Shui Po MTR to a peak with illuminated Kowloon night views. 20–30 minutes up. Best at sunset.

### Pei Ho Street Wet Market
Fresh produce market with Michelin-recommended street snacks nearby — an authentic local Hong Kong market experience.

## Practical Tips

- **Getting there:** MTR Sham Shui Po Station (Tsuen Wan Line) is the easiest option
- **Best time:** Afternoons for shopping; evenings for Garden Hill sunset and dai pai dong dining
- **Budget:** One of Hong Kong's most affordable neighborhoods for food and shopping
- **Combine with:** Garden Hill sunset hike, then dinner at a dai pai dong, then Apliu Street night browsing
- **Respect:** This is a lived-in working-class neighborhood; photograph respectfully
- **Language:** Less English spoken than Central/TST — basic Cantonese phrases appreciated
- **Markets close:** Most street vendors pack up by 7–8pm; fabric shops close by 6pm
