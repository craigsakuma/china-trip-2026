# Hong Kong Travel Guide for First-Time Visitors

**Source:** Becca Siegel (halfhalftravel.com), former Hong Kong exchange student (2009), conversational Cantonese speaker, 10+ return visits over 2 years. Published Jul 2023, updated Nov 2025.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Currency:** Hong Kong Dollar (HKD); ~7.8 HKD = 1 USD
- **Language:** Cantonese Chinese; English widely spoken in tourist areas; Mandarin understood
- **Visa:** Most nationalities visa-free for 90 days (US, Canada, Australia, UK, EU)
- **Power sockets:** Type G and D (British-style 3-pin); US travelers need adapter
- **Safety:** "One of the safest cities in Asia" — standard urban precautions in crowded areas
- **Emergency:** 999 (police, fire, ambulance)
- **Tipping:** Not expected; 10% service charge auto-added at upscale restaurants
- **Best seasons:** Oct–Dec (cool, clear), Mar–May (warm, manageable humidity)
- **Avoid:** Jun–Sep (extreme heat, humidity, typhoons)
- **Duration:** Minimum 3–5 days; 1+ week ideal for islands and day trips
- **Mosquitoes:** Carry repellent May–October

## Getting Around

### Octopus Card (八達通)
Essential purchase. Rechargeable transit card accepted on MTR, buses, ferries, trams, 7-Eleven, grocery stores. Buy at any MTR station; refund deposit at MTR customer service on departure day. Tourist version available at airport with HK$50 pre-loaded.

### MTR (Mass Transit Railway)
- Island Line (blue): Central, Admiralty, Wan Chai, Causeway Bay, Shau Kei Wan
- Green/Red lines: Kowloon (TST, Mong Kok, Kowloon Tong)
- Yellow line: Tung Chung (Lantau Island/airport)
- Efficient, clean, air-conditioned, English signage throughout

### Buses
Reach areas MTR doesn't: beaches, hiking trailheads, residential neighborhoods. Double-deckers offer excellent city views from the upper front seats.

### Star Ferry (天星小輪)
Historic harbor crossing between Central/Wan Chai and TST. Under HK$3 per ride. Operating since 1888 — one of the world's great budget travel experiences.

### Taxis
Start under US$4 (HK$27). Require cash — most drivers do not accept cards. Red taxis for urban areas; green for New Territories; blue for Lantau.

### Airport Express
24 minutes from airport to city center. 20% discount when booked online.

## Hong Kong Island

### Central & Sheung Wan (中環 & 上環)
- **Mid-Levels Escalators:** World's longest outdoor escalator system; ride up through SoHo dining district
- **Hollywood Road:** Antiques market and Man Mo Temple (文武廟) — incense coils create atmospheric photos
- **PMQ (元創方):** Former Police Married Quarters converted to creative hub with ~100 design galleries and local designer shops. Free entry; pop-up exhibits on weekends
- **Tai Kwun (大館):** Mid-1800s former police station/prison complex. Art and history exhibits, restaurants, speakeasy bar 001. Free entry; allow 1–2 hours
- **Hong Kong Park:** Aviary, koi lake, good for picnics near Central MTR
- **IFC Mall:** Top-level patio with Victoria Harbour views; great for sunset drinks
- **Instagram Pier (Western District Public Cargo Working Area):** Industrial pier past Sheung Wan; popular sunset photography spot

### Victoria Peak (太平山)
The essential Hong Kong viewpoint. Visit late afternoon for sunset over the skyline. Take the Peak Tram up (iconic experience); return by public bus (cheaper, less crowded).

- **Lugard Road Loop:** 90-minute shaded walk with unobstructed panoramic views superior to the commercial Peak Tower — take the right fork midway at a small park
- **Victoria Peak Gardens:** Walk left from Peak Tower 10–15 minutes; barely-visible stairs lead to nearly 360-degree views, often with no other visitors

### Causeway Bay (銅鑼灣)
Constantly bustling shopping and dining district. Times Square Mall, graffiti alleys, bubble tea shops. MTR: Causeway Bay.

- **Happy Valley Racecourse (跑馬地):** Wednesday evening horse racing with expat crowd. Free admission after halftime. Season through mid-July

### Quarry Bay (鰂魚涌)
- **Montane Mansion (怪獸大廈):** Towering public housing complex famous for photography — four buildings create a dramatic enclosed courtyard. Note: people live here; photograph respectfully

### South Side Beaches
- **Repulse Bay Beach (淺水灣):** Most popular; touristy but beautiful
- **Big Wave Bay & Shek O Beach:** Less crowded; reachable by minibus from Shau Kei Wan
- **Stanley (赤柱) & Stanley Market:** Day trip via Bus 6 or 6X from Central Bus Terminal

## Kowloon (九龍)

### Tsim Sha Tsui (尖沙咀)
Primary tourist hub. Victoria Harbour waterfront.

- **Avenue of Stars:** Harbourfront promenade honoring HK film industry
- **Symphony of Lights:** Nightly light show at 8pm along waterfront — free
- **Nathan Road (彌敦道):** Famous thoroughfare with neon signage
- **Peninsula Hotel (半島酒店):** Landmark across from Avenue of Stars; famous afternoon tea
- **Chungking Mansions (重慶大廈):** Multi-floor market on Nathan Road with exceptional Pakistani and Indian cuisine in upper-floor "mess halls"

### Mong Kok (旺角)
"Quintessential Hong Kong scene" — overwhelming crowds, shops, street food.

- **Ladies' Market (女人街):** Four-block vendor market for bargaining. Behind stalls: cheap Thai and Chinese food
- **Temple Street Night Market (廟街夜市):** Street food including king crab and seafood. MTR: Yau Ma Tei
- **Goldfish Market (金魚街):** Between Prince Edward and Mong Kok MTR
- **Flower Market (花墟):** Few blocks from Prince Edward MTR

### Jordan & Yau Ma Tei (佐敦 & 油麻地)
Back streets with local character and English-limited menus. Home to specialty markets.

- **Tin Hau Temple (天后廟):** Small local temple near Yau Ma Tei Station

### Sham Shui Po (深水埗)
Author's "number one favorite" — old-school neighborhood with local markets selling everything from violins to Hello Kitty watches. Notable for hand-painted vertical business signs, a declining art form. MTR: Sham Shui Po.

### Diamond Hill (鑽石山)
- **Chi Lin Nunnery (志蓮淨苑):** Renovated Tang-dynasty style temple and traditional Chinese garden. Juxtaposition against Kowloon high-rises creates excellent photography. Free entry. MTR: Diamond Hill

## Outlying Islands

### Lantau Island (大嶼山)
- **Tian Tan Buddha / Big Buddha (天壇大佛):** World's largest sitting bronze Buddha at Ngong Ping
- **Access:** MTR to Tung Chung, then Ngong Ping 360 cable car (spectacular mountain views)
- **On-site:** Hundreds of steps to statue; adjacent Po Lin Monastery with vegetarian cafe

### Cheung Chau (長洲)
Car-free island with fishing village charm, seafood restaurants, local temple. Annual Bun Festival is "one of the craziest (and tastiest) festivals in the world." Ferry from Central Pier 5: 30 min (fast) or 60 min (slow).

### Lamma Island (南丫島)
Most popular outlying island for expats. Ferry from Central Ferry Pier (cheap; service until ~midnight). Abundant seafood restaurants, beaches, hiking trails. Camping available.

## Day Trips

### Macau (澳門)
Portuguese-Chinese cultural blend. Ferry from Central (catamaran). Key sights: The Venetian, St. Paul Ruins. Portuguese-Chinese fusion cuisine. Higher gambling minimums than Vegas. Check visa requirements separately.

### Shenzhen (深圳)
- **High-Speed Rail:** 18 minutes from West Kowloon Station; ~HK$80 ticket
- **KCR Light Rail:** Older crossings at Lok Ma Chau and Lo Wu
- **Critical:** Most nationalities need advance Chinese visa. Post-border: no Facebook, Gmail, Google, Twitter without VPN. Chinese laws apply

## Food & Dining

### Budget Eats
- 7-Eleven and CircleK for drinks and snacks
- Behind Ladies' Market stalls for cheap Thai/Chinese
- Street food in Mong Kok and Jordan markets
- Egg waffles (雞蛋仔) at nearly every street corner — HK's signature street snack since the 1950s

### Mid-Range
- Cha chaan teng (茶餐廳) — Hong Kong-style diners serving milk tea, toast, noodles
- Dai pai dong (大排檔) — open-air street food stalls, especially in Sham Shui Po
- Dim sum at Tim Ho Wan or One Dim Sum

### Upscale
- SoHo international restaurants (via Mid-Levels Escalators)
- Lan Kwai Fong (蘭桂坊) bar district — or save money with 7-Eleven beers on the street
- Lamma Island seafood restaurants
- High tea at Mandarin Oriental's Clipper Lounge — signature raisin scones with rose jam and clotted cream; tables bookable (unlike Peninsula Hotel)

## Practical Tips

- **Octopus Card is essential** — buy immediately at airport or any MTR station
- **Take the Star Ferry at least once** — under HK$3 for an iconic harbor crossing
- **Ride the upper deck of double-decker buses** for free sightseeing
- **Learn basic Cantonese:** "mm goi" (唔該, thank you/excuse me), "nei hou" (你好, hello)
- **Carry mosquito repellent** May–October
- **Bring a power adapter** — Hong Kong uses British-style Type G plugs
- **Hydrate aggressively** in summer — subtropical humidity is brutal
- **Download offline MTR map** — cell service can be spotty underground
- **Avoid peak MTR hours** (8–9:30am, 6–7:30pm) if possible
- **Street photography:** Hong Kong is endlessly photogenic, but be respectful at residential buildings like Montane Mansion
