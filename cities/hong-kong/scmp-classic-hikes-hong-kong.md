# Classic Hikes in Hong Kong: Dragon's Back, Lantau Peak, Lion Rock & Beyond

**Source:** South China Morning Post (scmp.com), Hong Kong's leading English-language newspaper, with supplementary details from Slower Travels and Walk My World trail guides. Published 2022, updated 2024.

---

## Quick Facts

- Hong Kong is 75% green space — over 24 country parks with 100+ marked trails
- Best hiking season: October–April (cool, dry, clear skies); avoid June–September (heat, humidity, typhoons)
- Tap water is safe to drink but carry 1.5–2L minimum per hike
- Trail markings use the Hong Kong Trail (50km, HK Island), Wilson Trail (78km, cross-territory), MacLehose Trail (100km, New Territories), and Lantau Trail (70km)
- Emergency: call 999; most trails have cell coverage
- Apps: AllTrails or Trailwatch (HK government app) for GPS navigation

## Dragon's Back (龍脊) — Hong Kong Island

### Overview
Voted Asia's best urban hike, Dragon's Back follows an undulating ridge resembling a dragon's spine, with 360-degree views over Shek O, Big Wave Bay, and the South China Sea. The most popular hike in Hong Kong for good reason.

### Trail Details
- **Distance:** 7.2 km (main trail) or 8.5 km (including Section 8 extension)
- **Duration:** 2–3 hours (main), 4 hours (full)
- **Elevation gain:** 204 meters
- **Difficulty:** Easy to moderate — manageable for anyone comfortable with staircases
- **Start:** Junction of Sections 7 & 8 on Shek O Road
- **End:** Big Wave Bay Beach

### Route Description
1. **Forest climb (1 km):** Shaded forest track with abundant blue butterflies; glimpses of Tai Tam Harbour through trees
2. **Dragon's Back Ridge (3 km):** The main event — exposed ridgeline with panoramic South China Sea vistas. Benches at viewpoints. Crowds thin out along this section
3. **Descent to Big Wave Bay (3 km):** Can be slippery after rain. Path winds through village to beach with surfers and beachside cafe

### Getting There
- **MTR:** Shau Kei Wan Station, Exit A3
- **Bus:** No. 9 from Shau Kei Wan Bus Terminus toward Shek O; alight at To Tei Wan stop on Shek O Road
- **Alternative:** Red minibus marked "Shek O" from same terminus
- **Return:** Bus No. 9 from Big Wave Bay back to Shau Kei Wan. If first bus is full, walk uphill to catch the minibus

### Tips
- Start early morning on weekdays to avoid crowds
- One toilet facility along the route; no water refill points
- Finish with a swim at Big Wave Bay Beach (bring swimsuit)
- October–April offers the best visibility and temperatures

## Lantau Peak (鳳凰山) — Lantau Island

### Overview
Hong Kong's second-highest mountain at 934m, offering 360-degree views of Lantau Island and the Chinese mainland. A tough but rewarding hike that can be combined with visiting the Big Buddha.

### Trail Details
- **Distance:** ~5 km one-way from Pak Kung Au
- **Duration:** ~90 minutes up; descent options vary
- **Difficulty:** Tough — relentless climbing with steep, exposed summit
- **Start:** Pak Kung Au on Tung Chung Road

### Route Description
"Climbing basically never ends. Up and up you go." The summit features a steep and exposed rocky outcrop. On clear days, views extend to Hong Kong Island, Macau, and mainland China.

### Descent Options
1. **Same route back** to Pak Kung Au — safest option
2. **Continue to Ngong Ping** (shorter but with brutally steep large steps) — ends near Big Buddha (天壇大佛) and Po Lin Monastery for vegetarian lunch

### Getting There
- **Bus:** From Tung Chung MTR Station or Mui Wo Ferry Pier, take bus to Pak Kung Au
- **Cable car:** Ngong Ping 360 to Ngong Ping Village, then hike in reverse (uphill from Ngong Ping is steeper)

### Tips
- Pack plenty of food and water — no facilities on trail
- Popular for sunrise hikes; bring headlamp and warm layers
- Check weather: exposed summit is dangerous in wind, rain, or low visibility
- Can combine with Wisdom Path (心經簡林) — 38 poetry-inscribed wooden pillars near Ngong Ping

## Lion Rock (獅子山) — Kowloon

### Overview
A cultural symbol of Hong Kong representing the city's steadfast work ethic and resilience. The distinctive lion-shaped rock formation towers over Kowloon with sweeping views of the entire territory.

### Trail Details
- **Distance:** ~4 km to summit and back
- **Duration:** 2–3 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to challenging — steep stairs near summit
- **Start:** Sha Tin Pass

### Route Description
Follow the MacLehose Trail from Sha Tin Pass. At the 2km mark, turn sharply left up steep stairs to the summit. "Hong Kong stretches out in front of you" from the peak — Kowloon's dense cityscape on one side, Sha Tin's sprawl on the other.

### Getting There
- **MTR:** Wong Tai Sin Station, then taxi to Sha Tin Pass
- **Alternative:** Walk from Wong Tai Sin MTR (longer approach, adds ~30 minutes)

### Tips
- Multiple exit paths available — study the route beforehand and carry a charged phone
- The final scramble to the lion's "head" is optional and exposed — use caution
- Excellent sunset hike; allow time to descend before dark

## Pat Sin Leng Ridge / The Eight Immortals (八仙嶺) — New Territories

### Overview
Eight individual peaks on the Pat Sin Leng ridge forming the final section of the Wilson Trail. Lord Wilson, the penultimate British Governor of Hong Kong, described it as "very reminiscent of the ridges you get in the Highlands of Scotland."

### Trail Details
- **Distance:** ~10 km
- **Duration:** 4–5 hours
- **Difficulty:** Challenging — steep initial climb, exposed ridgeline with sheer drops
- **Start:** Hok Tau Wai Pavilion

### Route Description
Steep initial climb from the trailhead, then a breathtaking ridge walk with a sheer drop to one side and undulating peaks. The eight peaks are named after the Eight Immortals of Chinese mythology.

### Getting There
- **MTR:** Fanling Station
- **Minibus:** 56B from Fanling to Hok Tau Wai Pavilion
- **End point:** Near Tai Mei Tuk — local restaurants for post-hike meal

### Tips
- Exposed ridgeline is dangerous in wind or rain — check weather
- Bring plenty of water; no refill points
- Reward yourself with seafood at Tai Mei Tuk after finishing

## Additional Recommended Trails

### Kam Shan Country Park (金山郊野公園) — Kowloon
- **Access:** Taxi from Kowloon Tong or Sham Shui Po MTR
- **Features:** Wild monkeys (do not feed), steep hills with Victoria Harbour views
- **Duration:** 1–2 hours
- **Warning:** No lighting — do not stay after dark

### Garden Hill (嘉頓山) — Sham Shui Po
- **Access:** Walk from Sham Shui Po MTR
- **Features:** Short, steep climb to peak with illuminated Kowloon night views
- **Duration:** 20–30 minutes up
- **Best for:** Sunset/night photography over Kowloon

### Victoria Peak via Lugard Road (盧吉道) — Hong Kong Island
- **Access:** Peak Tram or bus to The Peak
- **Features:** Leisurely 90-minute shaded loop with unparalleled unobstructed panoramic harbor views — superior to the commercial Peak Tower observation deck
- **Tip:** About midway, take the right fork at a small park for the best clifftop viewpoints

### Victoria Peak Gardens (山頂花園)
- **Access:** From Peak Tower, walk left 10–15 minutes; look for barely-visible stairs
- **Features:** Nearly 360-degree panoramic views, typically experienced alone
- **Why go:** Peaceful hidden alternative to the crowded commercial Peak Tower

## Hiking Gear & Safety Checklist

- **Water:** 1.5–2L minimum; no refill points on most trails
- **Sun protection:** Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses — ridgelines are fully exposed
- **Footwear:** Grippy hiking shoes; trails can be slippery after rain
- **Phone:** Fully charged with offline trail maps downloaded
- **Food:** Energy bars, fruit — few/no facilities on mountain trails
- **Rain gear:** Quick afternoon showers possible even in "dry" season
- **Mosquito repellent:** Essential May–October

## Practical Tips

- **Best months:** October, November, December — cool, clear, dry
- **Worst months:** June–September — brutal heat, humidity, typhoon risk
- **Trail apps:** AllTrails (international), Trailwatch (HK government official app)
- **Emergency:** 999 from any phone; most trails have cell coverage
- **Post-hike food:** Many trails end near villages or transport hubs with local restaurants
- **Combining activities:** Dragon's Back + Big Wave Bay swimming; Lantau Peak + Big Buddha; Garden Hill + Sham Shui Po street food
