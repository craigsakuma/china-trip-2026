# The Best Hong Kong Dim Sum: Where to Find Yum Cha in the City

**Source:** Sassy Hong Kong (sassyhongkong.com), Hong Kong lifestyle and dining authority. Updated 2025.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Dim sum (點心)** literally means "to touch your heart" — a Cantonese tradition central to Hong Kong identity
- **Yum cha (飲茶)** means "drink tea" — the social ritual of eating dim sum with tea
- Hong Kong is the world's foremost dim sum destination, from HK$20 street-level to HK$1,000+ Michelin-starred
- Peak hours: weekends 10am–1pm; go early or late to avoid waits
- Trolley service (推車仔) is increasingly rare — Lin Heung Lau is one of the last

## Michelin-Starred & Award-Winning

### Duddell's (都爹利會館) — Central
- **Location:** 3/F, 1 Duddell Street, Central
- **Phone:** 2525 9191 | **Web:** duddells.co
- **Recognition:** Michelin star since 2013
- **Standout:** Popular yum cha brunch with free-flow Champagne option
- **Vibe:** Elegant art-filled dining room; as much about the scene as the food
- **MTR:** Central Station

### Seventh Son (家全七福) — Tsim Sha Tsui
- **Location:** 701 & 702, 7/F, H Zentre, 15 Middle Road, TST
- **Phone:** 2892 2888 | **Web:** seventhson.hk
- **Recognition:** Michelin Guide, Asia's 50 Best Restaurants
- **Focus:** Heritage Cantonese cuisine with premium ingredients
- **MTR:** Tsim Sha Tsui Station

### Rùn (潤) — Wan Chai
- **Location:** 2/F, The St. Regis Hotel, Wan Chai
- **Phone:** 2138 6808
- **Recognition:** Michelin star since 2022
- **Standout:** Seasonal ingredient-driven dim sum; vegetarian and gluten-free menus available
- **MTR:** Wan Chai Station

### Tim Ho Wan (添好運) — Multiple Locations
- **Web:** timhowan.com.hk | **Phone:** 2155 0001
- **Recognition:** World's cheapest Michelin-starred restaurant (Sham Shui Po branch)
- **Signature dish:** Baked char siu bun (酥皮叉燒包) — flaky pastry filled with barbecue pork; also truffle chicken variant
- **Price:** Under HK$100 per person
- **Tip:** New flagship branch is more polished and modern than the original

## Historic & Traditional

### Lin Heung Lau (蓮香樓) — Central (Relocating April 2026)
- **Current:** 160-164 Wellington Street, Central | 2156 9328
- **Moving to:** Tung Ning Building, 249-251 Des Voeux Road Central
- **TST branch:** 1-2/F Cheung Lee Commercial Building, 25 Kimberley Road
- **Signature:** Char siu bao (叉燒包), siu mai (燒賣)
- **Why go:** Century-old institution; one of the last restaurants using wheeled dim sum trolleys (推車仔). Chaotic, loud, communal tables — authentic old Hong Kong yum cha experience
- **Warning:** Relocating in April 2026 — check before visiting
- **MTR:** Central Station (current), Sheung Wan Station (new location)

### Luk Yu Tea House (陸羽茶室) — Central
- **Style:** Historic establishment with traditional yum cha atmosphere
- **Vibe:** Colonial-era interiors, old-school service, classic Cantonese preparations
- **Best for:** Experiencing dim sum as it was decades ago

## Budget & Local Favorites

### One Dim Sum (一點心) — Prince Edward & TST
- **Web:** onedimsum.hk
- **Philosophy:** Traditional classics without gimmicks — no fusion, no Instagram styling
- **Signature:** Turnip cake (蘿蔔糕), lo mai gai (糯米雞), egg twists with peanut-coconut topping
- **Price:** Extremely affordable; expect queues
- **MTR:** Prince Edward Station

### Dimdimsum (點點心) — Mong Kok & Jordan
- **Mong Kok:** 106 Tung Choi Street | 2309 2300
- **Jordan:** G/F Man King Building, 28 Wen Hui Street | 2771 7766
- **Known for:** Quality har gow (蝦餃), pineapple custard buns, affordable pricing
- **MTR:** Mong Kok or Jordan Station

### Dim Sum Square (點心坊) — Sheung Wan
- **Location:** G/F, 78 Jervois Street, Sheung Wan
- **Phone:** 2851 8088
- **Signature:** Spring rolls wrapped in rice rolls, siu mai, har gao, lo mai gai
- **Notable:** Strong vegetarian options
- **MTR:** Sheung Wan Station

### Sun Hing (新興食家) — Kennedy Town
- **Location:** Shop C, G/F, 8 Smithfield Road, Kennedy Town
- **Phone:** 2816 0616
- **Hours:** 3am–4pm daily
- **Signature:** Siu mai with mushroom, curry honeycomb tripe, salted egg yolk custard buns (奶黃流沙包)
- **Why go:** One of Hong Kong's few late-night/early-morning dim sum joints; genuine neighborhood gem
- **MTR:** Kennedy Town Station

### Duen Kee (端記) — Tsuen Wan (Tai Mo Shan)
- **Location:** 57-58 Chuen Lung Estate, Route Twisk, Tsuen Wan
- **Phone:** 2490 5246
- **Hours:** Closes 2pm
- **Concept:** Self-service dim sum in a village house setting near Tai Mo Shan
- **Standout:** Locally-sourced watercress dishes, homemade soybean pudding
- **Best for:** Combining with a Tai Mo Shan hike

## Practical Tips

- **Reservations:** High-end spots (Duddell's, Dim Sum Library, Rùn) accept bookings; budget spots are walk-in only with queues
- **Timing:** Weekday mornings are quietest; weekend brunch is the traditional social occasion but expect 30–60 minute waits
- **Ordering:** Most modern restaurants use paper checklists; tick dishes and hand to staff. At trolley restaurants (Lin Heung Lau), grab dishes as carts pass your table — be assertive
- **Tea:** Your pot will be refilled free; leave the lid ajar to signal you need more hot water
- **Price range:** HK$50–100/person (budget), HK$200–400 (mid-range), HK$500+ (upscale)
- **Tipping:** Not expected at casual spots; 10% service charge auto-added at upscale restaurants
