# Most Unique Places to Go in China

**Source:** Catherine Tour (findawayabroad.com), lived in China for six years starting 2015, originally from Mauritius. Published February 2022. Deep local knowledge from extended residence across multiple Chinese cities.

---

## Archaeological Ruins of Liangzhu City (良渚古城遗址) — Hangzhou, Zhejiang

UNESCO World Heritage Site inscribed in 2019, providing evidence that Chinese civilization started 5,000 years ago. The site features mountains, pampas grass, and rice fields in an expansive archaeological park setting.

- **Best time to visit:** Late October or early November, when the rice fields turn yellow
- **Recommended timing:** Visit at sunset — the author describes it as "magical"
- **Location:** Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province

## Guanyin of Nanshan (南山海上观音) — Sanya, Hainan Island

Statue of the Goddess of Mercy (观音), the 12th tallest statue in the world. Located within a large park on Hainan Island, often called "the Hawaii of China." The park offers stunning views, a peaceful atmosphere, gift shops, and food courts.

- **Location:** Sanya, southern tip of Hainan Island
- **What to expect:** Large park surrounding the statue with ocean views; plan several hours for the full experience
- **Author's note:** Even after years in China, this was a discovery — recommended by her father, underscoring that major attractions can fly under the radar

## Feng Xiaogang Movie Theme Town (冯小刚电影公社) — Haikou, Hainan Island

A film-and-architecture attraction on the outskirts of Haikou that integrates movie sets, historical architecture, and retail. Named after Chinese director Feng Xiaogang. Visitors can rent traditional Qipao (旗袍) dresses for photographs throughout the recreated historical streets.

- **Location:** Outskirts of Haikou, Hainan Island
- **What to do:** Walk through recreated historical street scenes, rent Qipao dresses for photos, explore the retail and food options
- **Author's opinion:** Feels like being "part of a movie set" — an "amazing time travel experience in ancient China times"

## Thousand Islands Lake (千岛湖) — Chun'an County, Zhejiang

A massive water reservoir with scattered islands, roughly two hours from Hangzhou. This is the source of Nongfu Spring (农夫山泉), one of China's most popular water brands. Hotels along the lake offer water activities and stunning views, with boat access to the islands.

- **Getting there:** Approximately two hours from Hangzhou by car or bus
- **What to do:** Boat trips to the islands, water activities, enjoy local restaurant cuisine
- **Author's tip:** Discovered through local recommendations rather than tourist guides; the authentic local restaurant food was a highlight

## Xixing Old Town (西兴古镇) — Hangzhou, Zhejiang

An authentic old town preserving slow-paced traditional Chinese life. Unlike commercialized old towns found across China, Xixing retains a genuine residential character — locals listening to radios, hanging laundry, playing Mahjong (麻将), and caring for animals in narrow alleys.

- **Location:** Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province
- **Why it stands out:** Not commercialized — a real neighborhood where people live, in stark contrast to tourist-oriented old towns
- **How it was found:** The author discovered it by zooming into Baidu Maps and exploring on foot

## Practical Tips

**Learn basic Chinese.** Most Chinese people cannot speak English, even in major cities. Language skills prevent tourist scams and enable meaningful conversations with locals.

**Use local apps for research:**
- **Dianping (大众点评)** — attractions, restaurants, and local reviews (China's Yelp equivalent)
- **XiaoHongShu / Little Red Book (小红书)** — social media with travel content (China's Instagram)
- **Gaode Maps (高德地图) and Baidu Maps (百度地图)** — zoom in to discover hidden spots not on Western mapping apps
- **Trip.com** — expat travel community with English-language reviews
- **Instagram hashtags** — search China-specific location tags for visual inspiration

**Stay flexible.** Abandon rigid itineraries. The author's best discoveries came from stumbling upon places rather than executing pre-planned routes.

**Explore by bus or bike.** Bus rides reveal urban architecture and local lifestyle that taxis and subways miss. Bike-sharing apps (available in all major cities) enable discovering hidden gems throughout megacities.
