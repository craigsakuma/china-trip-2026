# One-Month Itinerary for South & Southwest China

**Source:** Pete R. (bucketlistly.blog), solo traveler, travel blogger, and photographer with 11+ years of experience. Updated Feb 08, 2026. First-hand experience — the author followed this exact itinerary in October–November.

34-day nature-focused route through South and Southwest China: Macau > Hong Kong > Guangzhou > Guilin > Yangshuo > Xingping > Guiyang > Huangguoshu Waterfall > Xijiang > Dali > Lijiang > Shangri-La > Yading > Chengdu > Leshan > Jiuzhaigou > Huanglong > Fenghuang > Zhangjiajie. Emphasizes China's most breathtaking natural landscapes over typical tourist attractions like the Great Wall or Terracotta Army.

---

## Macau (澳门) — Days 0–1, 2 Nights

**Overview:** Starting point of the itinerary. Portuguese colonial heritage city with UNESCO sites, casinos, and distinctive architecture different from Hong Kong's British influences.

**Attractions:**
- **Ruins of Saint Paul's (大三巴牌坊):** Most iconic landmark. Visit early morning to avoid crowds; also beautiful at night when illuminated with lively atmosphere on the steps.
- **Monte Fort:** Historic Portuguese fortress from the early 17th century on a hilltop overlooking old town. Short hike up from the Ruins. Contains the Macao Museum inside.
- **Senado Square:** UNESCO World Heritage site connecting many top attractions via lively shopping street.
- **St. Dominic's Church:** Stunning pastel-yellow Baroque-style church near Senado Square.
- **Cathedral of the Nativity of Our Lady:** Another historic church in the area.
- **Mandarin's House:** Historic residential complex, once home to Zheng Guanying, 19th-century Chinese scholar. Beautiful decorations and artifacts.
- **A-Ma Temple (妈阁庙):** One of Macau's oldest temples, dating to 1488. Multiple shrines at different levels along a rocky hillside — hike to the top shrine.
- **Cotai Strip:** Las Vegas-style casinos with replicas of world landmarks (Eiffel Tower, Big Ben, Venice canals). Visit after sundown. Even non-gamblers will enjoy the outrageous architecture and light shows. Venice-themed shopping malls with fake canals and gondola rides. Expect 1–2 hours walking around.

**Food:** Ao Pu Restaurant — local spot near Senado Square, basement location. Spicy beef noodles, brisket sandwiches, egg tarts.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: London Hotel ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Caravel Hotel ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Mandarin Oriental, Macau ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Hong Kong:** High-speed ferry from Macau Outer Harbor to Hong Kong Macau Ferry Terminal. Bus #3A to harbor (25 min, 5 MOP/HKD). Ferry costs ~200 HKD, takes just under 1 hour. Book in advance.

## Hong Kong (香港) — Days 2–4, 3 Nights

**Overview:** 2 full days exploring. Vibrant mix of temples, modern skyline, and cultural landmarks.

**Day 1 Attractions:**
- **Tsim Sha Tsui (尖沙咀):** Vibrant shopping district with neon-lit Nathan Road, luxury malls on Canton Road. Good starting base.
- **Avenue of Stars HK:** Promenade along Tsim Sha Tsui waterfront, modeled after Hollywood Walk of Fame. Celebrates Hong Kong's film industry.
- **Nan Lian Garden (南莲园池):** Beautiful Chinese garden in Wong Tai Sin District at foot of Diamond Hill. "Feels more like Japan than Hong Kong." Quiet compared to rest of the city. Allow 1–2 hours. Reachable by metro.
- **Sik Sik Yuen Wong Tai Sin Temple (嗇色园黄大仙祠):** Large, highly revered Taoist temple. Colorful architecture, ornate carvings, landscaped gardens. Shopping mall next door for lunch.
- **Victoria Harbour:** Legendary skyline views at sunset. Arrive 1 hour before sunset. See Kowloon-Canton Railway Clock Tower nearby. Watch light shows from elevated bridge over pier.

**Day 2 Attractions:**
- **Victoria Peak (太平山):** Take a taxi up (20 min) instead of the tourist tram — tram queues can be hours long for a 10-minute ride. Skip Sky Terrace 428; go to Lions Pavilion instead for free identical views. Hike the Peak Trail for quieter experience.
- **Tian Tan Buddha / Big Buddha (天坛大佛):** Monumental 34-meter bronze statue on Lantau Island. ~2 hours from Central by metro + cable car. Include Po Lin Monastery visit. Buy cable car ticket in advance to skip long queues. Allow 2+ hours on site, plus 3–4 hours round trip. Cable car views are incredible — can see planes at nearby airport.

**Food:** Aberdeen Fish Ball King — fish ball noodles, multiple branches around Hong Kong. Author visited Fortress Hill branch.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Hi Backpackers ($, dorm)
- Mid-Range: Eco Tree Hotel Causeway Bay ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: The St. Regis Hong Kong ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Guangzhou:** High-speed train from West Kowloon Station to Guangzhou South Railway Station. ~30 USD, just over 1 hour. Arrive 2 hours early for immigration (both China and HK processed at West Kowloon). No further immigration check at Guangzhou. Take metro from Guangzhou South to city center.

## Guangzhou (广州) — Days 5–7, 3 Nights

**Overview:** First city in mainland China proper. 30-day visa clock starts here. 2 full days. Typical non-first-tier Chinese city feel.

**Day 1 Attractions:**
- **Chen Clan Academy (陈家祠):** Built 1894, Qing Dynasty. Academic and ancestral hall, now Guangzhou Folk Art Museum. Traditional Cantonese craftsmanship — embroidery, pottery, sculpture. Colorful ceramic carvings on the roof are a highlight.
- **Shamian Dao Island (沙面岛):** Historic enclave along Pearl River. Former 19th/20th-century foreign concession with European-style architecture, tree-lined avenues, colonial mansions. "Walking around, you almost forget you are in China." Peaceful at sunset with banyan and camphor trees, soft lamplight. Perfect first-night-in-China stroll.

**Day 2 Attractions:**
- **Moxing Mountain (摩星山):** 382 meters, highest point in Guangzhou. Half-day trip. Take Didi to Yuntai Garden, cable car to Baiyun Mountain Scenic Area (25 CNY up, 20 CNY down), then shuttle bus to summit (10 CNY each way). Panoramic views from two platforms — both worth seeing.
- **Beijing Street (北京路):** Famous 1.2 km shopping/cultural street built over archaeological ruins. Glass walkways let you see roads from Tang and Song dynasties underneath. Shops, restaurants, Cantonese snacks, street food.
- **Dafo Temple (大佛寺):** Significant Buddhist site near Beijing Street. Especially beautiful at sunset when lights turn on.
- **Canton Tower (广州塔):** 604-meter tower with twisting design. View from Flower City Square (reach via Huacheng Avenue Metro Station). Walk south to Canton Tower. Square has underground Mall of the World for food/drinks.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Yimi Hotel, Ximenkou Subway ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Yizhi Hotel, Beijing Road ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Kempinski Residences Guangzhou ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Guilin:** High-speed train from Guangzhou South to Guilin North. ~20 USD, ~3 hours. From Guilin North: bus (2 CNY, ~1 hour) or Didi taxi (~30 min).

## Guilin City (桂林) — Days 8–9, 2 Nights

**Overview:** Gateway to karst mountain scenery. 2 days including Longji Rice Terrace day trip.

**Day 1 Attractions:**
- **Seven Star Park (七星公园):** Largest park in Guilin. Hiking trails, rock formations, cave. Highlights: Seven Star Cave (stalactites/stalagmites), Camel Hill (karst formation), Star Pavilion — hike up staircases for incredible karst mountain views. Allow 2–3 hours.
- **Elephant Trunk Hill (象鼻山):** Famous elephant-shaped hill with trunk dipping into Li River. Natural arch creates moon-like silhouette reflection at evening. Very crowded in evening.
- **Sun and Moon Pagodas (日月双塔):** Twin Pagodas in Shanhu Lake, heart of city. Beautiful at night when illuminated and reflected on still lake.

**Day 2 — Longji Rice Terraces (龙脊梯田) Day Trip:**
- 2–3 hours drive from Guilin. Organized tour picks up at hotel ~7 AM, return ~4 PM. Tour cost ~288 CNY/person, separate entry fee 80 CNY.
- Scenery varies by season: spring (April–June) = mirror-like water reflections, summer = lush green, autumn (Sept–Oct) = golden harvest colors, winter = snow-dusted.
- Author visited mid-October: golden colors, dramatic cloud/sun interplay.
- Stop at Hongyao Stockaded Village (红瑶寨): Yao ethnic minority. Famous for woman with world's longest hair (5.627 m, Guinness Record). Traditional stilt houses. Commercialized but still worth a stop. Long hair performance.
- Tour operator texts WeChat the night before with pickup time and bus license plate.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Yue An Hotel ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: GangShe Rooftop View Hotel ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: LiCLOUDS HOTEL ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Yangshuo:** Li River Cruise — scenic 6-hour journey. Book at least 2 days in advance. Hotel pickup 7–8 AM, transfer to pier (~1 hour). Departs ~10 AM, arrives Longtoshun Wharf ~2 PM. 3-star cruise = 64 USD (author found quality difference between tiers not worth the premium). Includes comfortable seating, dry snacks, tea. Author spent 70% of time on deck photographing "hundreds, if not thousands" of karst mountains. WeChat notification night before with details.

## Yangshuo (阳朔) — Days 10–11, 2 Nights

**Overview:** Arrived via Li River Cruise. Commercialized but charming. Cobblestone streets, karst scenery, nightlife.

**Attractions:**
- **Yangshuo West Street (西街):** Main tourist strip. Shops, restaurants, lively at night with street food stalls.
- **Ruyi Peak (如意峰):** Author's #1 recommendation in Yangshuo. Cable car up to one of the highest karst mountains. 360-degree panoramic views. Glass bridge between two peaks (dramatic valley drop below), suspension bridge, large viewing platform. ~30 USD round-trip cable car. Go morning for fewer crowds. Allow 2–3 hours. Bring water. Premium prices for food on mountain. Rent e-scooter from corner of West Street (60 CNY/day) to reach it (45 min drive south).
- **Moon Hill (月亮山):** Another attraction reachable by e-scooter.
- **Bamboo Rafting:** From Shui'edi Pier to Gongnong Bridge.
- **Impression Liu Sanjie Show:** Large-scale evening cultural performance with dances, lights, music against karst mountain backdrop. Held by the river nightly.
- E-scooter range ~70 km, good for 3–4 hours of riding.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Shushe Hotel ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Tingshan Guanshui Branch ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Xitang River View Holiday Hotel ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Xingping:** Didi taxi, ~30 min, 20–30 CNY.

## Xingping Ancient Village (兴坪古镇) — Day 12, 1 Night

**Overview:** Smaller, quieter, more authentic than commercialized Yangshuo. Traditional wooden houses, cobblestone streets, stunning Li River views.

**Pre-dawn — Xianggong Viewpoint (相公山):**
- Organized tour picks up at 4 AM from Yangshuo hotel. Arrive by 5 AM for gate opening. 10-minute hike to viewing platforms. Wait 1–2 hours for sunrise (depending on season). "Rolling clouds below, countless karst peaks piercing through, set against purple and orange morning hues — felt almost like a Chinese painting." Return to Yangshuo hotel by 8:30 AM. Check out by noon.

**Xingping Attractions:**
- Well-preserved old streets, cafes, bakeries. Crowded during day with day-trippers; mornings and late evenings are best.
- **Lao Zhai Shan Hill (老寨山):** One of the most beautiful viewpoints in Guilin. Main reason to stay overnight in Xingping. Steep karst mountain next to village. Stunning views of Li River winding through karst peaks. Popular sunset spot. Hike is steep and challenging (hot/humid even in mid-October), ~30 minutes from trailhead without rest. Top has pavilion; scramble further up jagged karst for the iconic river-bend view. Be careful — path is narrow, rocks are sharp.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Bixi Hotel ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Yangshuo Autumn Inn ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Mercure Guilin Yangshuo Xingping ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Guiyang:** Didi or free hotel shuttle to Yangshuo Railway Station (~10–15 min). High-speed train to Guiyang, 3–3.5 hours, ~30 USD.

## Guiyang (贵阳) — Day 13, 3 Nights (base for day trips)

**Overview:** Capital of Guizhou province, "one of China's most underrated regions." City itself not remarkable but offers a glimpse of non-first-tier Chinese city life. Used as base for Huangguoshu Waterfall and Xijiang Village day trips.

**Attractions:**
- **Jiaxiu Tower (甲秀楼):** Historic pavilion built over Nanming River. Connected to both banks by scenic bridge. Main tourist attraction. Lively at night. Not much else to see in the city proper.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Sunlew Lychen Hotel, Guiyang North Railway Station ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Syria Mangju Village Hotel ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Yunbo Lijing Hotel ($$$, double room)

## Huangguoshu Waterfall (黄果树瀑布) — Day 14, Day Trip from Guiyang

**Overview:** Largest waterfall in Asia. 77.8 m high, 101 m wide, covering 120+ sq km scenic area.

**Getting There:** High-speed train from Guiyang North to Anshunxi Railway Station (~30 min, 6.65 USD). At station, turn right at exit — travel agency runs frequent shuttle buses to the waterfall (40–50 min, ~25 CNY one-way, pay with WeChat). Staff wear yellow vests.

**Three Areas to Visit:**
1. **Grand Waterfall Scenic Area:** The main waterfall. Take shuttle bus from entrance. 15–20 min hike down to viewing platforms. Climb back up is strenuous — bring water.
2. **Doupotang Waterfall (陡坡塘瀑布):** Smaller, charming waterfall downstream. Elevated path along the river.
3. **Tianxingqiao Scenic Area (天星桥景区):** Author's personal favorite. Dramatic karst formations, natural bridges, clear rivers. Massive Chinese Zen Garden feel with stone pathways where you hop stones across ponds. Includes a hidden cave system ("from outside looks small, but gets massive inside"). Trail ends at Yinlian Zhuitan Waterfall — not large but impressive due to water volume and proximity from cliffside trail.

**Timing:** Author arrived Anshunxi 9 AM, started park 10 AM, finished 4 PM. Full-day experience.

## Xijiang Ancient Village (西江千户苗寨) — Day 15, Day Trip from Guiyang

**Overview:** Largest and arguably most beautiful Miao ethnic minority village. Traditional Miao settlement in Leishan County.

**Getting There:** High-speed train Guiyang North to Kaili South Railway Station (~30 min, 8.20 USD). At station turn right — find carpool taxi agency. ~1 hour ride, 30–35 CNY one-way (pay driver with WeChat). Return: find carpool drivers at village entrance heading to Kailinan, negotiate 30–35 CNY.

**Attractions:**
- **Welcome Wine-Drinking Ceremony (十二道拦门酒):** "Twelve Rounds of Welcome Wine" at Visitor Center, ~10:30 AM. Miao people in traditional attire performing music and dances. 12 wine stations with rice wine.
- **Viewing Platform:** Take sightseeing shuttle to first stop. Incredible panoramic view of entire village from hillside — old rooftops, wooden houses stacked along hillside. "Most photogenic ancient village" the author has seen.
- **Village Streets:** 3 main streets — one through village, two along river. Shops, cafes, restaurants, museums, town squares. Commercialized but culturally rich.
- **Performances:** Traditional singing at No. 2 Wind and Rain Bridge (~11:30 AM), Lusheng performance at Old Lusheng Square, Miao batik demonstrations, cockfighting and bird-flying events on market days. "Miao have more events than days in a year."

## Dali (大理) — Days 16–17, 2 Nights

**Overview:** Start of Yunnan province — "one of the most beautiful and culturally rich regions in China" and author's favorite province.

**Getting There from Guiyang:** High-speed train Guiyang North to Dali Railway Station, ~5 hours, ~51 USD. Didi taxi to Dali Ancient Town (~30 min) or 5-CNY shuttle service following yellow signs.

**Day 1 Attractions:**
- **Chongsheng Temple and Three Pagodas (崇圣寺三塔):** Most iconic Dali landmark. Central Qianxun Pagoda is 69 m tall, 1,000+ years old. Two smaller pagodas 42.19 m each, 10 stories. Massive temple complex aligned facing east. 30-min walk to upper temple. Allow 1–2 hours. Closes 6:30 PM.
- **Dali Ancient Town (大理古城):** Visit after sundown. Former capital of Nanzhao and Dali Kingdoms. Commercialized but preserves traditional Bai architecture. Main streets: Fuxing Road, Renmin Road — Ming/Qing-style buildings, stone lanes, rivers. Foreigner Street (Huguo Road) — cafes, bars, art galleries, tie-dye shops. Street food haven in front of South Gate.

**Day 2 Attractions:**
- **Cangshan Mountain (苍山):** 4,000+ m peaks west of Dali. Good acclimatization hike for higher-altitude destinations ahead. Ximatan cable car ~20 min to upper station. Hike to highest viewpoint at 3,966 m — only 30 min but altitude is challenging. Buy oxygen canister and rent jacket at lower station. Half-day activity.
- **Xizhou Ancient Town (喜洲古镇):** Smaller, more rustic than Dali Ancient Town. 100+ well-preserved Bai courtyard homes. Sifang Street has shops, local snacks (Xizhou Baba — local dough pie), cafes, handicraft workshops. "Feels more like a market" — more authentic atmosphere. Allow 2–3 hours.
- **Erhai Lake (洱海):** Stunning freshwater lake, ~42 km north to south. Rent bike at Panxi S Bay (~30–40 CNY/day). Ride around lakeside until sunset.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: dali xianlai Inn ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Yunbo Light Luxury Homestay ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Yunduan Nanshe Homestay ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Lijiang:** High-speed train Dali to Lijiang, 2–3 hours, ~11 USD.

## Lijiang (丽江) — Days 18–20, 3 Nights

**Overview:** Beautiful cobbled lanes, wooden courtyard homes, flowing canals, snow-capped mountains. 2 full days with day trips.

**Day 1 — Lijiang Old Town (丽江古城):**
- One of China's best-preserved ancient towns. Maze-like layout with narrow alleyways, small bridges, quaint courtyards. Traditional Naxi architecture.
- **Sifang Street (四方街):** Central square. Nightly campfire dance ritual — Naxi cultural tradition where locals and travelers dance together.
- **Mu Fu Mansion (木府):** Residence of hereditary Naxi chieftains who ruled ~470 years (Yuan, Ming, Qing dynasties). Climb for city view from above.
- Beautiful at night with illuminated crisscrossing streets, performances, and shows.

**Day 2 — Yulong Snow Mountain (玉龙雪山) Day Trip:**
- 5,596 m peak, one of Yunnan's most iconic landmarks. Daily capacity limit — book cable car ticket at least 3 days in advance via Trip app. "Glacier National Park Cableway (with Shuttle Bus + Lijiang Impressions Show)" — ~285 CNY. Does NOT include 100 CNY park entry fee.
- Book shuttle bus separately: "Round-trip shuttle between Old Town of Lijiang and Yulong Snow Mountain + 1 oxygen bottle" — 46 CNY round trip. Two departures: 6:00–6:30 AM or 8:00–8:30 AM. Return bus ~3 PM. WeChat notification night before.
- 2–3 hours hiking at mountain. Then visit **Blue Moon Valley (蓝月谷)** — vivid turquoise rivers at foot of mountain, free shuttle within park.
- **Impression Lijiang (印象丽江):** Outdoor show by local Naxi people against Yulong backdrop. Runs 1:20 PM and 2:50 PM, ~1 hour. "Performance itself is nothing extraordinary, but the setting... is absolutely stunning."

**Day 3 Morning — Tiger Leaping Gorge (虎跳峡):**
- Stunning rapid gorge ~2 hours from Lijiang. Organized shuttle: 47 CNY one-way or 93 CNY round-trip. Half-day trip.

**Day 3 Afternoon — Baisha Ancient Town (白沙古镇):**
- Oldest Naxi settlement, ~20 min north of Lijiang Old Town. Smaller, more rustic, more authentic. Notable frescoes in Dabaoji Palace and Liuli Temple (Ming/Qing dynasties) blending Tibetan Buddhism, Taoism, and Naxi Dongba religion.

**Day 3 Evening — Black Dragon Pool Park (黑龙潭):**
- Crystal-clear lake reflecting Jade Dragon Snow Mountain on clear days. Northern edge of Old Town, accessible via riverside walking path.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Huaxu Inn ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: YinYue Sifu Designer Scenic Inn ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Yueji Homestay ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Shangri-La:** High-speed train Lijiang to Shangri-La, ~1.5 hours, ~10 USD.

## Shangri-La (香格里拉) — Days 21–22, 2 Nights

**Overview:** Formerly Zhongdian. Tibetan culture, dramatic landscapes, high-altitude town. Architecture resembles Himalayan/Tibetan region.

**Day 1 Attractions:**
- **Songzanlin Monastery (松赞林寺):** Largest Tibetan Buddhist monastery in Yunnan, nicknamed "Little Potala Palace." ~5 km north of town, 3,380 m elevation. 16,000+ sq m complex. Highlights: Zhacang Hall, main temple at highest point, Jikang Hall with murals and thangka art. Ticket: 75 CNY (includes shuttle bus). Take Didi to ticket office first. Monastery open 7:40 AM–6:30 PM, last admission 4:00 PM. Allow 1.5–2 hours.
- **Dukezong Ancient Town (独克宗古城):** Explore Guishan Park and the Great Prayer Wheel — world's largest prayer wheel. Too heavy for one person (author "couldn't even move it alone"). Requires several people to turn.
- **Sifang Street Square / Moonlight Square:** Evening guozhuang wu (锅庄舞), traditional Tibetan-style circle dance. Not a staged tourist performance — part of the town's evening social ritual. Multi-ethnic participation (Tibetan, Han, others). Join or observe.

**Day 2 — Potatso National Park (普达措国家公园):**
- Breathtaking nature reserve ~22 km NE of Shangri-La, ~3,500 m elevation. Alpine lakes, wetlands, forests, grasslands.
- Three stops: (1) **Youyou Budao Trail** — riverside walk, 45 min, spot yaks and horses. (2) **Shudu Lake (属都湖)** — alpine lake at 3,705 m, wooden boardwalks. Option: 2-hour hike or 15-min boat ride (50 CNY extra). (3) **Bitahai Lake (碧塔海)** — 15-min elevated walkway to lake.
- Scenic shuttle bus: ~243 CNY/person. Exchange email receipt at scenic bus stop. Buses depart 8:40 AM–1:00 PM, return after 1 PM, every 30 min. Back in town by 3 PM.
- **Important:** Buy bus ticket to Yading at Shangri-La bus station this afternoon. Cannot be booked online for foreigners. Two buses: ~8:20 AM and ~2 PM.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Luminous Gu Yu ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Shangri-La Yunbian View Homestay ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Z Hotel, Dukezong Ancient City ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Yading:** Bus from Shangri-La bus station, ~21 USD, ~6–6.5 hours. Buy physical ticket day before. Very bumpy on unpaved roads — avoid back seats if prone to motion sickness. One toilet break about 1 hour before arrival. Pack food and limit fluid intake.

## Yading (亚丁) — Days 23–24, 2 Nights

**Overview:** Author's favorite place on the entire itinerary. Snow-capped peaks, open grasslands, pristine lakes. Located in Daocheng County, Sichuan (not to be confused with Yunnan's Shangri-La). Town is called Shangri-La (Riwa/日瓦).

**Getting Around:** Carpool taxi from main road to park, ~15 min, 10 CNY/person. No advance tickets needed — no daily capacity limit. Entry ticket 266 CNY (includes unlimited scenic bus rides). Opening ~7 AM, no morning queue.

**Full-Day Hike Route:**
1. Shuttle bus from entrance to **Zhaguanbeng (扎灌崩)** — 45 min ride (sit on left for views).
2. Walk 20 min to **Chonggu Grasslands and Temple** — save for afternoon return.
3. Electric shuttle to **Luorong Cattle Farm (洛绒牛场)** — 70 CNY round trip. Stop at Shengshui Lake en route for snowy peak photos (calm morning water = perfect reflections).
4. At Cattle Farm: beautiful open grasslands with horses and cattle, snow-capped peaks surrounding. October visit = golden grass illuminated by sun.
5. Hike from Cattle Farm — flat scenic boardwalk. Horse hire available for 300 CNY to Gongacuo Lake but author says unnecessary; trail is flat, scenic, and enjoyable. 45-min walk.
6. **Gongacuo Lake:** Iconic postcard shot — almost-perfect triangular peak reflected in green lake.
7. **Steep ascent:** 4,000 m to 4,600 m on "steep staircases that seem to never end." Rest stops with oxygen refills. ~45 min.
8. **Qujiuzhaga Rest Stop** — intersection for Milk Lake and Five-Color Lake (connected in triangle loop).
9. **Milk Lake (牛奶海):** Larger, more photogenic. ~1 km flat trail from intersection.
10. **Five-Color Lake (五色海):** Great views of valley and hiked trail from above. ~20 min here.
11. Return to Cattle Farm, electric cart to **Chonggu Temple (冲古寺)** — Tibetan-style monastery with white exterior against snow-capped mountains.
12. Hike to **Pearl Lake (珍珠海)** — lowest holy lake at 4,100 m, 0.75 acres. ~1 hour hike. Finish ~3:30 PM.

**Accommodation (in Shangri-La Town/Riwa):**
- Budget: 7Days Inn Hotel, Daocheng ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Baiyue Mountain Lodge Hotel ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: All Seasons Hotel, Daocheng ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Chengdu:** Fly from Daocheng Yading Airport (DCY) — one of the world's highest airports at 4,411 m. Flight 1–2 hours, ~261 USD. Taxi from Riwa to airport: ~2 hours, ~400 CNY/car (share if possible). Book 10:30 AM flight, taxi pickup 7 AM. Incredible aerial views of Sichuan mountains (possibly Hailuogou Glacier and Siguniangshan). UFO-like airport exterior.

## Chengdu (成都) — Days 25–26, 5 Nights (base for side trips)

**Overview:** Base for 4 days of activities including overnight Jiuzhaigou/Huanglong trip and Leshan day trip. Book 5 nights to leave belongings during side trips — worth paying one overlapping night for convenience.

**City Attractions:**
- **Giant Panda Research Base (大熊猫繁育研究基地):** ~10 km north of downtown. Best time: 8–10 AM when pandas are most active/feeding. Also has red pandas, black-necked cranes. Big and crowded later in day. Metro/bus/Didi access.
- **Chunxi Road (春熙路):** Famous shopping street. Pedestrian strip with malls, brands, street food. Panda-climbing-building art installation, massive 3D LED screen.
- **Wenshu Monastery (文殊院):** One of oldest Buddhist temples in city, 1,400+ years.
- **Jinli Old Street (锦里):** Renovated historical area with lanterns, lights, outdoor restaurants, massive street food selection.
- **Kuanzhai Alley Scenic Area (宽窄巷子):** Best-preserved traditional street district in Chengdu.
- **Eastern Suburb Memory (东郊记忆):** Former Hongguang Electronic Tube Factory turned creative/cultural hotspot. Red-brick warehouses, old chimneys, vintage railway tracks. Great evening visit for Sichuan food at local restaurants.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Howdy Smart Hotel, Chunxi ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Orange Fruit Hotel, Kuanzhai Alley ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Home2 Suites by Hilton, Kuanzhai Alley ($$$, double room)

## Leshan (乐山) — Day 27, Day Trip from Chengdu

**Overview:** Home to the world's largest Buddha statue carved into a cliff face.

**Getting There:** High-speed train from Chengdu East/South/West to Leshan Railway Station, ~30 min, ~5 USD. Then Didi (~20 min) or local bus (~40 min) to scenic area.

**Leshan Giant Buddha (乐山大佛):**
- 71 meters tall, largest stone Buddha in the world. Carved into cliff at confluence of Min, Dadu, and Qingyi Rivers. Built over 90 years during Tang Dynasty (~1,200 years ago) by monk Hai Tong to calm turbulent waters — and the rock deposited during construction actually did help reduce turbulence.
- **Boat option:** ~20 min, great sense of scale, see hidden cliff carvings only visible from water.
- **Hiking option:** 2+ hours exploring statue and Buddha Park, descend to statue's foot. Extremely narrow staircases cause major bottleneck in peak season. Author visited in November on rainy day with short waits.
- Can do both if time allows.

**Return:** Train from Leshan to Chengdu East, ~45 min, ~65 CNY.

## Jiuzhaigou (九寨沟) — Day 28, 1 Night

**Overview:** Stunning valley of colorful lakes, waterfalls, and forests. Y-shaped park layout. Book train tickets AND park entrance at least 3 days in advance — both sell out fast.

**Getting There:** Train from Chengdu East to Huanglong-Jiuzhai Railway Station, ~2 hours, ~20 USD. Early 6:15 AM train recommended. At station, exit right to parking lot — bus drivers direct you. Bus to Jiuzhaigou ~2 hours, 51 CNY. Arrive park entrance ~10:15 AM.

**Park Route (Right branch recommended — more attractions):**
- **Swan Lake:** Starting point on right branch.
- **Arrow Bamboo Lake:** Altitude 2,629 m, 151,000 sq m. Year-round stable water level. Beautiful waterfall. Walk right up to water (no railings — be careful). ~30 min.
- **Five Flower Lake (五花海):** Author's highlight. Altitude 2,472 m, ~5 m deep, 90,000 sq m. Magical multicolored water from calcium carbonate, algae, and submerged plants.
- Walk (don't bus) from Five Flower to **Nuorilang Waterfall (诺日朗瀑布)** — 30 min on easy flat boardwalk. Most iconic sight in park. 2,365 m elevation, 320 m wide, 24.5 m drop. Very crowded.
- **Rhinoceros Lake:** Lower end of Y. (Alternative: shuttle from Central Station to left branch for Long Lake and Five-Color Pond if time allows.)
- **Reed Lake (芦苇海):** Near exit. Dense reeds, jade-colored "Jade Ribbon" brook dividing the lake.

**Accommodation:**
- Budget: Jiufeng Holiday Hotel ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: Starry Sky Hotel ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Rezen Retreat Four Seasons Manju Hotel ($$$, double room)

**Book for next day:** Shuttle from Jiuzhaigou to Huanglong — "Jiuzhaigou-Huanglong Attraction Shared Transfer," ~8 USD one-way, departures 7 AM–1 PM hourly from Moon Bay Parking Lot.

## Huanglong (黄龙) — Day 29, Return to Chengdu Same Night

**Overview:** World-class travertine terraces. Hiking trail through colorful pools, waterfalls, and temples at 3,200–3,600 m elevation.

**Getting There:** Shuttle from Jiuzhaigou, 2–3 hours. Book Huanglong entry ticket online in advance: ~170 CNY for hiking.

**Trail Highlights (ascending):**
1. **Welcome Pond (迎宾池):** Beautiful pools with crystal-clear water at ~3,230 m. First preview of what's ahead.
2. **Glowing Waterfall (飞瀑流辉):** ~10 m high, 60 m wide cascade. Dozens of tiered waterfalls shimmer like silver pearls.
3. **Lotus Terrace Waterfall (莲台飞瀑):** 3,240–3,270 m elevation, 167 m long, 19 m wide, 25 m drop.
4. **Mirror Reflection Pool (映秀池):** ~3,400 m elevation. 180+ pools. Pavilion over travertine. Water reflects snowy forest and mountains.
5. **Zhengyan Pools (争艳池):** 658+ colorful pools at 3,454 m. Second-largest cluster. Stunning orange travertine against snowy forest — one of author's favorites.
6. **Middle Temple (中寺):** Rest stop (inaccessible temple). Push through to Huanglong Temple for better views.
7. **Huanglong Temple (黄龙寺):** Larger rest stop. Beautiful courtyard, Buddha statues. Rare snowy landscape setting for a Chinese temple.
8. **Five-Colored Pond (五彩池):** "Most beautiful attraction in Huanglong." ~3,576 m. Largest and highest outdoor travertine pond cluster in the world. "How is this even real?"

**Return Options:** Hike back (~2 hours) or electric shuttle to Upper Cable Car Station + cable car down (~30 min). Find carpool taxi to Huanglong-Jiuzhai Railway Station (~30 CNY/person, ~30 min). Book return train for ~6 PM — author started hiking 10 AM, finished by 2:30 PM with plenty of buffer.

**Author timing:** Arrived and started hiking 10 AM, completed everything by 2:30 PM, train back to Chengdu ~6 PM, arrive 8–9 PM.

## Fenghuang Ancient Town (凤凰古城) — Day 30, 1 Night

**Getting There from Chengdu:** High-speed train from Chengdu East to Fenghuang Railway Station. 1 direct daily train at 10:20 AM, ~6.5 hours, ~70 USD. Arrive ~4:46 PM. Didi to town: 17–20 min, 20–25 CNY. Or shuttle bus from station: ~7 CNY, drops at Qifeng Square (walk to ancient town).

## Zhangjiajie National Park (张家界国家森林公园) — Days 31–32, 2 Nights in Wulingyuan

**Overview:** The "Avatar Mountains." Towering sandstone pillars, dramatic valleys, multiple mountain areas.

**Preparation:** Book entry ticket and cable car ticket before arrival. East Gate of park is 5 min walk from Wulingyuan. Start at 7 AM when park opens.

**Route (Cable Car first, Bailong Elevator last):**

**Tianzi Mountain (天子山):**
- Cable car up, ~25 min.
- **Helong Park (贺龙公园):** Imperial Brush Peak — pillar resembling a paintbrush tip, surreal in morning light. Fairy Scattering Flower Mountains — face east, perfect for sunrise. Start point for 10-Mile Gallery hike (save for later or return via shuttle).

**Yangjiajie Mountain (杨家界):**
- Shuttle from Helong Park, ~20 min. Skip Daguantai Bus Stop (accommodations only).
- **Tianbo Mansion (天波府):** Author's favorite viewpoint. Ten parallel stone wall peaks rising among trees, resembling city walls.

**Yuanjiajie Mountain (袁家界):** Most scenic area. Could skip Tianzi and Yangjiajie if short on time and focus here.
- **Greatest Natural Bridge (天下第一桥):** Rocky natural bridge connecting 2 peaks, 400 m vertical drop.
- **Five Ladies Expedition Rocks**
- **Hallelujah Mountain (哈利路亚山):** Inspired Avatar's floating mountains. Solitary vertical pillar. Author says "truly a sight to behold."
- Allow 1–2 hours for Yuanjiajie alone. Countless viewpoints along trails.

**Descent Options:**
- **Bailong Elevator (百龙天梯):** Fast but often crowded.
- **Golden Whip Stream (金鞭溪) hike:** Beautiful path following peaceful river, ~2–3 hours. Follow river east to end near Bailong lower station for shuttle to East Gate.
- **10-Mile Gallery (十里画廊) hike from Helong Park:** ~2.5 hours down. Stunning sandstone spires viewed from ground level. Light rail or walking along tracks — walking recommended.

**Accommodation (Wulingyuan):**
- Budget: Xiyuan Hostel ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: A Journey with Mountain, Wind & Hope ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: FUNGEE S Hotel ($$$, double room)

**Transport to Zhangjiajie City:** Carpool taxi ~30 CNY/person or Didi ~90 CNY/car, ~40 min. Go morning to drop bags and head to Tianmen Mountain same day.

## Tianmen Mountain (天门山) — Day 33, 1 Night in Zhangjiajie City

**Overview:** Absolutely incredible, must-visit even after Zhangjiajie Forest Park. Iconic natural arch "Heaven's Gate" (天门洞) rising 130 m. Glass skywalks on cliff edges. One of the world's longest cable cars.

**Three Routes (book in advance):**
- **Route A:** Cable car up to Upper Station > Middle Station via escalators > cable car down > bus to city. Books out fastest.
- **Route B (recommended):** Bus to Middle Station (Heaven's Gate) > cable car up to Upper Station > cable car down to city center. Best morning views — sea of clouds at Heaven's Gate.
- **Route C (not recommended):** Same path up and down, requires backtracking.

**Route B Details:**
- Follow Route B signs from city center cable car station. Bus 30 min to new cable car station. Cable car to Middle Station.
- **Tianmen Cave (Heaven's Gate):** 999 steps to the top (or pay for escalator — "but you're not here every day, so climb!"). Extremely steep. At top: stunning cavern, reservoir, mountain views.
- **Upper Station:** Winding trails, Tianmen Temple, Glass Bridge, many viewpoints. Allow 2–3 hours.
- Old cable car down to city center (~40 min).

**Evening — 72 Strange Buildings (72奇楼):**
- World's tallest stilted building (109.9 m). Central arch ~38 m. Cultural shows: traditional dances, drum shows, martial arts (Tujia culture). Night visit: 88 CNY (after 4:30 PM) or 48 CNY (before 4 PM). Open 8 AM–10:30 PM. Arrive ~6–7 PM, enjoy until ~10:30 PM.

**Accommodation (Zhangjiajie City):**
- Budget: Yunmengqi Boutique Inn ($, double room)
- Mid-Range: YICenXinSu Guesthouse ($$, double room) — author's top pick
- High-End: Wyndham Garden Zhangjiajie Tianmen Mountain ($$$, double room)

## Practical Tips

### Essential Apps & Setup
- **Alipay:** Download, link credit card. Contains mini-apps including Didi (ride-hailing). Connect Transport Card for metro/bus (requires passport verification).
- **WeChat:** Essential for communication (built-in translation), payments (works where Alipay doesn't, ~30% of the time). Setup requires verification from existing user — ask hotel reception to help verify upon arrival in China.
- **Trip.com / Trip App:** Book hotels, day trips, attraction tickets, train tickets, flights. Generous cancellation policy.
- **AMap (高德地图):** Google Maps doesn't work well in China. AMap is more accurate with metro information. Author used it the entire trip.
- **LetsVPN:** Install before arriving in China (cannot download VPN apps once in China). Free tier with limited data or 5.99 USD/month unlimited. Works well throughout China. Needed for Google, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, etc.
- **eSIM:** Get from Klook or Trip.com. Usually HK-based service, bypasses Great Firewall (access Instagram, YouTube without VPN).

### Passport
Always carry your passport. Required for attraction tickets (linked to passport), train boarding, and general ID purposes throughout China.

### Best Time to Visit
- **Best:** Spring (March–May) and Autumn (September–November) — comfortable weather, clear skies, scenic landscapes.
- **Autumn specifics:** Author visited October–November. Stunning fall colors. Jiuzhaigou was crowded but autumn colors at peak were worth it.
- **Summer (June–August):** Hot, heavy rainfall, can disrupt travel.
- **Winter (December–February):** Cold, some roads/scenic areas inaccessible. Not recommended.

### Getting to the Start
- Fly to Macau International Airport (MFM) via AirAsia.
- Or fly to Hong Kong, transit to Macau via Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge inside the airport (~65 HKD, ~45 min). During Golden Week may be free.

### Transport
- **High-speed trains:** Primary transport. Excellent network. Book via Trip.com.
- **Long-distance bus:** Used only once (Shangri-La to Yading). Cheap, slow, basic. Cannot book online as foreigner — buy at bus station day before.
- **Domestic flights:** Used once (Yading to Chengdu). Good time-saver but expensive. Warning: power banks without CCC certification will be confiscated at security.
