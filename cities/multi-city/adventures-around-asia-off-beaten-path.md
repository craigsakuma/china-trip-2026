# 10 Off-the-Beaten-Path Places to Visit in China

**Source:** Richelle (adventuresaroundasia.com), lived in China for six years, speaks Chinese. First-hand experience at all locations. Deep regional knowledge beyond the Beijing/Shanghai tourist circuit.

## Xi'an & Shaanxi Province (西安 / 陕西)

**Best for:** Adventure seekers, history buffs, food lovers | **Duration:** 2–3 days

Terracotta Warriors — life-size clay soldiers where every single one is individually different. Beyond the warriors: bike the ancient city walls, explore the Muslim Market (Huimin Jie / 回民街) for noodles and lamb kebabs. Nearby Hua Shan (华山) offers "the most dangerous hike in the world" with wooden platforms and rope lines.

## Chongqing (重庆)

**Best for:** Families, spicy food lovers, history buffs | **Duration:** 3+ days

Birthplace of hot pot cuisine. Key sites: Ciqikou (磁器口) old town street for traditional atmosphere, Dazu Rock Carvings (大足石刻) — UNESCO site with Tang Dynasty Buddhist carvings. Yangtze River cruises depart from here.

## Sichuan Province (四川)

**Best for:** Backpackers, nature lovers, adventurers | **Duration:** 5 days to 1.5 weeks

- **Chengdu:** Giant Panda Reserve — visit mornings when pandas are most active
- **Leshan Buddha (乐山大佛):** World's largest carved stone Buddha (71 meters tall)
- **Mt. Emei (峨眉山):** Sacred Buddhist mountain
- **Northern Sichuan:** Horse trekking on the Tibetan plateau
- **Jiuzhaigou (九寨沟):** "The most beautiful place I have ever seen" — crystal-clear turquoise pools reflecting mountains and clouds. UNESCO site.

## Zhangjiajie (张家界)

**Best for:** Hikers, Avatar fans, adventure seekers | **Duration:** 2–3 days

The "Hallelujah Mountains" that inspired Avatar's floating peaks. Three-day park pass for hiking. Tianmen Mountains (天门山) feature a hair-raising 30-minute cable car ride and glass-floor platforms overlooking vertical cliffs.

## Harbin (哈尔滨)

**Best for:** Winter travelers, festival lovers, culture buffs | **Duration:** 2–3 days

Ice and Snow Festival transforms the city into a winter wonderland with massive ice sculptures lit by colored lights. Russian cultural heritage shows in the architecture, food (sausage, borscht), and atmosphere. Siberian Tiger Park lets visitors observe tigers from elevated walkways.

## Macau (澳门)

**Best for:** Culture buffs, Hong Kong day-trippers | **Duration:** Day trip

Portuguese-influenced autonomous region with European architecture, casinos, and a distinct culture separate from mainland China. Easy day trip from Hong Kong.

## Guilin & Yangshuo (桂林 / 阳朔)

**Best for:** Outdoor enthusiasts, photographers | **Duration:** 4 days to 1 week

Guilin's famous karst limestone formations and golden rice terraces. Yangshuo is a smaller, more tourist-friendly base. Activities: bamboo rafting on the Li River, biking through countryside, hiking, visiting mud caves. Take the bamboo raft from Guilin to Yangshuo with your luggage for a one-way scenic trip.

## Yunnan Province (云南)

**Best for:** Hikers, backpackers, cultural explorers | **Duration:** 10 days

- **Dali (大理):** Picturesque mountain city near Erhai Lake. Cobblestone streets, artist shops, Bai minority March Fair festival.
- **Lijiang (丽江):** Jade Dragon Snow Mountain (玉龙雪山) accessible by cable car. Minority culture dance performances with mountain backdrop. Old town shops and spontaneous evening dances.
- **Tiger Leaping Gorge (虎跳峡):** Canyon surrounded by terraced farms and villages. "One of the most incredible" hikes — multi-day option with guest houses along the trail. Rushing river, zero light pollution, shooting stars.
- **Xishuangbanna (西双版纳):** Tropical region near Vietnam, good for plantation cycling.

## Xinjiang Province (新疆)

**Best for:** Cultural explorers, Silk Road enthusiasts | **Duration:** 10 days

- **Urumqi (乌鲁木齐):** Proto-Indo-European mummies with blonde/red hair in the regional museum. Local bazaars selling cloth and saffron.
- **Heavenly Mountains (天山):** Dramatic red cliffs and yurt stays.
- **Kashgar (喀什):** Predominantly Uyghur and Hui Muslim city housing the largest mosque in China. Known for lamb kebabs, naan, fresh honey, and authentic markets.
- **Yarkent (莎车):** Historic city with outdoor markets.

## Tibet (西藏)

**Best for:** Adventurers, culture seekers, photographers | **Duration:** 8–15 days

**Access:** Tours are mandatory. Author recommends custom-designed tours for flexible budgets.

- **Lhasa (拉萨):** Potala Palace, Buddhist temples, prayer flags
- **Route to Kathmandu:** 8-day overland journey through changing landscapes, ending in Nepal
- **Altitude prep:** Avoid caffeine/alcohol one week before. Drink water. Consider acclimatizing in Xining (西宁), Qinghai first, then take the train to Lhasa — one of the most beautiful train rides in the world.

## Practical Tips

- China extends far beyond Beijing and Shanghai — the western and southwestern provinces offer completely different experiences
- Sichuan and Yunnan are the strongest regions for extended independent travel
- Tibet requires mandatory guided tours; plan well in advance
- Xinjiang offers Silk Road culture distinct from Han Chinese mainstream
- Winter travel to Harbin requires serious cold-weather gear
- Altitude sickness is real for Tibet and parts of Sichuan/Qinghai — acclimatize gradually
