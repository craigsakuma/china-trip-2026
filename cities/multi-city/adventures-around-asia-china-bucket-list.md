# 50 Things for Your China Bucket List

**Source:** Richelle (adventuresaroundasia.com), lived in China for six years, speaks Chinese. Completed 40 of these 50 items personally. First-hand accounts throughout.

## 1. Hike the Great Wall (长城)

Multiple sections available. Skip touristy Badaling. Best sections: Mutianyu (restored, good facilities), Jinshanling (moderate crowds, great views), Gubeikou (wild/unrestored), Huanghuacheng (partially submerged in reservoir). Author visited 5 times. Camping on the wall is possible at unofficial sections.

## 2. Bike the Xi'an City Wall (西安城墙)

Ming Dynasty wall surrounding downtown. 1–2 hour bike circuit on top. Single bikes 45 RMB ($6), tandems 90 RMB ($13), 200 RMB deposit.

## 3. Eat Xiaolongbao in Shanghai (小笼包)

Soup dumplings — suck the broth before eating. Famous spot outside Yu Gardens has long lines. Available at restaurants citywide.

## 4. Wander Hangzhou's West Lake (西湖)

Marco Polo called Hangzhou "the most beautiful city." Boat rides, biking around the lake, Impression West Lake evening show. Features: Flower Pond, Leifeng Pagoda, willow-lined paths.

## 5. Harbin Ice and Snow Festival (哈尔滨冰雪节)

Massive ice buildings lit with colored lights. Sun Island for snow sculptures. Extremely cold — dress accordingly. Winter season only.

## 6. Visit Pandas in Chengdu (成都大熊猫基地)

Giant and red pandas at the Chengdu Panda Reserve. Successful breeding program. **Tip:** Visit mornings when pandas are most active.

## 7. Hike Longsheng Rice Terraces (龙胜梯田)

Near Guilin. Day hike between villages with guesthouse stays. Zhuang and Yao minority communities. **Best seasons:** April–June (water reflections on terraces) or September–October (golden harvest color).

## 8. See Urumqi Mummies

Proto-Indo-European mummies with blonde/red hair and Celtic-patterned cloth in the Xinjiang Regional Museum. Government museum design presents debatable historical framing.

## 9. Eat Roujiamo in Xi'an (肉夹馍)

Chinese pulled pork sandwich, ~$1 USD. Hole-in-the-wall shops are better than tourist-area vendors. Xi'an street food staple.

## 10. Beijing's Big Three — Forbidden City, Temple of Heaven, Summer Palace

Author prefers Temple of Heaven (天坛) and Summer Palace (颐和园) over the Forbidden City. Rent audio guides or hire English-speaking guides for context.

## 11. Ride Sleeper Trains

~$70 for 24-hour journeys. "Hard-sleeper" 6-bunk compartments recommended over soft sleeper (better value, more social). Best route: Xining to Lhasa — one of the most beautiful train rides in the world.

## 12. Terracotta Warriors (兵马俑)

One hour outside Xi'an. Life-size clay soldiers — every one individually different. Active excavation with archaeologists reassembling pieces. Three pits to explore.

## 13. Walk the Shanghai Bund (外滩)

Famous waterfront skyline. Daytime less crowded; nighttime more dramatic. Warning: aggressive crowds at night during peak season.

## 14. Take a Calligraphy Class

Learn Chinese characters with brushes and ink. TangBo Art Museum in Xi'an recommended. Learn the complex "biang" character (used for biangbiang noodles).

## 15. Raft the Li River (漓江)

Guilin to Yangshuo by bamboo raft among limestone karst cliffs. Take luggage for a one-way scenic transfer between cities.

## 16. Visit Potala Palace (布达拉宫)

Lhasa, Tibet. Built 1649 by the 5th Dalai Lama. Ornate Buddha rooms, golden stupas. **No interior photography allowed.**

## 17. Attend a Chinese Wedding

Excessive food, spotlights, multiple bride outfit changes, cake that's never eaten. Thousands of guests at major weddings. A cultural spectacle.

## 18. Get Lost in Beijing Hutongs (胡同)

Best neighborhoods: Gulou (鼓楼), Lama Temple (雍和宫), Andingmen (安定门). Hidden alleyway streets with cafes, bars, hole-in-the-wall restaurants. Guided tours: Context, UnTour, Lost Plate. Skip overcrowded Nanluoguxiang.

## 19. Brave the Hua Shan Plank Walk (华山)

"Most Dangerous Hike in the World." Wooden planks bolted to cliff face, flimsy harness, thousand-foot drops. Hair-raising cable car ride included. Near Xi'an.

## 20. See the Giant Leshan Buddha (乐山大佛)

Outside Chengdu. 233 feet (71 meters) tall — world's largest carved stone Buddha. Nearby Oriental Buddhist Park with caves and gardens.

## 21. Watch a Minority Culture Show

Jade Dragon Snow Mountain, Lijiang, Yunnan. Yunnan minorities perform traditional dances with snow-capped mountain backdrop. Group dances, costumes, horses.

## 22. Pose with Mao in Tiananmen Square (天安门广场)

Selfie with the giant Mao portrait. **Bring passport** — required for entry.

## 23. Hike Zhangjiajie Avatar Mountains (张家界)

Inspired Avatar's floating peaks. Three-day park pass. Glass elevator to the Avatar Hallelujah Mountain area. Heavenly Gate hike with cable car and glass-floor platforms.

## 24. Survive Sichuan Spice

Sichuan/Chongqing food is significantly spicier than anywhere else in China. Build tolerance gradually. Keep Imodium handy. Chinese pharmacies sell stomach medicine (traditional remedy: 15+ pills daily).

## 25. Hang Prayer Flags at Yamdrok Tso (羊卓雍措)

Turquoise Lake, Tibet. Write your name on prayer flags for health and wellness blessings. Deeply spiritual experience at altitude.

## 26. Boat on Qinghai Lake (青海湖)

Outside Xining. China's largest lake at 10,482 feet altitude. Yellow rapeseed flower fields, crystal blue water, explorable islands. Good acclimatization stop before Tibet.

## 27. Nanjing Massacre Memorial Museum (侵华日军南京大屠杀遇难同胞纪念馆)

WWII atrocity documentation. Heavy but essential. **Visit weekday off-season** for a contemplative, less crowded experience.

## 28. Eat Peking Duck in Beijing (北京烤鸭)

Crispy skin sliced tableside. Served with thin pancakes, scallions, cucumber, sweet bean sauce. Premium versions include pop rocks and blueberry sauce for the crispy skin pieces.

## 29. Hike Tiger Leaping Gorge (虎跳峡)

Northern Yunnan. Full-day or multi-day hike with guest houses along the gorge. Rushing river, zero light pollution, visible shooting stars at night.

## 30. Forbidden City View from Jingshan Park (景山公园)

2 RMB ($0.30) entry. Climb the hill for an aerial view over the entire Forbidden City. Best on clear days.

## 31. Stuff Your Face in Xi'an's Muslim Quarter (回民街)

Evening visits best — blue-lit trees, sunset photography. Lamb carcasses hanging in stalls, nut-pounding vendors, fresh spices. Street food paradise.

## 32. Try Fried Scorpion

Wangfujing Snack Street (王府井小吃街), Beijing. ~$1 USD. Visit non-peak times for shorter waits. Primarily for the photos and bragging rights.

## 33. Explore Mogao Caves (莫高窟)

Dunhuang, Gansu. Buddhist cave complex on the ancient Silk Road. 2,345 caves with up to 100,000 statues. Buddhas show evolution from Indian to Chinese artistic style over centuries.

## 34. Wander Dali Old Town (大理古城)

Yunnan. Cobblestone streets, artist shops, mountain backdrop. City Gate, Wuhua Tower, ancient temple, Catholic church. Bai minority March Fair festival in March.

## 35. Eat Russian Food in Harbin

Beef stroganoff, Russian bread, borscht, sarma (cabbage-wrapped meat). Harbin sausage is a Russian-influenced local specialty. Unique cultural fusion.

## 36. Hike Jiuzhaigou (九寨沟)

UNESCO site, northern Sichuan. "The most beautiful place I have ever seen." Turquoise pools with mountain and cloud reflections. Nearby: Huanglong (黄龙) for travertine terrace formations.

## 37. Eat Jianbing from a Street Vendor (煎饼)

Beijing street food staple. Crepe-like pancake with egg, lettuce, fried wonton, scallions, cilantro, tangy sauce. ~$1 USD. Perfect budget breakfast or lunch.

## 38. Ride a Camel in the Gobi Desert

Outside Dunhuang, Gansu. Camel rides through desert dunes. Hike up sand dunes (difficult — sand slides while climbing), slide down on descent. Learn desert history and culture from guides.

## 39. Eat Hot Pot in Chongqing (重庆火锅)

Spicy broth with thin meat slices, vegetables, tofu, noodles. Dip cooked items in peanut or garlic sauce. **Order two-broth pots** (spicy and non-spicy sides). Chongqing/Sichuan versions significantly spicier than Beijing.

## 40. Ride Horses on the Tibetan Plateau

Songpan (松潘), en route to Jiuzhaigou/Huanglong. 1–3 day treks with Tibetan guides who provide meals. Warm clothing essential. Ice Mountain three-day trek is the target.

## 40.5 Walk the Friendship Bridge: Tibet to Nepal

Tibet-Nepal border crossing. Orderly Chinese side contrasts with chaotic Nepalese side. Visa processing is an adventure in itself.

## 41. Explore Longmen Grottoes (龙门石窟)

Luoyang, Henan. UNESCO site. Buddhist art across 2,345 caves with 100,000 statues ranging from 1 inch to 57 feet tall.

## 42. Hike Yellow Mountain / Huangshan (黄山)

Anhui province. Named after the Yellow Emperor. Towering pines, dramatic rock formations, rolling mist. Sunrise viewing is legendary. Mountain hot springs available.

## 43. Drive the Karakoram Highway

Xinjiang to Pakistan border, 1,300 km. Also called the China-Pakistan Friendship Highway. Highlights: White Sands Lake, Taheman Grasslands, Subash Pass.

## 44. Visit Everest Base Camp (from Tibet side)

Watch sunset from the Chinese side. Postcard available from the world's highest post office. Camp under the stars.

## 45. Take a Yangtze River Cruise (长江)

Route from Chongqing downstream. Off-the-beaten-path river views through gorges. Author recommends booking a quality Western-operated cruise over budget Chinese options.

## 46. Visit the Ming Tombs in Beijing (明十三陵)

Imperial tombs outside Beijing. Often overlooked by tourists and even residents.

## 47. Camp in a Yurt in Inner Mongolia (内蒙古)

Horseback riding and traditional Mongolian culture experience on the grasslands. Best in summer.

## 48. Drink Tsingtao in Qingdao (青岛啤酒)

Coastal city where the beer originates — tastes better at the source. Beach drinking culture. Time your visit for the Qingdao Beer Festival.

## 49. Visit Heavenly Lake at Changbai Mountain (长白山天池)

Mountain crater lake. Rare wildlife area (Siberian tigers), hot springs, giant forests.

## 50. Play Ice Games on a Frozen Beijing Lake

Houhai Lake (后海), Beijing. Winter activities: ski-pole bench pushing, ice bicycles. Uniquely Chinese winter entertainment.

## Practical Tips

- **Crowds:** Avoid National Week (first week of October) — massive domestic tourism surge
- **Spice tolerance:** Build up gradually; Sichuan/Chongqing are far spicier than northern China
- **Altitude:** Train to Lhasa for acclimatization; Qinghai Lake is a good intermediate stop
- **Photography:** Potala Palace bans interior photos; Jiuzhaigou demands a quality camera
- **Transport:** Sleeper trains are efficient and affordable — hard-sleeper compartments recommended
- **Budget street food:** Roujiamo, jianbing, and fried scorpion are all ~$1 USD
