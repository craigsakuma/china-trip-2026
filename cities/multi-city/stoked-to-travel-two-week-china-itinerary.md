# China Itinerary: The Perfect Two Week Itinerary

**Source:** Stoked to Travel (stokedtotravel.com), UK travel and lifestyle blog by Claire. Published May 22, 2020; updated February 9, 2026. First-hand experience, independent travel in July.

**Route:** Beijing → Great Wall (day trip) → Suzhou → Shanghai → Zhangjiajie → Tianmen Mountain → Yangshuo → Chengdu
**Duration:** 14 days
**Style:** Independent / self-guided. Author notes China is "the most challenging place I have travelled around" due to language barriers and logistics.

---

## Days 1–3: Beijing (北京)

**Key Attractions:**
- **Forbidden City** — imperial palace, 500+ year history; plan half a day
- **Tiananmen Square (天安门广场)** — free entry; flag-raising ceremony at dawn and flag-lowering at dusk worth attending
- **Jingshan Park (景山公园)** — free entry; climb the hill for 360° panoramic city views (pairs with Forbidden City visit)
- **Hutongs** — wander Nanluoguxiang (南锣鼓巷) and Wudaoying Hutong (五道营胡同) for old Beijing character
- **Wangfujing Street (王府井)** — evening street food market; adventurous snacks available
- **Beijing Olympic Park** — Bird's Nest (鸟巢) and Water Cube (水立方); worth seeing from outside
- **798 Art District (798艺术区)** — galleries in converted factory complex; free to wander

**Transport within Beijing:**
- Subway strongly recommended: modern, clean, 2–5 CNY per ride (~20–50p)
- Avoid buses — language barriers make them confusing for non-Chinese speakers
- Taxis affordable but drivers rarely speak English; carry hotel business card with Chinese address

## Day 3: Great Wall of China (长城) — Day Trip from Beijing

**Recommended Section:** Mutianyu (慕田峪) — less crowded than Badaling, cable car up, optional toboggan/luge descent.

**Other Sections Mentioned:** Jiankou (箭扣), Simatai (司马台), Gubeikou (古北口), Jinshanling (金山岭), Huanghuacheng (黄花城) — all quieter but require longer travel from Beijing.

**Public Transport to Mutianyu:**
1. Subway to Dongzhimen Station (东直门)
2. Bus #916 from Dongzhimen Bus Station → Huairou (怀柔)
3. Local taxi from Huairou → Mutianyu entrance

**Author's Take:** "Getting there by public transport proved fiddly" but arriving early meant fewer crowds and an incredible experience. Tickets available at the gate. Cable car included in the standard tourist route.

---

## Day 4: Suzhou (苏州) — "Venice of the Orient"

Transit stop between Beijing and Shanghai. Known for classical gardens and canal waterways.

**Warning:** Multiple stations with "Suzhou" in the name — double-check your booking is for the correct Suzhou (the famous waterway city, not a remote location with a similar name).

---

## Days 5–6: Shanghai (上海)

**Key Attractions:**
- **The Bund (外滩)** — iconic waterfront promenade facing the Pudong skyline; best at night
- **French Concession (法租界)** — tree-lined streets, cafés, boutiques
- **Gardens and temples** — scattered across the city

**Shanghai Maglev Train (磁悬浮列车):**
- World's fastest commercial train: magnetic levitation, 430 km/h
- Route: Longyang Road Station (龙阳路) → Pudong Airport (浦东机场)
- Distance: 30 km in 8 minutes
- Cost: 50 CNY (~£5.75)
- Frequency: every 15–20 minutes
- Tickets from machines at Longyang Road Station

**Transport:** Subway system excellent — English signage, modern, clean, frequent.

---

## Days 7–8: Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (张家界国家森林公园)

Otherworldly sandstone pillar landscapes (inspiration for Avatar). Multiple hiking routes available; entrance fee required.

**Accommodation:** Limited to guesthouses in the area — book ahead.

**Dining:** Short restaurant street in town; author says "pick one and hope for the best" — limited options, especially for non-Chinese speakers.

**Author's Experience:** Found locals less willing to help with route-finding here; navigation was challenging. Independent travel requires patience.

---

## Day 9: Tianmen Mountain (天门山)

Dramatic cliff-face scenery accessed by cable car. Known for dizzying glass skywalks and the natural arch "Heaven's Gate" (天门洞).

---

## Days 10–12: Yangshuo (阳朔) — Author's Favorite

Charming riverside town surrounded by karst limestone peaks. Author calls it her "most personal favorite place in China."

**Highlights:**
- Longji Rice Terraces (龙脊梯田) — day trip from Yangshuo area
- Li River scenery
- Summer mango season: smoothie stands everywhere with "delicious mango drinks available all day"

**Accommodation:** Various guesthouses available in town.

---

## Days 12–14: Chengdu (成都)

**Key Attractions:**
- **Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding (成都大熊猫繁育研究基地)** — primary draw; go early morning when pandas are active
- **Chunxi Lu (春熙路)** — main shopping and pedestrian street
- **Leshan Giant Buddha (乐山大佛)** — day trip from Chengdu; world's largest stone Buddha

**Author's Note:** Visited in July; experienced heavy rain. Summer weather in Sichuan can be wet.

---

## Transport Between Cities

**Flights:**
- Most efficient for long distances across China
- Major carriers: China Southern, Air China, China Eastern, Shanghai Airlines, Sichuan Airlines
- Suggested routing: fly into Beijing, fly out from Chengdu

**High-Speed Rail (高铁):**
- Speeds up to ~350 km/h (author says "400mph" but actual top speed is ~350 km/h)
- Book at www.12306.cn (English interface available)
- Payment via Alipay
- Arrive at stations early — they are enormous and security checks take time
- Tickets can be delivered/stored via Alipay

**General Taxi Tips:**
- Affordable throughout China
- Drivers rarely speak English — always carry destination written in Chinese characters
- Some drivers reluctant to pick up foreign tourists

## Practical Tips

**Visa:** Most nationalities need a visa (apply at Chinese embassy in advance). As of February 2025, UK citizens may visit up to 30 days visa-free — check current rules before booking. Passport must be in good condition.

**Internet & VPN:** Facebook, YouTube, Instagram, Google all blocked. VPN essential — ExpressVPN, Surfshark, or NordVPN recommended. Download and configure before arriving in China. Local SIM cards available at airport (30-day data plans).

**Best Time to Visit:** Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) are optimal. Summer (July–August) brings extreme heat (author experienced 45°C / 113°F), humidity, and crowds. Winter is cold with possible snow. Avoid Chinese New Year (January/February) — massive domestic travel surge.

**Language:** Mandarin is the official language; English is uncommon even in major cities. Google Translate app is essential (download offline Chinese pack before arrival since Google is blocked without VPN). Author describes language barrier as "the most challenging" aspect of China travel.

**Food & Costs:** Generally affordable except at major tourist attractions. International restaurants available in big cities; rural areas have limited options and menus in Chinese only. Beer widely available and cheap.

**Dress Code:** Author advises women to avoid excessive skin exposure in some areas; cultural norms vary by region.

**Alternative Destinations Mentioned (not in this itinerary):** Xi'an (西安), Hua Shan (华山), Jiuzhaigou Valley National Park (九寨沟).
