## The Glass Bridge Experience

### What to Expect
Walk across 430 meters of transparent glass panels suspended 300 meters above the canyon floor. The composite glass is engineered to withstand extreme force — a BBC reporter famously failed to break a panel with a sledgehammer. Free shoe covers provided at entrance to protect the glass surface.

### Restrictions on the Bridge
- **Allowed:** Cell phone with case, small purses, waist bags
- **NOT allowed:** Backpacks, professional cameras, selfie sticks, tripods
- **Storage:** Lockers available before bridge entrance area
- **Health:** Not recommended for those with heart disease, high blood pressure, acrophobia, or intoxicated visitors

## Visiting Routes

### Route A: Canyon Only (2 hours)
Explore the grand canyon without the glass bridge. Descend through cliff walkways, waterfalls, streams, and lakes. Scenic but physically demanding with steep stairs.

### Route B: Glass Bridge + Summit + Canyon (3 hours, Recommended)
Start at glass bridge → cross to far side → descend through canyon trails → cliff-hanging walkways → exit at canyon base. Best combined experience.

### Route C: Glass Bridge Only (1 hour)
Walk across the bridge and return. Best for time-limited visitors or those who only want the glass bridge thrill.

## Best Times to Visit

### Optimal Months
April, May, September, October — mild weather, clear glass visibility, manageable crowds (except Golden Week Oct 1-7).

### Weather Considerations
- **Clear sunny days** provide best glass transparency and canyon views
- **Rain/fog/wind** significantly reduce visibility and glass transparency
- **Severe weather** (high winds, torrential rain, heavy snow, hail) causes temporary closure
- Check weather forecast and closure status before visiting

### Time of Day
- **Morning (9:00-11:00):** Fewer crowds, better light
- **Midday (11:00-14:00):** Peak crowds, hot in summer
- **Afternoon (14:00-15:30):** Thinning crowds, good canyon light

## Practical Tips

- **Footwear:** Comfortable shoes with grip; canyon trails include steep, sometimes slippery sections
- **Vertigo:** Many visitors feel nervous at first but adapt quickly; the glass is genuinely solid
- **Photography:** Phone cameras only on bridge; take your best shots from the center where height is most dramatic
- **Professional cameras:** Leave in lockers; phone photos are sufficient for the bridge
- **Canyon trail:** Bring water; the 2-3 hour canyon portion is physically demanding with many stairs
- **Combine with:** Wulingyuan scenic area (stay in Wulingyuan District for easiest access to both)
- **Avoid:** National Holiday (Oct 1-7) and Labor Day periods — extreme crowds
- **October timing:** After Oct 7, crowds drop significantly. Mid-October offers excellent conditions
- **Half day:** Glass bridge alone is a half-day outing; bridge + canyon is a full day
