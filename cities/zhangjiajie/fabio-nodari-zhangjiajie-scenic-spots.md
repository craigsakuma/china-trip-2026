# Zhangjiajie — Travel Guide to the Scenic Spots

**Source:** Fabio Nodari (fabionodariphoto.com), travel photographer based in China. Published May 30, 2025. Updated Feb 8, 2026. All first-hand experience. Fabio has mixed feelings — "otherworldly" landscapes but overcrowded, overdeveloped, and overpriced management.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Hunan Province (湖南省). "Zhangjiajie" can mean: the city (~30 km from park), the National Park, or the National Forest (Avatar mountains). Confusing naming.
- **Fame:** Inspired the floating mountains in Avatar (Heavenly Pillar). Towering sandstone pillars, often wrapped in mist, subject of traditional Chinese paintings for centuries.
- **UNESCO:** Wulingyuan Scenic Area listed 1992 (690 sq km). Zhangjiajie Geopark listed as UNESCO Global Geopark 2004.
- **Best time:** Spring or Fall. April probably best. Nov–Apr: fewer rain, ~8°C. AVOID national holidays at all costs (2-hour queues for elevators).
- **Minimum stay:** 3 days / 2 nights. Park ticket valid 4 consecutive days.
- **Budget:** Over 1,300 RMB if visiting all areas individually. Buy packages on Trip.com, book days in advance.

## Getting There and Where to Stay

Well-connected by plane, train, and bus. Bus from Zhangjiajie train station to Wulingyuan: ~40 minutes. Didi also available. Stay near the National Forest Park entrance (Pullman Hotel is convenient) OR in Zhangjiajie City (better for Tianmen Mountain). Trip.com or Booking.com for hotels at all budgets.

## Wulingyuan Scenic Area: Overview

Four parts: Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, Suoxi Valley Nature Reserve, Tianzi Mountain Nature Reserve, Yangjiajie Scenic Area. Check what's included in your ticket — some bundles exclude Bailong Elevator, cable cars, and internal transport.

## Huangshizhai (黄石寨)

"Yellow Stone Village" — first area developed for tourists, slightly fewer visitors than Yuanjiajie. Hike up (2–3 hours) or take scenic cable car. Loop trail at top: 2–3 hours depending on photo stops. Don't miss Five Fingers Peak, the landmark of Huangshizhai. After descending, take shuttle bus to Jinbianxi / Golden Whip Brook (金鞭溪) — beautiful 7.5 km trail through the heart of the sandstone pillars.

## Tianzi Mountain (天子山)

Smallest area (67 sq km) but most iconic peaks. Sandstone peaks often partially hidden in fog, possible sea of clouds. Highest peak: 1,262.5m. Three sections: Helong Park (贺龙公园) with the famous Imperial Writing Brush Peak (Yubi Peak), Shentang Bay (神堂湾), and Sanchakou (三岔口).

## Yuanjiajie (袁家界)

The "Avatar mountains" area. Beautiful peaks covered with lush vegetation. Access via Bailong Elevator — claimed world's highest and heaviest outdoor elevator, 2 minutes (vs 2-hour hike before 2002). Shuttle bus to elevator, then another shuttle to trail start. Loop trail with many scenic viewpoints. Spectacular.

## Yangjiajie (杨家界)

Northwest of Forest Park. Less crowded, more off-the-beaten-path. Fortress-like sandstone pillars. Key spots: Natural Great Wall (天然长城) — dramatic row of towering peaks. One Step to Heaven (一步登天) — small viewing deck reached by very narrow iron staircase (one person at a time). Clouds often surround the mountains.

## Suoxi Valley: Ten-Mile Gallery and Yellow Dragon Cave

**Ten-Mile Gallery / Shili Hualang (十里画廊):** 5.8 km valley in northeastern Wulingyuan. Walk or take electric monorail. Monkeys at the end.

**Yellow Dragon Cave / Huanglong Dong (黄龙洞):** 7.5 km long, 140m deep, largest karst cave in China. Underground river, ponds, stalagmites illuminated with "disco lights" as in every tourist cave in China. Fabio's verdict: "not worth a visit." Jiuxiang caves in Yunnan are better. Crowded, little natural feel left.

## Glass Bridge and Grand Canyon (张家界大峡谷玻璃桥)

About 1 hour from National Forest Park. Activities: bungee jumping off glass bridge, via ferrata, zipline, slide descent. 4 km scenic canyon trail — peaceful, not crowded, reminded Fabio of Vintgar Gorge in Slovenia but more spectacular. Only visit if you have extra time (half-day commitment).

## Tianmen Mountain (天门山)

10 km south of Zhangjiajie City, 1,518m elevation. Cable car or bus access. Fantastic views from top. Glass bridges and wooden paths connect peaks. At least half a day to explore. 30 escalators totaling 897 meters (one of world's longest escalator systems) to/from Tianmen Gate. Pro tip: buy VIP ticket if cable car queues are long.

## Shows and Rafting

**Charming Xiangxi (湘西大剧院):** Focuses on Tujia, Bai, and Miao minorities. Two parts (indoor theater + outdoor). Second part is basically a sales pitch — leave after first part.

**Mount Tianmen Fox Fairy (天门狐仙表演):** "One of the best shows I've seen in China." Outstanding choreography. Doesn't usually run Jan–Feb. Get VIP ticket if raining (covered seats).

**Maoyan River Rafting (茅岩河):** About 2 hours from Zhangjiajie, not popular yet. Beautiful mountains, local people on boats, no tourists, surrounded by nature. Wear a swimsuit — you'll get completely soaked despite what guides say.

## Practical Tips

- **VPN:** Essential. ExpressVPN and NordVPN don't work.
- **Crowds:** Always crowded year-round. Infrastructure (cable cars, elevators, escalators) means 98% of visitors never walk, so trails are often empty.
- **Combine with:** Furong Ancient Town, Fenghuang Old Town, Fanjingshan.
- **Photography:** Misty mountains are better than clear skies here. Rainy season can actually enhance the experience.
