## Major Hiking Areas

### 1. Huangshizhai + Golden Whip Stream (黄石寨 + 金鞭溪)

**Best for:** First-time visitors; easiest introduction to the park

**Huangshizhai (Yellow Stone Village):**
- Summit loop trail: 3-4 km, 1.5-2 hours
- Elevation: 1,080 m; 3,878 stone steps to top (or cable car)
- Viewpoints: Sky-pointing pillars, Five Fingers Peak, panoramic platforms
- Cable car: ¥65 single / ¥118 round-trip

**Golden Whip Stream (金鞭溪):**
- Length: 7.5 km valley walk
- Duration: 2-2.5 hours at relaxed pace
- Character: Flat, shaded trail along crystal-clear stream between towering sandstone cliffs
- Highlights: Golden Whip Rock, Flower & Fruit Mountain (monkeys!), Zicao Pond
- **Monkey warning:** Do not hold food in hands — wild monkeys will grab it and may scratch

**Combined Day Route:**
Forest Park Entrance → Oxygen Square → Huangshizhai cable car up → summit loop (1.5 hrs) → cable car or hike down → Golden Whip Stream walk (2.5 hrs) → exit. Total: 6-7 hours.

---

### 2. Yuanjiajie (袁家界) — The Avatar Mountains

**Best for:** Avatar fans, iconic photos, first-time visitors

**Key Sights:**
- **Hallelujah Mountain / South Sky Pillar (南天一柱):** The sandstone pillar renamed after Avatar; the park's most photographed formation
- **First Bridge Under Heaven (天下第一桥):** Natural rock bridge spanning two pillars at 357m elevation
- **Enchanting Terrace (迷魂台):** Panoramic viewpoint overlooking hundreds of pillars
- **Back Garden (后花园):** Hidden viewpoint with fewer crowds

**Access:**
- Bailong Elevator from Golden Whip Stream area: ¥72, 2-minute glass-walled ascent (world's tallest outdoor elevator, 326m)
- Lines can reach 2 hours midday — go early morning or late afternoon
- Alternative: hike up from Golden Whip Stream (steep, 2-3 hours)

**Hiking Time:** 2-3 hours for main viewpoint loop at top

---

### 3. Tianzi Mountain (天子山) — Sea of Clouds Panorama

**Best for:** Misty mountain photography, panoramic views

**Key Sights:**
- **He Long Park (贺龙公园):** Main viewing platform; bronze statue of Marshal He Long
- **Imperial Writing Brush Peaks (御笔峰):** Three thin pillars resembling calligraphy brushes — most photographed Tianzi formation
- **Monarch of the Peak Forest:** Panoramic overlook
- **Fairy Offering Flowers:** Rock formations at the cliff edge

**Access:**
- Cable car from Tianzi Mountain station, or hike from Yuanjiajie (1-2 hours)
- Free shuttle buses connect to other park areas

**Hiking Time:** 2-3 hours for summit viewpoints

**Best Conditions:** Early morning when mist and clouds settle between pillars — "sea of clouds" (云海) effect

---

### 4. Yangjiajie (杨家界) — The Adventurous Alternative

**Best for:** Experienced hikers, crowd-free exploration

**Character:** Densely clustered pillars with narrow crevices, steel ladders, and exposed sections. The least-visited major area.

**Key Sights:**
- **Natural Great Wall (天然长城):** Karst formations resembling a fortress wall; 10-min walk from main path
- **Wulong Village (乌龙寨):** Former bandit hideout requiring squeezing through narrow rock passages
- **One Step to Heaven (一步登天):** Vertigo-inducing viewpoint
- **Fields in the Sky (空中田园 / Laowuchang):** Rice paddies on clifftops with distant Bailong Elevator views — hidden gem for photography

**Hiking Time:** 6-7 hours combined with Yuanjiajie
**Difficulty:** Moderate to challenging; some sections with steel ladders and narrow passages

---

### 5. Ten-Mile Gallery (十里画廊)

**Best for:** Easy scenic walk or train ride between areas

- Length: 5.8 km one-way
- Duration: 1.5-2 hours walking; or take the tourist train (¥52 RT)
- Character: Valley floor walk between karst formations
- Highlights: Gathering of Generals, Picking Stars Platform, Rooster Crowing at Dawn

## Tianmen Mountain (天门山) — Separate from Forest Park

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Location | Downtown Zhangjiajie (not inside Wulingyuan) |
| Ticket | ¥278 (includes cable car + shuttle bus) |
| Cable Car | 7.5 km long, 30 min ride — longest in Asia |
| Summit | 1,518 m; 3-4 hour loop at top |

**Key Features:**
- **Tianmen Cave (天门洞):** 131.5m natural arch; 999 stone steps ("Stairway to Heaven")
- **99 Bends Road (通天大道):** 11 km mountain road with 99 hairpin turns
- **Glass Skywalks:** Three sections; Coiling Dragon Cliff is longest at 100m
- **West Line & East Line:** Cliff-hugging narrow trails carved into mountain face

**Difficulty:** High — cliff-edge trails with steel railings, glass floor sections, steep stairs

## Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon (张家界大峡谷)

- 40 min east of Wulingyuan
- Glass Bridge: 430m long, 300m high (separate ticket ¥138)
- Canyon hike: 2-3 hours additional (combined ticket ¥256)
- See dedicated glass bridge article for full details

## Recommended Multi-Day Itineraries

### 2-Day Park Itinerary (Most Popular)
**Day 1:** Forest Park Entrance → Huangshizhai (cable car + loop) → Golden Whip Stream → Bailong Elevator up → Yuanjiajie loop
**Day 2:** Tianzi Mountain (early for clouds) → Ten-Mile Gallery → Yangjiajie (if time)

### 3-Day Comprehensive
**Day 1:** Wulingyuan: Huangshizhai + Golden Whip Stream
**Day 2:** Wulingyuan: Yuanjiajie + Tianzi Mountain + Yangjiajie
**Day 3:** Tianmen Mountain (full day)

### 4-Day Complete Experience
**Days 1-2:** Wulingyuan scenic area (all major sections)
**Day 3:** Tianmen Mountain
**Day 4:** Grand Canyon + Glass Bridge

## Practical Tips

- **Start early:** Cable car and elevator lines shortest before 8:30 AM
- **Bailong Elevator lines:** Can reach 2 hours midday — go first thing or after 3 PM
- **Monkeys:** Keep food concealed; do not make eye contact or show teeth
- **Cash:** Bring cash — not all in-park vendors accept mobile payment
- **Rain gear:** Mountain weather changes rapidly; light rain jacket always in pack
- **Shoes:** Comfortable walking shoes with grip; stone paths slippery when wet
- **Water/snacks:** Bring your own; in-park prices inflated
- **Stay inside park:** Hotels at Wulingyuan entrance or inside park (Zhongtian International Hotel, Pipaxi Hotel) save commute time
- **Sightseeing buses:** Free within park but can be crowded; factor in wait times
- **October conditions:** Clear skies most days, morning mist possible, pleasant 15-22°C hiking temperatures
