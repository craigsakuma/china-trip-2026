# 3 Days in Zhangjiajie: A Complete Itinerary

**Source:** Pete R. (bucketlistly.blog), solo travel and photography blogger with 11+ years experience. Updated Dec 4, 2025. First-hand experience (visited twice — once in winter nearly a decade ago, and again recently in late autumn).

## Quick Facts

- **Duration:** 3 days / 3 nights across two bases + day trip
- **Day 1:** Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (base: Wulingyuan)
- **Day 2:** Tianmen Mountain + 72 Strange Buildings at night (base: Zhangjiajie City)
- **Day 3:** Fenghuang Ancient Town day trip / overnight
- **Park entry (Forest Park):** ~224–228 CNY for a 4-day pass (single-day tickets usually unavailable); does not include cable cars or elevators
- **Tianmen Mountain entry:** 288 CNY (includes transport within mountain depending on route)
- **72 Strange Buildings:** 88 CNY (night visit after 4:30 PM) or 48 CNY (before 4 PM)
- **Estimated total budget:** ~221 USD / 3 days (~74 USD/day) — lodging 64 USD, food 24 USD, transport 38 USD, activities 95 USD
- **Best season:** Autumn (Sep–Nov) — clear skies, vibrant foliage, fewer crowds than summer
- **Worst season:** Winter — some attractions closed due to ice, cold and slippery trails

## Day 1: Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (张家界国家森林公园)

**Base:** Wulingyuan (武陵源), closest town to the park's East Gate — 5-minute walk to entrance.

**Start time:** 7:00 AM when the park opens, to beat crowds.

**Pre-arrival:** Book entry ticket and cable car ticket in advance so you can scan your passport and go right in.

### Morning: Tianzi Mountain (天子山) via Cable Car

From the East Gate, take the shuttle bus to the cable car station. The cable car ride takes ~25 minutes to the upper station. Walk around and enjoy the views, then catch the shuttle bus to Helong Park (贺龙公园).

**Helong Park highlights:**
- **Imperial Brush Peak (御笔峰):** A pillar resembling the tip of a paintbrush — surreal in soft morning light
- **Fairy Scattering Flower Mountains (仙女散花):** Faces east, perfect sunrise spot; photographers gather here early

Author's note: Besides these two viewpoints, there's not much else at Helong Park other than a few breakfast spots. This is also the starting point for the hike down to 10-Mile Gallery — skip it for now.

### Mid-Morning: Yangjiajie (杨家界)

Take the shuttle bus from Helong Park (~20 minutes). The bus stops first at Daguantai Bus Stop (大观台) — do not get off here; there's little to see besides accommodations and unremarkable viewpoints.

Continue ~10 minutes to Yangjiajie, where you can visit **Tianbo Mansion (天波府)**. This viewing platform offers views of ten parallel stone wall peaks rising among green trees, resembling city walls. Reaching the viewpoint requires climbing down a narrow railing and ascending a separate peak — not recommended for those with a fear of heights.

### Afternoon: Yuanjiajie (袁家界) — The Best Part

Take another shuttle bus to Yuanjiajie Mountain. Author considers this the most scenic area of the park. If short on time, skip Tianzi and Yangjiajie and focus here.

The bus drops you at Tianqiao Bus Stop (天桥). Start with:

- **Greatest Natural Bridge (天下第一桥):** Rocky natural bridge connecting 2 peaks with a 400m vertical drop
- After crossing, find a small shrine and viewpoints along the loop path
- **Five Ladies Expedition Rocks (五女出征)**
- **Hallelujah Mountain (哈利路亚山):** The solitary pillar that inspired the landscapes in *Avatar* — "truly a sight to behold"

Author says you could easily spend 1–2 hours exploring Yuanjiajie alone.

### Descent Options (Pick One)

**Option A — Bailong Elevator (百龙电梯):**
- Quick but often crowded
- Cost: ~62 CNY
- You miss the best peaceful hiking trails

**Option B — Golden Whip Stream (金鞭溪) hike:**
- ~2–3 hours down
- Beautiful path following a peaceful river with rest pavilions
- Starts right at the end of Yuanjiajie
- Follow the river east to end up near the Bailong Elevator lower station for shuttle bus back to East Gate
- Going the wrong way leads to the South Gate

**Option C — 10-Mile Gallery (十里画廊) hike from Helong Park:**
- ~2.5 hours down
- Beautiful sandstone spires seen from ground level
- At the bottom, take the light rail to the bus stop or walk alongside the tracks (author recommends walking)
- Ideal if you started at Bailong Elevator and want to avoid paying for the cable car

Author's opinion: "Hiking down was my favorite part of Zhangjiajie National Forest Park. It let me enjoy the scenery in peace, away from the crowds." Saw fewer than ten people during the entire 2.5-hour hike on each trail.

## Day 2: Tianmen Mountain (天门山)

**Morning:** Wake at 6:30 AM, Didi taxi from Wulingyuan to Zhangjiajie City (~40 min). Drop bags at hotel, head to cable car station.

### Route Recommendation: Route B

Three routes exist (A, B, C) — each subject to availability:

| Route | Direction | Transport |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **A** | Cable car up (city → Upper Station) → escalators down to Middle Station → cable car down → bus to city | Most popular, books out fastest, long morning queues |
| **B (Recommended)** | Bus up → cable car to Middle Station → escalators to Upper Station → cable car down to city | More availability, faster lines, best morning views |
| **C** | Bus up → cable car to Middle Station → escalators to Upper → backtrack same way | Requires backtracking — not recommended |

**Why Route B:** Gets you to the Middle Station first, where the Gateway to Heaven is located. Morning arrival often means a sea of clouds and stunning morning views. The bus departs quickly, and the newer cable car station has faster-moving lines.

### Middle Station: Gateway to Heaven (天门洞)

The iconic natural arch — 131.5m tall, ~57m wide — carved into the cliff face. Climb the famous **999 stone steps** ("stairway to heaven") to reach it. The staircase is extremely steep. Author did it without rest in ~15 minutes and his knees were "throbbing like crazy."

**Escalator alternative:** 32 CNY one-way to the top in <5 minutes.

Author's opinion: "Since visits like this are rare, I recommend hiking the staircase if you are able. It's challenging, but fun and extremely rewarding."

At the top: stunning vantage point with clouds and mist swirling, plus a reservoir and mountain views from the other side. Then take escalators up to the Upper Station.

### Upper Station

Amenities: restaurants, street food stalls, cafes. Two trail options:

- **Red trail (right):** Recommended for Route B. Loops around the hill toward Tianmen Temple past Glass Walkways, suspension bridge, and viewpoints. Then open-air cable car to Yunmeng Fairy Peak (25 CNY one-way), elevator down back to Upper Station.
- **Yellow trail (left):** Alternative direction.

**Glass Skywalk:** Transparent cliff-face walkway. Three skywalks: East Line, West Line, and Coiling Dragon Cliff (100m long, 1.6m wide — best views of 99 Bends Road). Come early to avoid crowds.

**Yunmeng Fairy Peak (云梦仙顶):** Highest point at 1,518.6m. Multi-tiered pavilion with 360-degree views. Free elevator or hike up. Open-air cable car connects to Tianmen Temple (25 CNY one-way).

**Tianmen Temple (天门山寺):** Buddhist temple, Tang Dynasty origin, reconstructed after 2002. Active temple with monks chanting — peaceful atmosphere away from crowds.

After exploring, take old cable car back to city center.

**Entry:** 288 CNY per person (all routes). Open 8 AM – 7 PM daily.

### Evening: 72 Strange Buildings (72奇楼)

A cultural/tourism complex worth visiting at night when illuminated (shows run 4:30 PM – 10:30 PM).

- **Main tower:** 109.9m tall, world's tallest stilted building; central arch ~38m high
- Connected buildings, alleys, recreated historical ethnic village streets
- Cultural shows: traditional dances, drum shows, martial arts — "arguably the highlights"
- Street food, themed activities and workshops
- **Entry:** 88 CNY (night, after 4:30 PM) / 48 CNY (day, before 4 PM). Open 8 AM – 10:30 PM

Author arrived ~8:30 PM for the last series of shows: "It felt like a big open-air party." Recommends arriving 6–7 PM to enjoy the full atmosphere until 10:30 PM.

## Day 3: Fenghuang Ancient Town (凤凰古城)

Take high-speed train from Zhangjiajie West Railway Station to Fenghuang — only ~25 minutes.

### About Fenghuang

- Founded 1556, Ming Dynasty
- Home to Miao (苗) and Tujia (土家) ethnic minorities
- Built along the Tuo River (沱江), nestled among green hills
- **Wooden stilt houses (吊脚楼)** lining the riverbank, many over 100 years old
- Cobblestone streets, narrow alleys, stone-paved lanes from Ming and Qing dynasties

### Key Tips

- **From the train station:** 7 CNY shuttle to Qifeng Square, short walk to ancient town
- **Stay until sunset and beyond:** The town's true beauty comes alive at night — lanterns and lights reflect on the river, wooden houses glow softly
- Lights come on around 6:30 PM
- **Best view:** From Nanhua Bridge (南华桥), overlooking the entire town
- Author describes the night scene as "a fairy-tale-like scene reminiscent of Studio Ghibli animations"

## Getting to Zhangjiajie

- **By Air:** Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport (张家界荷花机场) — direct flights from Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, Changsha. 30–40 min taxi/shuttle to city or Wulingyuan.
- **By Train:** Zhangjiajie West Railway Station — HSR from Changsha ~4–5 hours. Carpool taxi to Wulingyuan 30 CNY/person (~40 min) or Didi ~90 CNY/car. Drivers approach you at the station.
- **By Bus:** Long-distance from Changsha, Fenghuang, Chongqing. Arrives at Zhangjiajie Central Bus Station.

## Getting Around

- **Shuttle buses:** Included with park ticket. Connect entrances, scenic spots, cable car stations.
- **Cable cars/elevators:** Extra cost — Bailong Elevator ~62 CNY, cable car ~75 CNY. Book in advance.
- **Didi:** Widely available. Access through Alipay mini app, no separate download needed.

## Best Time to Visit

| Season | Conditions | Author's Take |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Spring (Mar–May) | Lush green, misty, mild temps, smaller crowds | Beautiful but occasional rain/fog |
| Summer (Jun–Aug) | Full greenery, waterfalls peak, long days | Peak crowds, humid, rain |
| **Autumn (Sep–Nov)** | Cool, dry, excellent visibility, stunning foliage | **"Perfect. Highly recommend."** Clear blue skies, low drifting clouds, vibrant colors |
| Winter (Dec–Feb) | Snow-dusted, no crowds | Some attractions closed (ice), cold, slippery trails |

## Practical Tips

- **Book tickets in advance** for both the Forest Park and Tianmen Mountain — Route A sells out fastest
- **Carry your passport at all times** — required for ticket purchases, train boarding, and park entry (tickets are linked to passport)
- **Start at 7 AM** each day to beat crowds
- **Hike down instead of taking elevators** — the trails are the best part and nearly empty
- **Download AMap** before arriving — Google Maps doesn't work well in China
- **Set up Alipay + WeChat** before the trip for cashless payments
- **Get eSIM** (Hong Kong-based services bypass the Great Firewall)
- **Install VPN before entering China** — cannot download VPN apps once inside
- **Avoid Golden Week** (early October) and Chinese New Year
- **Mid-week visits** are noticeably less crowded than weekends
