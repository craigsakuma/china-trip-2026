# Visiting Tianmen Mountain: A Complete Travel Guide

**Source:** Pete R. (bucketlistly.blog), solo travel and photography blogger with 11+ years experience. Updated Feb 9, 2026. First-hand experience (visited twice — once in winter, once in late autumn).

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Zhangjiajie City (张家界市), Hunan Province — cable car departs from city center
- **Entry ticket:** ~288 CNY (includes cable car and bus transport depending on route)
- **Hours:** High season (Mar 1 – Nov 30) ~6:30–18:00; low season (Dec–Feb) from ~8:00 with adjusted closing
- **Time needed:** 5–7 hours (full day recommended)
- **Highest point:** Yunmeng Fairy Peak, 1,518.6m above sea level
- **Key highlights:** Gateway to Heaven (天门洞), Glass Skywalks, 99 Bends Road, Tianmen Temple, Forest Cable Car
- **Best season:** Autumn (Sep–Nov) — clear skies, vibrant colors, manageable crowds
- **Recommended route:** Route B (bus up → Middle Station → Upper Station → cable car down)

## Routes: How to Get Around Tianmen Mountain (天门山)

Three designated routes determine how you ascend/descend. You must follow your ticketed route.

| Route | Path | Pros/Cons |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **A** | Old cable car from city center → Upper Station → escalators to Middle Station → new cable car down → bus to city | Most popular, books out fastest, long morning cable car queues |
| **B (Recommended)** | Bus to entry gate (~20 min) → new cable car up to Middle Station → escalators to Upper Station → old cable car down to city | More availability, shorter lines, best morning views at Middle Station |
| **C** | Bus to entry gate → new cable car to Middle Station → escalators to Upper → backtrack same way down | Requires backtracking — not recommended |

**Author strongly recommends Route B:**
- Gets you to the Middle Station first thing in the morning, where the Gateway to Heaven and sea-of-clouds views are
- The newer cable car station has larger capacity and faster-moving lines
- Route A's old cable car has limited capacity and generates very long morning queues
- Generally more ticket availability for Route B

## 1. Middle Station: Views and 99 Bends Road (通天大道)

Arriving at the Middle Station via Route B early in the morning is the highlight. The cable car breaks through the clouds to reveal Tianmen's peaks rising above a drifting sea of clouds.

**Cliffside walkway** offers a distant view of the Gateway to Heaven — from here you appreciate the sheer scale of the cave.

### 99 Bends Road (天门山盘山公路)

Visible from the Middle Station cliff edge:
- Over 10 km long, rising from ~200m to ~1,300m elevation (1,100m gain)
- 99 hairpin turns, many nearly 180 degrees
- Originally the bus route to the Gateway to Heaven before the cable cars were built
- Famous worldwide after Italian driver Fabio Barone conquered it in a modified Ferrari 458 in under 11 minutes
- Now you view it from above at leisure — no need to drive it

## 2. Gateway to Heaven — Tianmen Cave (天门洞)

The main reason most travelers visit. A natural arch carved into the cliff face — approximately **131.5m tall and 57m wide**, one of the largest natural arches at high altitude in the world.

### The 999 Steps (上天梯)

Often called the "stairway to heaven," ascending from the lower plateau to the arch entrance.

- **Extremely steep** — pace yourself
- Author completed it without rest in ~15 minutes; knees were "throbbing like crazy"
- Food shops at the bottom for energy before climbing
- **Escalator alternative:** 32 CNY one-way, <5 minutes to the top

**Author's opinion:** "Since visits like this are rare, I recommend hiking the staircase if you are able. It's challenging, but fun and extremely rewarding once you reach the top."

**At the top:** Clouds and mist swirl around the cliff, giving the impression of standing at heaven's gate. Also find a reservoir and mountain views from the other side.

## 3. Upper Station Area

Reached via a series of escalators from the Gateway to Heaven. Amenities include restaurants, street food stalls, and cafes.

### Trail Options from Upper Station

Two hiking trails branch from the Upper Station:

**Red Trail (right) — Recommended for Route B:**
- Longer loop toward Tianmen Temple
- Passes Glass Walkways, suspension bridge, many viewpoints
- At Tianmen Temple, take open-air cable car to Yunmeng Fairy Peak (25 CNY one-way)
- From Fairy Peak, elevator down back to Upper Station

**Alternative:** Start by taking the elevator up to Yunmeng Fairy Peak, cable car to Tianmen Temple, then walk the long way back. Or for the shortest route, take cable car and elevator both ways.

## 4. Glass Skywalks (玻璃栈道)

Transparent cliff-face walkways — look straight down at valleys hundreds of meters below.

Three glass skywalks at the summit:
- **East Line Glass Skywalk**
- **West Line Glass Skywalk**
- **Coiling Dragon Cliff Glass Skywalk (盘龙崖玻璃栈道)** — the longest at 100m long, 1.6m wide; great viewpoint over 99 Bends Road

**Tips:** Come early morning to avoid crowds (they form quickly). Morning also offers best visibility before clouds roll in.

## 5. Yunmeng Fairy Peak (云梦仙顶)

Highest point on Tianmen Mountain at **1,518.6m** above sea level.

- Multi-tiered pavilion with panoramic 360-degree vistas
- On clear days: views of other Tianmen peaks, distant valley ranges, wider Zhangjiajie region
- From above, see glass walkways, suspension bridge, and Tianmen Temple
- Helps you decide whether to hike or take shortcuts to the temple

**Access:** Free elevator from Upper Station to the pavilion, then stairs to the summit. Or hike up via the red trail. Author notes: "Many visitors choose the elevator so they can save their energy for hiking other areas, as there is not much to see between the Upper Station and Yunmeng Fairy Peak."

## 6. Forest Sightseeing Cable Car (森林观光缆车)

Open-air, ski-style cable car connecting Yunmeng Fairy Peak to Tianmen Temple. Your feet dangle over cliffs and mountains.

- **Ride time:** ~10–15 minutes
- **Cost:** 25 CNY one-way
- Provides unique aerial perspective you cannot get by hiking
- Author: "Riding it is an exciting experience in itself... I highly recommend it."

**Strategy:** Take cable car one way, hike the other way to see both the aerial views and the trail attractions.

## 7. Tianmen Temple (天门山寺)

Buddhist temple at the summit. Series of halls, pavilions, and traditional Buddhist buildings.

- Originally built during the Tang Dynasty, destroyed in 20th century turmoil
- Current structure: modern reconstruction completed after 2002 in classical Chinese style
- Active, functioning temple — monks chanting, believers praying
- Surrounded by ancient trees and stunning mountains
- Peaceful and quiet atmosphere, unlike the more crowded mountain areas

## When to Visit Tianmen Mountain

| Season | Conditions | Author's Take |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Spring (Mar–May) | Mild temps, good visibility, lush green | Great for hiking and photography |
| Summer (Jun–Aug) | Warmest, busiest, rain/fog possible | Misty mountains can look atmospheric, but visibility reduced |
| **Autumn (Sep–Nov)** | Clear skies, comfortable temps, vibrant colors | **"Perfect." Best overall season. Author's second visit was late autumn: ideal weather, low crowds.** |
| Winter (Dec–Feb) | Cold, sometimes snowy | Winter wonderland effect, but Glass Walkways and Gateway to Heaven may close due to ice. Author's first visit was winter: cold, slippery, some areas closed. |

## How to Get to Zhangjiajie

- **By Air:** Zhangjiajie Hehua Airport — direct flights from Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, Changsha. 30–40 min taxi to city.
- **By Train:** Zhangjiajie West Railway Station — HSR from Changsha ~4–5 hours; from Chongqing and Guangzhou also available. ~15 min Didi to city.
- **By Bus:** Long-distance from Changsha, Fenghuang, Chongqing to Zhangjiajie Central Bus Station.

## Practical Tips

- **Book Route B tickets in advance** — Route A sells out fastest; Route B offers equivalent experience with better morning views and shorter queues
- **Arrive early morning** — beat crowds at Glass Skywalk and stairways; better visibility before fog/clouds build up
- **Visit weekdays (Mon–Fri)** for a more peaceful experience
- **Avoid Chinese holidays** (Golden Week, Chinese New Year) and peak summer season
- **Hike whenever possible** — crowds gather at cable car stations; trails are much emptier
- **Budget ~5–7 hours** for a full visit; 5 hours minimum if taking cable cars and focusing on key highlights
- **Bring comfortable hiking shoes** — the 999 steps are steep and demanding
- **Carry your passport** — required for ticket purchase and entry (tickets linked to passport)
- **Glass Skywalk + Gateway to Heaven stairs may close in winter** due to ice — check conditions before visiting
- **Escalator shortcut:** 32 CNY saves your legs on the 999 steps if needed
- **Forest cable car (25 CNY):** Worth it for the unique aerial perspective; save money by hiking one direction
