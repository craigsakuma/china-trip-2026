# Yunnan Practical Guide: Preparing Your Trip

**Source:** Mademoiselle Voyage (mademoiselle-voyage.fr), French travel blog with detailed Yunnan coverage and ethnic minority focus. First-hand experience.

## Quick Facts

- **Region:** Yunnan (云南), southwest China
- **Currency:** Renminbi (RMB/yuan)
- **Time zone:** UTC+8 (+7 hours from Paris in winter, +6 in summer)
- **Visa:** Required, single-entry tourist visa
- **Safety:** Generally very safe
- **Best season:** November-April (dry); avoid early May and October (national holidays)
- **Author's verdict:** "Exactly what I expected from China: ethnic groups, encounters, smiles, traditional and preserved places, authenticity and beautiful landscapes."

---

## Visa & Entry

- Chinese visa required; apply 3 months before travel
- Single-entry tourist visa: €160 (€126 + €34 agency fees)
- Permits stays up to 30 days; must be used within 90 days of issue
- Passport needs: 2 consecutive blank pages, valid 6+ months after application
- 72-hour transit visa exemption available in Beijing, Guangzhou, and Shanghai (no Yunnan cities)

---

## Flights

- Paris to Kunming: ~10 hours direct via China Eastern Airlines (Mon/Thu/Sat)
- Domestic flights: Air China and China Eastern serve regional routes within Yunnan

---

## Transportation Within Yunnan

- **No self-driving:** Foreign and international licenses not recognized in China. Must hire driver with vehicle for inter-city travel
- **Local transit:** Metros, buses, electric scooters in major cities
- **Taxis:** Equipped with meters; trips often under €1
- **Trains:** Available between major cities (e.g., Kunming-Dali)
- **Navigation:** Use Maps.me app to show drivers Chinese characters for destinations

---

## Money & Costs

- **Currency:** Renminbi (RMB), also called yuan
- **Exchange:** Euros at airports or city exchange offices; difficult on weekends
- **ATMs:** Available in Kunming, Dali, and Jianshui (limited elsewhere)
- **Credit cards:** Rarely accepted outside hotels and major stores — carry cash
- **Meal budget:** ~€4-5/person for shared meals; street food ~€1/dish
- **Site entries:** 35-200 RMB (€4-25) per attraction

---

## Best Time to Visit

| Season | Weather | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **Nov-Apr (dry)** | ~20C days, sunny | Best overall; November = low season pricing |
| **Spring/Autumn** | Ideal temps | Avoid early May and October (national holidays: crowds + high prices) |
| **Summer (Jun-Sep)** | 25-30C, rainy | Manageable but pack rain gear |
| **Winter** | Sunny days, no rain | Evenings near 0C at altitude (Shangri-La) |

---

## Health & Safety

- **Vaccines:** None mandatory; ensure routine vaccinations current
- **Water:** Not drinkable anywhere; bottled water readily available
- **Pack:** Disinfectant gel, bandages, cold remedies, digestive aids
- **Safety:** Generally very safe; exercise normal precautions

---

## Internet & Communication

- **SIM cards:** Purchase prepaid locally or use services like My Mobile in China (~€84 for 3GB)
- **VPN essential:** Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Gmail all blocked. Download Turbo VPN (free) before arrival from App Store / Google Play
- **WiFi:** Available at most hotels
- **Translation apps:** Reverso or Google Translate (download offline Chinese pack before trip)

---

## Language

Minimal English spoken, even in tourist areas. Younger generations occasionally helpful.

**Strategies:**
- Pictorial guides for food ordering
- Ask hotel staff to write destinations in Chinese characters
- Translation apps with offline packs
- Maps.me for showing drivers your destination in Chinese

---

## Food in Yunnan

- Regional cuisine heavy on soups, barbecues, fondues, and stir-fries
- Signature dish: "Noodles Crossing the Bridge" (过桥米线) — a Chinese fondue-style noodle soup
- Street food averages ~€1/dish
- Shared restaurant meals: €4-5/person
- Yak meat and yak butter tea common in Shangri-La area

---

## Electricity

- 220 volts throughout China
- Bring a universal adapter and multi-plug socket

---

## What to Pack

- Lightweight clothing for lowlands
- Sweaters and jackets for mountains and evenings (Shangri-La at 3,200m gets cold)
- Comfortable walking shoes suitable for muddy paths
- Sun protection: cream, sunglasses
- Rain jacket if visiting June-September

---

## Souvenirs

Markets and boutiques offer:
- Spices (especially Sichuan pepper)
- Batiks and embroidered/straw bags
- Red clay pottery (Jianshui specialty)
- Silver jewelry (Xizhou markets)
- Tea (Yunnan is famous for pu'er tea)
- Decorative objects and local alcohol

---

## Practical Tips

- Download all apps and VPN before arriving in China
- Carry cash — card acceptance is rare outside major hotels
- ATM access is limited outside Kunming, Dali, and Jianshui
- Hire drivers for all inter-city travel; book through your hotel or tour operator
- Allow altitude acclimatization time for Shangri-La (3,200m) and mountain excursions
- Weekend exchange offices may be closed — plan ahead
- Hotel WiFi is your main internet lifeline; VPN needed for Western apps
