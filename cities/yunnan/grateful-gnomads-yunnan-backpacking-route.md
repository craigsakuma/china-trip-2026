# Classic Yunnan Backpacking Route: Kunming to Shangri-La

**Source:** Grateful Gnomads (gratefulgnomads.com) and Joni's Travelling (jonistravelling.com). First-hand budget backpacking accounts with detailed costs, routes, and practical tips.

## Quick Facts

- **Route:** Kunming → Dali → Lijiang → Tiger Leaping Gorge → Shangri-La (+ optional Yubeng, Xishuangbanna)
- **Duration:** 2-4 weeks recommended; minimum 10 days for golden route
- **Budget:** ~$1,400 USD/month all-in (flights, visa, food, transport, accommodation)
- **Daily budget:** $30-50 USD for comfortable backpacking
- **Best time:** March-May and September-November (avoid July-August heat and October Golden Week)
- **Difficulty:** Easy to moderate; altitude at Shangri-La (3,300m)

---

## The Golden Route

### Kunming (昆明) — 2 Days
- **Role:** Gateway city and unavoidable transport hub
- **Must-do:** Stone Forest ($30 entry), Green Lake Park, Zhuanxin Market
- **Accommodation:** Dorms ¥50 ($8); The Hump Hostel (best location/beds); Cloudland Hostel (best food/common area)
- **Vibe:** "Clean streets and quality hostels" — better than most Chinese mega-cities
- **Food:** Crossing the Bridge Noodles, wild mushroom hot pot, rose pastries
- **Transport out:** Bullet train to Dali (2-3 hours) or overnight train

### Dali (大理) — 2-4 Days
- **Vibe:** "The most westernised town in Yunnan" — relaxed backpacker hangout
- **Must-do:** Three Pagodas (worth the cost), Erhai Lake cycling, Dali Old Town wandering
- **English:** Widely spoken; extensive Western food options
- **Accommodation:** ¥80 ($12) at Tibetan Lodge; many hostels in Old Town
- **Markets:** Xizhou daily, Shaping Monday, Shaxi Friday
- **Day trips:** Shaxi Ancient Town, Xizhou Village, Shuanglang

### Lijiang (丽江) — 1-2 Days
- **Must-do:** Old Town (UNESCO), Black Dragon Pool, Jade Dragon Snow Mountain views
- **Reality check:** "Too many tourists stalking the streets" — beautiful but very crowded
- **Accommodation:** ¥80 ($12) for guesthouses in Old Town
- **History:** Former stop on the Tea Horse Road; Naxi minority culture
- **Tip:** Visit early morning or evening when day-trippers leave

### Tiger Leaping Gorge (虎跳峡) — 2-3 Days
- **Highlight:** "Main reason for visiting Yunnan" for many backpackers
- **Trek:** 2-day high trail along Yangtze River canyon with Jade Dragon Snow Mountain views
- **Accommodation:** Guesthouses along the trail; Naxi Family Guesthouse (¥30 private room); Tea Horse Guesthouse ("one of the coolest guesthouses" in Asia)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate; significant elevation gain on Day 1
- **Entry:** ~¥65; start from Qiaotou town
- **Warning:** Do not attempt in heavy rain; landslide risk

### Shangri-La (香格里拉) — 2 Days
- **Altitude:** 3,300m — altitude sickness possible; acclimate gradually
- **Must-do:** Ganden Sumtseling Monastery, Pudacuo National Park, Dukezong Ancient Town
- **Reality:** Town largely destroyed by 2014 fire; minimal old town remains
- **Food:** Helen's Pizza (Italian-run) is exceptionally good
- **Accommodation:** Dorms ¥30-40 ($5-6); rooms ¥80+ ($12+)
- **Tip:** Wrap up warm — much colder than rest of Yunnan

### Yubeng (雨崩) — 3-4 Days (Optional)
- **Description:** Remote village requiring challenging trek; "some of the best scenery I've ever seen"
- **Author's verdict:** Top recommendation for Asian travel
- **Access:** Bus from Shangri-La to Deqin, then trek in
- **Entry:** ~$35
- **Difficulty:** Hard — high altitude, long days
- **Reward:** Exceptional peace, sacred waterfalls, Meili Snow Mountain views

## Transportation

### Between Cities
- **Trains preferred:** More comfortable, cheaper, better sleep than buses
- **Overnight trains:** "2 of the best night's sleep" — save on accommodation
- **Buses:** 4-8 hours between major destinations; sometimes only option
- **Internal flights:** Kunming-Xishuangbanna worth flying (saves 8+ hours)

### Key Routes
| Route | Mode | Duration | Cost |
|-------|------|----------|------|
| Kunming → Dali | Bullet train | 2-3 hours | ¥120-150 |
| Dali → Lijiang | Bullet train | 1.5-2 hours | ¥80-100 |
| Lijiang → Tiger Leaping Gorge | Bus | 2-3 hours | ¥40-50 |
| Tiger Leaping Gorge → Shangri-La | Bus | 3-4 hours | ¥50-60 |
| Kunming → Xishuangbanna | Flight | 1 hour | ¥400-800 |

## Getting to Yunnan

- **Flights:** Cheap flights from Southeast Asia to Kunming (~$150 one-way from Malaysia)
- **Land borders:** Laos (Mohan/Boten crossing) and Vietnam (Hekou crossing)
- **Visa:** Standard tourist visa required; ~$80 USD; 3-day processing
- **144-hour transit visa:** Free transit through Kunming for 53 nationalities

---

## Challenges & Honest Assessment

- **Language barrier:** "Hard to get around if you don't speak Chinese" — biggest challenge
- **Bathroom quality:** Deteriorates significantly outside major towns
- **Crowds:** Tourist attractions experience heavy local crowds; visit early, avoid weekends
- **Western food:** Available in Dali and Lijiang; eating it will inflate budget
- **Altitude:** Shangri-La and Yubeng require acclimatization
- **Student IDs:** Reportedly work for attraction discounts (even homemade)

---

## Practical Tips

- Allow 3-4 weeks minimum to experience major sites without rushing
- Download Maps.me or Amap for offline navigation with Chinese characters
- Get a Chinese SIM card in Kunming for data; WeChat Pay is essential
- VPN needed for Google, WhatsApp, Instagram (download before entering China)
- Pharmacies everywhere for basic medications
- Pack layers: Kunming is mild, Shangri-La is cold, Xishuangbanna is tropical
- Best hostels have communal kitchens — cooking saves money
- Tiger Leaping Gorge guesthouses are highlight accommodations
- Avoid Chinese national holidays: October 1-7 (Golden Week) and May 1-5 (Labor Day)
- Yunnan is one of China's safest provinces for solo travelers
