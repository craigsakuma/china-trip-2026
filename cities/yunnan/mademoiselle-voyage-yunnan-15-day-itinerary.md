# China: 2-Week Itinerary in Yunnan Province

**Source:** Mademoiselle Voyage (mademoiselle-voyage.fr), French travel blog with detailed Yunnan coverage and ethnic minority focus. First-hand experience. Trip arranged through China Roads collaboration.

## Quick Facts

- **Region:** Yunnan (云南), southwest China
- **Duration:** 15 days
- **Route:** Kunming → Shilin → Yuanyang → Jianshui → Xizhou/Dali → Tiger Leap Gorge → Shangri-La → Lijiang/Baisha → Chengdu
- **Best for:** Ethnic minorities (25 of China's 55), mountain landscapes, traditional architecture
- **Budget:** Meals €1-9/person; site entries 35-200 RMB (€4-25)
- **Author's verdict:** "A real favourite on every level" — praised ethnic diversity, mountain authenticity, and "China of yesteryear" aesthetics

---

## Days 1-2: Kunming (昆明)

Provincial capital at 1,890m elevation; 3+ million people but retains old neighborhoods with narrow streets.

**Getting there:** 10-hour flight from Paris + stopover in Chengdu.

**Activities:**
- Early morning street vendors selling baozi (meat buns)
- Flea market (weekends, 9am-6pm)
- Flower and Bird Market (Jinxing/Guanghua Streets)
- Emerald Lake park
- Yuantong Temple (oldest Buddhist temple in Kunming)

**Accommodation:** Moon & Chalice boutique hotel in old quarter.

**Dining:**
- Lao Fang Zi — traditional house restaurant, ~€6.50/person
- Grandma's Kitchen / Waipo Weidao — ~€1/dish
- Xiang Yun Jie neighborhood restaurants

**Cafes:** Mr Kun, Lazy, As You Like (~€5/person), Origin Club.

---

## Day 3: Shilin Stone Forest (石林)

UNESCO limestone karst formations — 8 hectares, 270 million years old, peaks up to 30m. Stone labyrinths require 2-3 hours to explore.

**Getting there:** 1.5-2 hour drive from Kunming.

**Costs:**
- Entrance: 130 RMB/person
- Shuttle (recommended): 25 RMB/person

**Tip:** Visit early morning on weekdays to avoid tourist crowds.

---

## Days 4-5: Yuanyang Rice Terraces (元阳梯田)

UNESCO World Heritage terraces carved by Yi and Hani minorities. Author's "favorite part of Yunnan."

**Getting there:** 7-8 hours from Kunming (consider breaking journey at Shilin).

**Details:** See dedicated Yuanyang article for viewpoints, markets, accommodation, and costs.

---

## Days 6-7: Jianshui (建水) & Tuanshan (团山)

**Getting there:** 4-hour drive from Yuanyang rice terraces.

**Jianshui highlights:**
- Confucius Temple — second-largest in China after Qufu
- Zhu family Zen gardens
- Chaoyang Gate — red door, built 1389
- Tofu factories in western neighborhood
- Red clay pottery specialties

**Tuanshan** (20 min away): Unspoiled village with gray-roofed buildings, temples, local juice vendors. Allow 2 hours.

**Accommodation:** Xiu Ju Xian Ting (traditional Chinese house hotel).

**Dining:**
- Chez Lin — ticket-based system, ~€1/dish
- Xi Men Twelve Flavours Workshop — ~€4/meal

**Costs:**
- Confucius Temple: 40 RMB
- Zhu family garden: 35 RMB
- Tuanshan entrance: 35 RMB

---

## Days 8-9: Xizhou (喜洲) & Dali (大理)

**Getting there:** 4-hour drive + 2-hour train.

### Xizhou
Less touristy than Dali, divided by central square. Bai minority area.
- Silk-thread embroidery workshop
- Authentic market (Sichuan pepper, spices, silver jewelry)
- Cobbled alleyway with coffeehouses and boutiques
- Bicycle rentals for lake exploration

### Dali
Tourist hub of the Bai minorities. UNESCO city.
- Old town with perpendicular alleys
- Catholic church (temple-shaped)
- Temple of 3 Pagodas (largest Buddhist temple)
- Optional: Weibaoshan hike (2,500m altitude, 3-hour return)

**Accommodation:** Linden Center — traditional house, American owner since 1984.

**Dining:**
- Noodles Across the Bridge (Chinese fondue, ~€9/person) at Linden Center
- All-you-can-eat vegetarian buffet near Wumiao Temple — ~€0.60/person
- Street food: melted cheese skewers, chocolate waffles

**Cafes:** Linden Center terrace — 180-degree rural views.

**Costs:**
- Temple of 3 Pagodas: €9/person (or 110 RMB)
- Bus access: €4

---

## Day 10: Tiger Leaping Gorge (虎跳峡)

One of the world's most spectacular gorge hikes: 16 km long, 2,000m deep, 900m canyon walls. Yangtze River flows through. Flanked by Mount Haba (5,300m) and Mount Shanzidou (5,500m).

**Getting there:** 2.5-hour drive from Dali.

**Hiking options:**
- Full 2-day trek: 6 hours daily
- Afternoon hike to halfway refuge: 2.5 hours

**Accommodation:**
- Jane's Guest House (trailhead)
- Tea Horse Guesthouse (trailhead)
- Halfway Guest House (midpoint, ~35 yuan/person)

**Food:** Tea Horse Guesthouse. Try yak butter tea (described as "mushroom soup-like").

**Cost:** 45 RMB entrance fee (~€5/person).

---

## Days 11-12: Shangri-La (香格里拉)

Mountain village at 3,200m. Formerly Zhongdian, renamed Shangri-La in 2001. Mostly rebuilt after 2014 fire. Author felt it was "designed for Chinese tourists" after reconstruction.

**Getting there:** 3 hours from Tiger Leaping Gorge.

**Key sites:**
- Songzanlin Tibetan Monastery — 700+ monks, golden architecture
- Lake Napa — free-roaming yaks, horses, pigs
- Temple of 100 Chickens / Bai Ji Si (hilltop)
- China's largest prayer wheel (requires ~12 people to operate)
- Shangri-La market (yak meat butchers, local produce)
- Shika Mountain cable car — 4,500m, 40-minute ascent, -10C at summit

**Accommodation:** Arro Khampa boutique hotel.

**Dining:**
- Huksuk Kitchen — hotpot with yak meat, local beer
- Flying Tiger — yak burgers, run by two French owners
- Kailash restaurant
- Ge La Xia Can Ba
- Three Brothers — momos/local dumplings

**Cafes:** Karma Cafe & Lodge and surrounding establishments.

**Costs:**
- Songzanlin Monastery: 90 RMB (~€15)
- Shika Mountain gondola: 200 RMB
- Prayer wheel: Free

---

## Days 13-14: Lijiang (丽江) & Baisha (白沙)

**Getting there:** 3 hours from Shangri-La.

### Lijiang
UNESCO World Heritage city. Known for views of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain (玉龙雪山).
- Best visited at sunset over the lake
- Old town with grey architecture and souvenir stalls
- Mufu Palace (zen gardens)

### Baisha
Small village north of Lijiang, much less crowded.
- Cobbled streets, traditional Chinese houses
- Antique shops
- Mu family silk embroidery school

**Accommodation:** Arro Khampa by Zinc Journey — outside city center, modern design.

**Cafes:** Yuegulou Bar — elevated terrace overlooking old town.

**Costs:**
- Black Dragon Pool: 80 RMB (~€10)
- Mu Lijiang Residence: 40 RMB

---

## Day 15: Chengdu (成都) & Departure

**Getting there:** Flight from Lijiang to Chengdu for international connections.

**Activity:** Panda Research Center (half-day visit).

---

## Practical Tips

- **Accommodation range:** Budget (Lost Garden Guest House) to mid-range boutique (Moon & Chalice, Arro Khampa properties, Linden Center)
- **Meal costs:** Street food/local restaurants €1-4; tourist-oriented venues €5-9
- **Driving required:** Foreign licenses not recognized in China; hire driver with vehicle for inter-city travel
- **Altitude:** Shangri-La is 3,200m — allow acclimatization time; Shika Mountain reaches 4,500m
- **Author's context:** Initially hesitated about returning to China after disappointing 2015 trip to Sichuan/Guangxi (felt overly modern and commercialized). Yunnan proved the opposite — authentic, diverse, and preserved
