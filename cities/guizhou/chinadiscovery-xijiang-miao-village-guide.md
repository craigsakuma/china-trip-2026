# Xijiang Qianhu Miao Village (西江千户苗寨) — Complete Culture & Visiting Guide

**Source:** China Discovery (chinadiscovery.com), comprehensive travel guide. Detailed coverage of China's largest Miao settlement with cultural context, logistics, and photography advice.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Leishan County (雷山县), Qiandongnan Miao and Dong Autonomous Prefecture, Guizhou Province.
- **Size:** 1,432 households, 5,000+ residents — 99.5% Miao ethnic minority (苗族).
- **Status:** China's largest Miao village and a living museum of Miao culture.
- **Distance:** 32 km from Kaili (凯里), 195 km from Guiyang (贵阳).
- **Admission:** 100 RMB per person; sightseeing bus 20 RMB (4 rides included).
- **Hours:** Open 24/7.

## Getting There

**From Guiyang:**
- Private car: ~3 hours direct.
- High-speed train to Kaili South Station (凯里南站): 30–45 minutes, then 50–70 minutes by car to Xijiang.
- Direct coach from Longdongbao Bus Station (龙洞堡汽车站): departs 10:30 AM, 3–4 hours.

**From Kaili:**
- Private car: under 1 hour.
- Public bus: ~1.5 hours from Kaili South HSR Bus Station.

## Architecture — Diao Jiao Lou (吊脚楼) Stilted Houses

Three-story wooden structures built without nails on 30–70 degree hillside slopes. Ground floor stores tools and houses livestock. Second floor contains living quarters, kitchen, and embroidery workspace. Upper floor stores grain and goods. The entire hillside of dark wooden houses creates Xijiang's signature stepped silhouette.

## Wind and Rain Bridges (风雨桥)

Traditional wooden bridges crossing the Bai River. Originally built without nails, now reconstructed with concrete and wood foundations. Serve as community rest areas and are believed to bring good feng shui.

## Cultural Performances & Events

- **Miao singing at Wind and Rain Bridge:** Mornings around 11:30 AM — villagers in traditional dress welcome visitors with songs.
- **Lusheng dancing (芦笙舞):** Traditional reed-pipe instrument performances at Old Lusheng Square.
- **Batik demonstrations:** Watch artisans create wax-resist indigo-dyed fabrics.
- **Cock fighting and bird flight:** During market days.
- **"Beautiful Xijiang" night show (美丽西江):** 20:30–22:00, weather permitting — dance and music performance.

## Long Table Banquet (长桌宴)

A centuries-old Miao hospitality tradition. Hosts seated on the left, guests on the right. Served family-style with rice wine toasts and call-and-response songs. Multiple restaurants in the village offer this experience — a highlight for visitors.

## Major Festivals

- **Guzang Festival (鼓藏节):** The grandest ancestral worship ritual. Annual version lasts 5–9 days with bull fighting and Lusheng dancing. Grand version every 13 years with extended celebrations.
- **Miao New Year (苗年):** September–November (lunar calendar) — largest celebration cycle.
- **Sisters' Meal Festival (姊妹饭节):** Mid-April, romance-focused. Women wear elaborate silver jewelry and colorful clothing.
- **New Rice Festival:** June–July.
- **Lusheng Festival:** January–March.

## Miao Silver Jewelry (苗银)

Miao women wear elaborate silver headdresses, necklaces, and bracelets during festivals — some sets weigh several kilograms. Silver symbolizes wealth, beauty, and protection from evil spirits. The craft is passed down through generations. Shops throughout the village sell silver jewelry; quality varies.

## Viewing Decks (观景台)

Located on the opposite hillside from the main village. Electric cars operate 8:00 AM to midnight to reach the decks. The panoramic view at dawn and after dark (when houses light up) are the two most photographed moments.

## Food to Try

- **Sour soup fish (酸汤鱼):** Signature Miao dish — tangy tomato-based broth with fresh river fish.
- **Ancient Zang meat (古藏肉):** Cured pork aged in traditional methods.
- **Sour fish soup (酸鱼汤):** Lighter, more delicate fermented-rice broth.
- **Qingyan tofu (青岩豆腐):** Crispy grilled tofu with chili dip.

## Photography Tips

- Best light: ~8:00 AM for morning mist and activity; late afternoon for golden light on wooden houses.
- Evening: Houses illuminated after dark create spectacular hillside panorama from viewing deck.
- Subjects: Wooden stilted houses, villagers in traditional dress, performances, terraced rice paddies.
- Festival periods offer the richest photographic opportunities.

## Practical Tips

- Allow minimum 1–2 days for a full experience including day and nighttime panoramic views.
- Best months: April–May (Sisters' Meal Festival), June–July (New Rice Festival), November (Miao New Year), January (Lusheng Festival).
- Wear comfortable walking shoes — the village is built on steep hillsides with stone paths.
- Combine with Langde Miao Village (朗德苗寨) for a quieter, more authentic counterpart.
- The village is heavily commercialized along the main road; venture into the back lanes for more genuine village life.
