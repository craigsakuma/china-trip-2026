# Off-the-Beaten-Track Villages in Guizhou (贵州)

**Source:** Alex Reynolds (lostwithpurpose.com), solo female traveler who lived in China for a year. First-hand experience from extensive off-the-beaten-path travel in ethnic minority villages around Kaili.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Base city:** Kaili (凯里), capital of Qiandongnan Miao and Dong Autonomous Prefecture
- **Best approach:** Stay in Kaili, day-trip to villages
- **Getting to Kaili from Guiyang:** High-speed train 38–46 min / 60 RMB ($9.50); slow train 3 hrs / 15–30 RMB ($2.50–$5)
- **Taxi Guiyang airport → station:** 30–40 RMB to Guiyang station; 60 RMB to North station
- **Peak seasons:** Golden Week (October) — guaranteed traditional dress performances; Lusheng Festival (Feb/Mar, lunar calendar)
- **Off-season advantage:** Author was the only tourist, enabling authentic unscripted interactions

---

## Kaili (凯里) — Base City

Lively street life with evening vendors, games, and food stalls. The **Sunday market** draws women from surrounding villages in full traditional dress, selling handicrafts. Dog meat is openly sold at the market (heads up for sensitive travelers).

**Accommodation:** New Mill Inns — clean, affordable, convenient location.

---

## Matang (麻塘) — Gejia Wax Batik Village

**People:** Gejia (革家), a subgroup of the Miao. Known for distinctive indigo and bright orange traditional costumes.

**What to see/do:**
- Wax batik workshops — participate hands-on in making batik textiles
- Shop handmade textiles directly from artisans
- Author found locals "a lot of fun" and genuinely welcoming

**Getting there:** Mini-bus from Beijing West Road (北京西路) headed to Longchang (龙场). ~25 minutes. Ask the driver for the Matang stop, then walk 2 km to the village.

---

## Langde (郎德) — Stilted Houses and Rice Paddies

**Highlights:** Picturesque traditional Diaojiaolou (吊脚楼) wooden stilted houses. Scenic rice paddies and river landscape.

**What to see/do:**
- Hike along the river into the rice fields
- Optional traditional hairstyling with flowers (author's verdict: "pretty painful")
- Photography of stilted wooden architecture

**Getting there:** Bus to Leishan (雷山) from North Wenhua Road (北文化路) station. ~40 min, 10 RMB ($1.60). Then 20-minute walk along the river to the village.

**Accommodation:** Homestays with local families. Contact Louisa: wuminlouisa@yahoo.com

---

## Zhouxi (舟溪) — Indigo Dyeing and Buffalo Fighting

**Character:** Most relaxed of the three villages. Fewer tourists. Traditional houses climb a steep hillside. Split by the main road.

**What to see/do:**
- Watch local women practicing indigo dyeing
- Water buffalo fighting ring by the river
- Feels genuinely "off the beaten track"

**Getting there:** 15 km southwest of Kaili. Mini-bus from corner of Huancheng West Road (环城西路) and Beijing West Road (北京西路). ~20 minutes.

---

## Practical Tips

- **Skip peak season** for authentic encounters — Golden Week and Lusheng Festival bring crowds and staged performances. Off-season means real village life and genuine interactions.
- **Slow down** — the pace of village life and the natural setting matter more than checking off sights.
- **Kaili as hub** — all three villages are easy half-day trips; no need to move accommodation.
- **Language:** Very little English spoken. Basic Mandarin phrases or a translation app are essential.
- **Respect:** These are living communities, not theme parks. Ask before photographing people.
