# Zhenyuan Ancient Town (镇远古镇) — Eastern Guizhou Stopover Guide

**Source:** China Highlights (chinahighlights.com), practical guide. Positions Zhenyuan as a strategic stopover between Fanjingshan and Kaili with 2,000+ years of history along the Wuyang River.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Eastern Guizhou, ~200 km south of Guiyang (2.5 hours by car).
- **History:** Over 2,200 years old, served as a military and trade gateway between Hunan and Guizhou.
- **River:** Wuyang River (舞阳河) curves through town in an S-shape, giving it the nickname "Oriental Venice."
- **Size:** 3.1 square kilometers, 160+ scenic spots.
- **Strategic position:** ~2 hours from both Mount Fanjing (梵净山) and Kaili (凯里) — ideal midpoint stopover.
- **Best time:** March–May and September–November, temperatures 15–25°C.

## Main Attractions

### Qinglongdong Ancient Building Complex (青龙洞古建筑群)

The primary architectural highlight — a cliff-hanging complex combining Buddhist temples, Taoist palaces, and Confucian halls. Known as the "Hanging Monastery of Southwest China." The highest point offers panoramic views of the town and river. Allow ~1 hour for exploration.

### Wuyang River (舞阳河) Evening Cruise

The town is built along both riverbanks. After dark, traditional architecture is illuminated and reflected in the water. Evening boat cruises are a highlight — the S-shaped river bend creates mirror-like reflections.

### Historic Streets — Fuxing Alley (复兴巷) and Renshou Alley (仁寿巷)

Preserved Ming and Qing dynasty residences with distinctive horse-head walls (马头墙). Morning visits capture local market activity, breakfast preparation, and daily life.

## Time Allocation

- Historic alley exploration: 1–2 hours
- Qinglongdong complex: 1 hour
- Wuyang River evening cruise: flexible
- Recommended: arrive afternoon, explore alleys, visit Qinglongdong, stay for evening river views. Half-day visitors should prioritize Qinglongdong.

## Local Cuisine

- **Tofu-Fish Hotpot (豆腐鱼火锅):** Local signature dish.
- **Sour Beef Hotpot (酸汤牛肉):** Tangy fermented broth with beef.
- **Fried fern root cakes (蕨粑):** Crispy, earthy mountain snack.
- **Sweet rice wine (甜米酒):** Local homebrew, mild and slightly sweet.

**Recommended restaurants:**
- Old Town Sour Soup (riverside branch) — authentic local hotpots.
- Yuan Family Tofu Shop (Fuxing Alley) — traditional soy-based snacks.
- Xunmi Riverside Restaurant — elegant venue with classic local dishes.

## Accommodation

- **Zhenyuan Dahe Guanyi Inn:** Opposite Qinglongdong, pedestrian street location. River views.
- **Zhenyuan Qiujiang Wandu Jiangnanyuan Inn:** Core ancient town location with private balconies overlooking the river.

## Getting There

- **From Guiyang:** High-speed train (~1.5 hours) or driving (~2.5 hours, 200 km).
- **From Kaili:** ~2 hours by car, 97 km.
- **From Fanjingshan (梵净山):** ~2 hours by car.

## Suggested Guizhou Circuit Integration

A 7-day Guizhou route placing Zhenyuan in context:
- Days 1–2: Guiyang (including Huangguoshu Waterfall option)
- Days 3–4: Kaili (Miao and Dong villages — Xijiang, Langde)
- Day 5: Zhenyuan (overnight stopover)
- Day 6: Mount Fanjing (梵净山)
- Day 7: Return to Guiyang or continue to Fenghuang (凤凰) in Hunan

## Practical Tips

- Zhenyuan is best experienced as an overnight stopover rather than a primary destination — especially if you've already visited other Chinese ancient towns like Dali or Lijiang.
- Late afternoon arrival captures sunset photography over the river followed by illuminated night scenery.
- The town is commercially developed but less tourist-saturated than Xijiang or Zhaoxing.
- Combine with Fanjingshan for a 2–3 day eastern Guizhou mini-loop.
- Photography: sunset and blue hour from the south bank looking north toward Qinglongdong is the classic shot.
