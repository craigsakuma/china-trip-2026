# Guizhou Cuisine (贵州菜/黔菜) — Complete Food Guide

**Source:** China Highlights (chinahighlights.com), comprehensive food guide. Covers the philosophy of Guizhou's sour-and-spicy cuisine, essential dishes, street food, night markets, and regional dining culture rooted in mountain fermentation traditions.

## Quick Facts

- **Flavor profile:** Sour (酸) and spicy (辣) — fundamentally different from Sichuan's numbing spice.
- **Core technique:** Fermentation — rice-washing water, wild tomatoes, mountain pepper used for centuries as a salt substitute in mountainous terrain where salt was scarce.
- **Philosophy:** "Mountain People's Wisdom" — preservation through fermentation rather than salting.
- **Comparison:** Similar to Thai cuisine's sour-spicy balance but achieved through entirely different fermentation methods.
- **Noodle culture:** 40+ varieties of rice and wheat noodles, bridging northern wheat and southern rice traditions.

## Signature Dishes

### Sour Soup Fish (酸汤鱼) — The Must-Try

Fresh river fish cooked in a tangy broth with tofu, bean sprouts, and seasonal vegetables. Served hotpot-style.

**Two versions:**
- **Red Sour Soup (红酸汤):** Tomatoes and red chilies create the broth. Associated with celebrations and festivals.
- **White Sour Soup (白酸汤):** Rice water fermentation alone, no tomatoes. Traditionally used during mourning periods.
- **Yin-Yang Pot (鸳鸯锅):** Both broths combined in a split pot for varied preferences.

**Where to eat:** Lianghuanzhai in Kaili (两环寨); "Lao Kaili Sour Fish Soup" (老凯里酸汤鱼) chain in Guiyang.

### Sour Beef Hotpot (酸汤牛肉)

Beef in the same tangy broth, cooked hotpot-style. A hearty variation on the sour soup theme.

### Changwang Noodles (肠旺面)

Guiyang's signature breakfast noodle. Contains pig intestines and blood pudding in a spicy, savory broth — "spicy, savory, and rich but not greasy."
**Where:** Nanmenkou Changwang Noodles (南门口肠旺面), Jiangjia Changwang Noodles (蒋家肠旺面) in Guiyang.

### Lamb/Beef Rice Noodles (羊肉/牛肉米粉)

Bone broth made with 10+ secret spices, customizable with chili oil and Sichuan pepper.
**Where:** Zunyi Xiazzi branches (遵义虾子) throughout the city.

### Tofu Pudding Noodles (豆花面)

Soft tofu with wide wheat noodles, served with a dipping sauce of scallions, garlic, chili oil, and soy sauce.

### DaPanJi — Big Plate Chicken (大盘鸡)

Whole chicken with potatoes, onions, and vegetables on one plate. Shared family-style with flatbread for sopping sauce.

## Street Food & Snacks

### Siwawa (丝娃娃) — "Thread Baby"

Thin rice wrappers wrapped around shredded vegetable threads (radish, cucumber, fish mint/折耳根). Topped with a sour-and-spicy dipping sauce. Interactive hand-wrapping experience — named because the rolled shape resembles a baby wrapped in a blanket. Created during Ming-Qing migrations.

### Sticky Rice (糯米饭)

Glutinous rice mixed with soy sauce, lard, chili, crispy pork bits, and scallions. Historically cultivated in poor mountain soils; portable for farming and hunting. Guiyang's quintessential breakfast.

### Love Tofu Pudding (恋爱豆腐)

Grilled tofu with crispy golden skin, served with fish mint, chili, and soy dipping sauce. Named for young couples' dating tradition in Guiyang — vendors set up near dating spots.

### Qingyan Braised Pork Hock (青岩卤猪脚)

Signature dish from Qingyan Ancient Town (青岩古镇). Braised for hours over low flame with 10+ herbs and spices. Rich, gelatinous, and deeply flavored.

### Rose Iced Jelly (玫瑰冰粉)

Popular summer dessert to cool down after spicy food. Sweet, floral, and refreshing.

## Night Markets & Food Streets

- **Qingyun Road (青云路), Guiyang:** "Decades-old establishments alongside trendy viral food stalls." The premier nighttime food destination.
- **Erqi Road Snack Street (二七路小吃街), Guiyang:** Hundreds of meters long, featuring snacks from across the province.

## Coffee Culture (Unexpected Highlight)

Guiyang hosts "over ten champion baristas" despite growing no coffee beans. Local food's diverse and intense flavors inspire creative innovations like fish mint or Moutai-flavored coffee.
**Try:** Duide Coffee (对得咖啡) — founded by Hu Ying, World Brewers Cup champion; Captain George (乔治船长咖啡).

## Fish Mint / Houttuynia (折耳根)

Guizhou's most polarizing ingredient. A pungent root with a fishy, earthy flavor that appears in salads, stir-fries, and as a garnish on nearly everything. Loved by locals, challenging for outsiders. Try it at least once.

## Practical Tips

- Guizhou food is genuinely spicy — if you have low spice tolerance, ask for "bu la" (不辣, not spicy) or "wei la" (微辣, mildly spicy).
- Kaili is the epicenter of sour soup fish — seek it there rather than in Guiyang for the most authentic version.
- Night markets peak between 7–10 PM; arrive early for the best stall selection.
- Many Miao and Dong village meals include rice wine toasts — pace yourself, the alcohol content varies wildly.
- Qingyan Ancient Town (30 minutes from Guiyang) is worth a half-day trip just for the braised pork hock and local snacks.
