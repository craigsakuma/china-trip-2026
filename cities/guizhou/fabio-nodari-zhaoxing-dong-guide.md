# Zhaoxing Dong Village — Quick Travel Guide

**Source:** Fabio Nodari (fabionodariphoto.com), travel photographer based in China. Published Jan 11, 2026. All first-hand experience. Fabio has mixed feelings — heavily developed but worthwhile for first-time China visitors with right expectations.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Liping County, Qiandongnan Miao and Dong Autonomous Prefecture, Guizhou Province (贵州省).
- **Established:** ~1160 AD during the Southern Song Dynasty.
- **Ethnic group:** Dong minority (侗族) — Sino-Tibetan language family. Famous for wooden architecture without nails, advanced terraced rice cultivation.
- **Key features:** Drum Towers (Gulou), Wind and Rain Bridges (Fengyuqiao), traditional wooden houses, terraced rice paddies.
- **Best time:** Spring (Apr–Jun) for water-filled rice fields. Rains almost nonstop year-round (keeps Guizhou green). No extreme summer heat. AVOID national holidays.

## Getting There

HSR to Congjiang Station (从江站), then taxi to old town. Well connected to Guiyang, Guangzhou, and Guilin. Train tickets on Trip.com.

## Where to Stay

Plenty of options at all budgets. Trip.com is cheaper than Booking.com for Chinese hotels. Stay along the main road so attractions aren't far.

## Drum Towers (鼓楼)

Centerpiece of Zhaoxing's architecture and Dong community life. Multi-story wooden structures built entirely without nails. Each clan traditionally built its own tower — served as timekeeping, meeting place, and venue for festivals, ceremonies, and public discussions. Remain powerful symbols of Dong culture and community gathering points.

## Wind and Rain Bridges (风雨桥)

Built without nails, featuring unique roofed structures spanning streams and connecting different parts of the village. Architectural hallmark of the Dong people.

## Traditional Houses and Daily Life

Multi-story wooden houses — living quarters above, storage/livestock below. Decorated with detailed wood carvings and tiled roofs. Walking through the village you see farming, weaving, and community gatherings alongside the architecture.

## Dong Embroidery and Batik Workshops

Elaborate embroidery with geometric and floral patterns (symbolic meanings tied to local beliefs). Batik: hot wax on fabric before dyeing creates blue-and-white patterns unique to Dong tradition. Watch artisans work and buy handmade textiles — embroidered clothing, accessories, batik wall hangings.

## Viewpoint

The iconic entrance view with rice terraces is from the viewpoint right outside the main gate. Don't miss it.

## Tangan Dong Zhai (堂安侗寨)

Small village about 30 minutes from Zhaoxing by local minibus or taxi (ask hotel to book). Fewer tourists, many Dong people still live here. Nice contrast with Zhaoxing. Spend a few hours exploring and hiking rice terraces. About 2 hours from Longji Rice Terraces in Guangxi.

## Local Cuisine

- **Sour Soup Fish (酸汤鱼):** Tangy broth with fresh river fish — signature dish.
- **Bamboo Rice (竹筒饭):** Cooked inside bamboo tubes.
- **Dong Sour Soup (酸汤):** Pickled vegetables and chili peppers.
- **Steamed River Fish (蒸鱼):** Fresh and simple preparation.

## Fabio's Honest Assessment

Village is heavily developed with little regard for preserving ancient customs. Most stores and hotels managed by people from other areas with no interest in local culture. But if it's your first time in China and you have realistic expectations, Zhaoxing is a good option — especially combined with less touristy nearby villages. Xijiang (Miao village) is bigger and has more to explore if choosing between the two.
