# Baisha & Shuhe Ancient Towns (白沙古镇 & 束河古镇) — Lijiang, Yunnan

**Source:** Composite — travelchinawith.me, yunnanexploration.com, chinaxiantour.com

## Quick Facts — Baisha

- **Chinese name:** 白沙古镇 (Báishā Gǔzhèn)
- **Location:** 8 km north of Lijiang Old Town, at the foot of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain
- **Founded:** Tang Dynasty (618–907); original Naxi capital
- **Significance:** Birthplace of the Mu clan (木氏); political/cultural center during Song Dynasty
- **Entrance fee:** ~CNY 80 adults (includes mural access); village streets free
- **Hours:** 08:00–18:00
- **Best months:** March–May, September–November
- **Time needed:** 2–3 hours

## Baisha Murals (白沙壁画) — Ming Dynasty Masterpieces

The Baisha Murals are the artistic crown jewel of Naxi heritage, created primarily during the Ming Dynasty (1368–1644).

### Key Mural Sites

**Dabaoji Palace (大宝积宫) — Main Collection**
- Contains 28 fresco groups — the largest collection
- Depicts Buddhist, Taoist, and Tibetan Buddhist narratives
- Unique fusion of Naxi, Tibetan, Han, and Bai artistic styles
- Most vivid and best-preserved murals

**Liuli Temple / Colored Glaze Temple (琉璃殿)**
- Vibrant colors; Buddhist iconography
- Smaller but exceptionally well-preserved

**Dading Pavilion (大定阁)**
- 16 mural paintings from the Qing Dynasty
- Later period works showing evolution of style

### What Makes Them Special
- One of the few places in China where Buddhist, Taoist, and Tibetan Buddhist imagery coexist on the same walls
- Reflect the multicultural tolerance of the Naxi people under Mu clan rule
- Painted by artists from multiple ethnic backgrounds over several centuries
- Some murals contain rare depictions of everyday Naxi life alongside religious scenes

## Traditional Crafts in Baisha

### Naxi Embroidery Institute (纳西刺绣院)
- Located steps north of the main market square
- Preserves traditional Naxi embroidery techniques
- Motifs: suns, flowers, animals, human figures inspired by nature and Dongba mythology
- Visitors can watch artisans at work and purchase pieces

### Dongba Paper Making (东巴造纸)
- Handmade from local tree bark using methods unchanged since the 14th century
- Used for Dongba religious texts and now sold as art/souvenirs
- Some workshops offer hands-on demonstrations

### Woodcarving
- Traditional Naxi architectural and decorative woodcarving
- Visible on temple doors, window frames, and household items throughout the village

## Baisha Village Atmosphere

- Quiet, unhurried pace — sharply contrasts with bustling Lijiang Old Town
- Main street lined with small shops, cafes, and artisan workshops
- Stone-paved lanes lead to traditional courtyards
- Jade Dragon Snow Mountain visible as constant backdrop
- Local morning market with fresh produce and snacks
- Fewer tourists than Shuhe or Dayan, maintaining authentic village character

## Quick Facts — Shuhe

- **Chinese name:** 束河古镇 (Shùhé Gǔzhèn)
- **Location:** 4 km northwest of Lijiang Old Town
- **Founded:** ~800+ years ago; earliest Naxi settlement in Lijiang region
- **UNESCO status:** Part of the Old Town of Lijiang World Heritage site (with Dayan and Baisha)
- **Historical role:** Major stop on the Ancient Tea-Horse Road (茶马古道)
- **Entrance fee:** Officially CNY 30–40; often not collected (enter via back streets)
- **Best months:** Year-round; spring and autumn optimal
- **Time needed:** 2–4 hours

## Shuhe Ancient Town Highlights

### Tea-Horse Road Heritage (茶马古道)
- Shuhe was a key waystation for horse caravans trading tea, salt, and goods between Yunnan, Sichuan, and Tibet
- The town's leather-working tradition originated from making horse saddles and gear for caravans
- Leather shops remain a Shuhe signature — bags, belts, journals handmade by local craftsmen

### Sifang Street (四方街/束河四方街)
- Central gathering square with stone benches
- Surrounded by shops, restaurants, and tea houses
- Less crowded than Lijiang's Sifang Square
- Daily Dongba calligraphy stalls and small performance squares

### Jiuding Dragon Pool (九鼎龙潭)
- Clear spring-fed pool at the town's north end
- Ancient willows reflected in turquoise water
- Connected to Shuhe's canal system
- Photography highlight: reflections of surrounding traditional buildings

### Shuhe Leather and Craft Shops
- Handmade leather goods: bags, journals, belts, wallets
- Watch craftsmen at work in open-front workshops
- Higher quality and more authentic than Lijiang Old Town tourist shops
- Bargaining expected; prices CNY 50–500+ depending on item

### Ancient Bridges and Canals
- Stone bridges crossing clear-water canals
- Dashi Bridge (大石桥) — oldest and most photographed
- Water channels connect through entire town, similar to but smaller than Dayan's system

## Shuhe Atmosphere

- Quieter and more residential than Lijiang Old Town
- Mix of guesthouses, cafes, and working households
- More trees and green spaces; feels like a garden village
- Popular with travelers seeking a relaxed alternative to Dayan's crowds
- Small bars and live music venues along back lanes

## Getting There from Lijiang Old Town

### To Baisha (8 km north)
- **Bus:** No. 6 to Baisha Intersection, then walk/taxi; ~30 min total
- **Taxi:** ~CNY 40 (15 min)
- **Bicycle:** ~40 min; flat road through farmland; popular cycling route
- **E-bike:** ~20 min; rentals available in Old Town

### To Shuhe (4 km northwest)
- **Bus:** No. 11 from Old Town; ~15 min
- **Taxi:** ~CNY 15–20 (10 min)
- **Walk:** ~45 min along road or canal path
- **Bicycle/e-bike:** ~15 min

### Combined Baisha + Shuhe Day Trip
- Cycle from Lijiang Old Town → Shuhe (morning) → Baisha (midday) → return
- Total cycling distance: ~20 km round trip
- Perfect half-day or full-day excursion

## Practical Tips

- **Baisha morning market** (before 09:00): Fresh produce, local snacks, authentic village life
- **Baisha murals need context** — consider hiring a local guide (CNY 50–100) to explain the religious imagery
- **Shuhe's back streets** are more interesting than the main tourist drag
- **Photography:** Baisha is best in morning light with snow mountain backdrop; Shuhe's Dragon Pool best at midday
- **Both villages are flat** — suitable for all fitness levels
- **Combine with:** Jade Dragon Snow Mountain (Baisha is at its base), Lijiang Old Town evening activities
- **Respect:** Both are living villages — be considerate of residents; ask before photographing people
- **Altitude:** Same as Lijiang (~2,400 m); stay hydrated, wear sun protection
