# Tiger Leaping Gorge Hiking Guide (虎跳峡徒步指南)

**Source:** Composite — chinadiscovery.com, nickkembel.com (2026 updated guide)

## Quick Facts

- **Chinese name:** 虎跳峡 (Hǔ Tiào Xiá) — "Tiger Leaping Gorge"
- **Location:** Jinsha River canyon between Jade Dragon Snow Mountain and Haba Snow Mountain
- **Administrative area:** Shangri-La County, Diqing Prefecture (technically not in Lijiang, but accessed from there)
- **Canyon length:** 15 km; total vertical relief 3,790 m (river to peak)
- **Altitude range:** 1,600–2,650 m (hiking trail); river at ~1,800 m
- **Entrance fee:** CNY 65 per person
- **Trek duration:** 2–3 days (high trail); half-day (Upper Gorge viewpoint only)
- **Best months:** Late March–early June, September–early November
- **Avoid:** June–September rainy season (landslide risk)

## Trail Overview: High Trail vs Low Trail

### High Trail (上路/高路) — The Classic Trek
- **Distance:** ~22 km total
- **Duration:** 9–11 hours of hiking over 2 days
- **Elevation:** Climbs from ~1,800 m to ~2,650 m, then descends
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to strenuous; the 28 Bends section is demanding
- **Scenery:** Dramatic canyon views, terraced hillsides, Naxi villages
- **Signage:** Blue trail markers; well-trodden path; blue emergency poles along route

### Low Trail (下路/公路) — Road Route
- **Follows:** The paved road along the river
- **Difficulty:** Easy, flat
- **Scenery:** Closer to water but less dramatic than high trail
- **Best for:** Those with limited fitness or time; can be driven

## Day-by-Day Itinerary (2-Day High Trail)

### Day 1: Qiaotou (桥头) to Halfway Guesthouse
- **Total hiking:** ~6–7 hours
- **Start:** Ticket office near local school at Qiaotou town (new trailhead as of 2025)

**Leg 1 — Qiaotou to Naxi Family Guesthouse (~2 hours)**
- Gradual ascent through farmland and scattered villages
- Coffee and rest stop available at Naxi Family Guesthouse (纳西雅阁)

**Leg 2 — Naxi Family to Tea Horse Guesthouse via 28 Bends (~3 hours)**
- The 28 Bends (二十八拐) — the most famous and challenging section
- 28 steep switchbacks climbing ~800 m elevation gain
- **Horse rental available** at base of 28 Bends for tired hikers (negotiate price)
- Arrives at Tea Horse Guesthouse (茶马客栈) — possible overnight if exhausted

**Leg 3 — Tea Horse to Halfway Guesthouse (~2 hours)**
- Relatively flat traverse with spectacular canyon views
- **Halfway Guesthouse (中途客栈):** Most popular overnight stop; balcony views of gorge
- Recommended Day 1 endpoint

**Advanced option:** Continue 1.5–2 hours past Halfway to Tina's Youth Hostel (on highway level)

### Day 2: Halfway Guesthouse to Middle Gorge and Exit
- **Total hiking:** ~4–5 hours

**Leg 1 — Halfway to Tina's Youth Hostel (~1.5–2 hours)**
- Downhill descent to highway level
- Tina's is the base for Middle Gorge access

**Leg 2 — Middle Tiger Leaping Gorge Side Trip (~2 hours round trip)**
- Descent from highway to river level via "Teacher Zhang's Path" (张老师小路)
- **Extra fee:** CNY 10–15 for trail access (maintained by locals)
- Most spectacular section: raging river, massive boulders, closest views
- **Safety note:** Teacher Zhang's Path is the safest descent option; avoid the "iron ladder" route (rickety, dangerous)
- **Guide to Middle Gorge:** CNY 600 as of late 2025 (required if going with escort)

**Exit:** Return to highway, catch bus back to Lijiang or onward to Shangri-La
- **Bus warning:** Last bus departs ~15:00–15:30; sometimes canceled — have backup plan

## Three Gorge Sections Explained

### Upper Gorge (上虎跳) — Most Visited
- 75% of famous Tiger Leaping Gorge photos come from here
- Iconic tiger sculpture at viewpoint
- Accessible as short half-day trip (30-min walk from parking lot)
- Where the tiger allegedly leaped across the narrowest point

### Middle Gorge (中虎跳) — Most Spectacular
- Raging whitewater, massive boulders, deepest canyon walls
- Reached via trails from highway (Teacher Zhang's Path recommended)
- Not officially maintained; built by local villagers
- The "must-do" for serious hikers

### Lower Gorge (下虎跳) — Least Visited
- Accessed from Lijiang side; less developed
- Rapids and calmer stretches

## Getting There

### From Lijiang (丽江)
- **Distance:** 78 km to Qiaotou trailhead
- **By bus:** From Lijiang Coach Station; ~2 hours; schedules vary
- **By private car:** ~2 hours; more reliable than bus
- **Recommended:** Arrange private car through guesthouse or tour operator

### From Shangri-La (香格里拉)
- **Distance:** 103 km to Upper Gorge
- **By bus:** From Shangri-La Coach Station; ~2.5 hours
- **Useful for:** Exiting trek and continuing north to Shangri-La

### Luggage Forwarding
- Available as of 2025: Send bags from trailhead to trail end
- Arrange through guesthouses or guides at trailhead

## What to Pack

- **Water:** At least 2 liters (very few shops on trail)
- **Snacks:** Energy bars, trail mix, fruit
- **Rain gear:** Lightweight shell jacket
- **Footwear:** Sturdy hiking boots with ankle support and grip
- **Sun protection:** Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
- **Layers:** Morning starts cool; midday sun can be hot
- **Headlamp:** If starting early or arriving late to guesthouse
- **Cash:** Guesthouses and local vendors may not accept digital payment

## Safety Tips

- **Watch for falling rocks** after rain — stay alert on exposed sections
- **Start Day 1 early** (before 09:00) to ensure arrival at guesthouse before dark
- **Do not attempt 28 Bends if exhausted** — hire a horse instead
- **Teacher Zhang's Path is safer** than the iron ladder descent to Middle Gorge
- **Altitude is moderate** (max ~2,650 m) — most fit hikers have no issues
- **Emergency poles** with blue buttons are placed along the high trail
- **Rainy season (Jun–Sep):** Trail can be muddy, landslides possible; not recommended

## Practical Tips

- **Book Halfway Guesthouse in advance** during spring and autumn peak
- **Carry enough cash** for guesthouse stays (CNY 50–150/night) and meals
- **Start early on Day 1** for time to enjoy views and photograph the canyon
- **The trek is point-to-point** — arrange transport at both ends
- **Combine with:** Shangri-La (continue north) or return to Lijiang (south)
