# Lijiang Travel Guide — Top Things to Do

**Source:** Fabio Nodari (fabionodariphoto.com), travel photographer based in China. Published Oct 17, 2025. Updated Jan 19, 2026. All first-hand experience.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Yunnan Province (云南省), ~2,400m elevation. On the ancient Tea Horse Road.
- **Ethnic groups:** Naxi (纳西) minority — wooden houses, tiled roofs, intricate carvings. Mosuo (摩梭) — matriarchal society near Lugu Lake.
- **UNESCO:** Lijiang Old Town (丽江古城) = Dayan + Shuhe + Baisha, all UNESCO World Heritage.
- **Best time:** Spring (Mar–May) for clear air and flowers. Autumn (Sep–Nov) for blue skies and harvest charm. Summer rainy but still crowded. Winter cold but quiet (except CNY), sunny dry days.
- **Known reality:** One of the most visited cities in China. Always been busy (Tea Horse Road heritage). Heavily commercialized Old Town — most buildings rebuilt after 1996 earthquake.

## Getting to Lijiang

**Flights:** From most major Chinese cities (Shanghai, Beijing, etc.).
**Train:** HSR from Kunming. Also HSR to Shangri-La.
**At the station:** Don't take taxis waiting for tourists — overpriced. Take a bus outside the station for 3 RMB to Old Town.
**Recommended stay:** At least a few nights; more if venturing to lesser-known areas.

## Lijiang Old Town / Dayan Old Town (丽江古城 / 大研古城)

**Clarification:** "Lijiang Old Town" is the broader UNESCO site (Dayan + Shuhe + Baisha). "Dayan Old Town" is the historic heart that most people mean — Sifang Street (四方街), narrow alleys, canals, stone bridges. Entrance is now free (was 80 RMB). Black Dragon Pool (黑龙潭) just outside still requires a ticket — worth visiting.

Famous for Naxi architecture. However, a powerful 1996 earthquake destroyed much of the historic architecture — what you see today is almost entirely rebuilt in traditional Naxi style. People don't really live here anymore; most houses are shops, restaurants, and hotels.

**Photography tip:** Go before sunrise when streets are empty and quiet. Then visit Zhongyi Market (忠义市场) for local produce shopping.

## Shuhe Old Town (束河)

Part of UNESCO site, just outside Lijiang. Smaller, far fewer tourists, especially clear water. Important Tea Horse Road stop. More local atmosphere. Quick Didi ride from Lijiang (~20 min).

## Baisha Old Town (白沙)

Gaining popularity but still quieter. 20 minutes from Lijiang. Former political and cultural center of the Naxi people. Old murals and temples. Growing number of coffee shops and studios. Visit sooner rather than later before it gets busy.

## Yuhu Village (玉湖村)

At the foot of Jade Dragon Snow Mountain. People still live here (unlike Lijiang Old Town). Stone houses, quiet alleys, mountain views — more authentic feel. Not many tourists, especially early morning. Former home of Austrian explorer Joseph Rock (first Westerner to write about Naxi people), though all traces have been removed. Easy Didi access.

## Jade Dragon Snow Mountain / Yulong (玉龙雪山)

Sacred to the Naxi people. Snow-covered peak visible from Lijiang on clear days. Visit early for clearest skies and less wind. Cable cars often packed, thousands of visitors daily. Weather changes quickly at altitude. Detailed separate guide available for transport, tickets, and photography tips.

## Blue Moon Valley (蓝月谷)

Inside Jade Dragon Snow Mountain National Park. Turquoise water from melting glacier — color is almost unreal on sunny days. Surrounded by forested hills and snow-capped peaks. One of most photogenic spots in the park but also busiest. Go early to avoid mid-morning tour group rush.

## Tiger Leaping Gorge (虎跳峡)

One of the deepest and most spectacular river canyons in the world — maximum depth ~3,790 meters. Jinsha River (main Yangtze tributary) carves through the gorge. Getting there by public transport is complicated — hire a driver from Lijiang recommended.

## Shigu and the First Bend of the Yangtze (石鼓)

Small town where the Yangtze makes a dramatic 180-degree turn. Important Tea Horse Road stop. Impressive viewpoint with surrounding countryside good for light hiking. Area changing due to Central Yunnan Water Diversion Project and planned expressway — visit before more changes come.

## Lugu Lake (泸沽湖)

3-hour drive from Lijiang via new road. Stunning clear blue water surrounded by mountains. Home to Mosuo minority — one of the last matriarchal societies (women head households, inheritance through female line). Recently seeing massive tourism increase.

## Shangri-La (香格里拉)

Tibetan town reachable by bus or HSR from Lijiang. Original old town destroyed in 2014 fire, rebuilt in traditional style. Good and more affordable way to experience Tibetan culture if you can't travel to Tibet or Western Sichuan. Monasteries, prayer flags, high-altitude landscapes. Can be extremely overcrowded now that it's connected by train.

## Where to Stay

Hostels from ~5€/night, hotels and guesthouses for every budget. Use Trip.com (more options than Booking.com). Be aware: beds usually very hard (Chinese medicine tradition). Most rooms lack central heating — nights surprisingly cold, especially in winter. Electric blankets standard.

## Practical Tips

- **VPN:** Essential — all social media and Google/Gmail blocked without one.
- **Commercialization:** Lijiang is beautiful but don't expect a quiet untouched village. For something quieter: Jingmai Mountain or Samaba Rice Terraces (southern Yunnan).
- **Base camp:** Use Lijiang as a base to explore Shuhe, Baisha, Yuhu, Jade Dragon Snow Mountain, Tiger Leaping Gorge, and Shangri-La.
- **Onward:** Continue journey through Yunnan to Dali Old Town and Shaxi.
