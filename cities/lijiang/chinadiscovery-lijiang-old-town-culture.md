# Lijiang Old Town Culture & History (丽江古城文化历史)

**Source:** China Discovery — chinadiscovery.com/yunnan/lijiang/lijiang-old-town.html

## Quick Facts

- **Chinese name:** 丽江古城 / 大研古城 (Lìjiāng Gǔchéng / Dàyán Gǔchéng)
- **UNESCO World Heritage:** Listed 1997; includes Dayan, Shuhe, and Baisha Old Towns
- **Founded:** Late Song / early Yuan Dynasty (~800+ years ago)
- **Elevation:** ~2,400 m
- **Ethnic group:** Naxi (纳西族) — ~30% of 25,000 residents
- **Maintenance fee:** CNY 50 (valid one week; 08:30–19:00)
- **Best months:** Year-round; March–May and September–November ideal

## Historical Significance

### Origins and the Mu Family (木氏)
- Established as trading hub at the junction of Sichuan–Yunnan–Tibet trade routes
- Ruled by the Mu Family (木氏土司) as Tusi administrators for centuries
- **Why no city walls:** The character 木 (Mu/wood) enclosed becomes 困 (kun/trapped) — the Mu clan deliberately avoided fortification to escape this omen
- Connected the southern Silk Road with the Ancient Tea and Horse Road (茶马古道)

### Cultural Crossroads
- 800+ years of exchange between Naxi, Han, Bai, Tibetan, and Yi peoples
- Architecture, art, and urban planning reflect this multicultural blend
- The town's canal system, wooden buildings, and bridge network are uniquely preserved

## Architecture & Urban Layout

### Water System (水系)
- Complex canal network fed by Black Dragon Pool (黑龙潭) and Jade River (玉河)
- Three main channels flow through the town, subdividing into countless smaller waterways
- Canals run alongside streets, under bridges, and through courtyards
- The system still functions today for irrigation, washing, and aesthetics
- **354 bridges** spanning the canals — stone arches, plank bridges, wind-and-rain covered bridges

### Street Layout
- **Sifang Square (四方街):** Central civic hub; four main streets radiate outward
- **Xinhua Street (新华街):** Main commercial corridor
- **Wuyi Street, Guangyi Street, Qiyi Street:** Other main arteries
- No grid pattern — streets follow natural contours and waterways
- Naxi buildings: timber-framed, tile-roofed, two-story with courtyards; blend Han, Bai, and Tibetan elements

## Key Cultural Sites

### Mu's Residence (木府) — "Miniature Forbidden City"
- **Entrance:** CNY 60
- Originally ~100 buildings; destroyed and rebuilt multiple times
- Tusi administrative halls, ceremonial spaces, gardens
- Showcases the power and cultural sophistication of the Mu clan
- **Allow:** 1–1.5 hours

### Wangu Tower (万古楼) on Lion Hill (狮子山)
- **Entrance:** CNY 50
- 33-meter, five-story wooden pagoda
- **Best panoramic viewpoint** in Lijiang — 360-degree views of Old Town, Jade Dragon Snow Mountain
- ~30-minute climb from Sifang Square
- **Photography tip:** Go at sunset for golden light over the rooftops

### Black Dragon Pool Park (黑龙潭公园)
- Moon-Embracing Pavilion (得月楼), Dragon God Temple, Five-Phoenix Tower (built 1737)
- Iconic reflection photo: pool with Jade Dragon Snow Mountain backdrop
- **Best time:** Early morning for still-water reflections
- Free entry (maintenance fee covers access)

### Dongba Culture Museum (东巴文化博物馆)
- **Entrance:** CNY 30
- 12,000+ artifacts including Dongba scriptures, religious instruments, paintings
- Explains Dongba religion, Naxi history, and the pictographic script
- **Allow:** 1 hour

## Naxi Culture (纳西族文化)

### Dongba Script (东巴文)
- World's only living pictographic writing system
- Used by Dongba priests to record religious texts, myths, and historical events
- Each symbol is a picture representing a word or concept
- UNESCO Memory of the World Register
- **See it:** Dongba Culture Museum, calligraphy stalls in Shuhe, painted on shop signs throughout Old Town

### Dongba Religion (东巴教)
- Shamanistic belief system blending animism, nature worship, and Tibetan Buddhist elements
- Dongba priests serve as intermediaries between human and spirit worlds
- Key ceremonies: Sacrifice to Heaven, Sacrifice to Shu (nature god), ancestor worship
- Elaborate rituals involve chanting, dancing, offerings, burning of Dongba texts

### Naxi Ancient Music (纳西古乐)
- **Origin:** 14th century; called a "living fossil of Chinese music"
- **Instruments:** Lute, flute, erhu, various percussion — some centuries old
- **Character:** Melancholic melodies integrating Taoist, Confucian, and literary traditions from Tang, Song, and Yuan dynasties
- **Performance:** Naxi Concert Hall (纳西古乐会), Old Town
- **Schedule:** Nightly, 20:00–21:30
- **Performers:** ~20 elderly musicians in purple ceremonial gowns; led by Xuan Ke (宣科) and successors
- **Note:** Musicians play without sheet music — melodies passed orally for generations; instruments were hidden underground during the Cultural Revolution
- **Tickets:** ~CNY 100–160

## Festivals

| Festival | Timing (Lunar) | What |
|----------|---------------|------|
| **Bangbang Festival (棒棒会)** | Jan 15 | Agricultural tool market and celebration |
| **Sanduo Festival (三朵节)** | Feb 8 | Naxi national festival honoring protective deity Sanduo |
| **Torch Festival (火把节)** | Jun 25–27 | Fire celebrations shared with Yi and Bai peoples |
| **July Festival (七月会)** | Mid-Jul | Trading fair and cultural performances |

## Nightlife and Entertainment

### Bar Street (酒吧街)
- Concentrated along Xinhua Street and around Sifang Square
- Mix of loud music bars and quieter lounge-style venues
- Live music (Chinese pop, folk, Western) nightly
- Peak hours: 21:00–01:00

### Dongba Palace Show (东巴宫)
- Grand live theater: "Mountain and River Show" (印象丽江 — separate from Zhang Yimou's mountain show)
- Naxi customs, daily life, fairy tales portrayed through dance and music

## Shopping

- **Dongba paper:** Handmade from tree bark using 14th-century techniques
- **Copperware:** Traditional Naxi metalwork — pots, jewelry, decorative pieces
- **Leather goods:** Shuhe Ancient Town is known for leather workshops
- **Yak wool scarves and shawls**
- **Dongba script calligraphy:** Custom pieces at calligraphy stalls
- **Silver jewelry:** Naxi-style; buy from established shops to ensure quality

## Food Specialties

- **Lijiang Baba (丽江粑粑):** Flaky wheat pastry, savory or sweet
- **Jidou Pea Jelly (鸡豆凉粉):** Cold chickpea jelly with chili oil
- **Naxi Copper Pot (纳西铜锅):** Hotpot-style dish cooked in traditional copper vessel
- **Butter Tea (酥油茶):** Tibetan-influenced; rich, salty, warming
- **Wild mushroom hotpot:** Seasonal specialty (summer–autumn)

## Practical Tips

- **Best exploration time:** Early morning (07:00–09:00) before tour groups; evening for atmosphere
- **Altitude 2,400 m** — drink extra water; sun is strong year-round
- **Photography:** Sunrise from Lion Hill; reflections at Black Dragon Pool; rooftop views from cafes on Wuyi Street
- **Getting there:** 29 km from Lijiang Airport (40 min taxi); 8 km from railway station
- **Duration:** Half day for highlights; full day to explore thoroughly
- **Combine with:** Shuhe Ancient Town (4 km north), Baisha Village (8 km north)
- **Crowding:** Sifang Square and main streets are packed during holidays; side alleys are quieter
