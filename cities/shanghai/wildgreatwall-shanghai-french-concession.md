# Former French Concession: Best Walks, Culture & Hidden Gems

**Source:** Wild Great Wall (wildgreatwall.com), China travel guide with local expertise. Published 2025.

---

## Quick Facts

- **History:** Leased to France in 1849, handed back to China in 1943
- **Nickname:** "Paris of the East"
- **Character:** Tree-lined streets, colonial buildings, boutique cafes, art galleries, historic villas
- **Metro access:** Lines 10/11 (Jiaotong University Station for Wukang Road area); Lines 7/9 (Zhaojia Bang Road Station, Exit 4 for Wulumuqi Road)
- **Best time to visit:** Early morning or late afternoon for fewer crowds and better photography light
- **Footwear:** Comfortable walking shoes essential — this neighborhood rewards wandering

---

## Wukang Road 武康路 — The Star Street

A picturesque historic street featuring 37 significant buildings. European-style architecture meets modern cafes under leafy plane trees.

### Must-See Photo Spots

**Wukang Building 武康大楼** (No. 1850 Huaihai Zhong Road)
Shanghai's most iconic residential Art Deco building — the flatiron-shaped landmark at the intersection of Wukang Road and Huaihai Middle Road. Always crowded with photographers.

**The Yellow House** (Lane 1818, Huaihai Zhong Road)
Charming yellow residence across from Song Qingling's former home. One of the most photographed spots in the French Concession.

**The Famous Balcony** (No. 129-1)
Originally featuring a butterfly bow decoration, now displaying sunflowers. Became a Shanghai Instagram sensation.

**Romeo Balcony** (No. 210)
Famous for romantic movie scenes and wedding photos — the most cinematic balcony on Wukang Road.

**Gu Zhutong's Former Residence** (No. 109)
Known for its distinctive brown door and historical significance.

### Dining & Shopping on Wukang Road

- **Pistacchio Restaurant & Bar** — Italian cuisine in a stylish setting
- **Rose Gallica** — Flower shop with an enchanting garden
- **Gathering Cafe** — Cozy spot with an internal garden
- **Birdie Cup** — Known for mint-infused drinks
- **Fluffy** — Ice cream shop with unique flavors: Unicorn Swirl, Sea Salt Cheese

## Wulumuqi Road 乌鲁木齐路 — Preserved Charm

One of Shanghai's 64 streets designated never to be widened — preserving its authentic, narrow character. Located near Fumin, Changshu, and Wukang Roads.

**Metro:** Zhaojia Bang Road Station (Lines 7/9, Exit 4)

### Historic Residences

**Xia Yan's Former Residence** (178 South Wulumuqi Road, Building 2)
Home of the renowned writer honored as "National Outstanding Contributor to Film Arts."

**Cao Ying Book Room** (Building 3)
Home of the translator who rendered Tolstoy's complete novels into Chinese.

### Cafes on Wulumuqi Road

- **T12 Cafe** — Australian Flat White served under sycamore trees
- **Luneurs Ice Cream** — Famous for their caramel sea salt ice cream (must-try)

## Gaolan Road 皋兰路 — The 278-Meter Gem

A tiny, beautiful street originally named Gaonaiyi Road after French poet Pierre Corneille. Built 1914, renamed during Japanese occupation, restored to Gaolan Road in 1946.

### Key Sites on Gaolan Road

**Russian Orthodox Church of St. Nicholas** (No. 16)
Now the **Sinan Bookstore Poetry Shop** — a stunning conversion of an onion-domed church into a literary space. Closed Wednesdays.

**Zhang Xueliang's Former Residence** (No. 1)
Spanish-style garden villa. Zhang Xueliang was the warlord famous for the Xi'an Incident that changed Chinese history.

**Fuxing Park** (No. 2)
French-style garden — watch locals dance, play cards, and practice tai chi in the mornings. Romantic atmosphere.

---

## Sinan Mansions 思南公馆 — Historic Garden Villas

Former residences of China's most influential 20th-century figures, now transformed into trendy restaurants, cafes, and cultural spaces.

### Historic Residences

**Kong Xiangxi's Former Residence** (No. 553 Fuxing Zhong Road)
Dutch-style building with distinctive twin-pitched roof. Kong was Chiang Kai-shek's brother-in-law and finance minister.

**Mei Lanfang's Former Residence** (No. 87 Sinan Road)
Home of China's most celebrated Peking opera performer. Surrounded by flowers and trees.

**Zhou Enlai's Residence** (No. 73)
Former home of China's first premier — currently under renovation.

### Nearby Cultural Sites

**Sun Yat-sen Memorial House** (No. 7 Xiangshan Road)
- **Admission:** 20 RMB
- **Hours:** 9am–5pm
- Historic residence of the founding father of the Republic of China

**Sinan Bookstore Poetry Shop** (No. 16 Gaolan Road)
Inside the converted Russian Orthodox church. Closed Wednesdays.

**Antique Garden** (No. 44 Sinan Road)
Quirky antique shop that doubles as a TV filming location.

### Cafes Near Sinan Mansions

- **Starbucks** (No. 50 Sinan Road) — Inside a historic villa, one of the most atmospheric Starbucks in Shanghai
- **Hu Shui Roastery** — Japanese-style cafe with warm atmosphere
- **Xixing Coffee** — Corner cafe with loft seating

## Anfu Road 安福路 — Boutique Shopping Street

The go-to street for boutique shopping: independently owned stores selling handmade jewelry, vintage clothing, and home goods. Also packed with hip cafes, concept bars, breweries, art galleries, and antiques shops.

---

## Key Nearby Attractions

**Fuxing Park 复兴公园** (No. 516 Fuxing Zhong Road)
French-style garden with romantic atmosphere. Free admission. Best in early morning when locals gather for tai chi and dancing.

**Xintiandi 新天地**
Trendy area with restaurants, shops, and galleries — built in restored shikumen (石库门) lane houses. Also the site of the First CCP Congress.

**Tianzifang 田子坊**
A maze of narrow alleys with art studios, creative shops, and cafes. More bohemian and casual than Xintiandi.

**Shanghai Museum of Contemporary Art (MOCA)**
Modern Chinese art and rotating exhibitions.

**The Bund 外滩**
Colonial architecture with views of Shanghai's futuristic skyline across the Huangpu River.

## Practical Tips

- **Wear comfortable shoes** — the French Concession rewards extended, unhurried walking through quiet lanes
- **Visit early morning or late afternoon** — fewer crowds, better light for photography, and you'll catch locals doing tai chi in the parks
- **Allow time to wander** — the best discoveries (hidden cafes, secret gardens, vintage shops) happen when you get off the main streets
- **Plane trees (法国梧桐)** are the signature of the French Concession — most photogenic in autumn when leaves turn gold
- **The neighborhood's charm lies in its peaceful ambiance** — slow down and explore the lilongs (里弄, lane houses) between main streets
- **Coffee culture:** The French Concession has Shanghai's densest concentration of specialty coffee shops — ideal for cafe-hopping between historic sites
