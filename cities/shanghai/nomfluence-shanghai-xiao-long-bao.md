# Best Xiao Long Bao in Shanghai: The Definitive Guide

**Source:** Rachel Gouk (rachelgouk.com / NOMFLUENCE), Shanghai-based food and drink blogger since 2011. Updated 2026.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Dish origin:** Invented ~200 years ago in Nanxiang village (南翔), now Jiading district
- **What they are:** Ping-pong-ball-sized soup dumplings with ground pork filling and broth inside a pleated wheat wrapper
- **How the soup forms:** Pork jelly (from collagen in pork skin) is mixed into the filling; when steamed, it melts into broth
- **Sold by:** The basket, called a "long" (笼) — basket sizes vary (5, 6, 8, or 12 dumplings)
- **Price range:** ¥10–¥70+ per basket depending on filling and venue
- **How to eat:** Dip in Zhenjiang brown vinegar with shredded ginger → place on spoon → bite the top → slurp the hot soup → eat the rest

## Lai Lai Xiao Long 莱莱小笼 — Best Overall / Best Crab XLB

The place to go for crab xiao long bao in Shanghai. An institution near Nanjing Dong Lu pedestrian street since 2006. Michelin Bib Gourmand recipient (Shanghai 2025).

- **Signature:** Crab roe with pork (蟹膏鲜肉) ¥70/8; crab meat with pork (蟹粉鲜肉) ¥40/8
- **Premium:** Pure crab meat (纯蟹粉) ¥78/4; pure crab roe (纯蟹膏) ¥158/4
- **Other:** Pork with matsutake (鲜肉松茸) ¥39/4; crab xiao huntun (蟹粉小馄饨) ¥30/10
- **Ginger vinegar:** ¥1
- **All dumplings made to order** — thin wrappers hold "a river of soup"
- **Tip:** Go after 1:30pm to avoid peak crowds. They take an hour lunch break. Jing'an location opened April 2023.
- **Locations:** Tianjin Lu (original), Jing'an

## Jia Jia Tang Bao 佳家汤包 — Classic Mom-and-Pop, Since 1986

One of the most popular stops for XLB in Shanghai. Nine locations citywide. Thin wrappers, abundant soup, deliciously porky fillings. All steamed to order (expect 10+ minute wait).

- **Pork:** ¥23/12 (鲜肉)
- **Pure crab:** ¥108/12 or ¥55/6 (纯蟹粉)
- **Crab and pork:** ¥38/12 (蟹粉鲜肉)
- **Best location:** Liyuan Lu store (shorter wait). The famous Huanghe Lu location moved from #90 to #127 — beware impostor "Jia Le Tang Bao" at old address.
- **Also serves:** Other dumplings, noodles, frozen huntun to go

## Fuchun Xiao Long Bao 富春小笼馆 — Legendary Since 1885

China Time-Honored Brand (老字号). Originally on Yuyuan Road, now in Xuhui District. Two-story eatery with cult following among Shanghainese dumpling purists.

- **Pork XLB:** ¥18/6 — extra-porky, rich flavor, slightly thicker "rustic" skins
- **Crab roe XLB:** ¥58/6
- **Also excellent:** Crab roe and crabmeat-topped noodles
- **Character:** More rustic than Lai Lai — thicker wrappers, porky filling

## Man Man Tang Bao 曼曼汤包 — Hole-in-the-Wall, Open Late

Tiny shop specializing in XLB, congee, and street snacks. Run by Mr. Li (in Shanghai since the 90s) and his son. All made to order despite the tiny size. Delivers via apps.

- **Prices:** Super cheap
- **Unique offering:** Shepherd's Purse (荠菜) version — uncommon for Shanghai XLB
- **Also good:** Black sesame tangyuan
- **Morning:** Congee and Chinese breakfast foods
- **Atmosphere:** Pull up a stool, order from picture menus, grab drinks at the convenience store next door

## Fumai Baozi 上海富麦包子·吴苑 — Sister of Fuchun

On Yanping Lu, run by the same crew as Fuchun. Beloved for extremely porky pork filling. 1930s Shanghai-style interiors, clean, air-conditioned.

- **Pork XLB:** ¥16/6
- **Crab XLB:** ¥38/6
- **Crab noodles:** ¥98
- **Shepherd's purse yellow croaker spring rolls:** ¥20/plate
- **Also known for:** Steamed baozi (from ¥4/piece), shaomai, baked rice cakes

## Nanxiang Mantou Dian 南翔馒头店 — The Original, in Yu Gardens

The original shop that brought XLB from Nanxiang village to downtown Shanghai. Founded 1900. Michelin Bib Gourmand. Second and third floors are modern and clean.

- **Pork:** ¥42/6
- **Crab meat and pork:** ¥72/6
- **Matsutake and pork:** ¥108/6
- **Clam and pork:** ¥48/6
- **Supersized crab XLB:** ¥42/1 (drink the soup with a straw)
- **Author's take:** "Can be a bit of a hassle to go through the queues" at Yu Gardens, not as good as Lai Lai or Jia Jia, but a worthwhile first-timer experience

## Din Tai Fung 鼎泰丰 — The Global Chain

Started in Taiwan ~50 years ago. Famous for extremely high standards: wrapper thickness, pleat count, service, hygiene. "Critics call it soulless and overpriced. Admirers call it clean and consistent."

- **Shanghai locations:** IFC, Xintiandi Style, Grand Gateway, Qiantan Taikoo Li, Taikoo Hui
- **Delivery tip:** DTF delivers XLB in plastic trays with individual holsters — works in a pinch

## Paradise Dynasty 乐忻皇朝 — Colorful Flavors from Singapore

Known for their basket of 8 non-traditional flavors in colored wrappers: mala, cheese, truffle, foie gras, and more.

- **Special Dynasty basket:** ¥72/8 (multi-flavor)
- **Original pork:** ¥29/5 or ¥42/8
- **Chili oil dumplings (红油抄手):** ¥36
- **Locations:** iapm, LuOne, Reel Mall, Shanghai Tower

## More Budget/Local Options

### Shan Shan 珊珊小笼馆
Working-class chain for Shanghainese snacks. Less finesse, more community atmosphere. Thicker wrappers. Pork ¥10/6, Wuxi sweet-style ¥10/4. 749 Kangding Lu. Daily 6am–8:30pm.

### Lao Sheng Chang 老盛昌汤包 & Lao Sheng Xing 老盛兴汤包馆
Ubiquitous snack chains doing soy-sauce-based XLB, often sweetened with sugar.

### Wan Shou Zhai 万寿斋
No-frills spot in Hongkou near Lu Xun's old house. Smaller-than-average, light-flavored dumplings. "Many Shanghainese consider this the best place in Shanghai." 123 Shanyin Lu. Daily 5am–9pm.

### Nanxiang Town 南翔镇 — The Source Village
For XLB purists: take Metro Line 11 to Nanxiang. Multiple dumpling spots around Guyi Park. The Guyi Garden restaurant (上海古猗园餐厅) claims to have invented the genre. 218 Huyi Gong Lu. Daily 8am–8pm.

## Practical Tips

- **Peak hours:** Lunch rush is the busiest; visit after 1:30pm or go early morning for shorter waits
- **Ordering:** Look at the menu for "zhi" (只) count per basket — it varies by shop
- **Burn warning:** XLB are steamed to order and extremely hot — always bite carefully and slurp soup from spoon
- **Crab season:** Traditional hairy crab season is September through winter, but crab XLB are increasingly available year-round
- **Budget strategy:** Shan Shan or Man Man for ¥10–16 per basket; splurge at Lai Lai for crab varieties
- **Delivery:** Man Man and Din Tai Fung both deliver via Chinese food apps
