# Top 10 Things to Do in Shanghai (上海)

**Source:** Nick & Dariece (goatsontheroad.com), long-running couple travel blog, lived in China 13+ months and visited Shanghai 10 times. Published July 2013, updated December 2022. Budget-focused, first-hand experience.

## Quick Facts

- **Population:** 23+ million
- **Vibe:** Mix of modernity and ancient ways; "probably the coolest big city in China"
- **Authors' connection:** Visited 10 times over 14 months living/travelling in China

## 1. Walk the Bund (外滩)

Promenade along the Huangpu River with the famous Shanghai skyline across the water. On the Bund side are old buildings from the French and British colonial occupation. Best experienced at night when the skyline is lit up. A must-do.

- **Cost:** Free
- **Best time:** Evening/night

## 2. Visit Jing'an Temple (静安寺)

Buddhist temple on West Nanjing Road, surrounded by modern highrises. Stepping through the doors brings "instant peace and serenity from the craziness of the outside world."

- **Entrance:** 30 RMB
- **Location:** West Nanjing Road

## 3. Wander Around the Old Town (老城厢)

Area with crumbling buildings, traditional Chinese architecture, and locals living their day-to-day lives. The area around Renmin & Zhonghua streets, a few blocks from the Bund, is ideal for wandering, chatting with locals, and photography. Authors' personal favourite activity in Shanghai.

- **Cost:** Free
- **Where:** Renmin & Zhonghua streets area, near the Bund

## 4. Go Up the Oriental Pearl Tower (东方明珠塔)

468 meters high. Best visited at night when the views back at the old Bund buildings are spectacular.

- **Height:** 468 m
- **Entrance:** 100 RMB
- **Best time:** Night

## 5. Laocheng Old Town / Yuyuan Garden Area (豫园)

Touristy but worthwhile. Unique artwork, sculptures, and woodwork for sale. Wander around the pond and visit Yuyuan Garden (Garden of Contentment) to escape the heat.

- **Yuyuan Garden entrance:** 40 RMB
- **Shopping:** Chinese artwork, sculptures, woodwork

## 6. Eat Xiaolongbao Dumplings (小笼包)

Shanghai is famous for xiaolongbao — dumplings with meat and soupy broth inside. Available steamed or fried on the bottom with sesame seeds.

- **Where to go:** Yang's Dumplings, #97 Huanghe Road (People's Square area), and the shop across the street at #99
- **Styles:** Steamed or pan-fried with sesame seeds

## 7. Party in the French Concession (法租界)

More upscale part of town with tree-lined streets, designer shops (Gucci, Prada), and excellent restaurants and bars. Expensive but a great time.

- **Vibe:** Upscale, tree-lined streets
- **Note:** Budget-unfriendly area

## 8. Visit the Pedestrian Walking Street (南京路步行街)

Fairly quiet during the day but comes alive at night. Best around 8:00 PM when street performers, dancers, and neon-lit buildings create the atmosphere. Walk this road all the way down to the Bund to end the night.

- **Best time:** Around 8:00 PM
- **Tip:** Follow the street all the way to the Bund for the full experience

## 9. Take a Boat Ride on the Huangpu River (黄浦江)

Hop on one of the many ferries crossing the Huangpu River daily. More interesting than taking the metro to cross from one side to the other.

## 10. Dance on the Street

At night, large groups of people gather across the city to learn choreography together. Typically one woman leads, teaching steps while Chinese music blasts from large speakers. A fun, free cultural experience.

- **When:** After sunset
- **Cost:** Free

## Practical Tips

- **Getting around:** Shanghai has an extensive metro system; the boat across the Huangpu is a fun alternative to the metro
- **Budget food:** Dumplings from street vendors are cheap; restaurant meals in non-French Concession areas are affordable
- **Key prices:** Jing'an Temple 30 RMB, Oriental Pearl Tower 100 RMB, Yuyuan Garden 40 RMB
- **Night plan:** Start at the Pedestrian Walking Street around 8 PM, walk down to the Bund, enjoy the lit-up skyline
- **Authors' advice:** "Get a map and set off to explore this massive metropolis. Finding a piece of Shanghai for yourself and getting lost in the back streets is a great way to spend the day."
