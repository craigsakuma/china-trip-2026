# Shanghai: Essential Tips for First-Time Visitors

**Source:** Wild Great Wall (wildgreatwall.com), China travel guide. Published 2025.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Population:** 24+ million residents — China's largest city
- **Language:** Mandarin Chinese; English signage in popular tourist areas
- **Currency:** Chinese Yuan (CNY/RMB)
- **Districts:** 16 total; Pudong (financial hub) and Huangpu (home to the Bund) are most visited
- **Best time to visit:** Spring (late March–April, avoid Qingming Festival) or autumn (mid-October–November, after National Day). September also good.
- **Payments:** 99% cashless — WeChat Pay and Alipay essential
- **Safety:** Very safe city; standard precautions for personal belongings on public transport

## Top Attractions

### Historical & Cultural Sites

**Yu Garden 豫园**
Classical Chinese garden with pavilions, ponds, and stone bridges dating to the Ming Dynasty. A must-visit for traditional Chinese garden aesthetics.

**Jade Buddha Temple 玉佛寺**
Working Buddhist monastery in Putuo District housing two jade Buddha statues. Serene atmosphere — a genuine glimpse into living Buddhist culture.

**Shanghai Museum 上海博物馆**
Ancient artifacts, jade carvings, calligraphy. Free admission. One of China's best museums.

**Xujiahui Cathedral 徐家汇天主教堂**
Gothic-style church with stained glass windows — a quiet escape in the busy Xujiahui area.

**Longhua Temple & Pagoda 龙华寺**
Shanghai's oldest temple complex with seven-story pagoda.

### Modern Landmarks

**The Bund 外滩**
Waterfront promenade with "colonial-era buildings on one side and futuristic skyscrapers on the other." Best at sunrise and sunset. Lights turn off at 11pm.

**Shanghai Tower 上海中心大厦**
China's tallest building — panoramic observation deck views.

**Shanghai World Financial Center 上海环球金融中心**
Glass-floor sky deck for the vertigo-inclined.

**Oriental Pearl Tower 东方明珠塔**
Iconic retro-futuristic tower with multiple observation decks.

### Neighborhoods & Districts

**French Concession 法租界**
Tree-lined streets, colonial architecture, trendy cafes, boutiques. Key streets: Wukang Road, Anfu Road, Wulumuqi Road.

**Xintiandi 新天地**
Trendy district with boutiques, galleries, upscale dining — also the site of the First CCP Congress.

**Tianzifang 田子坊**
Maze of narrow lanes with art studios, creative shops, and cafes.

**Nanjing Road 南京路**
Premier pedestrian shopping street.

**Sinan Mansions 思南公馆**
Garden villas where Zhou Enlai, Mei Lanfang, and other notable figures once lived.

## Recommended Walking Routes

### Wukang Road & Surroundings
Explore Huaihai Middle Road → Fuxing West Road → Anfu Road. Tree-lined streets with historic architecture and cafes.

### Suzhou River to The Bund
1.2km walk featuring Shanghai Postal Museum → Waibaidu Bridge → Roosevelt House → The Bund promenade.

### Xujiahui Circuit
Catholic church → local food options → shopping district.

**Pan-fried soup dumplings 生煎包** — "Crispy on the bottom, tender on top, with a juicy filling inside"
**Crab roe noodles** — Seasonal autumn specialty

## Transportation

### Metro (Best Option)
- **One-day pass:** 18 RMB (unlimited 24-hour rides)
- **Three-day pass:** 45 RMB (unlimited 72-hour rides)
- **Single ride:** Starting from 3 RMB
- **Apps:** Metro Dapp, SuiShenGo app, or ticket machines at stations

### Buses
- Flat 2 RMB fare
- **Route 20** passes the Bund, Nanjing Road, and temples
- Day passes do NOT work on buses

### Taxis
- Starting fare: 14 RMB (first 3km), then 2.7 RMB/km

### Huangpu River Ferries
- **Fuxing East → Dongchang Road:** 7am–10pm (7pm ideal for night views)
- **Jinling East → Dongchang Road:** 7am–6:30pm (may close holidays)
- "Ferries get crowded at dusk"

### Shared Bikes
- ~1 RMB per 30 minutes

### Maglev Train
- Airport to city in 8 minutes; top speed 431 km/h; 50 RMB

## Shopping

### Nanjing Road Pedestrian Street 南京路步行街
Major brands and local boutiques — Shanghai's premier shopping strip.

### Bailian Outlet Plaza (Qingpu)
- Metro Line 17 to Jiasong Middle Road (Exit 2), 1km walk or taxi
- Free parking
- Brands: Nike, Adidas, MaxMara, Coach, Gucci
- Extra discounts available

### Qipu Road Wholesale Market 七浦路
- Metro Line 10/12 to Tiantong Road (Exit 6)
- Hours: 8am–6pm daily
- Bargaining encouraged — wholesale prices on clothing

**Day 1:** City God Temple → Yuyuan Garden → Nanjing Road → The Bund (evening lights)
**Day 2:** Qibao Ancient Street → Xujiahui (cathedral + local food) → Jing'an Temple
**Day 3:** Xintiandi → First CCP Congress site → Tianzifang → French Concession walk
**Day 4 (optional):** Shanghai Disneyland or Happy Valley
**Day 5 (optional):** Day trip to Zhujiajiao Water Town or other ancient town

## Practical Tips

- **Bund lights turn off at 11pm** — plan your evening walk accordingly
- **North Bund** offers Bund photos without the heavy crowds of the main promenade
- **East Nanjing Road and Jing'an Joy City** have anime and gaming shops
- **Plane-tree-lined streets** (Wukang, Wuyuan, Yanqing, Anfu) each have unique vibes — best for slow morning walks
- **Chopstick etiquette:** Never stick chopsticks upright in a bowl — it symbolizes bad luck
- **Simple phrases:** "Ni hao" (hello) and "xie xie" (thank you) go a long way
- **Author's observation:** "Even the smallest roadside shops are beautifully decorated, with custom designs instead of store-bought items" — Shanghai's attention to aesthetic detail is unmatched in China
