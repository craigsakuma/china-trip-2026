# Zhujiajiao Water Town: A Detail-Packed Day Trip Guide from Shanghai

**Source:** Nick Kembel (nickkembel.com), seasoned travel writer based in Taipei/Edmonton, visited 2025.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Chinese name:** Zhujiajiao Shuixiang (朱家角水乡) or Zhujiajiao Guzhen (朱家角古镇, the local term)
- **Meaning:** "Zhu Family Corner" — named for the original settler family
- **Location:** Qingpu district, outskirts of Shanghai — feels "worlds apart" from the city
- **River:** Dianpu River (淀浦河), nicknamed "Pearl Stream" (珠溪), flows from Dianpu Lake (Shanghai's largest lake)
- **Historical economy:** Rice, silk, and textiles shipped via waterways
- **Time needed:** 3-6 hours (morning departure, back to Shanghai by mid-afternoon)
- **Hours:** 8:30am-4:30pm (some shops stay open later)
- **Entry to ancient town:** Free — individual attractions charge separately, or buy a pass
- **Best day:** Weekday. Arrive early to beat crowds. Go right at opening (8:30am) for 2 hours of relative peace before midday rush.

---

## Getting There from Central Shanghai

### By Metro (Recommended)
1. Take **Line 2** to **Hongqiao Railway Station**
2. Transfer to **Line 17** (reddish-brown line)
3. Ride to **Zhujiajiao Station** (end of line)
4. **Total travel time:** About 1 hour 15 minutes from People's Square

### Payment
Use Alipay app, set to Shanghai, click "transport", swipe metro QR code at entry/exit.

### Walking from MRT to Entrance (About 15 minutes)
- Exit 1 from Zhujiajiao Station, face the waterway
- Walk up **Zhuxi Street (珠溪路)** for a few minutes
- Turn right on **Xiangningbang Road**
- Turn left onto **Caoping Road (漕平路)**
- Cross a canal, then a larger arched pedestrian bridge
- Continue to **Zhujiajiao Visitor's Center** and main entrance (northwestern corner of the water town)

**Maps note:** Google Maps doesn't work well in China. Use Apple Maps (works once you're physically in China) or AMap.

## Pass Options

| Pass | Price | Includes |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard Pass** | About 60 RMB | Kezhi Garden, Garden of Inseparable Hearts, Shanghai Helong Art Gallery |
| **Complete Pass** | About 80 RMB | Everything above + Yuanjin Buddhist Temple + City God Temple |
| **Individual tickets** | Varies | Pay per attraction |
| **Free entry** | 0 RMB | The ancient town itself, canals, bridges, and streets |

**Author's recommendation:** Get the Complete Pass — only 20 RMB (about $3 USD) more than standard, and Yuanjin Temple's second-floor view alone is worth it.

**Secret free entry:** Enter from the south side via Zhuxi Street to Caohe Street and you can skip the visitor center entirely. Pay individual fees at attractions you want to enter.

## Attractions — Recommended Walking Route (North to South)

### Quanhua Shuicai Art Gallery (全华水彩艺术馆)
Watercolor paintings inside an ancient home. 5-minute walkthrough. Steep separate fee. **Author's verdict:** "Nice but not worth the extra cost."

### Kezhi Garden (课植园) — Highlight
Nearly 2-hectare garden dating to 1912. Residential mansion area with rooms and objects on display, then expansive garden with ponds, pavilions, arched bridges, a beautiful library, bamboo, maples, and cherry blossoms. **"Definitely worth visiting."** Almost empty early in the morning.

### Garden of Inseparable Hearts (和心园)
Named because officials living here were said to have "hearts joined with the people they ruled." Smaller than Kezhi but "equally worth visiting." Ancient stone tablets, stunning fabric screens, Qing dynasty two-story pavilion.

### Canal and Yongan Bridge (永安桥) — Free
Narrow canal where boats wait for tourists. Boat rides cost 200-300 RMB (up to 6 people per boat). Nice spot to watch boats passing under the bridge.

### Apo Teahouse (阿婆茶楼) — Free to Enter, Tea Fee
"Grandma" teahouse — named for the custom of elderly women coming here for needlework and tea. Second floor has a lovely tea room overlooking Dianpu River and Yuanjin Temple.

### Fangsheng Bridge (放生桥) — Free, Most Famous Bridge
- **Built:** 1571 by a monk who raised funds
- **Name means:** "Releasing Life" — the temple regularly released saved fish and tortoises
- **Stats:** 71 meters long, five arches — largest of its kind in Shanghai
- **Note:** Gets extremely crowded. Guards prevent blocking and sitting on sides (no railings). The author personally preferred some of the smaller bridges.

### Great North Street (北大街) — Free, Main Shopping Street
The busiest street in town, following the river and canal with several smaller bridges. Lined with traditional and tourist-oriented shops on both sides.

### Jiangnan First Teahouse (江南第一茶楼) — Author's Highlight
Historical teahouse facing Dianpu River. Long, skinny Shanghai longtang-style brick building.
- **Tea set:** 138 RMB (about $20 USD) — comes with enormous snack spread: fresh and dried fruits, tea-flavored cakes, chestnuts, sunflower seeds, traditional candies
- **Best seat:** Back of the second floor with gorgeous river view
- **"Sitting in this spot was my most memorable experience in Zhujiajiao"**

### Shanghai Helong Art Gallery (上海市鹤龙美术馆) — On Both Passes
Small — two rooms, in and out in 5 minutes. "Nothing special" but included in pass.

### Handalong Sauce Shop (函大隆酱园) — Established 1886
Generations-old shop selling homemade soy sauce, vinegar, fermented bean curd, and pickled vegetables. "Legit and non-touristy" — Chinese locals still flock here for condiments.

### Four Cool Bridges — Free
Four ancient bridges crossing the canal between Great North Street and Caohe Street (漕河街):
1. **City God Temple Bridge**
2. **Lang Bridge (Corridor Bridge)** — Only wooden bridge in town; covered bridge rebuilt in 1996; the author's favorite
3. Two additional stone bridges
4. **Qijia Bridge (戚家桥)**

### Yuanjin Buddhist Temple (圆津禅院) — Complete Pass Only, Best View
"Worth getting the complete pass specifically for this." Monks chanting at the back. Second floor of the main hall offers **one of the best views in town** — looking down on the river and canals.

### City God Temple (城隍庙) — Complete Pass Only
Standard city deity temple. "Didn't really stand out" but on the pass and on the way out.

### Gingko Tree Square (银杏树广场) — Autumn Detour
From City God Temple Bridge, follow Meizhou Road east. Large gingko trees with beautiful yellow leaves in autumn.

### Post Office of Qing Dynasty (大清邮局) — Free
Dating to 1903, one of only 13 major post offices in the Shanghai region at that time. "One of the best-known buildings in town" but not much to see beyond the exterior and small shop inside.

## Local Food to Try

- **Stewed pork wrapped in leaf** — Fatty meat, super soft and flavorful
- **Zongzi (粽子, steamed rice dumplings)** — Egg yolk and five-flowers meat (五花肉) version — "wow was this ever good!"
- **Flower and tea cigarettes** — Novelty items in herbal tea shops (no nicotine)

## Practical Tips

- **Arrive at opening (8:30am)** — Gardens are practically empty for the first 2 hours; midday crowds are heavy
- **Visit gardens first** (Kezhi and Garden of Inseparable Hearts) before heading to the main shopping streets
- **Budget 3-6 hours** — enough for gardens, bridges, a tea stop, and browsing shops
- **Bring cash** for small vendors, though many accept mobile payments
- **The walk back:** Follow Caohe Street to Xihu Street east out of the tourist area. This passes through a "more authentic" part of the water town with locals hanging laundry by the canal. Connects back to Zhuxi Street for a straight shot to the MRT.
- **Combine with other Shanghai sights:** Early start means you can be back in central Shanghai by mid-afternoon for evening activities (author did Bund walk the same evening)
- **Repetition warning:** If visiting multiple water towns on the same China trip, they start to "feel repetitive" — Zhujiajiao may be the best single choice near Shanghai
