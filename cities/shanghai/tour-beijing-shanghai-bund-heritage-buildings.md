# Historic Buildings on the Bund: The 22 Heritage Buildings of Shanghai

**Source:** Tour-Beijing.com, China travel guide site. Published 2023.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** No. 01 East Zhongshan Road (中山东一路), a 1km strip along the Huangpu River
- **Boundaries:** Eastern tip of Yan'an Road (south) to Waibaidu Bridge (north)
- **Total buildings:** 22 well-preserved and renovated heritage structures
- **Time to explore:** About 1 hour to examine facades and visit open interiors
- **Golden era:** 1920s–1930s, before wartime disruption
- **Architectural styles:** Neoclassic, Renaissance, eclecticism, baroque, modernism, Art Deco
- **Etymology:** "Bund" is an Anglo-Indian word for embankment of a muddy waterfront, brought by the British from India
- **History:** Originally a towpath for rice barges; British Concession established 1845 (north), French Concession 1849 (south)

---

## The 22 Heritage Buildings (South to North)

### 1. Asia Building 亚细亚大楼 (The Bund No. 1)
- **Built:** 1916 | **Floors:** 7 (later +1)
- **Style:** Mixed neoclassic and eclecticism with baroque touches
- **Character:** "Simplicity, elegance and magnificence" — key state-protected cultural relic
- **Current use:** China Pacific Insurance Company

### 2. Shanghai Club 上海总会 (The Bund No. 2)
- **Built:** 1910 | **Floors:** 6
- **Architect:** H. Tarrant (British)
- **Style:** Renaissance exterior with baroque decorative elements; ionic columns between 3rd–4th floors; two baroque pavilions on top
- **Famous feature:** "Longest bar in the world" — 34-meter mahogany bar, original social club for British expats (est. 1861)
- **Current use:** Waldorf Astoria Shanghai on the Bund (Hilton chain)

### 3. Union Building 有利大楼 (The Bund No. 3)
- **Built:** 1916 | **Floors:** 6
- **Style:** Renaissance with baroque exterior
- **Significance:** First steel-frame building in Shanghai (steel from Germany's ThyssenKrupp)
- **History:** Taken by Chartered Mercantile Bank in 1937; transformed by architect Michael Graves in 2004
- **Current use:** The Bund No. 03 — food, art, culture, music, fashion destination

### 4. Nissin Building 日清大楼 (The Bund No. 5)
- **Built:** 1925 | **Floors:** 6
- **Style:** Japanese elements with modern concrete design
- **History:** Built by Nissin Bank for Nisshin Kisen Kaisha Steamship Company
- **Current use:** World designer restaurant

### 5. China Merchants Bank Building 中国通商银行大楼 (The Bund No. 6)
- **Originally built:** 1886 (by Russell & Co.) | **Renovated:** 1907
- **Style:** Victorian Gothic — fan-shaped dormer windows, strong European religious colors
- **Significance:** China's first native bank (China Merchants Bank) established here in 1897
- **Current use:** Designer restaurants, bars, flagship luxury brand stores

### 6. Telegram Building 大北电报公司大楼 (The Bund No. 7)
- **Built:** 1907 | **Architects:** Atkinson and Dallas
- **Style:** Late French Renaissance with two baroque domes and rococo touches
- **History:** Office for the Great Northern Telegraphy Corporation
- **Current use:** Royal Thai Consulate General (3rd floor); Bangkok Bank China (盘谷银行)

### 7. Russell & Co. Building 轮船招商局大楼 (The Bund No. 9)
- **Originally built:** 1846 | **Reconstructed:** early 1900s | **Floors:** 3
- **Style:** Neoclassicism with interior balconies and classical columns
- **History:** Originally Russell & Co.; sold to China Merchants Steamship Company in 1877
- **Current use:** SHIATZYCHEN Shanghai flagship store (夏姿上海旗舰店)

### 8. HSBC Building 汇丰银行大楼 (The Bund No. 12) — Most Eye-Catching
- **Completed:** 1923 | **Floors:** 7 + semi-sphere roof
- **Architects:** Palmer and Turner
- **Style:** Western classical — ionic columns, sunk-panel ceiling, marble floor
- **Distinction:** "Most outstanding piece of Western style classical architecture in China modern history"; once called "the most exquisite architecture from Suez Canal to Far-East Bering Strait"
- **Structure:** Steel framework, brick and wood exterior
- **Current use:** Pudong Development Bank

### 9. Customs House 上海海关大楼 (The Bund No. 13)
- **Built:** 1927 | **Floors:** 8 (east part)
- **Architects:** Palmer and Turner
- **Style:** Neoclassical with Doric columns
- **Famous feature:** Huge clock tower with four clocks (largest in Asia at the time) — clocks built in London
- **Current use:** Still serves customs function

### 10. Bank of Communications 交通银行大楼 (The Bund No. 14)
- **Built:** 1948 (last building ever built on the Bund) | **Floors:** 6
- **Architect:** C.H. Gonda
- **Style:** Art Deco pragmatism — combining modern design with traditional Chinese symmetry
- **History:** China Bank of Communications (founded 1908) took over Deutsch-Asiatische Bank holdings after WWI
- **Current use:** Shanghai Federation of Trade Unions

### 11. Russo-Asiatic Bank Building 华俄道胜银行大楼 (The Bund No. 15)
- **Built:** Early 1900s | **Floors:** 3
- **Architect:** Heinrich Beck (German)
- **Style:** Italian Renaissance — ionic columns, granite base, white marble staircase
- **Distinction:** First building on the Bund to install an elevator
- **Current use:** Chinese Foreign Exchange and Trade Center; Shanghai Space Aeronautics Bureau

### 12. Taiwan Bank Building 台湾银行大楼 (The Bund No. 16)
- **Floors:** Small (904 sq meters)
- **Style:** Japanese with classical European column touches
- **Current use:** China Merchants Bank

### 13. North China Daily News Building 上海字林西报大楼 (The Bund No. 17)
- **Architects:** Lester, Johnson & Morriss
- **Style:** Modernism and neoclassicism — baroque towers, Renaissance relief sculpture, classic pillars
- **Distinction:** Highest concession-era building on the Bund
- **History:** Home of the "North China Daily News," Shanghai's first English newspaper (ceased 1951)
- **Current use:** AIA Shanghai branch (since 1996 renovation)

### 14. Chartered Bank Building 麦加利银行 (The Bund No. 18)
- **Style:** Eclectic Renaissance
- **History:** Chartered Bank founded by James Wilson, established in England, approved by Queen Victoria 1853, Shanghai branch opened 1858
- **Current use:** Cartier flagship store (bank moved to Pudong)

### 15. Former Palace Hotel 和平饭店南楼 (The Bund No. 18b)
- **Floors:** 6 | **Style:** Renaissance, brick and wood
- **History:** Hosted Sun Yat-sen in December 1911 (as provisional president)
- **Current use:** South Wing of Peace Hotel (now connected to Sassoon House across Nanjing Road)

### 16. Sassoon House 沙逊大厦 / Peace Hotel North 和平饭店北楼 (The Bund No. 19)
- **Floors:** 10 | **Architects:** Palmer and Turner
- **Style:** First modernism-style building on the Bund — occupies important place in Shanghai's architectural history
- **Built for:** Victor Sassoon
- **Famous feature:** Green pyramid roof — iconic Shanghai landmark
- **Current use:** Fairmont Peace Hotel (north building)

### 17. Bank of China Building 中国银行大楼 (The Bund No. 23)
- **Floors:** 17 (east) + 4 (west)
- **Distinction:** Only Bund building designed and built entirely by Chinese architects
- **Style:** Harmonious mix of Western architecture and Chinese traditional architecture
- **Current use:** Bank of China Shanghai branch

### 18. Yokohama Specie Bank 横滨正金银行大楼 (The Bund No. 24)
- **Floors:** 7 | **Style:** Japanese with ionic columns above doorway
- **Current use:** Industrial and Commercial Bank of China

### 19. Yangtze Insurance Building 扬子水火保险公司大楼 (The Bund No. 26)
- **Architects:** Palmer and Turner | **Style:** Neoclassical
- **History:** Yangtze Water and Fire Insurance Company established 1863
- **Current use:** Agricultural Bank of China, Bund Branch

### 20. Jardine Matheson Building 怡和洋行大楼 (The Bund No. 27)
- **Originally built:** Early colonial era | **Floors:** 5 (+2 added 1983)
- **Style:** British neoclassical — Corinthian columns through 3rd to 5th level
- **Current use:** Shanghai Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation Department

### 21. Glen Line Steamship Building 格林邮船大楼 (The Bund No. 28)
- **Built:** 1922 | **Floors:** 7 | **Architects:** Palmer and Turner
- **Style:** Renaissance with reinforced concrete, granite facade on lower two stories
- **Current use:** Shanghai Cleaning House

### 22. Banque de l'Indochine Building 东方汇理银行大楼 (The Bund No. 29)
- **Built:** 1914
- **Style:** French Renaissance with tall baroque arches as centerpiece
- **Current use:** China Everbright Bank, Shanghai Branch

### Bonus: British Consulate Building 英国领事馆 (Waitanyuan No. 33)
- **Style:** British Renaissance
- **Location:** Confluence of Huangpu River and Suzhou Creek — 23,000 sq meters
- **Significance:** Starting point of Shanghai concession development
- **History:** Served as Consulate-General and British Supreme Court for China until Japanese occupation (December 8, 1941)

## Practical Tips

- **Best time to visit:** Late afternoon for golden light on facades, or evening when the Bund lights illuminate (lights turn off at 11pm)
- **Walking direction:** South to north gives chronological building progression
- **Photo tip:** Cross to the Pudong side for the classic panoramic shot of all 22 buildings together
- **Key architects:** Palmer and Turner designed multiple buildings (HSBC, Customs House, Sassoon House, Glen Line, Yangtze Insurance)
- **Interior access:** Some buildings have public lobbies (HSBC/Pudong Development Bank has stunning marble interior); the Waldorf Astoria, Fairmont Peace Hotel, and restaurants at Bund 3, 5, 6, and 18 are also accessible
- **Nearest metro:** Nanjing Dong Lu Station (Lines 2, 10) or East Nanjing Road
