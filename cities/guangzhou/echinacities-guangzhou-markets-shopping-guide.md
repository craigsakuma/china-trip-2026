# Old School Shopping: Guide to Guangzhou's Markets (广州市场)

**Source:** eChinacities — echinacities.com/guangzhou/city-guide/Old-School-ShoppingAn-Introduction-to-Guangzhous-Markets

## Overview — China's Market Capital

Guangzhou hosts over 1,000 wholesale markets covering 40+ types of commodities. The city is China's southern fashion and textile capital, with dozens of specialized wholesale markets clustered in distinct commercial districts. From traditional Chinese medicine to jade, tea to fashion, Guangzhou's markets offer a raw, authentic shopping experience far removed from malls.

## Featured Markets

### Qingping Market (清平市场) — Dried Goods & Chinese Medicine
The most famous of Guangzhou's old markets, located just north of Shamian Island. Stretching along Qingping Street, it is the biggest trade port and distribution center of tonic herbal medicine in southern China, and the only state-approved Chinese herbal medicine trading place in Guangzhou.

**What you'll find:** Dried centipedes, bushels of dried seahorses, dried lizards, premium herbs like cordyceps sinensis, cubilose (bird's nest), and ganoderma lucidum. Also common soup ingredients like poria cocos and polygonatum. Shopkeepers offer slicing and grinding services. The market covers 11,200 square meters with over 1,200 booths selling 600+ varieties. The street connects to a traditional Chinese medicine market near the Affiliated Guangzhou Hospital of Traditional Chinese Medicine. Also features pet shops with fish, turtles, and accessories.

**Metro:** Line 1 to Huangsha Station, Exit D.

### Hualin Lu Jade Market (华林路玉器市场) — Jade, Antiques & Jewelry
Historic shopping area with twisting lanes of stalls selling jade and jadeite, alongside modern air-conditioned shops. Vendors offer custom jewelry creation. Back alleys contain antique shops. The Hualin Buddhist Temple provides a peaceful retreat within the market area.

**Metro:** Line 1 to Changshou Lu Station.

### Fangcun Tea Market (芳村茶叶城) — Tea Capital
An enormous grouping of shops, malls, and stalls selling teas from across China, plus teapots, tea sets, and accessories. This is one of the largest tea wholesale markets in the country. You can sample before buying and learn about different varieties (pu'er, tieguanyin, longjing, etc.).

**Metro:** Line 1 to Fangcun Station, Exit C.

### Wende Lu Culture Street (文德路文化街) — Art Supplies & Calligraphy
Art-focused shopping street with Chinese calligraphy brushes, rice paper, ink stones, painting scrolls, and framed artworks. Centered around No. 71 Wende Lu. Good for traditional Chinese art supplies and souvenirs.

**Metro:** Line 1 or 2 to Gongyuanqian Station.

### Haizhu Square Area (海珠广场) — Accessories, Shoes & Instruments
Three distinct markets clustered near Haizhu Square:
- **Haizhu Accessories Market:** Silk flowers, costume jewelry, hair accessories
- **Guangzhou Metropolis Shoes City:** Massive shoe wholesale center
- **Daxin Lu Music Instruments Street:** Traditional and modern instruments

**Metro:** Line 1 to Haizhu Guangchang Station.

### Zhanxi Lu Fashion Street (站西路) — Fashion & Watches
Behind the Long Distance Bus Station near Guangzhou Railway Station. Features multilevel fashion malls with clothing at wholesale prices, plus watch and timepiece shops. Chaotic energy but great deals for those who bargain.

**Metro:** Line 2 to Guangzhou Railway Station, Exit F.

## Bargaining Tips and Shopping Etiquette

- **Standard hours:** Most markets operate 10:00 AM - 6:00 PM. Beijing Road stores stay open until 10:00 PM.
- **Bargaining expected:** Start at 30-50% of the asking price and negotiate from there. Walk away to test the vendor's floor price.
- **Wholesale preference:** Some vendors prefer wholesale buyers. Small quantity purchases are sometimes declined, but most will accommodate tourists.
- **Cash advantage:** While Alipay/WeChat Pay are everywhere, cash can sometimes unlock better bargaining power at traditional markets.
- **Quality check:** For jade and tea, knowledge matters. Consider bringing a knowledgeable friend or guide to authenticate quality.
- **Counterfeit goods:** Branded goods at wholesale markets are almost certainly counterfeit. Buy accordingly.

## Beyond Markets — Modern Shopping

### Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street (上下九步行街)
Traditional shopping street in Liwan District with Qilou (骑楼) arcade architecture. Mix of local brands, food stalls, and souvenir shops. Best for street food and architectural photography.

### Beijing Road (北京路)
Guangzhou's premier retail strip with department stores, international brands, and a glass-covered archaeological site revealing layers of ancient road surfaces dating back 2,000 years.

### Tee Mall / Tianhe Shopping Districts
Modern mall complexes in Tianhe District. Grandview Mall, Tee Mall, and IGC offer international luxury brands and dining. The underground Shishang Tianhe (时尚天河) connects multiple buildings via subway.
