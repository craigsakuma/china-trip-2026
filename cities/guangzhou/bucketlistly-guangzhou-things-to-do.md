# Explore Guangzhou: 12 Incredible Things to Do & More

**Source:** Pete R. (bucketlistly.blog), solo travel and photography blogger. Published 2025. First-hand experience.

Guangzhou (广州) is the fourth largest city in China with a population of over 17 million. One of China's four first-tier cities alongside Beijing, Shanghai, and Shenzhen. With a history of over 2,200 years, it was a major terminus of the Silk Road and the birthplace of Cantonese cuisine and culture.

## Quick Facts

- **Population:** 17+ million
- **Also known as:** Canton (historic English name)
- **History:** 2,200+ years; major Silk Road terminus
- **Best for:** Cantonese food, colonial architecture, urban parks, mountain hikes
- **Getting around:** Metro (starts at 2 CNY, +1 CNY per 4–8 km); Didi is cheap and reliable
- **Payment:** Alipay essential — use "Transport" button for metro/bus QR codes
- **Language:** Cantonese is the local language, but Mandarin is widely understood

## Flower City Square & Canton Tower (花城广场 & 广州塔)

Flower City Square is a vibrant public plaza offering breathtaking views of the Canton Tower. Access via **Huacheng Avenue Metro Station**.

**Canton Tower (广州塔)** stands 600 meters high — the tallest sightseeing tower in China and third tallest in the world. Its unique slender twisting design and light shows dazzle the cityscape at night. Worth viewing from the square even if you don't go up.

- **Views:** Stunning both daytime and at night
- **Tip:** The square itself is free and offers excellent photo angles of the tower

## Chen Clan Ancestral Hall (陈家祠)

Built in 1894 during the Qing Dynasty as an academic and ancestral hall for the Chen clan. Today it functions as the **Guangzhou Folk Art Museum** (广州民间工艺博物馆). Features incredibly colorful carvings, intricate decorative work on the roof ridges, and well-preserved Lingnan architecture.

- **Ticket:** ~8 CNY per person
- **Recommended for:** Anyone interested in history, traditional architecture, and folk art
- **Metro:** Chen Clan Academy station (Line 1)

## Shamian Island (沙面岛)

A charming historic enclave along the Pearl River (珠江). Once a foreign concession during the 19th and early 20th centuries, which explains its distinctive **European-style architecture**, tree-lined avenues, and colonial-era mansions.

The streets are quiet, shaded by banyan and camphor trees, with soft light from roadside cafes and lamps around small gardens. A peaceful escape from the bustling city.

- **Cost:** Free, open 24/7
- **Best time:** Evening stroll — atmospheric lighting and cooler temperatures
- **Getting there:** Short walk from Huangsha Metro Station (Line 1/6)

## Yuexiu Park (越秀公园)

The largest park in downtown Guangzhou, covering over 860,000 square meters. Filled with lush gardens, lakes, walking paths, and historic monuments. You can spend hours enjoying nature away from the crowds.

### Zhenhai Tower / Guangzhou Museum (镇海楼 / 广州博物馆)

An ancient 5-storey pagoda built in 1380 during the Ming Dynasty, located within Yuexiu Park. Now houses the **Guangzhou Museum**, chronicling the city's 2,200-year history.

- **Park:** Open 24/7, free entry
- **Museum:** Daily 9 AM – 5:30 PM, 10 CNY per person
- **Combine with:** Dr. Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall (中山纪念堂) is nearby

## Moxing Mountain (摩星岭)

At 382 meters, this is the highest point in Guangzhou city. Located north of the city center, just a 20-minute Didi ride away. Stunning panoramic views of the entire city from the summit. Surrounded by a beautiful nature park for hiking, jogging, or relaxing amid lush greenery.

### Getting There via Baiyun Mountain (白云山)

The easiest approach: Didi to **Yuntai Garden** (云台花园), then catch the cable car up to Baiyun Mountain Scenic Area. From there, a shuttle bus takes you to Moxing Mountain summit.

- **Cable car:** ~25 CNY up, ~20 CNY down
- **Shuttle bus to Moxing Mountain:** ~10 CNY per way
- **Time needed:** Half day
- **Author's opinion:** One of the best things to do in Guangzhou — the views are worth the effort

## Beijing Road Pedestrian Street (北京路步行街)

A bustling hub for shopping, street food, cafes, and entertainment. This is Guangzhou's most famous pedestrian street, with archaeological excavations of ancient road surfaces visible beneath glass panels on the street.

- **Best time:** Around sunset, when it's at its liveliest
- **Hours:** Open 24/7
- **Getting there:** Beijing Lu Metro Station (Line 1/2)
- **Food:** Street food stalls and traditional Cantonese snack shops throughout

## Sacred Heart Cathedral (石室圣心大教堂)

Stunning Gothic Revival cathedral built in the 19th century entirely from granite by French missionaries of the Paris Foreign Missions Society. One of the few all-granite churches in the world and the largest Catholic church in southern China.

- **Hours:** Daily 8 AM – 5:30 PM (can only walk around outside of mass times)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Metro:** Yide Lu station (Line 2/6)

## Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street (上下九步行街)

Over a kilometer long in the Liwan District (荔湾区), lined with historic **Lingnan-style buildings** featuring ornate facades, traditional tiled roofs, and decorative carvings. Famous for shopping, dining, and street food.

- **Food highlights:** Authentic Cantonese snacks — dim sum, roasted meats, traditional desserts
- **Architecture:** One of the best-preserved examples of Lingnan commercial architecture
- **Access:** Walkable from Shamian Island; accessible by metro or Didi

## Dr. Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall (中山纪念堂)

Dedicated to the founder of the Republic of China. Located near Yuexiu Park. A significant historical and architectural landmark.

- **Combine with:** Yuexiu Park and the Guangzhou Museum in a single morning

## Getting Around Guangzhou

### Metro
Extensive system connecting most attractions. Fares start at 2 CNY, increasing by 1 CNY per 4–8 km. Use the **Alipay "Transport" button** to generate a QR code for metro/bus entry — no need to buy physical tickets.

### Didi
More reliable and affordable than traditional taxis. See the price in advance. Hail via the Didi mini-app inside Alipay.

### Walking
Many attractions cluster in districts (Liwan for Shamian + Shangxiajiu; Yuexiu for the park + memorial hall), making walking between them practical.

## Practical Tips

- **Food priority:** Guangzhou is the birthplace of Cantonese cuisine. Dim sum (yum cha) is a must — look for busy local parlors over tourist spots
- **Best districts:** Yuexiu for culture/history; Liwan for old Guangzhou charm; Tianhe for modern/upscale
- **Weather:** Hot and humid in summer; spring and autumn are more comfortable
- **Language:** Cantonese is spoken locally but Mandarin works everywhere; English is limited
- **Evening plan:** Shamian Island stroll → Pearl River night views → Canton Tower lit up
- **Day planning:** Group nearby attractions — Chen Clan + Shangxiajiu + Shamian in one half; Yuexiu Park + Sun Yat-sen + Sacred Heart in another; Baiyun Mountain + Moxing as a half-day excursion
- **Duration:** 2–3 days covers the highlights comfortably
- **High-speed rail:** Guangzhou South Station connects to Hong Kong (48 min), Shenzhen (30 min), Guilin (2.5 hrs), and most major Chinese cities
