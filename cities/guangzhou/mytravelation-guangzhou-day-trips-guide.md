# 8 Day Trips from Guangzhou: Kaiping, Foshan, Zhaoqing & More

**Source:** MyTravelation — mytravelation.com/day-trips-from-guangzhou/

## Kaiping Diaolou (开平碉楼) — UNESCO World Heritage Fortress Towers

**Distance:** 130 km southwest | **Travel time:** ~2 hours by car/bus

The Diaolou of Kaiping are multi-story fortified towers blending Chinese and Western architectural styles, built between the late Qing Dynasty and early Republican era (1850s-1930s). Local overseas Chinese, particularly those who emigrated to North America, sent money home to build these defensive towers against bandits. They represent a unique fusion of Chinese village architecture with Western elements (Roman columns, Byzantine domes, Art Deco facades).

**UNESCO Status:** Inscribed as a World Heritage Site in 2007. One of China's top 50 must-see places.

### Key Villages to Visit

**Zili Village (自力村):** The most photogenic cluster with 15 diaolou rising above rice paddies. Well-preserved interiors showing early 20th-century life. Admission: ~$8 (50 RMB).

**Jinjiangli Village (锦江里):** Another impressive cluster with some of the tallest and most elaborate towers. Admission: ~$8 (50 RMB).

**Li Garden (立园):** A privately built garden estate blending Western and Chinese landscape design, created by a Chinese emigrant who returned from America. Features pavilions, bridges, and fortified residences. Admission: ~$10 (65 RMB).

**Tip:** Hire a local guide for historical context. The stories of overseas Chinese families, bandits, and architectural ambition make the towers far more compelling.

## Foshan (佛山) — Martial Arts and Ceramics Capital

**Distance:** 40 km | **Travel time:** 30-45 minutes by metro or car

Foshan is easily reached from Guangzhou by metro (Guangfo Line), making it one of the most convenient day trips. Known as the "Hometown of Martial Arts" (武术之乡) and birthplace of southern martial arts styles including Wing Chun (咏春拳). Notable residents include Wong Fei-hung (黄飞鸿) and Ip Man (叶问, Bruce Lee's teacher).

### Must-See Attractions

**Foshan Ancestral Temple (佛山祖庙):** Built during the Northern Song Dynasty (1078-1085). Features elaborate Lingnan architecture, ceramic ridge decorations, and a museum of traditional Cantonese opera and martial arts. Admission: ~$6 (40 RMB).

**Nanfeng Ancient Kiln (南风古灶):** A 500-year-old dragon kiln, the oldest continuously operating pottery kiln in the world. Try your hand at pottery-making workshops. Admission: ~$5 (30 RMB).

**Shiwan Ceramics Market:** Adjacent to the ancient kiln. Shop for ceramics, figurines, and decorative pottery at wholesale prices.

**Wing Chun experience:** Several schools offer drop-in classes or demonstrations. Learn the basics of this famous martial art from a local master.

**Cost:** $20-40 total for a full day including metro, admission, and lunch.

## Qingyuan (清远) — Hot Springs and Gorges

**Distance:** 70 km north | **Travel time:** ~1.5 hours by car

Natural landscapes featuring rivers, hot springs, and the Gulongxia Scenic Area with its famous glass bridge and gorge hiking trails.

**Highlights:** Glass bridge walk, gorge hiking, natural hot spring soaking.

**Tip:** Bring swimwear for hot springs. Book activities ahead on weekends. Cost: $40-70 total.

## Zhaoqing (肇庆) — Seven Star Crags and Dinghu Mountain

**Distance:** ~110 km west | **Travel time:** ~1.5 hours by car or intercity train

Known for the Seven Star Crags (七星岩), a cluster of limestone karst peaks surrounded by lakes and gardens, often compared to a miniature Guilin. Dinghu Mountain (鼎湖山) is one of China's earliest nature reserves with ancient temples, waterfalls, and subtropical forest.

**Highlights:** Lake boating among karst peaks, Dinghu Mountain hiking, Buddhist temple visits.

## Shunde (顺德) — Culinary Capital of Cantonese Cuisine

**Distance:** ~50 km south | **Travel time:** ~1 hour by metro/bus

Recognized by UNESCO as a City of Gastronomy. Shunde is considered the true birthplace of Cantonese cooking, even more than Guangzhou itself. Famous for double-skin milk pudding (双皮奶), steamed fish, and a long list of refined Cantonese dishes.

**Must-try:** Minxin Dairy for double-skin milk pudding. Daliang district for local restaurants.

## Conghua Hot Springs (从化温泉) — Natural Spa Retreat

**Distance:** ~75 km northeast | **Travel time:** ~1.5 hours by car

One of China's most famous hot spring areas. Natural mineral-rich waters with various temperature pools. Many resort hotels offer day-use access.

## Pearl River Evening Cruise (珠江夜游)

Not technically a day trip but worth noting. Evening cruises from Tianzi Pier showcase Guangzhou's illuminated skyline including Canton Tower, Haixinsha Island, and the Liede Bridge. Cruises run 7:00-10:00 PM nightly.

## Practical Tips

- **Foshan** is the easiest day trip thanks to direct metro connection (Guangfo Line)
- **Kaiping** requires more planning but is the most culturally rewarding. A private driver or organized tour is recommended since the diaolou villages are spread across the countryside.
- **Shunde** is essential for serious food lovers, but the best restaurants are scattered and require local knowledge or a guide
- All day trips are possible as round-trips from Guangzhou, returning in time for dinner
