# Best Dim Sum in Guangzhou: A Cantonese Yum Cha Guide

**Source:** Eating Adventures — eatingadventures.com/Best-Dim-Sum-Guangzhou-blog.html

## Yum Cha Culture (饮茶) — The Heart of Cantonese Life

Yum Cha, literally "drinking tea," represents the core of Cantonese daily life. This weekend family ritual involves drinking tea while enjoying a parade of small dim sum dishes. In Guangzhou, yum cha runs from early morning until early afternoon. Most restaurants do not serve dim sum in the evenings. English menus are "highly unlikely" at authentic establishments, so observe other tables' dishes and point to indicate preferences.

## Top Dim Sum Restaurants in Guangzhou (广州)

### Bei Yuan Restaurant (北园酒家)
- **Address:** 200-202 Xiao Bei Lu, Yuexiu district
- **History:** Over 70 years old; traditional garden setting with Guangdong architecture
- **Specialties:** Red rice paper rolls, prawn dumplings
- **Atmosphere:** Classic Cantonese garden dining

### Nan Yuan Restaurant (南园酒家)
- **Address:** 142 Qian Jin Lu, Haizhu district
- **Founded:** 1958
- **Recognition:** Platinum Five Diamonds national award
- **Specialties:** Chaozhounese cuisine including "Buddha Jumps Over the Wall" (佛跳墙), suckling pig
- **Note:** Expect long queues on weekends. Arrive early.

### Guangzhou Restaurant (广州酒家)
- **Address:** 2 Wen Chang Lu, Liwan district
- **Founded:** 1935; dubbed "The No. 1 Place to Eat in Guangzhou"
- **Recognition:** Top 10 restaurants in China
- **Specialties:** Outstanding prawn dumplings, beef balls, royal banquet replicas

### Dian Dou De (点都德)
- **Main address:** 587 Long Jin Zhong Lu, Liwan district
- **Beijing Road branch:** 470 Hui Fu Dong Lu, Yuexiu district
- **Founded:** 1933
- **Offerings:** Up to 108 dim sum varieties blending traditional and contemporary
- **Specialties:** Four-color ginseng soup prawn dumplings, rice paper rolls with dough and prawns
- **Note:** Multiple branches across the city; consistently good quality

### The Jade River at White Swan Hotel (白天鹅宾馆 玉堂春暖)
- **Address:** Level 3, White Swan Hotel, No. 1 Shamian Street, Liwan
- **Recognition:** Michelin Guide 2018
- **Character:** Premier training ground for top dim sum chefs. Authentic yet creative, health-conscious approach.
- **Note:** Premium pricing but a "once in a lifetime experience" with Pearl River views

### Qiu Da 6 Zai Ji (秋大6仔记)
- **Address:** 2nd Floor, 525 Binjiang Dong Lu, Haizhu district
- **Chef:** Chef Qiu with 30 years experience, formerly cooked for Queen Elizabeth II and Deng Xiaoping
- **Specialties:** Shredded duck spring rolls with lemon sauce, lamb shao mai, grilled ox tongue, durian mousse

### No. 5 Tea House (5号茶居)
- **Address:** Ground level, Lingnan No. 5 Hotel, 277 Yanjiang Zhong Lu
- **Innovation:** Prawn dumplings served in tea cups with high-grade tea poured over them
- **Specialties:** Black truffle dim sum dishes (mushroom buns, shao mai, wonton)

### Yin Deng Restaurant (银灯食府)
- **Address:** 3/F Guangzhou Hotel, 2 Qi Yi Road (near Haizhu Square Metro)
- **Character:** Old-style Cantonese restaurant with loyal local following
- **Hours:** Opens 7 AM for yum cha
- **Specialties:** Prawn dumplings, chicken feet, osmanthus pudding
- **Note:** Very reasonable prices. Arrive early to beat queues.

### The Hui People's Restaurant (回民饭店)
- **Address:** 325 Zhong Shan Liu Lu, Yuexiu
- **Specialization:** Halal dim sum (no pork); specializes in lamb
- **Specialties:** Scallion pancakes, beef and lamb innards soup, barbecue lamb buns, ginger and beef dumplings
- **Note:** Long queues; arrive early

### Fo Shi Jie Vegetarian Restaurant (佛世界素食)
- **Address:** 2-8 Niu Nai Chang Street, Tong Fu Zhong Lu, Haizhu
- **History:** Located in former Buddhist nuns' residence
- **Specialties:** Pan-fried bean curd rolls, vegetarian roast goose, vegetarian glutinous rice

## Practical Yum Cha Tips

- **Timing:** Arrive by 7-8 AM on weekends to secure seating at popular spots. Service typically runs until 2 PM.
- **Tea selection:** You will be asked which tea you want. Common options: pu'er (普洱, earthy/fermented), chrysanthemum (菊花, light/floral), tieguanyin (铁观音, oolong), jasmine (茉莉花, fragrant).
- **Ordering:** Most restaurants use paper menus with stamps or checkboxes. Some still use dim sum carts rolled between tables.
- **Language:** Point at other tables' dishes or use phone photos. Servers understand "zhe ge" (这个, "this one").
- **Bill:** Signal for the check by saying "mai dan" (买单). Tea charge is typically 5-10 RMB per person.
- **Budget:** A full yum cha meal ranges from 50-150 RMB per person depending on venue.
