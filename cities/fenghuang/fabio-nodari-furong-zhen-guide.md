# Furong Zhen Old Town — Quick Travel Guide

**Source:** Fabio Nodari (fabionodariphoto.com), travel photographer based in China. Published Dec 3, 2024. Updated Jan 11, 2026. All first-hand experience. Became one of Fabio's favorite ancient towns in China.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Yongshun County, Xiangxi Tujia and Miao Autonomous Prefecture, Hunan Province (湖南省).
- **Name:** Furong (芙蓉) means hibiscus in Chinese — blooms abundantly in summer. Originally called Wangcun, renamed after the 1986 movie "Hibiscus Town."
- **Ethnic group:** Tujia minority — known for rich cultural heritage. Traditional houses with wooden roofs, stone walls, intricate carvings.
- **What makes it unique:** Built around a river with a beautiful waterfall (Wangcun/Furong Waterfall) right in the middle of town, cascading into the Tuojiang River.
- **Entrance fee:** ~100 RMB (whether staying inside or visiting for the day).

## Best Time to Visit

**Summer:** Waterfall at most powerful, but heavy rain, humid, hot, exhausting outdoors.
**Spring:** Pleasant weather, but waterfall usually weak (hasn't rained for months).
**Autumn:** Best balance — heat eases off, still good water flow from rainy season.
**Winter:** Cold, sometimes snows. Main issue: lack of heating in most hotels (common problem in southern China).

## Getting There

**HSR:** New high-speed train station (Furongzhn) just 10 minutes from old town — best option from outside the province.
**Bus:** Direct buses from Zhangjiajie bus station.
**Combine with:** Zhangjiajie, Fanjingshan, and Fenghuang Old Town on the same trip.

## Where to Stay

Hotels inside and outside the old town. Inside: advantage of walking around in early morning/late evening when day-trippers are gone. Outside: cheaper, short walk to gates. Staying the night is worth it — atmosphere changes completely when lights are on.

## Exploring Furong Zhen (芙蓉镇)

Built along the waterfall with wooden houses perched on cliffs and walkways hanging over the water. Town stretches on both sides of the falls, connected by bridges and narrow paths. Constant roar of the cascade creates special atmosphere.

Architecture: Most houses built in traditional Tujia style — dark wooden beams, sloping roofs, carved balconies facing the river. Walk a bit from central streets and you'll notice people still live in many houses (keeps it from feeling like a theme park).

**Highlight:** Walk behind the waterfall — a path carved into the rock passes directly behind the curtain of water. Especially nice in summer.

Usual tourist shops selling souvenirs and snacks, but the waterfall-and-architecture combination makes Furong stand out from other ancient towns.

## Fabio's Assessment

Didn't expect much but ended up enjoying it a lot. Compared to Fenghuang (overwhelmed by mass tourism), Furong still feels calmer and more authentic. Balance between local life and sightseeing still exists. One of the most rewarding stops in this part of Hunan.
