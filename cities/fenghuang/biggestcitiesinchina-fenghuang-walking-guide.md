# Fenghuang Ancient Town (凤凰古城): Complete Walking Guide

**Source:** The Biggest Cities in China — thebiggestcitiesinchina.com/fenghuang-ancient-town-complete-guide/

## Overview — China's Most Beautiful Ancient Town

Fenghuang Ancient Town (凤凰古城, Phoenix Ancient Town) sits on the banks of the Tuojiang River (沱江) in Xiangxi Tujia and Miao Autonomous Prefecture, western Hunan Province. Named after green mountains that resemble a phoenix spreading its wings, the town was established as a military post during the Ming Dynasty (14th century) to defend China's southern border. It prospered as a trading center during the Qing Dynasty (1636-1912), with communities trading timber, spices, and salt.

Today it has significant Miao (苗族) and Tujia (土家族) populations living alongside Han Chinese, creating a culturally layered old town.

## Key Landmarks for a Walking Tour

### Dongzheng Street (东正街)
The main commercial street with historic architecture, food stalls, and silver jewelry shops along stone-paved pathways. This is the busiest street and the heart of tourist activity.

### Laoying Street (老营街) — The Quieter Alternative
Fewer crowds and more authentic atmosphere. Home to the 100-year-old Yang Family Batik Shop and a 150-year-old Ancient Tea House. Recommended for those seeking a less touristy experience.

### Hong Bridge / Rainbow Bridge (虹桥)
A wooden two-floor covered bridge originally built in 1670, rebuilt in 2000. The Chaoyang Palace Tea House on the upper floor offers some of the best sunset views in town. Cross the bridge for a classic Fenghuang experience.

### Tiaoyan Bridge / Stepping Stone Bridge (跳岩桥)
Built in 1703, this unique crossing consists of two rows of independent square stone piers stretching across the Tuojiang River. One of the most photographed spots in Fenghuang. Best at sunrise when mist hangs over the water.

### Shen Congwen Former Residence (沈从文故居)
The 19th-century home of celebrated Chinese writer Shen Congwen (1902-1988), whose novel "Border Town" (边城) immortalized Fenghuang in Chinese literature. The house was built in 1866 and has become the most famous cultural site in town.

### East Gate Tower (东门城楼)
Built in 1573 during the Ming Dynasty. Features a stone archway base with a wooden upper structure. Climb for panoramic views over the river and rooftops. One of the town's most iconic silhouettes.

### Ancient Stilt Houses (吊脚楼 Diaojiaolou)
The distinctive wooden houses built on stilts over the riverbank are a hallmark of Tujia architecture. Designed to prevent flood damage and address land scarcity. Features include slanted roofs, delicate carvings, and cantilevered balconies. The oldest surviving example is the Yang Family Stilt House from the early 1800s. Best viewed from a river boat.

### Wanming Tower (万名塔)
A 69-foot-tall, 7-story hexagonal pagoda near Hong Bridge, dedicated to literary arts. Beautifully illuminated at night. Best photographed from the Tiaoyan Bridge.

### Ancient City Wall (古城墙)
The 1.2-mile (2 km) Ming Dynasty wall remains partially intact. The western section is the least reconstructed and offers a quieter, more atmospheric walk than the heavily restored eastern portions.

### Wanshou Palace (万寿宫)
Built in 1749 as a merchant guild building, now housing the Miao and Tujia Culture Museum. Contains silver objects, batik items, stone carvings, and a 5-story pagoda.

### Ancient City Museum (古城博物馆)
Located in Cheo Baozhen's 1890s mansion on Shijia Long, displaying original furniture, calligraphy, and artifacts from the town's history.

## River Experiences on the Tuojiang (沱江)

**Morning bamboo raft:** Traditional bamboo rafts offer 40-minute tours starting early in the morning. You can see local women washing clothes along the banks and hear the town waking up. The quietest and most atmospheric option.

**Evening electric boat:** Nighttime boat tours showcase the lantern-lit town reflected in the water. Magical atmosphere but more crowded.

## Practical Tips

- The town is best explored on foot due to narrow cobblestone streets
- Visit early morning or evening for the best atmosphere and fewer crowds
- Nighttime is magical when locals light lanterns along the river
- Steep slopes and uneven stones require comfortable walking shoes
- The combination ticket is worth it if you plan to visit multiple sites
- Avoid sightseeing maglev train between station and town (overpriced)
