# Fenghuang Surrounding Villages: Furong Town, Miao Villages & Day Trips

**Source:** Our Wander — ourwander.com/p/fenghuang-ancient-town-furong-town-and-morong-miao-village

## Beyond Fenghuang — The Western Hunan Circuit

Fenghuang Ancient Town sits within the Xiangxi Tujia and Miao Autonomous Prefecture (湘西土家族苗族自治州), a region rich with minority villages, waterfalls, and ancient architecture. Combining Fenghuang with nearby Furong Town and Miao villages creates a deeper experience of western Hunan's cultural landscape.

## Furong Town (芙蓉镇) — The Waterfall Ancient Town

### Overview
Formerly called Wang Cun (王村), this 2,000-year-old town was renamed Furong Town in 2007 after the 1986 film "Furong Town" (芙蓉镇) was shot here. It is the main gathering place and cultural birthplace of the Tujia people (土家族). Rated 4A scenic area.

### The Waterfall (芙蓉镇瀑布)
The region's largest cascade, spanning 60 meters high and 40 meters wide. During peak water season, sounds are audible 10 miles away. The unique feature: visitors can walk under and behind the waterfall on a stone pathway. One of the most dramatic town settings in China.

### Cultural Sites
- **Xizhou Tusi Square (溪州土司广场):** Features cultural performances of Tujia minority arts
- **Xizhou Bronze Pillar Garden (溪州铜柱园):** Houses a sacred Tujia artifact crucial for studying ancient Tujia history. The bronze pillar dates to 940 AD.

### Local Food
Tujia specialties: spicy pork, beef hotpot, osmanthus fish, and the famous rice tofu (米豆腐) — a scene from the original film featured the heroine selling rice tofu.

### Getting There
- **From Zhangjiajie:** High-speed train from Zhangjiajie West Station, approximately 25 minutes
- **From Fenghuang:** Approximately 40 minutes by high-speed train
- **Shuttle buses** available from the train station to the town
- **Admission:** 95 RMB (purchase online recommended)
- **Tip:** Skip the separate boat ride across Youshui River — views from the bank are sufficient

## Shanjiang Miao Village (山江苗寨)

### Overview
Located in a valley 20 km northwest of Fenghuang, Shanjiang is a small Miao (苗族) village offering a window into traditional Miao life. Less commercialized than some alternatives, though tourism is developing.

### What to Experience
- Traditional silver jewelry crafting — the Miao are famous for elaborate silver headdresses and ornaments
- Batik-making (蜡染) — traditional wax-resist dyeing technique using indigo
- Embroidery demonstrations — intricate cross-stitch patterns unique to Miao culture
- Miao songs and dances — performances featuring call-and-response singing
- Local rice wine tasting (米酒)
- Miao Minority Museum (苗王宫, Palace of the Miao King)

### Getting There
Accessible by local bus or taxi from Fenghuang, approximately 30-40 minutes.

## Dehang Miao Village (德夯苗寨) — Mountain Waterfalls and Miao Culture

### Overview
Located between mountains and waterfalls, Dehang is the most famous and accessible Miao village for visitors. Characterized by traditional wooden stilt houses (吊脚楼) set against dramatic karst cliff scenery.

### Highlights
- **Liusha Waterfall (流纱瀑布):** One of the tallest waterfalls in Hunan
- Traditional Miao village layout with communal gathering areas
- Hiking trails through karst valleys and along river gorges
- Cultural performances during festivals (especially during Miao New Year / 苗年)

### Miao Cultural Practices to Witness
- **Bamboo pole dancing (竹竿舞):** Interactive game where visitors can participate
- **Silver jewelry:** Miao women wear elaborate silver headdresses, necklaces, and bracelets during festivals — some weighing several kilograms
- **Batik and embroidery:** Living artistic traditions passed through generations
- **Acid soup fish (酸汤鱼):** Traditional communal meal

## Morong Miao Village (墨戎苗寨) — Honest Assessment

### Overview
Also known as Longbizui (Dragon Nose Mouth) Village. Comprises 13 Miao villages with 1,018 households and 5,568 residents. Rated 3A scenic area.

### What to Expect
Miao drum performances, local rice wine tasting, and handcrafted silverware shopping. Numerous stone steps (challenging for elderly visitors).

### Honest Assessment
The site feels "over-commercialized for tourists." Most visitors complete exploration in about an hour. Guided tours emphasize silverware sales. The source recommends **skipping this attraction** to allocate more time to Fenghuang itself. If you want authentic Miao culture, Dehang or Shanjiang are better options.

### Getting There
Less than one hour drive from Furong Town. Bus from Jishou East Bus Station (吉首汽车东站) if not on organized tour.

## Practical Tips for Village Visits

- **Passport required:** All attraction and train tickets in China require passport ID verification. Always carry your passport when traveling — do not leave it locked in your hotel room.
- **Toilet warning:** Almost all toilets in rural Hunan are squat-style. Seated toilets are rare. Bring personal sanitizer and tissue paper everywhere.
- **Respectful photography:** Ask permission before photographing Miao villagers, especially women in traditional dress. Some may request a small fee.
- **Purchase authentic crafts:** Genuine hand-made Miao silver and embroidery can be found at village workshops, not factory-produced tourist souvenirs.
- **Festivals:** If your visit coincides with Miao New Year (苗年, typically October-November) or the Dragon Boat Festival (端午节), villages come alive with authentic celebrations.
