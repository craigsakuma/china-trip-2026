# Tibetan Plateau Road Trip: Sichuan to Qinghai (Week 2)

**Source:** Suzanne Jones (thetravelbunny.com), UK-based travel blog with off-the-beaten-path itineraries. Published February 6, 2026; updated February 11, 2026. First-hand experience.

Week 2 of a two-week Tibetan Plateau road trip with Eastogo Travel. Continues from Baiyu (Sichuan) through Dege, crosses into Qinghai's Yushu Prefecture, traverses the high plateau to Maduo, and finishes in Xining. See companion article: [Week 1: Sichuan & Qinghai Itinerary](travel-bunny-sichuan-qinghai-itinerary.md).

The author emphasizes "immersion, perspective and travelling slowly" through regions "that see very few international visitors." Travel pace is regulated by altitude, terrain, and stops for wildlife spotting.

---

## Day 8: Baiyu (白玉) to Dege (德格) -- 270 km

**Route & Scenery**
- 270 km drive following a river valley
- Yaks grazing in grassland alongside the road
- Clouds obscure hilltops creating what the author calls a "mystical feel"

### Zhuqing Monastery / Dzogchen Monastery (竹庆寺)

- **Location:** Dege County, Sichuan Province, at Mount Que'er Glacier
- **Elevation:** Nearly 4,000 meters
- **Significance:** One of the most important monasteries of the Nyingma school of Tibetan Buddhism
- **Setting:** Surrounded by mountains on three sides with snow-capped peaks
- **Vegetarian lunch with monks at Baima Hall:** Self-service buffet prepared by monks; waste-reduction policy where diners face small fines for leaving food on plates. Author advocates for broader adoption of this practice -- appreciates it as promoting mindfulness
- **Author's opinion:** A spiritual and atmospheric highlight of the trip

**Overnight:** Dege area

## Day 9: Dege (德格) to Yushu (玉树) -- Crossing into Qinghai

### Dege Parkhang Sutra-Printing House (德格印经院)

- **Significance:** One of the most important Tibetan cultural and religious printing facilities in existence
- **Function:** Traditional woodblock printing of Buddhist sutras and texts
- **Historical importance:** Major repository of Tibetan Buddhist literature
- **Visit:** Morning before departing Dege

**Border Crossing:** Sichuan Province into Qinghai Province during this day's drive

**Overnight:** Yushu (Jiegu town)

---

## Day 10: Yushu (玉树) Area Exploration

### Jiegu Monastery (结古寺)

- Located above Jiegu town (the main town of Yushu Prefecture)
- Part of the regional monastery circuit
- Views over the town and surrounding valley

### Jiana Mani Stone City (嘉那嘛呢石经城)

- **What it is:** Massive sacred site with accumulated layers of carved mani stones bearing Buddhist inscriptions, prayers, and wishes
- **Scale:** Terraces of stones with prayer wheels; one of the largest mani stone collections in the world
- **Cultural practice:** Pilgrims and visitors carve personal wishes into stones and add them to the growing collection
- **Significance:** Major pilgrimage destination for Tibetan Buddhist practitioners
- **Author's description:** "Layers of faith" visible in the accumulated stones
- **Atmosphere:** Deeply spiritual; the accumulated devotion of generations is tangible

### Xianggu Temple

- Notable for association with Tibetan bears in the area
- Visited as part of Yushu day exploration

**Local Food**
- Silken tofu noted as a local specialty in Yushu

**Overnight:** Yushu

## Day 11: Yushu (玉树) to Maduo (玛多) -- Wildlife Safari Day

**Route & Terrain**
- Extended drive across Qinghai Tibetan Plateau grasslands
- High-altitude open steppe landscape
- Very remote -- minimal settlements

**Wildlife Safari**
- Wildlife spotting opportunities throughout the drive
- Native plateau species across the remote landscape
- The author describes slowing down to observe animals in their natural habitat
- Travel pace dictated by wildlife encounters and altitude

**Overnight:** Maduo

---

## Day 12: Maduo (玛多) to Xining (西宁)

Long drive from the remote plateau down to Qinghai's provincial capital.

### Ta'er Monastery / Kumbum Monastery (塔尔寺)

- **Location:** Huangzhong, approximately 25 km southwest of Xining
- **Significance:** One of the six great monasteries of the Gelug school of Tibetan Buddhism; birthplace of Tsongkhapa, founder of the Gelug order
- **Visit:** Stop en route to Xining

### Xining (西宁) -- Provincial Capital

- **Contrast:** Return to a modern Chinese city after days in remote plateau regions
- **Night Market:** Bustling street food scene
  - Rose cakes (玫瑰饼) -- a local specialty the author enjoyed
  - Vendor robots noted as a quirky modern touch
- **Accommodation:** Hotel with robot room service -- the author found this amusing after days of rustic plateau lodging
- **Author's description:** Xining feels "retro" with an unexpected mix of traditional and futuristic

**Overnight:** Xining

## Key Themes & Author Opinions

**Pace of Travel**
- The author strongly advocates slow travel on the plateau: "immersion, perspective and travelling slowly"
- Altitude and terrain naturally enforce a slower pace, which she sees as a feature, not a limitation
- Wildlife spotting requires patience and flexibility in the schedule

**Tourism Levels**
- "Very few international visitors" throughout the route
- Authentic encounters with monks, pilgrims, and local communities
- No tourist crowds even at major monasteries

**Spiritual Dimension**
- The accumulated mani stones at Jiana represent generations of devotion
- Monastery lunches with monks provide intimate cultural exchange
- The waste-reduction policy at Dzogchen reflects Buddhist mindfulness in daily practice

**Contrast: Plateau vs. City**
- The jump from remote plateau to Xining's robot room service and night markets is jarring but entertaining
- Xining offers a decompression zone before flying home

## Practical Tips

**Altitude**
- Dzogchen Monastery sits at nearly 4,000m; the plateau averages 4,500m
- Maduo is one of the highest county-level towns in China (~4,300m)
- By Week 2, acclimatization from Week 1 helps, but altitude effects persist
- Ascend gradually; the route naturally does this from Chengdu

**Road Conditions**
- Remote mountain roads; private vehicle with experienced driver essential
- River valley roads (e.g., Day 8) can be narrow and winding
- Weather can affect road conditions at any time

**Food on the Plateau**
- Monastery vegetarian meals are available and recommended
- Silken tofu in Yushu is worth trying
- Food options become more limited in remote stretches (Maduo)
- Xining offers full range of Chinese cuisine and street food

**Connectivity**
- Very limited in remote areas (Yushu to Maduo stretch especially)
- Xining has full connectivity
- Download offline maps and entertainment before entering plateau

**Photography**
- Mountain and grassland light is extraordinary
- Monasteries may have restrictions on interior photography
- Ask permission before photographing monks and pilgrims

**Getting Out**
- Xining Caojiabao Airport has flights to major Chinese cities
- Train connections also available from Xining (including the famous Qinghai-Tibet Railway)
