# Jiuzhaigou & Huanglong (九寨沟 & 黄龙) — Complete Visiting Guide

**Source:** China Discovery (chinadiscovery.com, 2025 update) + Bucketlistly Blog (bucketlistly.blog) + Asia Odyssey Travel (asiaodysseytravel.com). Comprehensive guide covering both parks with new train access, ticket systems, and seasonal advice.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Aba Tibetan and Qiang Autonomous Prefecture (阿坝州), Northern Sichuan.
- **UNESCO:** Both are World Heritage Sites and World Biosphere Reserves.
- **Jiuzhaigou elevation:** 2,000–3,100 meters. "Nine Village Valley" — named for nine Tibetan villages.
- **Huanglong elevation:** 3,070–3,600+ meters. Higher altitude, more altitude sickness risk.
- **Key features:** 108 turquoise lakes, 3 waterfall groups, colorful travertine pools.
- **Wildlife:** 220 bird species, 74 state-protected plant species, 18 endangered animal species (including giant pandas).
- **Best time:** Late September through October — "the entire Jiuzhaigou is filled with diverse colors" from autumn foliage.

## Getting There

### New Train Access (Opened 2024)

**Zhenjiangguan Railway Station (镇江关站):** Connects Chengdu to Jiuzhaigou in ~1.5 hours by high-speed train. ~3 runs per day. Cheaper than flying. However, the station requires 1.5–2.5 hours additional driving to reach the parks.

### By Flight

- **Jiuzhai-Huanglong Airport (九黄机场):** Elevation 3,448 meters — one of China's highest airports.
- From Chengdu: ~7 daily flights, ~45 minutes.
- From Chongqing: 1–3 daily flights.
- **Warning:** Winter flights frequently cancelled due to weather. Airport sits at high altitude — altitude sickness possible immediately upon arrival.

### By Road

- **Distance from Chengdu:** 440 km via Dujiangyan (都江堰), Wenchuan (汶川), and Maoxian (茂县).
- **New Chengdu–Jiuzhaigou Expressway (2025):** Significantly reduced drive time.
- Recommended for gradual altitude acclimatization.

## Jiuzhaigou Valley (九寨沟) — Main Park

### Must-See Lakes

- **Five-Color Pond (五彩池):** Smallest but most colorful — shifts between turquoise, emerald, azure, and yellow depending on light and minerals.
- **Long Lake (长海):** Largest and deepest lake in the valley. Snowcapped mountain backdrop.
- **Mirror Lake (镜海):** Perfect reflections in still morning water. Best before 9:00 AM.
- **Panda Lake (熊猫海):** Clear turquoise water where giant pandas once came to drink.
- **Pearl Shoal (珍珠滩):** Wide, shallow cascade that sparkles like scattered pearls.

### Waterfalls

- **Nuo Ri Lang Waterfall (诺日朗瀑布):** 20 meters high, 320 meters wide — the widest travertine waterfall in China.
- **Pearl Shoal Waterfall:** Cascades below Pearl Shoal with rainbow effects in morning light.
- **Panda Lake Waterfall:** Three-tiered falls with emerald pools.

### Visiting Strategy

- Park sightseeing buses run a Y-shaped route through three valleys (Rize, Zechawa, Shuzheng).
- **One-day plan:** Focus on Rize Valley (morning) and Zechawa Valley (afternoon). Hit Five-Color Pond and Long Lake first.
- **Two-day plan:** Full exploration of all three valleys with time for boardwalk hikes. Requires separate daily tickets.
- Average temperature: 9–18°C year-round. Annual rainfall: 761mm.

## Huanglong Valley (黄龙) — Travertine Pools

### Key Features

- **Travertine terraces:** Over 3,400 colorful calcified pools cascading down the valley — reminiscent of Turkey's Pamukkale but in a forested mountain setting.
- **Five-Color Ponds (五彩池):** The crown jewel at the top of the valley — hundreds of small pools in brilliant blue, green, yellow, and orange.
- Temples, waterfalls, and caves along the route.

### Visiting Strategy

- Cable car available to reduce hiking (recommended due to altitude).
- Walking down from the top takes 2–3 hours through forested boardwalks.
- Most visitors feel altitude effects here more than at Jiuzhaigou due to 500+ meters higher elevation.
- Allow 3–4 hours total.

## Altitude & Health

- **Jiuzhaigou:** 2,000–3,100 meters. Most tourists do not experience significant altitude sickness.
- **Huanglong:** 3,070–3,600+ meters. Higher altitude means greater risk. Take the cable car up, walk down.
- **Jiuzhai-Huanglong Airport:** 3,448 meters — altitude sickness possible immediately upon landing.
- **Prevention:** Hydrate heavily, avoid alcohol, move slowly on arrival, consider Rhodiola Rosea tablets 1 week before.
- **Recommended approach:** Drive from Chengdu rather than fly for gradual acclimatization.

## Recommended Combined Itinerary

**3-Day Plan:**
- Day 1: Travel to the area (train/drive), visit Huanglong in afternoon.
- Day 2: Full day in Jiuzhaigou (arrive early, stay until closing).
- Day 3: Second Jiuzhaigou day or return to Chengdu.

**Efficient 2-Day Plan:**
- Day 1: Bullet train from Chengdu, visit Huanglong. Stay near Jiuzhaigou.
- Day 2: Full day in Jiuzhaigou, return via train/drive.

## Practical Tips

- **Tickets:** Book online in advance during peak season (Oct 1–7 especially). Daily visitor limits enforced.
- **Accommodation:** Stay in Jiuzhaigou County (Zhangzha Town/漳扎镇) — numerous hotels at all price levels.
- **Food:** Local Tibetan and Sichuan cuisine in the town. Yak meat hotpot and butter tea are specialties.
- **Clothing:** Layer for temperature swings — mornings are cold, midday can be warm, evenings drop sharply.
- **Boardwalks:** Well-maintained wooden boardwalks throughout both parks. Comfortable walking shoes sufficient (no hiking boots needed).
- **Photography:** Early morning for Mirror Lake reflections. Midday for Five-Color Pond colors. Sunset for Long Lake mountain backdrop.
- **Combine with:** Chengdu (pandas, hotpot) for a 5–7 day Sichuan trip.
