# Sichuan-Tibet Highway (川藏公路) — G318 vs G317 Route Guide

**Source:** Asia Odyssey Travel (asiaodysseytravel.com, 2025). Detailed comparison of the Southern Route (G318) and Northern Route (G317) from Chengdu to Lhasa with key stops, elevations, road conditions, and photography highlights.

## Quick Facts

- **Total routes:** Two — Southern G318 and Northern G317, both connecting Chengdu (成都) to Lhasa (拉萨).
- **G318 Southern Route:** 2,142 km, 7–10 days, altitude 2,000–3,500 m. More natural landscapes, better road conditions.
- **G317 Northern Route:** 2,200 km, 8–11 days, altitude 2,000–4,000 m. More cultural immersion, fewer tourists, rougher roads.
- **Tibet Travel Permit:** Mandatory once you enter the Tibet Autonomous Region (TAR). Not needed within Sichuan.
- **Best time G318:** October–November for autumn colors and clear skies.
- **Best time G317:** June–August for warmer temperatures at higher altitude.

## Southern Route — G318 (国道318)

### Key Stops

**Chengdu (成都)** → **Kangding (康定, 2,560 m)** → **Xinduqiao (新都桥)** → **Litang (理塘, 4,014 m)** → **Batang (巴塘)** → **Markam/Mangkang (芒康)** → **Ranwu (然乌)** → **Bome (波密)** → **Nyingchi (林芝)** → **Lhasa (拉萨)**

### Highlights Along the Way

- **Hailuogou Glacier Park (海螺沟):** Lowest-elevation glacier of its type in the world. Hot springs + glacier views.
- **Kangding (康定):** Gateway to the Tibetan Plateau. Love song capital. Mugecuo Lake nearby.
- **Xinduqiao (新都桥):** "Photography Paradise" (摄影天堂). Autumn light on Tibetan houses, poplar trees, and rolling grasslands.
- **Tagong Grassland (塔公草原):** Vast highland meadows with Yala Snow Mountain (雅拉雪山, 5,820 m) backdrop. Tagong Monastery.
- **Litang (理塘):** One of the world's highest towns at 4,014 m. Birthplace of several Dalai Lamas. Horse Racing Festival (first week of August).
- **Daocheng Yading (稻城亚丁):** Sacred Three Holy Mountains side trip from Litang.
- **72 Turns of Nujiang River (怒江72拐):** Dramatic hairpin bends through a deep gorge — one of China's most spectacular road segments.
- **Ranwu Lake (然乌湖):** Crystal-blue glacial lake surrounded by snowcapped peaks.
- **Midui Glacier (米堆冰川):** Accessible glacier near Bome.
- **Lulang Forest (鲁朗林海):** Dense old-growth forest in a river valley, sometimes called "Switzerland of the East."

## Northern Route — G317 (国道317)

### Key Stops

**Chengdu** → **Wolong (卧龙)** → **Mount Siguniang (四姑娘山)** → **Danba (丹巴)** → **Daofu (道孚)** → **Ganzi/Garze (甘孜)** → **Dege (德格)** → **Chamdo (昌都)** → **Lhasa**

### Highlights Along the Way

- **Wolong Panda Base (卧龙大熊猫基地):** Giant panda research and breeding center in natural mountain habitat.
- **Mount Siguniang (四姑娘山):** "Oriental Alps" with four peaks. Shuangqiao and Changping Valleys for trekking.
- **Danba Tibetan Villages (丹巴藏寨):** Ancient watchtowers, Jiarong Tibetan architecture. Zhonglu, Jiaju, and Suopo villages ranked among China's most beautiful.
- **Dege Parkhang Printing House (德格印经院):** 18th-century Buddhist scripture printing center — "Encyclopedia of Tibetan Culture." Still hand-printing using 320,000 carved woodblocks.
- **Zizhu Temple (孜珠寺):** 3,000-year-old sacred Bon religion site on Zizhu Mountain.
- **Galden Jampaling Monastery:** Eastern Tibet's largest monastery in Chamdo.

## Route Comparison

| Factor | G318 (South) | G317 (North) |
|---|---|---|
| Distance | 2,142 km | 2,200 km |
| Duration | 7–10 days | 8–11 days |
| Max altitude | ~3,500 m average passes | ~4,000 m average passes |
| Road quality | Mixed but mostly paved | Paved with some rough sections |
| Scenery focus | Natural landscapes, glaciers | Cultural sites, monasteries |
| Tourist density | More popular, more services | Fewer tourists, more remote |
| Best season | Oct–Nov (autumn) | Jun–Aug (summer) |

## Combined Route Option

Travel G318 southbound (Chengdu → Litang), detour to Daocheng Yading, continue to Mangkang, then explore the northern Tibet route before heading to Lhasa. This captures the best of both routes.

## Accommodation Towns (G318)

Kangding, Yajiang (雅江), Xinduqiao, Litang, Batang, Markam, Baxoi (八宿), Bome, Nyingchi. Hotels available at all levels in larger towns; basic guesthouses in smaller stops.

## Accommodation Towns (G317)

Siguniangshan Town, Danba, Xinduqiao, Daofu, Ganzi, Dege, Chamdo, Dingqing (丁青), Baqing (巴青), Nagqu (那曲).

## Practical Tips

- **Tibet Travel Permit:** Mandatory for the TAR section (roughly west of Markam on G318 or west of Dege on G317). Must be arranged through a registered travel agency at least 20 days in advance. Independent travel not permitted in TAR.
- **Within Sichuan (Garze/Aba prefectures):** No special permits needed. Much more travel freedom than TAR.
- **Altitude acclimatization:** Essential. Spend 1–2 nights in Kangding (2,560 m) before going higher.
- **Road conditions:** Improved dramatically in recent years but landslides and rockfalls still possible, especially July–August rainy season.
- **Fuel:** Gas stations at regular intervals on G318. Less frequent on G317 — fill up whenever possible.
- **Weather gear:** Layers essential. Temperatures can swing 20°C within a single day. Rain gear mandatory.
- **Photography:** Xinduqiao and Tagong offer the richest photography opportunities in autumn. Golden hour light on Tibetan houses is extraordinary.
- **Hire experienced local drivers** — mountain driving at altitude requires skill and knowledge of road conditions.
