# Off the Beaten Path China Itinerary: Two Weeks in Sichuan & Qinghai

**Source:** Suzanne Jones (thetravelbunny.com), UK-based travel blog with off-the-beaten-path itineraries. Published December 16, 2025; updated February 11, 2026. First-hand experience.

Two-week private road trip across the Tibetan Plateau through Western Sichuan (Garze/Ganzi Prefecture) and Qinghai (Yushu Prefecture), organized by Eastogo Travel. The route deliberately avoids the Tibetan Autonomous Region (TAR) -- where foreigners need permits and must join organized tours -- instead exploring Tibetan autonomous prefectures in adjacent provinces where independent/private travel is possible.

---

## The Tibetan Plateau (青藏高原)

- World's largest plateau, approximately 2.5 million square kilometers
- Average elevation: 4,500 meters (14,800 feet); range 3,100--8,848m
- Known as "the roof of the world"
- Spans China (Sichuan, Qinghai, Gansu, Yunnan, TAR), Pakistan, Nepal, Bhutan, India
- This itinerary crosses Garze (Ganzi) Prefecture in Sichuan and Yushu Prefecture in Qinghai

## Why Skip the TAR

Foreigners need a permit to enter the Tibetan Autonomous Region and must travel with a local agency on a fixed itinerary. This route offers authentic Tibetan culture, monasteries, grasslands, and nomadic communities without those restrictions. The Tibetan autonomous prefectures in Sichuan and Qinghai are open for private/custom travel.

## Week 1: Sichuan -- Garze (Ganzi) Prefecture (甘孜藏族自治州)

### Day 1: Chengdu (成都) to Zhonglu Tibetan Village (中路藏寨) via Wolong

**Wolong Shenshuping Panda Base (卧龙神树坪基地)**
- Stop en route from Chengdu
- Giant pandas in semi-natural habitat settings
- Worth a half-day visit

**Zhonglu Tibetan Village (中路藏寨)**
- Ancient Tibetan village with stone watchtowers in a river valley
- Cultural introduction to Tibetan village life
- Overnight in village accommodation

### Day 2: Zhonglu Village to Tagong Grassland (塔公草原)

- Morning to explore Zhonglu village and its watchtowers
- Drive to Tagong Grassland, a vast high-altitude grassland area
- Overnight in Tagong area

### Day 3: Tagong Grassland (塔公草原) -- Nomadic Yak Herder Experience

- Full day spent with a family of nomadic yak herders
- Cultural immersion: meals, daily activities, herding observation
- Horse riding on the grasslands
- Tagong Monastery visible on the grassland
- Author considers this one of the trip's highlights -- "unforgettable memories"
- Overnight in Tagong area

### Day 4: Tagong (塔公) to Litang (理塘)

- Mountain pass driving through dramatic scenery
- Altitude adjustment period -- Litang sits at approximately 4,014m
- Known as one of the highest towns in the world
- Overnight in Litang

### Day 5: Litang (理塘) to Zeba Village via Changquingchunkeer Monastery

**Changquingchunkeer Monastery**
- Located in the Letong area between Litang and Zeba
- Monastery visit with spiritual exploration and prayer wheels
- Meditation opportunity with resident monks

**Zeba Village**
- Remote village accommodation
- Overnight in Zeba

### Day 6: Zeba Village to Batang (巴塘)

- Continued mountain travel through western Sichuan
- Overnight in Batang

### Day 7: Batang (巴塘) to Baiyu (白玉)

- Progression toward the Qinghai border
- Baiyu is the jumping-off point for Week 2's crossing into Qinghai
- Overnight in Baiyu

## Practical Tips

**Altitude**
- Average plateau elevation 4,500m; some passes higher
- Litang at ~4,014m; plan for acclimatization days
- Altitude sickness is a real concern; build in rest days and ascend gradually

**Access & Permits**
- No TAR permit needed for this route (Sichuan and Qinghai prefectures)
- Standard China visa required
- Private tour recommended due to remoteness, language barriers, and road conditions

**When to Go**
- Author traveled in autumn; grasslands, clear skies, and manageable temperatures
- Summer (June--September) is green season but wetter
- Winter is extremely cold at altitude

**Getting There**
- Start/end: Chengdu (fly in) / Xining (fly out), or reverse
- Internal travel by private vehicle with driver (arranged by Eastogo)

**Packing**
- Layers essential for altitude temperature swings
- Sun protection critical at high elevation
- Comfortable walking shoes for monastery and village visits

**Connectivity**
- Remote areas have limited mobile signal and internet
- Download offline maps before departure

**Food**
- Vegetarian monastery meals available
- Tibetan butter tea and yak-based dishes common
- Village homestay meals are simple but authentic
