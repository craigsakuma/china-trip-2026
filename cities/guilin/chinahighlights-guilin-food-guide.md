## Signature Dishes

### Guilin Rice Noodles (Mifen)
The city's iconic dish, invented 2,000+ years ago as soldier rations during the Qin Dynasty:
- **What it is:** Silky white rice noodles in bone broth simmered with 20+ spices
- **"Dry-tossed" style (ganban fen):** Noodles topped with crispy pork, braised beef, pickled beans, peanuts, scallions -- mixed by diner, broth on the side
- **Soup style (tang fen):** Noodles in mild pork/beef broth with greens
- **Where:** Every street corner; stainless-steel-bowl shops are most authentic
- **Cost:** 3-10 CNY per bowl
- **Tip:** Locals add ladle of broth to dry noodles after mixing toppings -- follow their lead

### Beer Fish (Pijiu Yu)
Yangshuo's most famous dish, made with Liquan Beer:
- Fresh Li River carp fried crispy with scales still on
- Braised slowly with beer, tomatoes, bell peppers, garlic, and secret sauce
- Beer flambe creates dramatic kitchen show
- **Best in:** Yangshuo restaurants with live fish tanks and transparent kitchens
- **Cost:** 80-120 CNY per 500g
- **Also:** Beer Duck (Pijiu Ya) -- same technique with duck, equally popular

### Oil Tea ("Chinese Coffee")
600-year-old ethnic minority brew:
- Fresh tea leaves stir-fried with aged ginger and garlic
- Creates amber, invigorating infusion
- Paired with crispy puffed rice, peanuts, scallions
- First cup tastes bitter; sweetness emerges by the third cup
- **Origin:** Ethnic minority villages in Longsheng area
- Served in small bowls -- meant to be drunk in succession

## Regional Specialties

### Stuffed Delicacies (Niangcai)
A Guilin hallmark -- hollowed vegetables or snails refilled with seasoned pork:
- **Stuffed River Snails (Luosi Niang):** Most famous version; snails hollowed, refilled with seasoned pork and fresh mint
- **Stuffed Tofu (Doufu Niang):** Soft tofu pockets filled with pork, mushrooms
- **Stuffed Eggplant (Qiezi Niang):** Sliced eggplant with pork filling, pan-fried
- **Stuffed Bitter Melon (Kugua Niang):** Rings of bitter melon with meat stuffing
- **Cost:** 20-50 CNY per portion

### Lipu Taro Dishes (Lipu Yutou)
Guilin's famous taro from Lipu County, two signature preparations:
- **Braised Pork with Taro (Yutou Kourou):** Sliced pork belly layered alternately with soft taro slices, braised in soy sauce -- classic banquet dish
- **Candied Taro (Basi Yutou):** Taro cubes coated in golden caramel with characteristic pulled sugar strands

### Bamboo Tube Cuisine (Zhutong Fan/Ji)
Rustic countryside cooking:
- **Bamboo Tube Rice:** Glutinous rice with preserved pork and taro, sealed in fresh bamboo and roasted over firewood. Bamboo imparts subtle fragrance
- **Bamboo Tube Chicken:** Whole chicken with mushrooms and goji berries, slow-cooked in bamboo
- **Cost:** 20-30 CNY per portion
- Best found at countryside restaurants near Longji Rice Terraces

## Street Food and Snacks

### Morning Markets (6-7 AM optimal)
- **Heishan Market:** Steaming sticky rice wraps, freshly sliced pork belly
- **Xishan Market:** Mugwort cakes (ai baba) handmade from scratch, rice porridge
- Authentic local breakfast experience -- arrive early for best selection

### Night Markets
- **Chuanshan Market:** Sizzling woks with Li River fish, fried rice noodles, grilled squid
- **Qixing Road Market:** Street snacks, fruit, local specialties
- **Shijiayuan Road Market:** Smaller but authentic
- **Suan ye:** Tart fruits dipped in chili salt -- a Guilin signature street snack, about 5 CNY

### Sweet Treats
- **Sticky Rice Balls (Nuomi Fantuan):** Portable breakfast, stuffed with sugar, peanuts, or savory fillings
- **Water Chestnut Cake (Mati Gao):** Translucent jelly-like cake with crunchy water chestnut pieces
- **Osmanthus Cake (Guihua Gao):** Floral, chewy texture; Guilin's namesake flower (gui means osmanthus) featured prominently

## Snail Rice Noodles (Luosifen)
Originally from Liuzhou (2 hrs from Guilin) but widely available:
- Spicy soup with rice noodles in fermented snail-based broth
- Topped with pickled bamboo shoots, tofu skin, peanuts, fresh vegetables
- The famously pungent smell comes from fermented bamboo shoots
- Acquired taste but beloved across China
- 15-25 CNY per bowl

## Where to Eat in Guilin

### Zhengyang Pedestrian Street
Main tourist dining street with both tourist traps and authentic spots:
- Look for crowded local shops, not the ones with English menus outside
- Best for casual browsing and rice noodle shops

### Yangshuo: West Street and Back Alleys
- West Street: International food, tourist restaurants
- Back alleys: Where locals eat -- cheaper, more authentic
- Countryside restaurants along Yulong River for bamboo tube dishes

## Practical Tips

- **Breakfast like a local:** Rice noodles at a stainless-steel-bowl shop, 5-8 CNY
- **Beer fish authentication:** Transparent kitchen, live fish tank, Liquan Beer visible
- **Oil tea etiquette:** Always drink at least 3 cups; stopping at 1 is considered rude
- **Adventurous eaters:** Try stuffed snails, horse meat noodles (marou mifen), and dog hotpot (if you dare -- still served in some local restaurants)
- **Osmanthus season:** October is peak osmanthus bloom -- osmanthus-flavored everything appears: tea, cake, wine, candy
- **Budget:** A full day of street food and local restaurants: 50-100 CNY per person
- **Dietary note:** Vegetarian options limited at traditional restaurants; communicate clearly. Buddhist temple restaurants offer vegetarian meals
