## Three Main Areas Compared

### Ping'an Zhuang Village (Ping'an Zhuangzhai Titian)
- **Distance:** 2 hours from Guilin
- **Character:** Most accessible, well-maintained stone paths, family-friendly
- **Best for:** Day trips, moderate hikers, cultural immersion with Zhuang minority
- **Key viewpoints:** "Seven Stars Accompany the Moon" and "Nine Dragons and Five Tigers"
- **Hiking time:** 2-3 hours for full loop
- **Drawback:** Smaller scale terraces, more commercialized

### Jinkeng/Dazhai Yao Village (Jinkeng Dazhai Yaozhai Titian)
- **Distance:** 2.5 hours from Guilin
- **Character:** Largest terrace area, most dramatic landscapes, Red Yao long-hair village
- **Best for:** Photographers, overnight stays, cable car users
- **Key viewpoints:** "West Hill Music" (highest at 1,180m), "Large-Scale Thousand-Layer Terraces", "Golden Buddha Peak"
- **Cable car:** Goes directly to viewpoint -- ideal for those who cannot hike
- **Highlight:** Huangluo Yao Village at base -- women with world's longest hair (avg 1.8m)

### Ancient Zhuang Village (Gu Zhuangzhai)
- **Distance:** 1.5 hours from Guilin
- **Character:** Smallest, least crowded, most authentic
- **Best for:** Cultural purists, avoiding tourist crowds
- **Drawback:** Less dramatic terrace views than the other two areas

## Seasonal Calendar (Critical for October Trip)

| Period | Terraces Look Like | Photography Rating |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Late Apr - mid Jun | Water-filled paddies, mirror reflections | Excellent |
| Mid Jun - mid Sep | Lush green growing rice | Very Good |
| Late Sep - mid Oct | Golden harvest, rice cutting | Excellent |
| Late Oct - Feb | Brown/bare terraces, possible snow | Fair |
| Mid Mar - late Apr | Plowing, early planting | Good |

**October 10-25 timing:** Perfect for golden harvest season. Rice cutting typically happens Oct 10-25, meaning you may see both golden fields and active harvesting -- prime photography conditions.

## Hiking Routes

### Ping'an Loop (Easy, 2-3 hours)
Start from Ping'an parking lot, through village entrance, to Seven Stars Accompany the Moon viewpoint, then Nine Dragons and Five Tigers viewpoint, return through village. Well-marked stone paths, moderate stairs.

### Ping'an to Ancient Zhuang (Easy, 2 hours, 5 km)
Connects two villages through terraced landscapes. Can take minibus one direction to conserve energy. Gentle terrain.

### Dazhai to Ping'an Traverse (Moderate, 5 hours, 17 km)
Full cross-terrace hike connecting both major areas. Descending sections more comfortable than ascending. Bring water and snacks -- limited food stops mid-route.

### Cable Car + West Hill Music Loop (Easy, 1-1.5 hours)
Take Jinkeng cable car to top, walk to West Hill Music viewpoint (highest point, 1,180m), panoramic terrace views, cable car back down. Best for limited mobility or time.

## Ethnic Minority Culture

### Zhuang People at Ping'an
- Largest ethnic minority in China (16+ million)
- Distinctive tilted wooden houses built against mountainsides
- Traditional songs and dances, embroidered clothing
- Famous for their rice wine and pickled dishes

### Red Yao People at Dazhai/Jinkeng
- Known for their striking red clothing and silver ornaments
- Women at Huangluo Village maintain hair averaging 1.8 meters long
- Hair-washing ceremony using rice water (said to prevent graying)
- Traditional long-hair dance performances for visitors
- Village entrance: 80 CNY (includes performance)

## Getting There from Guilin

### By Bus (Budget Option)
Guilin Qintan Bus Station to Longji Terraces Intersection. Departures every 20 min, 7:00 AM - 5:20 PM. About 1.5 hours, 60 CNY including transfer shuttle to Dazhai or Ping'an.

### By Private Car/Driver (Recommended)
About 1.5 hours via highway (opened 2017). Flexibility to visit both areas. 400-500 CNY round trip. Can combine with stops at hot springs or minority villages en route.

### Shuttle from Scenic Area Entrance
Free shuttle buses run between parking lots and village entrances within the scenic area.

## Accommodation

### Ping'an Village
- **Baike Hotel** -- From $56/night, terrace-view rooms
- Multiple guesthouses with basic but clean rooms
- Best for those wanting easy sunrise access

### Dazhai/Jinkeng
- **Longji Panorama House** -- From $36/night, stunning terrace panoramas
- Homestays with Yao families available
- Better for photographers wanting sunrise/sunset from elevated positions

### Luggage Warning
Villages are car-free with narrow stone steps. Avoid large suitcases. Porters available at village entrances for a fee (30-50 CNY per bag).

## Practical Tips

- **Sunrise photography:** Stay overnight; both Ping'an and Dazhai offer dawn viewpoints. West Hill Music at Dazhai is the premium sunrise spot
- **Footwear:** Sturdy shoes essential -- stone paths become slippery when wet
- **Cash:** Bring cash; card/mobile payment spotty in remote areas
- **Rain gear:** Mountain weather changes quickly; misty rain enhances terrace photography
- **Best photo time:** Early morning mist and golden hour; midday harsh light flattens the terraces
- **Day trip vs overnight:** Overnight strongly recommended for sunrise/sunset photographers; day trip sufficient for casual visitors
