# 22 Things to Do in Yangshuo (阳朔): Adventure, Culture & Hidden Gems

**Source:** Omeida Chinese Academy — omeidachinese.com/22-yangshuo-activities/

---

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Location | 65 km south of Guilin, on Li River |
| Getting There | Li River cruise (4 hrs), bus from Guilin (1.5 hrs), train to Yangshuo Station + bus (30 min) |
| Base | West Street (西街) — restaurants, bars, hotels, tour agencies |

## Adventure Activities

### Rock Climbing (攀岩)
Yangshuo is the **rock climbing capital of China** with thousands of limestone karst routes across all difficulty levels:
- Beginners: Half-day guided sessions with English-speaking instructors, all gear provided
- Intermediate/Advanced: Multi-pitch routes on towering karst cliffs
- Top crags: Moon Hill area, Riverside Crag, White Mountain
- **Operators:** Wei Hua's Rock Climbing (highest rated), multiple shops on West Street
- **Cost:** ~¥200-400 for half-day guided session with gear

### Bamboo Rafting on Yulong River (遇龙河竹筏)
Hand-poled bamboo rafts through rural karst countryside — quieter and more scenic than Li River:
- **Cost:** ~¥100/person
- **Duration:** 1-2 hours depending on section
- Shuttle returns passengers upstream
- Multiple starting points along the river
- More intimate than Li River cruise boats

### White Water Rafting on Longjing River (龙颈河漂流)
Inflatable raft adventure through mountain rapids:
- Located ~1 hour north of Yangshuo, near the train station
- Tickets bundle rafting + transport
- Wet and wild — bring change of clothes
- Seasonal: best May-October

### Ziplining & Via Ferrata
Multiple locations including near Guilin Romance Park:
- Spans between mountain peaks
- Various outdoor guide groups run trips
- Book through West Street agencies

## Water & River Experiences

### Cormorant Fishing Show (鸬鹚捕鱼)
Traditional sunset performances on the Li River:
- Trained cormorants dive for fish from bamboo rafts
- Atmospheric: lantern-lit river at dusk
- **Cost:** ¥70 from tour offices on West Street
- Best for: Photography, cultural experience

### Swimming in Yulong River
Locals and travelers swim in designated calm sections:
- Free and refreshing in summer/early autumn
- Water quality excellent upstream from Yangshuo
- Combine with cycling along the river road

## Cultural & Educational Experiences

### Cooking Classes (烹饪课)
Yangshuo Cooking School is the standout option:
- Market tour: instructors take you to local markets to buy fresh ingredients
- Countryside kitchen location near Yulong River
- Learn Guangxi recipes: beer fish, stuffed tofu, stir-fried greens
- English-speaking instructors
- Shared meal of your creations at the end
- **Cost:** ~¥200-300 per person, half-day

### Tea Plantation Visit (七星茶园)
Seven Stars Tea Plantation, 12 km north of Yangshuo:
- Pick tea leaves yourself
- Watch roasting demonstration
- Learn brewing techniques and ceremony
- Taste multiple varieties
- Combine with cycling route

### Tai Chi Classes (太极)
Authentic tai chi among karst landscapes:
- Classes designed for foreigners
- Morning sessions in Yangshuo Park
- Multiple schools on West Street advertise

## Sightseeing & Exploration

### Moon Hill (月亮山)
Natural arch in a karst peak visible for miles:
- Located in Shi Li Hua Lang scenic area
- Hike up or view from village below
- Best photographed in late afternoon light

### Xianggong Hill (相公山) — Li River Panorama
Premium sunrise location with Li River S-bend views:
- 1.5 hours from Yangshuo by car
- Entrance fee required
- Famous for sea-of-clouds dawn shots
- Go early (before 6 AM) for best position

### Cave Exploring (溶洞探险)
Multiple caves in the karst landscape:
- **Silver Cave (银子岩):** Largest, illuminated formations, touristic
- **Gold Water Cave:** Features underground hot springs
- **Butterfly Springs Cave:** Stalactite formations
- **Treasure Cave:** Local secret, no ticket required — ask locals for directions

### TV Tower Mountain Hike
Near West Street, accessible quick hike:
- ¥5 for tower access
- Great city and karst panorama views
- 30-minute climb
- Best at sunset

## Entertainment & Night Life

### West Street (西街)
The social hub of Yangshuo — "where all the action is":
- Pedestrian street along the Li River
- Bars, clubs, restaurants, hotels, souvenir shops
- Lively after dark, especially weekends
- Good for: People-watching, craft beer, international food

### Impression Sanjie Liu Show (印象刘三姐)
Directed by Zhang Yimou, performed on the Li River itself:
- 600+ performers on water stage with karst mountain backdrop
- Musical and light show based on a local folk legend
- **Cost:** ¥198+ from West Street agencies
- Evening performances; book ahead in peak season

### Guilin Romance Park (桂林千古情)
Theme park with indoor theater performance:
- Similar to Sanjie Liu but indoors with tech effects
- Also: beach, water park, haunted house, street performances
- **Cost:** ¥250-350
- Considered expensive for what it offers

## Hidden Gems & Day Trips

### Pu Bu Tang Waterfall (瀑布塘)
Secret waterfall 30 min by scooter north of Yangshuo:
- Few tourists know about it
- No entrance fee
- Shallow water for wading
- Rolling hills and Li River views en route

### Fuli Bridge & Village (福利桥)
Stone arch bridge with near-perfect circle reflections:
- Fewer crowds than famous Dragon Bridge
- Best photographed in morning calm water
- Bicycle-accessible from Yangshuo

### Xingping Ancient Village (兴坪古镇)
1,700-year-old village on a Li River peninsula:
- 45 min by bus from Yangshuo
- Shops, restaurants, raft launches
- 20-yuan banknote viewpoint nearby
- More authentic feel than Yangshuo town

## Cycling Routes (Yangshuo's Best Way to Explore)

### Yulong River Route (Most Popular)
Yangshuo → Yulong Bridge → Dragon Bridge → Yulong River scenic area:
- Flat terrain, quiet farm lanes, bamboo groves
- Traditional villages, water buffalo, rice paddies
- Multiple bamboo raft launch points along the way
- 2-4 hours round trip

### Ten-Mile Gallery (十里画廊)
Scenic road south of Yangshuo through karst formations:
- Dedicated bike path
- Moon Hill, Butterfly Spring, various peaks
- 1-2 hours

## Practical Tips

- **Rent bicycles** for countryside exploration — cheapest and most immersive transport
- **Book tours** through West Street agencies for best prices on shows and rafting
- **Hidden spots** (Treasure Cave, Pu Bu Tang Waterfall) offer crowd-free alternatives
- **Avoid:** Peak season weekends when domestic tourists flood in
- **Budget:** Most activities ¥70-350; cycling and swimming are free
- **E-scooter note:** Gas scooters require Chinese license; electric scooters do not
- **October timing:** Ideal weather, post-Golden-Week crowds thin after Oct 7
