# Visiting Guilin: A Complete Travel Guide

**Source:** Pete R. / Pete Rojwongsuriya (bucketlistly.blog), solo travel and photography blogger with 11+ years of experience. First-hand experience.

Guilin's karst mountains, peaceful rivers, and ancient villages feel like they were pulled straight out of a painting. The peaks of Guilin famously inspired the landscape in the Japanese manga/anime *Dragon Ball*. The region spans Guilin City, Yangshuo, and Xingping -- each with distinct character and worth multiple days.

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Recommended duration** | At least 5 days total |
| **Guilin City** | 1--2 nights (2 if visiting Longji Rice Terraces) |
| **Yangshuo** | 2--3 nights for rivers, viewpoints, and activities |
| **Xingping** | 1 night minimum for sunset hike |
| **Best time to visit** | Spring (Mar--May) and autumn (Sep--Nov); mild temps, clear skies |
| **Summer** | Hot, humid, rainy; occasional typhoons |
| **Winter** | 5--15 C, quieter but cooler |
| **Peak / avoid** | Chinese New Year (Jan/Feb) and National Day (Oct 1--7) bring crowds and inflated prices |
| **Currency** | CNY (Chinese Yuan) |
| **Getting there** | High-speed train to Guilin; Yangshuo also has a HSR station near Xingping |

## Guilin City Attractions

### Elephant Trunk Hill (象鼻山)

Guilin's most famous landmark, named for its shape resembling an elephant dipping its trunk into the Li River. The natural arch between the "trunk" and the hill is called **Water Moon Cave** -- on calm evenings the reflection creates a moon-like silhouette on the water.

- **Hours:** 7:00 AM -- 9:30 PM daily
- **Cost:** Free with passport
- **Location:** Central Guilin, along the Li River

### Reed Flute Cave (芦笛岩)

A spectacular limestone cave filled with stalactites, stone pillars, and rock formations sculpted by dripping water over millennia. Key formations include Tower-shaped Pine and Crystal Palace, all illuminated with colorful lighting.

- **Cost:** ~90 CNY adults; 45 CNY children (1.2--1.4 m); free under 1.2 m
- **Location:** Northwest Guilin, about 5 km from city center

### Seven Star Park (七星公园)

The largest park in Guilin City, named after seven peaks that resemble the Big Dipper constellation. Full of hidden hiking trails, unique rock formations, and a cave to explore. A good half-day outing.

- **Location:** East bank of the Li River

### Sun and Moon Pagodas (日月双塔)

Twin pagodas standing in Shanhu Lake in the heart of the city. The Sun Pagoda is golden (~41 m tall) and the Moon Pagoda is silver (~35 m tall). Especially beautiful at night when illuminated and reflected on the still lake.

- **Cost:** ~35 CNY entry to the complex
- **Tip:** Visit at night for the best photo opportunity with reflections

### Two Rivers and Four Lakes (两江四湖)

A scenic area connecting the Li River and Taohua River to four inland lakes (Ronghu, Shanhu, Guihu, Mulonghu). Covers ~6.15 million square meters and links landmarks including Elephant Trunk Hill, Fubo Hill, and Seven Star Mountain.

- **Night cruise:** Popular way to see the illuminated scenery; Pete recommends an evening stroll around the lakes as a free alternative
- **Tip:** The nighttime atmosphere is the main draw

## Longji Rice Terraces (龙脊梯田)

Beautiful rice paddies climbing the slopes of karst mountains in intricate layers, about 2--3 hours from Guilin City by car (1.5 hrs via highway opened 2017). The name means "dragon's spine" -- the water-filled terraces resemble rippling scales.

### Best seasons for terraces

| Season | What you see |
|--------|-------------|
| Spring (Apr--Jun) | Water-filled terraces with mirror reflections |
| Summer (Jul--Aug) | Lush green rice |
| Autumn (Sep--Oct) | Golden fields before harvest (early Oct) |
| Winter | Quiet, occasional snow dusting |

### Ping'an Village (平安寨)

The first tourism-developed terrace area. Two main viewpoints: **Nine Dragons and Five Tigers** and **Seven Stars Around the Moon**. Half a day of hiking reveals the full highlights.

- **Hiking route:** Ancient Zhuang Village > Seven Stars with the Moon > Nine Dragons and Five Tigers > Ping'an Village (~2 hours total)

### Dazhai Village (大寨)

Features three viewpoints: **Music from Paradise**, **Thousand Layers to the Heavens**, and **Golden Buddha Peak**. A cable car runs from Dazhai parking lot to Golden Buddha Peak.

- **Hiking between villages:** Ping'an to Dazhai is ~6 hours on foot
- **Tip:** Plan 2 nights in Guilin City if doing a Longji day trip

## Li River Cruise (漓江游船)

The classic Guilin experience: a cruise down the Li River through towering karst peaks.

- **Duration:** ~6 hours total
- **Logistics:** Hotel pickup at 7--8 AM, transfer to pier (~1 hour), departure ~10 AM, arrival at Longtoshun Wharf ~2 PM
- **Cost:** ~64 USD for a 3-star cruise; 4-star and 5-star options available at higher prices
- **Tip:** The cruise ends near Yangshuo, so plan your accommodation transition accordingly

---

## Yangshuo (阳朔)

The heart of the karst landscape. Spend 2--3 nights here to explore rivers, viewpoints, and activities.

### Ruyi Peak (如意峰)

Pete's top recommendation for Yangshuo. Take a cable car up to one of the highest karst mountains in the area for a **360-degree panoramic view** surrounded by hundreds of other karst peaks. Features a stunning **glass bridge** stretching between two peaks.

- **Transport:** Cable car up and down
- **Tip:** If you do one thing in Yangshuo, make it this

### Bamboo Rafting on the Yulong River (遇龙河竹筏)

A tranquil drift through karst scenery on a traditional bamboo raft with a guide navigating using a bamboo pole. Each raft seats two people and has an umbrella for shade.

- **Cost:** ~30 USD per raft (2 people)
- **Route:** The Jima to Gongnong Bridge section is ~6 km and the most scenic

### E-Scooter Exploration

Rent an e-scooter to freely explore the countryside and karst scenery at your own pace.

- **Cost:** 50--60 CNY per day
- **Range:** ~70 km per charge (3--4 hours of riding)
- **Rental location:** Corner of Yangshuo West Street
- **Note:** Shops close at ~6 PM; return before then

### Xianggong Hill Viewpoint (相公山)

One of the most famous viewpoints in the Guilin region for sunrise photography, overlooking the Li River winding through karst peaks.

---

## Xingping (兴坪)

An ancient town right next to Yangshuo High-Speed Railway Station -- a perfect final stop before leaving the region. Charming old-town streets and one of the best sunset hikes in the area.

### Lao Zhai Shan Hill (老寨山)

A steep karst mountain next to Xingping Ancient Village. The hike from the trailhead to the top takes about **30 minutes without rest** on an extremely steep trail. From the summit, see the Li River winding around jagged karst peaks. If you scramble further up a jagged karst peak, you can see the river bending around hundreds of peaks from the other side.

- **Cost:** Free
- **Best time:** Sunset
- **Tip:** Pete considers this one of his favorite viewpoints in all of Guilin

## Food

### Guilin Rice Noodles (桂林米粉)

The signature local dish -- rice noodles served in various preparations. Cheap, filling, and available everywhere in the city. A must-try.

### Beer Fish (啤酒鱼)

A Yangshuo specialty: fresh river fish braised in local beer. Best tried at restaurants along West Street in Yangshuo.

## Practical Tips

- **Passport:** Carry your passport for free entry to Elephant Trunk Hill and potential discounts elsewhere
- **Suggested flow:** Guilin City (1--2 nights) > Li River Cruise > Yangshuo (2--3 nights) > Xingping (1 night) > depart via Yangshuo HSR
- **Transport within Yangshuo:** E-scooter is the best way to explore independently; rent early as shops close by 6 PM
- **Li River cruise timing:** Book a morning pickup; the whole process takes most of the day
- **Longji day trip:** Budget a full day (2--3 hrs each way + hiking time); consider adding a second night in Guilin City
- **Photography:** Sunrise at Xianggong Hill, sunset at Lao Zhai Shan; the glass bridge at Ruyi Peak for dramatic shots
- **Crowds:** Avoid National Day week (Oct 1--7) and Chinese New Year; spring and autumn shoulder periods offer the best balance of weather and crowd levels
- **Budget:** Many attractions are affordable (free to ~90 CNY); the Li River cruise is the biggest single expense
