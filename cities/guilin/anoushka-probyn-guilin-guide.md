# The Essential Travel Guide to Guilin, Guangxi China

**Source:** Anoushka Probyn (anoushkaprobyn.com), lifestyle and travel photographer/blogger with emphasis on landscape photography and aesthetic cafes. Published July 25, 2025. First-hand experience during a six-month Asia trip; spent six nights in the Guilin region and wished for more time.

---

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Region** | Guilin (桂林), Yangshuo (阳朔), Xingping (兴坪), Longji/Longsheng Rice Terraces (龙脊梯田) |
| **Best time to visit** | Spring (April–May) and Autumn (September–October); author visited June with good weather |

## China Essentials — Apps & Connectivity

- **E-SIM recommended** over VPN (VPNs unreliable within China); author used Nomad e-SIM. Local SIM cards helpful for activating apps.
- **AMaps (高德地图):** Primary navigation app; Google Maps is blocked in China.
- **DiDi (滴滴):** Ride-hailing app, China's Uber equivalent.
- **Rednote (小红书):** Chinese social media app (similar to TikTok); excellent for discovering local restaurant and cafe recommendations, scenic viewpoints, and tagged locations with direct navigation links to AMaps.
- **Alipay / WeChat Pay:** QR-code-based payment used virtually everywhere (Alipay = blue, WeChat = green).

---

## Getting Around the Guilin Region

- **Foreigners cannot rent cars** — international driving licenses are not recognized (temporary Chinese licenses are theoretically possible but impractical).
- **Options:** Regional buses between destinations, DiDi ride-hailing, scooter rentals (available in Yangshuo area), organized tours.
- **Author's opinion on tours:** Limiting, especially for photographers who want to control timing and duration at viewpoints.

## Guilin City (桂林市)

Guilin city itself is primarily a transit hub; most travelers spend one night before heading to Yangshuo or the rice terraces.

### Elephant Trunk Hill (象鼻山)

Iconic karst formation resembling an elephant drinking from the Li River. Viewable from an observation area or by boat. **Author's honest take:** "I don't entirely get the hype!"

### Sun and Moon Pagodas (日月双塔) on Shan Hu Lake

Twin pagodas best visited at night when they are dramatically illuminated.

### Reed Flute Cave (芦笛岩)

Limestone cave filled with colorfully lit stalactites and stalagmites; known locally as the "Palace of Natural Arts."

### Seven Star Park (七星公园)

Nearly 300-acre park featuring caves, rivers, and ancient stone carvings.

### Zhengyang Road Pedestrian Street (正阳路步行街)

Central walking street packed with shops, restaurants, and street food stalls. Touristy but worthwhile for atmosphere and local snacks.

### Guilin Regional Cuisine

- **Guilin rice noodles (桂林米粉):** The signature local dish
- **Beer fish (啤酒鱼):** Regional specialty, especially popular in Yangshuo
- **Bamboo rice (竹筒饭):** Rice cooked inside bamboo tubes
- **Adventurous options:** Snails, frog, intestines — widely available at street-food stalls and local restaurants

## Li River Cruise — Guilin to Yangshuo (漓江游船)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Duration** | ~3.5 hours scenic journey |
| **Cost** | ~¥230 (~£26) per person for 3-star cruise when booked locally |
| **Online price** | ~$78 — significantly more expensive; wait until arrival in Guilin to book |
| **Booking tip** | Book through local travel agents or your hotel, NOT online platforms |
| **Departure docks** | Mopanshan or Zhujiang Dock, about 1 hour outside Guilin; group coach pickup typically included |
| **Options** | 3-star or 4-star cruise; 4-star includes meals and fewer passengers but same route |
| **Guides** | Speak Mandarin only; includes product sales pitch mid-journey |

**Highlights:** View of the famous 20 Yuan banknote landscape. Top deck becomes less crowded as the journey progresses — head up later for better photo spots.

**Arrival in Yangshuo:** Docks 1.5 km outside Yangshuo town. Walking route available along the river, or taxis (expect overcharging from dock taxis).

## Yangshuo (阳朔)

County town built around West Street (西街), a pedestrianized shopping and dining strip. Very touristy, full of souvenir shops and tourists posing in traditional costume, but a convenient base for exploring the surrounding karst scenery.

### Accommodation in Yangshuo

**Riverview Hotel (author's pick):**
- Overlooks the river with affordable pricing
- English-speaking staff (rare in the region)
- Central location eliminates taxi expenses
- Popular restaurant downstairs
- Room upgrades available and worthwhile

**Other recommended options:**
- **Shijian Homestay** — Floor-to-ceiling windows, bathtub with karst views
- **Xitang River View** — Simple but has a river-view upgrade option
- **Yangshuo Percent Hotel** — Central with panoramic rooftop views
- **LIANGSU Hotel** — Rural setting with infinity pool, Instagram-popular
- **Banyan Tree Yangshuo** — Luxury, exclusive riverside setting
- **Yangshuo Ancient Garden Boutique Hotel** — Heritage property in a forest setting

## Fuli Bridge (富利桥)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Distance from Yangshuo** | ~20 minutes by scooter/DiDi |
| **Cost** | Free entry |
| **Best time** | Early morning (author visited 8 AM and had the bridge to themselves) |
| **Parking** | Nearby car park available |

Ancient stone bridge famous for its perfect circular reflection in the water below. **Author calls this her favorite view in the entire Guilin region.** Nearby: bamboo rafting hub, photo costume rental shops, small food vendors.

## Xianggong Mountain Sunrise (相公山日出)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Distance from Yangshuo** | 40–50 minutes by car |
| **Entry fee** | ¥60 |
| **Hike to platform** | 15-minute steep walk |
| **Wake-up call** | ~3 AM to catch sunrise |

Famous for dramatic sunrise views where low clouds fill the valley between karst peaks, creating an ethereal layered scene.

**Author's honest verdict:** "Probably not" worth the 3 AM alarm — the viewing platform was extremely crowded, and her particular sunrise was underwhelming. However, she acknowledges it could be spectacular with ideal weather (low cloud layer + clear sky above). **Tips:**

- Lower viewing platforms are significantly less crowded with similar views.
- Multiple cafes along the route offer views without the ¥60 entry fee.
- Arrive very early to claim a good spot on the main platform.

## Xingping Ancient Town (兴坪古镇)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Distance from Yangshuo** | ~45 minutes up the Li River |
| **Character** | Small, somewhat underwhelming town itself, but dramatic river viewpoints |

### 20 Yuan Note Viewpoint (二十元背景)

The most iconic view in the Guilin region — the exact landscape printed on China's ¥20 banknote. The observation point is extremely crowded. Light is better in the early morning or at sunset (late afternoon sun creates harsh, in-your-eyes glare).

**Bamboo raft photoshoots** available along the river for a small fee — scenic and worth considering.

### Restaurant: Yuelu Private Kitchens (岳麓私房菜)

**Author's best meal in the entire Guilin region.** Specifically recommends the pepper beef. Located in Xingping town.

## Wuzhi Hill, Cuiping (翠屏五指山)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Near Putao Town (葡萄镇), ~30 minutes from Yangshuo |
| **Entry fee** | ¥50 |
| **Hike** | Easy stepped path, ~15 minutes at a leisurely pace |
| **Best time** | Sunset |
| **Crowds** | Notably less crowded; author had it to themselves |

Panoramic viewpoint with multiple viewing levels offering different vantage points over karst peaks and river valleys. More remote and difficult to access — options include bus to Putao then scooter rental, or DiDi directly. **Author's strong recommendation** for a quieter, more peaceful alternative to Xianggong Mountain.

## Cafes with Karst Views Near Yangshuo

The author discovered numerous aesthetic cafes via Rednote app location tags, overlooking rice paddies and karst formations. **Finding method:** Search Rednote by location, tap navigation icon to get AMaps directions.

### Xiangyue Tingxiaoyuan Moon River Cafe

"Serene spot in a small village outside town" with mystical landscape views over karst peaks. Author's top cafe pick.

### Mr Zhang's

Nearby the Moon River Cafe; popular spot with drinks, sweet treats, and (randomly) sausages.

### Zhizi Huaxia / Under the Gardenias (栀子花下)

South of Yangshuo with a full food menu. **Warning:** Author ordered a mysterious chicken dish that was inedible — stick to drinks and simpler items.

## Longji / Longsheng Rice Terraces — Dazhai (龙脊梯田 — 大寨)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | 2+ hours north of Guilin city |
| **Entry fee** | ¥80 per ticket (valid 48 hours, covers both Ping'an and Dazhai areas) |
| **Recommended duration** | 2–3 nights minimum; author felt one night was insufficient |
| **Two areas** | Ping'an (平安) and Dazhai (大寨) — Dazhai is larger; author's guide focuses here |

### Best Seasons for Photography

| Season | Terraces look like |
|--------|--------------------|
| **Mid-May to mid-June** | Water-filled terraces reflecting sky and clouds (mirror effect) |
| **Summer (July–August)** | Lush green landscape |
| **September–October** | Golden rice ready for harvest — most striking, most photographed |
| **Winter** | Post-harvest stubble; avoid |

### Getting to the Rice Terraces

**From Guilin:** 2.5-hour bus journey; no obvious international-friendly online booking. Hotels or tourist offices in Guilin can arrange.

**From Yangshuo:** Direct buses; two daily in high season (8:00 AM and 1:30 PM); journey ~3.5 hours including a 30-minute toilet break stop in Guilin. Hotel can arrange tickets at ¥120 per person (likely includes a markup but is convenient). Entry tickets (¥80) are separate.

**From Dazhai parking lot to village:** ~40 minutes on foot. Free hotel pickup is possible if arranged in advance. Walking is fine if baggage is light.

### Accommodation: Xia Tiruijing Homestay

Author's choice in Dazhai village:
- Large bedroom with bathtub overlooking the terraces
- Free pickup from Dazhai parking lot (arrange in advance)
- Simple included breakfast
- On-site restaurant serves meals throughout the day (author notes food quality is unremarkable but convenient in rainy weather)
- Walking distance to two of the three main viewpoints
- Near village cafes and restaurants

### Three Main Terraces / Viewpoints

1. **Large Scale Thousand Layer Terraces (千层天梯)** — near Dazhai village hotels, generally quieter
2. **West Hill Music (西山韶乐)** — also near hotels, similar crowd level
3. **Golden Buddha Peak (金佛顶)** — connected to the parking lot by cable car; most accessible for day-trippers and therefore the most crowded

Viewpoints 1 and 2 are within walking distance of Dazhai village accommodations and are significantly quieter than Golden Buddha Peak. Signage is in English but sometimes confusing (signs pointing different directions to the same place). Hikes are straightforward for anyone with base fitness. More challenging trails connect Dazhai to Ping'an and lead up to Golden Buddha Peak.

**Author's favorite activity:** Simply sitting and taking in the views from the terraces near the village — no hiking required for a memorable experience.

## Practical Tips

- **Crowds are unavoidable.** The Guilin region is extremely popular with domestic Chinese tourists. Expect selfie sticks, drones, and camera crews at every major viewpoint. Accepting this reality makes the trip more enjoyable.
- **Go early.** Early morning visits (before 8 AM) dramatically reduce crowds and improve photographic light at most viewpoints.
- **Book transport locally.** Li River cruises, bus tickets to the rice terraces, and other transport are significantly cheaper when booked through local agents or hotels versus online platforms.
- **Use Rednote (小红书) for local discovery.** The app is invaluable for finding hidden cafes, restaurants, and viewpoints that don't appear on English-language travel sites. Use location tags and the navigation link to AMaps.
- **Pack light layers and hiking shoes.** Weather can shift; terrain at viewpoints and rice terraces is uneven.
- **Bring mosquito repellent.** Especially for rice terrace areas.
- **Allow more time than you think.** Author spent six nights and regretted not extending. Transit between areas (Guilin, Yangshuo, Xingping, Longji) eats into sightseeing time.
- **Little reliable online information exists** for this region in English. The author notes much of what she found beforehand was "ambiguous or just straight up wrong." Local advice and Rednote are more reliable than English-language blogs.
- **Sunset at Wuzhi Hill (Cuiping) over Xianggong Mountain sunrise** — author's strong recommendation if choosing between the two; far less crowded and equally dramatic.
