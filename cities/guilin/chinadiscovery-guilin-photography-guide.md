## Top Photography Locations

### 1. Xianggong Hill (相公山) — The Crown Jewel
The most celebrated landscape photography spot in all of Guilin:
- **What you see:** Elevated panorama of Li River S-bend winding through karst peaks
- **Best for:** Sunrise; sea-of-clouds mornings after rain create floating mountain effect
- **Season:** Apr-Nov; Oct mornings often deliver mist + golden light combo
- **Timing:** Arrive before 5:30 AM for position; sunrise ~6:00-6:30 AM in October
- **Access:** 1.5 hrs from Guilin city, 40 min from Yangshuo; entrance fee required
- **Gear:** Wide-angle for panorama; medium telephoto for isolating peak clusters
- **Tip:** "Strings of glows" illuminating karst formations with floating sea of clouds creates the signature shot

### 2. Yellow Cloth Shoal, Xingping (黄布倒影)
The "back of the 20-yuan banknote" composition:
- **What you see:** Karst peaks perfectly reflected in calm Li River water
- **Best for:** Sunrise; still water before boat traffic begins
- **Season:** Year-round; spring mist and autumn golden light are best
- **Access:** River cruise, bamboo raft from Xingping, or river bank path
- **Gear:** Standard to medium telephoto; tripod for dawn exposures

### 3. Cormorant Fishermen on Li River (鸬鹚渔翁)
Vanishing tradition — increasingly staged but still iconic:
- **What you see:** Silhouetted fisherman on bamboo raft with cormorant birds, lantern-lit
- **Best for:** Sunrise and sunset; lantern light at dawn is the classic shot
- **Critical:** Few real fishermen remain — **must book 1+ month in advance** through local photography guides
- **Location:** Yellow Cloth Shoal area near Xingping
- **Gear:** Avoid slow shutter (moving subjects on water); tripod + torch essential
- **Cost:** Photography session fees apply (arranged through tour operators)

### 4. Longji Rice Terraces (龙脊梯田)
Winding curves of mountain terraces forming a giant landscape painting:

**Ping'an Viewpoints:**
- Seven Stars around the Moon (七星伴月) — sunrise/sunset
- Nine Dragons and Five Tigers (九龙五虎) — sunrise/sunset

**Dazhai/Jinkeng Viewpoints:**
- West Hill Music (西山韶乐) — premium sunrise spot, highest at 1,180m
- Golden Buddha Peak (金佛顶) — panoramic
- Thousand-Layer Terraces (千层梯田) — sweeping curves

**Seasonal Photography Calendar:**
| Period | Terraces | Color |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Mid Apr - mid Jun | Water-filled, mirror reflections | Silver/blue |
| Mid Jun - mid Sep | Growing rice | Lush green |
| Late Sep - early Oct | Harvest time | Gold |

**Gear:** Tripod essential for sunrise/sunset; torch for pre-dawn access; wide-angle dominates

### 5. Cuiping Five Fingers Hill (翠屏五指山)
Sunset photography paradise:
- **What you see:** Karst pinnacles backlit against sunset sky, countryside foreground
- **Best for:** Sunset; Apr-May is peak, but Oct provides golden light
- **Location:** Near Putao County, Yangshuo area
- **Timing:** 4:00-5:00 PM on sunny days
- **Technique:** Shoot into backlight for dramatic silhouettes

### 6. Yulong River (遇龙河)
Quieter alternative to Li River with similar karst compositions:
- **What you see:** Ancient stone bridges, bamboo rafts, water buffalo, karst reflections
- **Best for:** Morning reflections before raft traffic; golden hour
- **Season:** Apr-Nov
- **Highlight:** Fuli Bridge creates near-perfect circle reflection in calm water
- **Gear:** Wide-angle for bridge + reflection; standard for countryside scenes

## Hidden Photography Gems

### Guzhuang Village (古壮寨)
Less commercialized Zhuang minority village:
- Picturesque wooden houses, narrow alleys, peaceful atmosphere
- Authentic daily life scenes — farming, cooking, weaving
- Combine with Longji Rice Terraces visit

### Jinglong Bridge (景龙桥)
Upper reaches of Yulong River:
- Ancient stone bridge stretching across river
- Surrounded by karst hills
- Few tourists know about it — uncrowded

### Daxu Ancient Town (大圩古镇)
2,000-year-old town 18 km from Guilin city:
- Flagstone streets, Qing Dynasty architecture
- Sharp contrast between ancient town and modern Guilin
- Best in morning light; local life scenes

### Laozhai Hill, Xingping (老寨山)
Sunset alternative to Xianggong Hill:
- 30-min steep hike with rope-assisted sections
- Panoramic Li River and Xingping town views
- Fewer photographers than Xianggong
- Free entry

## Photography by Season (October Focus)

### October Conditions
- **Karst peaks:** Clear autumn air, golden afternoon light, occasional morning mist
- **Rice terraces:** Peak golden color at Longji (harvest Oct 10-25)
- **Rivers:** Moderate water levels, good reflections, autumn color beginning
- **Sunrise:** ~6:00-6:30 AM; cooler mornings increase chance of mist/clouds
- **Weather:** Generally dry with occasional rain — rain enhances mist shots

### Camera Settings Guide
| Scenario | Aperture | Shutter | ISO | Notes |
|----------|----------|---------|-----|-------|
| Karst panorama | f/8-11 | 1/125+ | 100-200 | Maximize sharpness |
| Sunrise/mist | f/8-11 | 1/30-2s | 100-400 | Tripod required |
| Cormorant fisherman | f/2.8-4 | 1/250+ | 800-1600 | Fast shutter for moving subjects |
| Rice terrace curves | f/8-11 | 1/60+ | 100-400 | Polarizer reduces glare on water |
| Night river reflections | f/4-5.6 | 2-10s | 400-800 | Tripod + remote shutter |

## Ethnic Minority Subjects

### Huangluo Yao Village (黄洛瑶寨)
Women with world's longest hair (avg 1.8 meters):
- Red costumes, silver ornaments
- Hair-washing ceremony with rice water
- Traditional dance performances
- Year-round access at base of Longji terraces

### Ping'an Zhuang Village
Traditional tilted wooden houses against mountainside:
- Daily life: farming, cooking, weaving
- Zhuang clothing, songs, traditional festivals

## Practical Tips

- **Guilin weather changes fast** — always carry rain gear; post-rain mist creates best shots
- **Li River: avoid midday** — harsh light flattens the karst formations; dawn and dusk only
- **Tripod is non-negotiable** for sunrise/sunset at every location
- **Torch/headlamp essential** for pre-dawn access to hilltop viewpoints
- **Polarizing filter** recommended for reducing glare on water surfaces and enhancing sky contrast
- **Drone note:** Drones increasingly restricted in scenic areas; check current regulations
- **Local photography guides** can arrange cormorant fisherman sessions and access to off-trail viewpoints
