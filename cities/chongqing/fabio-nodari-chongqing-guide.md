# Travel and Photography Guide to Chongqing — China's Cyberpunk City

**Source:** Fabio Nodari (fabionodariphoto.com), travel photographer based in China. Published Nov 14, 2025. Updated Jan 19, 2026. All first-hand experience. Fabio calls Chongqing his favorite city in China alongside Shanghai.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Direct-controlled municipality in southwestern China, at confluence of Yangtze and Jialing Rivers. Separated from Sichuan Province in 1997.
- **Population:** 30+ million. Known as "Fog City" (雾都) and China's "Cyberpunk City."
- **Key landmarks:** Hongya Cave, Raffles City, Liziba Station, Shibati, Jiefangbei.
- **Best time:** Spring (Mar–May) and Autumn (Oct–Nov). Avoid Jul–Sep (one of "Three Furnaces" of Yangtze, 35°C+). Weekend visits best — Saturday drone show, lights on only weekends.
- **Typical stay:** 2 full days in city + 1 day trip. Hotels relatively cheap vs Shanghai.
- **Where to stay:** Jiefangbei area (northern tip of peninsula).

## Getting There

One of China's best-connected cities. Six train stations — Chongqing East is newest and one of the world's largest. Use Trip.com for booking.

## Daytime: Luohan Temple (罗汉寺)

One of the oldest and most photogenic temples in Chongqing, built during the Song Dynasty. Hundreds of Arhat statues with unique expressions and poses, plus intricate Buddhist carvings. Metro Line 1 or Line 6 to Xiaoshizi Station (小什字站), Exit 6, then 5-minute walk. Traditional hanfu dress rentals and photographer services available nearby. Very close to Jiefangbei.

## Daytime: Jiefangbei District (解放碑)

Main square marked by the People's Liberation Monument (人民解放纪念碑). Captures the spirit of modern Chongqing, especially lit up at night. Nearby: Chongqing Art Museum / Guotai Arts Center (重庆国泰艺术中心) — architecture inspired by Chinese chopsticks. Chaodonglu Street (朝东路) — iconic photography spot with Raffles City towers compressed against warehouse workers. Dongshuimen Bridge (东水门长江大桥) — cablecar crossing river with apartment buildings backdrop. Huguang Guild Hall (重庆湖广会馆) — Qing Dynasty merchants' meeting place, beautiful for drone photography against bridge and Raffles City.

## Daytime: Raffles City (来福士广场)

Chongqing's most famous building at the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers. Designed by Moshe Safdie (same architect as Marina Bay Sands in Singapore — hence similar look). Skybridge is second-highest in world after Kingdom Center in Riyadh. Observation deck available, but best view of skyline is from the opposite bank.

## Daytime: Kuixing Tower (魁星楼) and Liziba Station (李子坝)

**Kuixing Tower:** Went viral because you think you're on ground floor but you're actually on the 20th floor. Quick photo stop. Walk to Linjiangmen subway (临江门站, Line 2) to reach Liziba.

**Liziba Station (李子坝):** The train passes directly through middle floors of a residential building. Building and station were constructed together — not a retrofit. One of most photogenic subway scenes in the world.

## Daytime: Hong'en Pavilion (鸿恩阁) and The Ring (重庆光环购物公园)

**Hong'en Pavilion:** Multi-story wooden pavilion in traditional Chinese style. Beautiful at night (lights usually after 20:00). Hong'en Park surrounds it — quiet, good for photography.

**The Ring:** Viral indoor garden similar to Singapore. Small library on 3rd floor (only good in photos, like most scenic libraries in China). Only worth visiting if creating social media content — far from city center with nothing else in the area.

## Food: Hot Pot in an Air-Raid Shelter

Chongqing was China's wartime capital during WWII. From 1938–1943, heavy Japanese bombing led to thousands of tunnels being dug into hillsides. Many repurposed today as cafes, museums, or restaurants. Restaurant 红九格老火 (解放碑防空洞店) — super cool location but food is "just mid." Go for the experience, not the food quality.

## Sunset: Lotus Mountain (重庆莲花山)

Bars and BBQ restaurants on the mountain with fantastic view of Jiefangbei district skyline. Reach by taxi then short hike. Food/beverages overpriced but you're paying for the view. Perfect drone spot for capturing the entire district.

## Sunset: Huangjueya Old Street (黄桷垭老街)

Fabio's "secret" favorite spot for drone photography. Late Qing Dynasty area with well-preserved traditional architecture. Once a bustling stop for Yangtze River merchants. Narrow stone-paved streets with teahouses and snack shops. Quiet — great for a peaceful afternoon. Don't miss Sanmao coffee shop (三毛印记咖啡馆). Without a drone, probably not worth the trip.

## Night: Hongya Cave (洪崖洞)

One of Chongqing's most iconic places. Built into hillside, wooden buildings inspired by traditional stilt houses (diaojiaolou, 吊脚楼). At night, warm golden illumination makes it look like a fantasy movie set. Must-see even though touristy. Best photographed from the opposite side of the river (park with pedestrian area).

## Night: Shibati (十八梯) and Xiahao Lane (下浩里)

**Shibati:** "Eighteen Steps" — famous old neighborhood reconstructed with restored wooden façades and new commercial spaces. Metro: Jiaochangkou Station (较场口站).

**Xiahao Lane (下浩里):** Nan'an District, southern bank of Yangtze. Stepping into 1930s Chongqing — narrow alleys, vintage buildings, old shop signs. Fewer tourists than Hongya Cave. Houses converted to stylish cafes and boutique shops. Every corner perfect for photography, no drone needed.

## Night: Hugui Shihua Steps Park (呼归石花阶公园)

Best spot for the Saturday drone show. Best area for skyline photographs. Surprisingly not too busy. Excellent restaurants overlooking the bay. No drones allowed (too close to airport).

## Day Trip: 816 Nuclear Town (816地下核工程)

Massive underground complex built during the Cold War for China's nuclear weapons program. Labyrinth of tunnels, reactor halls, and preserved machinery. Top secret for decades. HSR from Chongqing North to Fuling North Station (30–40 minutes), then taxi. English-speaking guide usually available at ticket office — highly recommended for historical context.

## Day Trip: Wulong Karst National Park (武隆)

UNESCO South China Karst World Heritage site. Dramatic karst landscape with caves, natural bridges, gorges, and sinkholes. Highlights: Three Natural Bridges (天生三桥) — three limestone arches over deep valleys; Furong Cave (芙蓉洞); Fairy Mountain (仙女山). Train from Chongqing to Wulong South (武隆南站, 1 hour), then taxi to tourist center. Massive park — easily a full day. Can combine 816 + Wulong in one day (tight, need driver).

## Practical Tips

- **VPN:** Essential. ExpressVPN and NordVPN don't work in China.
- **Weather:** Often foggy/overcast. Creates moody atmosphere good for photography. Don't expect blue skies.
- **Stairs:** Be prepared for endless walking up and down hills the entire visit.
- **Drone:** Must register before coming to China. Some areas restricted (near airport). Saturday evenings best for city lights.
- **Weekend:** Visit on weekends for drone show and illuminated landmarks.
