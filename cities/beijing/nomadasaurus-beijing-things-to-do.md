# 26 Awesome Things to Do in Beijing

**Source:** Nomadasaurus (nomadasaurus.com), long-term travel bloggers with extended time in Beijing. Updated 2026.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Subway:** 27 lines, max 9 RMB/journey, cards 20 RMB deposit (refundable). Ends 11 PM.
- **Best base:** Dongcheng district — historic heart, walkable to major sites
- **Currency:** RMB; WeChat Pay / Alipay essential
- **Language:** English rarely spoken; translation apps necessary
- **Top tip:** Book Forbidden City tickets online with passport number — sells out days ahead

## Major Landmarks

### Tiananmen Square (天安门广场)
World's seventh-largest public square. Daily flag ceremony at sunrise and sunset — the sunset viewing is particularly atmospheric with the Forbidden City lit up behind. Free entry but security screening required; carry passport. Metro: Tiananmen East (Line 1).

### Forbidden City / Palace Museum (故宫博物院)
Home to 24 emperors from 1368-1911. Palaces, courtyards, temples, gardens, and museums across a massive complex. **Tickets 60 RMB — must book online with passport.** Closed Mondays. Hours: 8:30-17:00 (Apr-Oct), 8:30-16:30 (Nov-Mar). Audio guide available. Allow 3-5 hours minimum.

### Great Wall (长城)
Multiple sections accessible from Beijing:
- **Mutianyu (慕田峪):** Well-restored, cable car, toboggan ride down. Good balance.
- **Jinshanling (金山岭):** Less crowded, excellent for hiking and photography.
- **Simatai (司马台):** Only section open at night. Adjacent to Gubei Water Town.
- **Gubeikou (古北口):** Wild, unrestored, for adventurous hikers.
- **Badaling (八达岭):** Most accessible but extremely overcrowded. Avoid.

Transport: Public buses from Dongzhimen or Deshengmen bus stations, or organized tours.

### Summer Palace (颐和园)
18th-century imperial retreat. 800 acres with Longevity Hill, Kunming Lake, 17-Arch Bridge. 60 RMB (Apr-Oct), 50 RMB (Nov-Mar). Metro: Xiyuan (Line 4). Allow 2-3 hours for a thorough visit.

## Temples & Cultural Sites

### Lama Temple / Yonghegong (雍和宫)
Spectacular Tibetan Buddhist temple. Three archways, five main halls, 18-meter Maitreya Buddha carved from a single white sandalwood tree. 25 RMB. Hours: 9:00-16:30 (Apr-Oct), 9:00-16:00 (Nov-Mar). Metro: Yonghegong (Lines 2 & 5).

### Confucius Temple & Imperial College (孔庙和国子监)
Where imperial examinations were held. 190 stelae inscribed with Confucian classics. Ethereal, contemplative atmosphere. 30 RMB. Near Yonghegong subway. Pair with Lama Temple.

### Temple of Heaven (天坛)
UNESCO World Heritage Site. Tai chi groups at dawn. Occasional "marriage markets" where parents post children's resumes for potential matches. Book tickets online to skip lines. 35 RMB (combined, Apr-Oct), 30 RMB (Nov-Mar). Metro: Tiantandongmen (Line 5).

### Marco Polo Bridge & Wanping Town (卢沟桥 + 宛平城)
Where the 1937 Japanese invasion of China began — bullet-riddled structures remain. 501 individually carved stone lions on the bridge. 20 RMB. Metro: Beijing West Station + Bus 309. A sobering and uncrowded historical site.

## Art, Markets & Modern Beijing

### 798 Art District (798艺术区)
Former electronics factory complex transformed into galleries, studios, and cafes. Contemporary Chinese and international art. Mostly free entry. Metro: Wangjing South (Line 14). 10:00-18:00. Good for a half-day browse.

### Panjiayuan Antiques Market (潘家园旧货市场)
Southeast Beijing's treasure trove. Vintage propaganda posters, jade, ceramics, calligraphy, Mao memorabilia, old photographs. **Haggling essential** — start at 30% of asking price. Best on weekends when full outdoor market operates. Metro: Panjiayuan (Line 10).

## Hidden Gems & Day Trips

### Cuandixia Village (爨底下村)
A 400-year-old medieval stone village in a valley, formerly a trading post. Preserved ancient houses built on slopes. About 1.5-2 hours from Beijing by car. Access via Pingguoyuan subway (Line 1) then minibus (30-50 RMB). Worth a full day trip.

### Xiangshan / Fragrant Hills Park (香山公园)
Northwest Beijing park with Xianglu Peak views. Famous for autumn red leaves (October-November). Zhao Temple and Azure Clouds Temple (10 RMB) within grounds. Popular sunrise spot. Metro: Xiangshan station.

### Fahai Temple (法海寺)
Stunning Buddhist frescoes from the Ming Dynasty — among the finest surviving examples in China. Guided fresco tour 100 RMB (grounds only 20 RMB). Closed Mondays. A true hidden gem away from tourist circuits.

### Tianyi's Mausoleum (田义墓)
Dedicated to a eunuch, with exhibits on the unusual role of eunuchs in imperial court life. 8 RMB. Hours: 9:00-16:00. Metro: Pingguoyuan (Line 1) + Bus 116/396. Quirky and unique.

## Practical Tips

- **Subway cards:** 20 RMB deposit, refundable. Or use Alipay/WeChat QR codes directly.
- **Bus announcements:** In English and Mandarin on major routes. Download Baidu Maps for route planning.
- **Taxi scams:** Use Didi (ride-hailing app) instead of flagging cabs near tourist areas.
- **Getting lost in hutongs** is "part of the experience" — embrace the maze.
- **Forbidden City strategy:** Enter at opening (8:30 AM) and head to the western or eastern side courts first — most tourists follow the central axis only.
- **Great Wall timing:** Arrive before 9 AM or after 3 PM to miss the tour bus crush.
- **October weather:** Peak season, beautiful autumn colors, but also National Day holiday crowds (Oct 1-7). Book everything far in advance.
