**Source:** Lost Plate (lostplate.com), food tour company operating in Beijing since 2014. Supplemented with Nomadette (nomadette.com). Updated 2026.

## Quick Facts

- **Signature dish:** Peking Duck — wood-fired, tableside slicing
- **Payment:** WeChat Pay / Alipay everywhere; cash at street stalls
- **Tipping:** Not expected
- **Meal times:** Lunch 11:00-13:30, dinner 17:30-21:00
- **Budget meal:** 25-50 RMB; mid-range: 80-150 RMB; Peking duck dinner: 200-400 RMB
- **Booking:** Walk-in for most; Forbidden City-view restaurants need early arrival

## Must-Try Beijing Dishes

### Peking Duck (Beijing Kaoya)

The city's defining dish. Whole duck roasted in wood-fired oven, skin lacquered and crispy, carved tableside. Eaten wrapped in thin pancakes with scallion, cucumber, and sweet bean sauce. Locals judge restaurants by skin crispness and fat rendering.

**Top picks:**
- **Siji Minfu (四季民福)** — Half duck 159 RMB, whole set 259 RMB. Imperial Palace branch at 11 Nanchizi Ave, Dongcheng. Hours 10:30-21:30. Wood-fired ovens visible from dining area; some tables have Forbidden City views. Go off-peak to avoid 1-2 hour waits.
- **Bianyifang (便宜坊)** — Oldest duck restaurant in Beijing (est. 1416). Closed-oven roasting method produces juicier meat.
- **Quanmantang Roast Duck** — Michelin-awarded. Rich, flavorful preparation.
- **Ziguang Yuan** — Crispy skin, tender meat. Locals rate highly; also does excellent beef baozi.

### Zhajiangmian (炸酱面)

Thick wheat noodles topped with fermented soybean paste, diced pork, julienned cucumber, and bean sprouts. Add chili oil and vinegar to taste. Hearty, savory, cheap (15-30 RMB). Found everywhere from hutong stalls to dedicated noodle shops. Best experienced at hutong courtyard kitchens in the Xisi North cluster.

### Hotpot (Huoguo)

Beijing-style uses clear bone broth (not Sichuan spicy), with paper-thin sliced mutton (shuan yangrou) as the star. Dip in sesame paste sauce with fermented tofu, chive flower, and chili oil.

- **Jubaoyuan Hotpot** — Top-rated among locals. Incredibly friendly staff.
- **Haidilao** — Chain but famous for service (free manicures while waiting).

### Jianbing (煎饼)

Beijing's breakfast crepe. Thin batter spread on circular griddle, egg cracked on top, crispy wonton cracker pressed in, folded with scallions, cilantro, and sweet/spicy sauces. 8-15 RMB. Available from morning carts citywide; best eaten before 10 AM.

## Restaurant Guide by Category

### Yunnan and Regional Chinese

**Little Yunnan (小云南)**
- Cuisine: Southeastern Chinese with Myanmar/Laos/Vietnam influences
- Location: No. 89 Di'anmen East Street, South Luogu Lane area
- Hours: 11:00-22:00 daily
- Signature: Yunnan beef with mint, grilled tilapia with lemongrass
- Atmosphere: Plant-filled interior; bilingual menus

### Imperial Vegetarian

**Yebozhai Imperial Vegetarian (叶钵斋宫廷素菜)**
- Specialty: Mushroom-focused cuisine; customizable oil/spice levels
- Location: Wudaoying Hutong, near Lama Temple Subway (Lines 2 and 5)
- Atmosphere: Statues, redwood furniture, water features
- Best for: Lighter, vegetable-forward meals in a meditative setting

### Sichuan and Spicy

**Chongqing Laojiexiao Noodles (重庆老街小面)**
- Specialties: Minced pork with smashed peas noodles; pan-fried potatoes
- Location: Andingmen branch, 103 Annei Ave
- Hours: 10:00-21:30 daily
- English menu available; brown sugar jelly dessert worth ordering

### Dumplings

**Manjie Dumpling House (满姐饺子)**
- Largest dumpling selection in Beijing; vegetarian options available
- Minimum order: 10 dumplings per variety (enough for sharing)
- Location: Dongsishitiao Yi, near Dongsishitiao Subway (Line 2, Exit D)
- Hours: 10:30-21:00 daily
- English menu; complimentary pickled garlic and vinegar

### Neo-Chinese Desserts

**Yuan Gu (元古本店)**
- Seasonal desserts blending Western and Chinese techniques
- Location: Shajing Hutong, South Luogu Lane
- Hours: 11:00-24:00 daily
- Rooftop hutong views; Instagram-popular

## Food Streets

### Guijie / "Ghost Street" (簋街)
Dongzhimennei Avenue. Beijing's late-night food strip. Famous for spicy crayfish and stewed liver. Neon-lit, raucous, open until 2-4 AM. Metro: Beixinqiao (Line 5).

### Niujie / Halal Quarter (牛街)
Xicheng District. Beijing's Hui Muslim neighborhood. Beef and mutton specialists. Niujie Food Street for authentic halal snacks. Metro: Caishikou (Line 4/7).

### Shichahai Food Street (什刹海)
Houhai area. Traditional food alongside bar street. Instant-boiled mutton, grilled meat, hutong snacks. Touristy but atmospheric. Metro: Shichahai (Line 8).

### Qianmen Street (前门大街)
Historic commercial street near Tiananmen. Mix of traditional stalls and modern shops. Tanghulu, fried dough twists, jianbing. Metro: Qianmen (Line 2).

## Practical Tips

- **Reservation apps:** Dianping is China's Yelp — use it to check ratings, photos, and wait times
- **Allergies:** Peanut oil and sesame are ubiquitous; gluten is in most noodles and dumpling wrappers
- **Vegetarian challenge:** "Vegetarian" dishes often contain oyster sauce or chicken stock; specify chun su for fully plant-based
- **Duck strategy:** Order duck first at mixed restaurants — it takes 45+ minutes to roast
- **Water:** Never drink tap water; bottled water 2-5 RMB everywhere
- **Spice levels:** Beijing food is milder than Sichuan/Hunan; ask for wei la for mild spice
