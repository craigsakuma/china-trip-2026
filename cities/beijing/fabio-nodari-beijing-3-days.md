# Beijing in Three Days — Travel Guide

**Source:** Fabio Nodari (fabionodariphoto.com), travel photographer based in China. Published Jan 21, 2025. Updated Feb 8, 2026. All first-hand experience.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Northern China, bordered by Hebei Province and Tianjin Municipality.
- **History:** Capital since the Yuan Dynasty (1271). Over 3,000 years of history. Political and cultural hub of China.
- **Population:** Diverse mix of Han Chinese and ethnic minorities.
- **Key landmarks:** Forbidden City, Tiananmen Square, Temple of Heaven, Summer Palace, Great Wall, historic hutongs, Bird's Nest (National Stadium), 798 Art District.

## Best Time to Visit

- **Spring (March–May):** Mild weather, 10–25°C, blooming flowers. Avoid first week of May (Labor Day holiday).
- **Autumn (September–November):** Pleasant weather, beautiful red foliage. More crowded. Avoid Mid-Autumn Festival and National Day holidays.
- **Winter:** Freezing. Not recommended.
- **Summer:** Scorching, humid, peak tourist season. Not recommended unless visiting mountainous areas like Tibet or Northern Yunnan.

## Where to Stay

Stay near a subway station, close to the Forbidden City. Beijing is huge — poor location means wasted commute time. Use Trip.com for flights, trains, and hotels in China (better selection and prices than Booking.com).

## Day 1: Tiananmen Square, Forbidden City, and Hutongs

### Tiananmen Square (天安门广场)

One of the world's largest public squares. Surrounded by the Monument to the People's Heroes, the Great Hall of the People, and the Mausoleum of Mao Zedong. Free entry but reservation required one day in advance via official website or WeChat mini program: `小程序://天安门广场预约参观/GZaBrCfoOzvhxTq`

### Forbidden City (故宫)

UNESCO World Heritage Site. Imperial palace of the Ming and Qing dynasties. Over 900 buildings with golden roofs, grand halls, and courtyards. One of the largest palace complexes in the world, built early 15th century.

**Ticketing (critical info):**
- Daily cap of 10,000 tickets — sells out fast.
- Tickets go on sale one week in advance at 20:00 Beijing Time.
- Closed on Mondays.
- Book immediately when tickets become available.
- **Tip:** If booking errors, remove the space between first and last names.
- Official website or WeChat mini app: `小程序://故宫博物院/I4FEe4eAFCafdja`
- **Backup option:** East Gate (Donghuamen 东华门) has a ticket booth for foreigners. Subway Line 8 to Jinyu Hutong Exit C (金鱼胡同地铁站-C西南口).
- Always bring your passport — checked multiple times.
- Alternative: Book a tour via GetYourGuide.

### Jingshan Park (景山公园)

Directly north of the Forbidden City. Artificial hill built from earth excavated for the palace moat. Short climb to the top yields the best panoramic view of Beijing — golden Forbidden City roofs stretching below. Ideal for photography, especially at sunset.

### Hutongs

Ancient narrow alleyways dating to the Yuan Dynasty (1271–1368). Traditional courtyard homes (siheyuan). Most have been destroyed in recent years for new development.

**Recommended hutong:** Nanluoguxiang (南锣鼓巷)
- **Getting there:** Line 6 or Line 8 to Nanluoguxiang Station (南锣鼓巷站), Exit E — arrives directly at the south end.
- Other options: Qianmen, Wudaoying.

## Day 2: Great Wall and 798 Art District

### Great Wall of China

Over 21,000 km across northern China. Built from 7th century BC to protect against invasions. Features watchtowers, battlements, winding mountain paths.

- **Badaling and Mutianyu:** Most popular sections near Beijing. Often overcrowded. Easy to reach.
- **Jinshanling (金山岭) — recommended:** Less crowded, more beautiful. Located in Hebei Province, about 3 hours from Beijing.
  - **Public transport:** Jinshanling Tourist Bus from Dongzhimen Bus Station. Departs 7:40, returns 16:00.
  - **Recommended:** Hire a driver, leave Beijing at night, arrive early morning for fewest crowds. Return to Beijing by noon.

### 798 Art District (798艺术区)

Former military factory complex transformed into Beijing's most vibrant cultural zone. Contemporary art galleries, experimental studios, cafes, design boutiques. Large-scale murals and art installations throughout industrial-style buildings.

**Getting there (not directly on subway):**
- Option 1: Line 14 to Wangjing South Station → cross street to Xibajianfang Station → Bus 403 → Dashanzi Road East Station.
- Option 2: Line 14 to Jiangtai Station, Exit A → Jiangtai Road North bus stop → Bus 401, 445, 988, or 991 → Dashanzi Road South Station.

## Day 3: Temples and Parks

### Temple of Heaven (天坛)

Vast imperial complex where Ming and Qing emperors prayed for good harvests. Built early 15th century by Emperor Yongle (same ruler who built the Forbidden City).

**Key structures:**
- Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests — three-tiered circular wooden building constructed entirely without nails.
- Circular Mound Altar — site of the most important ceremonies to honor heaven.

**Surrounding park:** Popular with locals practicing tai chi, group singing, playing badminton. Park opens 6:00 AM, temple area opens 8:00 AM. Early visit recommended.

**Getting there:** Line 5 to Tiantan Dongmen Station (天坛东门站), Exit A — directly at the East Gate.

### Summer Palace (颐和园)

Imperial garden built in the 18th century as a royal retreat. Centered around Kunming Lake and Longevity Hill. Elegant pavilions, painted corridors, bridges connecting scenic islands. One of the finest examples of classical Chinese landscape design.

**Getting there:**
- Line 4 to Beigongmen Station (北宫门站), Exit D — near the North Gate.
- Or Line 4 to Xiyuan Station (西苑站) — closer to the East Gate.

### Lama Temple / Yonghe Temple (雍和宫)

One of the most important Tibetan Buddhist temples in China. Still an active religious center. Originally a Qing dynasty royal residence, converted to a monastery in 1744. Architecture blends Han and Tibetan elements — golden roofs, decorated halls, courtyards. Houses an 18-meter Maitreya Buddha carved from a single piece of sandalwood, one of the largest wooden statues in the world.

**Getting there:** Line 2 or Line 5 to Yonghegong Station (雍和宫站), follow signs. A few minutes' walk.

### Beihai Park (北海公园)

One of China's oldest imperial gardens, over 1,000 years of history. Large central lake, walking paths, pavilions, historic temples. Jade Flower Island (琼华岛) at the center is crowned by the White Dagoba, a 17th-century Tibetan-style stupa.

**Getting there:** Line 6 to Beihai North Station (北海北站), Exit B. 5–10 minute walk to the north entrance.

## Practical Tips

- **Beijing Duck:** One of the best dishes in China — don't miss it.
- **VPN:** Get a VPN and/or eSIM before traveling to China. Foreign apps (Google, etc.) are blocked.
- **Passport:** Carry it at all times — required for ticket checks at major sites.
- **Day trip option:** Tianjin is 30 minutes by high-speed train. Home to the iconic Binhai Library.
- **Three days is the bare minimum.** You could easily spend a week without getting bored.
- Beijing has much richer history and cultural heritage than Shanghai, though Shanghai is more international.
