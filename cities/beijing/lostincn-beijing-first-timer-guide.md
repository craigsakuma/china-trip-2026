# Complete Beijing Travel Guide: Everything You Need to Know

**Source:** Lost in China (lostincn.com), China-focused travel blog. Updated 2025.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Language:** Mandarin; English rare outside international hotels
- **Currency:** RMB/CNY (元). $1 USD ~ 7.2 RMB
- **Payment:** WeChat Pay and Alipay dominate; link foreign credit card before arrival
- **Visa:** Required for most nationalities; 4-7 business days processing
- **Electricity:** 220V, Type A/C/I plugs — bring universal adapter
- **Water:** Do NOT drink tap water; bottled water everywhere (2-5 RMB)
- **Time zone:** CST (UTC+8), no daylight saving

---

## When to Visit

### Autumn (September-November) — Best Season
Locals call it Beijing's most beautiful time. Crisp air, golden ginkgo trees, fall foliage at Fragrant Hills. October averages 10-20C. Low humidity. This is when you should go.

### Spring (March-May)
10-25C, fewer crowds than summer. Occasional sandstorms in March-April. Cherry blossoms at Yuyuantan Park.

### Summer (June-August)
Intense heat (35C+), high humidity, peak tourist season. Sudden thunderstorms. Longest daylight hours for sightseeing.

### Winter (December-February)
Harsh cold (-10 to 0C) but crystal-clear skies and virtually no crowds. Frozen Kunming Lake at Summer Palace. Cheapest flights and hotels.

## Getting There & Around

### Airport to City
- **Capital Airport (PEK):** Express train 28 min to Dongzhimen (25 RMB). Taxi 80-120 RMB, 45-90 min depending on traffic.
- **Daxing Airport (PKX):** Newer, farther south. Express to Caoqiao station 19 min (35 RMB). Taxi 150-200 RMB.

### Subway (地铁)
Beijing's 27-line network covers 783 km. Fares 3-9 RMB per journey. Operates 5:00 AM - 11:30 PM. Download **Beijing Subway** app or use Alipay/WeChat mini-programs for QR code entry. Security screening at every station (carry passport).

### Taxis & Ride-Hailing
Base fare 13 RMB for first 3 km, then 2.3 RMB/km. Use **Didi** (China's Uber) — link international credit card. Avoid unlicensed taxis outside train stations.

### Bike-Sharing
Mobike, Hellobike, Meituan Bike. 1-2 RMB per 30 minutes via app. Excellent for hutong exploration. Flat terrain makes cycling easy.

### Within the City
Baidu Maps is essential (Google Maps doesn't work). Download offline maps. Bus system extensive but Mandarin-only announcements on most routes.

## Where to Stay

### Dongcheng (东城区) — Historic Heart
Closest to Forbidden City, Tiananmen, Temple of Heaven, Lama Temple. Walkable to major sites. Higher prices and crowds. **Best for first-timers with limited time.**

### Xicheng (西城区) — Balanced Choice
Houhai lakes, hutongs, Beihai Park. Mid-range pricing. Good mix of historic atmosphere and convenience. Less hectic than Dongcheng.

### Chaoyang (朝阳区) — Modern Beijing
798 Art District, Sanlitun nightlife, CBD. International restaurants, modern amenities. Less "authentic" but most comfortable for Western visitors.

### Hutong Homestays (胡同民宿)
Courtyard guesthouses inside actual siheyuan. Authentic but basic facilities (shared bathrooms common). Budget-friendly. Best booked via Airbnb or Trip.com.

## Must-See Attractions by Priority

### Tier 1 — Full Day Each

**Forbidden City (故宫)**
Home to 24 emperors, 1368-1911. 9,999 rooms across 180 acres. **Book tickets online with passport** — sells out days in advance. 60 RMB (Apr-Oct), 40 RMB (Nov-Mar). Closed Mondays. Enter south (Meridian Gate), exit north. Allow 3-5 hours minimum. Audio guide 40 RMB or use the Palace Museum app (free).

**Great Wall (长城)**
Multiple sections near Beijing. **Mutianyu (慕田峪):** Best balance of restoration and scenery, cable car available, 2 hours from city. **Jinshanling (金山岭):** Less crowded, great for hiking/photography, 2.5 hours. **Badaling (八达岭):** Most accessible but extremely crowded — avoid. **Gubeikou (古北口):** Wild/unrestored section, adventure hikers only.

**Temple of Heaven (天坛)**
UNESCO site. Tai chi groups at dawn. Massive park surrounding the prayer halls. 15 RMB (park only) or 35 RMB (combined). Metro: Tiantandongmen (Line 5). Go early morning for the best atmosphere.

### Tier 2 — Half Day Each

**Summer Palace (颐和园)**
800-acre imperial garden with Kunming Lake, Longevity Hill, 17-Arch Bridge, Marble Boat. 60 RMB (Apr-Oct), 50 RMB (Nov-Mar). Metro: Xiyuan (Line 4). Allow 2-3 hours.

**Tiananmen Square (天安门广场)**
World's seventh-largest public square. Flag ceremony at sunrise/sunset. Free but security screening required; carry passport. Metro: Tiananmen East/West (Line 1).

**Lama Temple / Yonghegong (雍和宫)**
Spectacular Tibetan Buddhist temple with five main halls and 18m Maitreya Buddha. 25 RMB. Hours 9:00-16:30. Metro: Yonghegong (Lines 2 & 5). Pair with nearby Confucius Temple.

### Tier 3 — 2-3 Hours Each

**798 Art District (798艺术区)** — Former factory complex, now galleries and cafes. Free entry to most. Metro: Wangjing South (Line 14). 10:00-18:00.

**Prince Gong's Mansion (恭王府)** — Best-preserved Qing aristocratic residence. 40 RMB. Xicheng, near Houhai.

**Confucius Temple & Imperial College (孔庙和国子监)** — 190 stelae with Confucian classics. 30 RMB. Near Yonghegong subway.

**Hutong Exploration** — Drum Tower district, Shichahai area, Nanluoguxiang. Free to wander. Half day minimum.

## Health & Safety

- **Insurance:** International health insurance essential; hospitals require upfront payment
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A/B recommended; routine shots up to date
- **Air quality:** Check AQI daily; bring N95 masks for bad days (AQI 150+)
- **Altitude:** Beijing is essentially sea level (43m)
- **Scams:** "Art student" and "tea ceremony" scams target tourists near Tiananmen and Wangfujing — politely decline unsolicited English speakers
- **Emergency:** 110 (police), 120 (ambulance), 119 (fire)

## Practical Tips

- **Forbidden City tickets** sell out 7+ days in advance in October — book immediately when window opens
- **Rush hours** 7-9 AM and 5-7 PM are brutal on the subway; plan around them
- **Comfortable shoes** are non-negotiable — you will walk 15-25 km daily
- **Tissue paper:** Carry your own; public restrooms rarely supply it
- **Squat toilets** are standard in public facilities; shopping malls and hotels have Western-style
- **Photos:** Sensitive areas (military buildings, some government zones) prohibit photography
- **Subway security:** Bags X-rayed at every station; liquids may be checked
- **Haggling:** Expected at markets (Panjiayuan, Silk Street); never in restaurants or shops with fixed prices
- **Great Wall timing:** Go early (before 9 AM) or late afternoon to avoid tour bus crowds
