# Meili Snow Mountain & Yubeng Village Trekking Guide (梅里雪山 & 雨崩村)

**Source:** China Discovery — chinadiscovery.com/yunnan/shangri-la/meili-snow-mountain.html

## Quick Facts

- **Chinese name:** 梅里雪山 (Méilǐ Xuě Shān) / Kawagebo (卡瓦格博)
- **Location:** Deqin County (德钦县), 176 km northwest of Shangri-La city
- **Highest peak:** Kawagebo (卡瓦格博峰), 6,740 m — never summited; sacred in Tibetan Buddhism
- **Peak count:** 13 peaks above 6,000 m
- **Significance:** One of the most sacred mountains of Tibetan Buddhism; the 13 peaks represent 13 Tibetan deities
- **Best viewing months:** October–May (dry season); October–November optimal
- **Avoid:** June–September (rainy season; clouds obscure mountain)
- **Drive from Shangri-La:** 3.5–4 hours (176 km)

## Viewing Points (No Trekking Required)

### Feilaisi Observation Deck (飞来寺观景台)
- **Altitude:** 3,470 m
- **What:** Premier viewpoint for all 13 peaks; eight white pagodas frame the vista
- **Sunrise:** The "Golden Summit" (日照金山) effect — Kawagebo illuminated in gold at dawn
- **Sunrise times:** January ~08:11; June ~06:29 (varies by season)
- **Lancang River Gorge:** 4,700 m drop visible from the deck
- **Tip:** Stay overnight at Feilaisi to catch sunrise; depart Shangri-La the afternoon before

### Wunongding Viewing Deck (雾浓顶观景台)
- **Altitude:** 3,500 m
- **What:** 13 white Tibetan pagodas; wider panoramic angle than Feilaisi
- **Character:** Less crowded; some say the broader view is superior

## Mingyong Glacier Trek (明永冰川) — Beginner Level

- **Distance:** 25 km round trip
- **Duration:** ~6 hours (half-day)
- **Altitude range:** 2,230–3,100 m
- **Difficulty:** Easy to moderate; good for those not attempting Yubeng
- **Route:** Park entrance → Taizi Monastery (太子寺, 8 km, ~3 hours) → Lotus Temple (莲花寺, 2.5 km more) → Glacier Observation Deck
- **Sightseeing bus:** Optional motorized transport for part of the route
- **What you see:** Retreating glacier tongue, monastery, mountain views

## Yubeng Village Trek (雨崩徒步) — Advanced

### Overview
- **Duration:** 3–4 days
- **Total distance:** ~60 km
- **Altitude range:** 2,650–4,500 m
- **Difficulty:** Medium to strenuous
- **Highlights:** Sacred Waterfall, Ice Lake, pristine alpine scenery, Tibetan village life

### Day-by-Day Itinerary

**Day 1: Feilai Temple → Xidang Spring → Nazongla Pass → Yubeng Upper Village**
- **Hiking time:** 6–7 hours
- **Route:** Transfer to Xidang Spring (西当温泉) trailhead; ascend through forest to Nazongla Pass (那宗拉垭口, ~3,700 m); descend to Yubeng Upper Village (雨崩上村, ~3,200 m)
- **Character:** Steady uphill through dense forest; pass is the high point of Day 1
- **Accommodation:** Basic guesthouses in Yubeng Upper Village

**Day 2: Upper Village → Xiaonong Base Camp → Ice Lake → Lower Village**
- **Hiking time:** ~7 hours
- **Route:** Climb from Upper Village to Xiaonong Base Camp; continue to Ice Lake (冰湖, ~4,000 m); descend to Yubeng Lower Village (雨崩下村)
- **Ice Lake:** Alpine glacial lake at the base of Kawagebo's subsidiary peaks; turquoise water surrounded by moraine
- **Character:** Strenuous; highest altitude day

**Day 3: Lower Village → Sacred Waterfall → Lower Village**
- **Hiking time:** ~5 hours round trip
- **Route:** Trek to Sacred Waterfall (神瀑, ~3,600 m) and return
- **Sacred Waterfall:** Holy site for Tibetan pilgrims; walk behind the falls for blessings
- **Character:** Moderate; spiritual highlight of the trek

**Day 4: Lower Village → Ninong Bridge (Exit)**
- **Hiking time:** ~4 hours
- **Route:** Descend through Ninong Gorge (尼农峡谷) to Ninong Bridge
- **Character:** Dramatic gorge trail; some exposed sections; beautiful riverside scenery
- **Transport:** Arranged vehicle at Ninong Bridge back to Feilaisi or Deqin

## Getting to Meili Snow Mountain

### From Shangri-La (香格里拉)
- **Distance:** 176 km
- **By bus:** 4 morning coaches from Shangri-La Coach Station to Deqin County (德钦); ~6 hours; then 20-min connection to Feilaisi
- **By private car:** 3.5–4 hours; more flexible for stops
- **Recommended:** Private car allows stops at scenic points en route

### En Route Attractions (Shangri-La → Meili)
- **First Bend of Jinsha River (金沙江第一弯):** Dramatic river oxbow
- **Baima Snow Mountain (白马雪山):** Pass at 4,290 m; highest road point
- **Dongzhulin Monastery (东竹林寺):** Active Tibetan Buddhist monastery

## Accommodation Near Meili

### Feilaisi Area (Sunrise Viewing)
- Stay here the night before to catch the "Golden Summit" sunrise
- **Options:** Deqin Teng Yue Hotel (4-star), Deqin Shard Homeland Hotel, Feilaisi Mingzhu Hotel
- **Character:** Basic but functional; book rooms with mountain-facing windows

### Yubeng Village
- Basic guesthouses with dormitory or private rooms
- Simple Tibetan meals available (noodles, rice, yak meat)
- No luxury accommodation; expect cold water and basic facilities
- **Book ahead** during October peak season

## What to Pack for Yubeng Trek

- **Hiking boots:** Ankle-high, broken-in, waterproof
- **Layers:** Base layer + fleece + waterproof shell; temperatures range widely
- **Down jacket:** Essential for Yubeng evenings and Ice Lake altitude
- **Rain gear:** Even in dry season, mountain weather is unpredictable
- **Headlamp:** Guesthouses may have limited lighting; early morning starts
- **Water purification:** Stream water available but treat it
- **Snacks and energy food:** Limited food options on trail
- **Oxygen cans:** 1–2 for Ice Lake day (above 4,000 m)
- **Sun protection:** SPF 50+, hat, sunglasses — extreme UV at altitude
- **Cash:** No ATMs in Yubeng; bring enough for 3–4 days of food and accommodation
- **Basic first aid:** Blister care, pain relief, altitude sickness medication

## Mule/Horse Hire

- Available at Xidang trailhead and within Yubeng for side trips
- Useful for Day 1 ascent if carrying heavy pack
- **Cost:** Negotiate locally; typically CNY 200–400 per section
- **Tip:** Mules carry your pack, not you; you still walk

## Practical Tips

- **October–November is ideal** — clear skies, golden foliage, "Golden Summit" sunrise most likely
- **Acclimatize first** — spend 1–2 days in Shangri-La (3,300 m) before trekking to 4,000+ m
- **"Golden Summit" is not guaranteed** — sunrise illumination depends on cloud-free conditions; stay 2 mornings at Feilaisi for better odds
- **Tibetan pilgrimage:** Walk clockwise around sacred sites and through the Sacred Waterfall
- **Ninong exit is more scenic** than returning via Xidang
- **No phone signal** in much of Yubeng — inform someone of your itinerary
- **Travel insurance:** Recommended for the Yubeng trek; nearest hospital is in Deqin
- **The mountain is unconquered** — Chinese and Japanese climbers died in a 1991 expedition; all future climbing attempts are banned as the mountain is too sacred
