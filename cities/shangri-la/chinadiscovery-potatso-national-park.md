# Potatso / Pudacuo National Park (普达措国家公园) — Shangri-La

**Source:** China Discovery — chinadiscovery.com/yunnan/shangri-la/pudacuo-national-park.html

## Quick Facts

- **Chinese name:** 普达措国家公园 (Pǔdácuò Guójiā Gōngyuán)
- **Status:** China's first national park meeting IUCN standards; part of UNESCO World Heritage Site (Three Parallel Rivers of Yunnan)
- **Location:** 23 km east of Shangri-La city
- **Area:** 1,300 sq km
- **Altitude range:** 3,500–4,159 m
- **Entrance fee:** CNY 258 (CNY 138 entrance + CNY 120 mandatory shuttle bus)
- **Boat rides:** CNY 50 per lake (optional)
- **Hours:** April–October 08:00–17:00; November–March 08:00–16:00
- **Time needed:** 4–6 hours
- **Best months:** April–November; peak beauty June–October

## Three Main Scenic Areas

### Shudu Lake (属都湖) — Currently Open

- **Altitude:** 3,595 m
- **Lake size:** 1.2 sq km
- **Boardwalk trail:** 3.3 km (1–2 hours hiking)
- **Boat option:** CNY 50; 10–15 minutes across the lake
- **What you see:**
  - Highest tectonic lake in Yunnan Province
  - Swamped meadows and marshlands
  - Primeval dark coniferous forest (fir, spruce)
  - Rare double-lipped golden fish (重唇鱼) — endemic species
  - Livestock grazing on meadows creating pastoral scenes
  - Crystal-clear water reflecting surrounding peaks
- **Trail character:** Flat wooden boardwalk through forest and lakeside; easy walking
- **Photography:** Reflections best in early morning; meadow views best in afternoon light

### Militang Pasture (弥里塘牧场) — Closed for Maintenance

- **Altitude:** 3,700 m
- **Size:** 2.4 sq km
- **Status:** Closed since September 2017; no confirmed reopening date
- **Normal viewing time:** 10–15 minutes from shuttle bus stop
- **What it is:** Long, narrow alpine meadow with wetlands, brooks, and grazing yak herds
- **Flowers bloom** through September when open

### Bita Lake (碧塔海) — Check Current Status

- **Altitude:** 3,539 m
- **Lake size:** 1.4 sq km
- **Boardwalk trail:** 4.2 km (2–3 hours hiking)
- **Boat option:** CNY 50; 15 minutes
- **Status:** Was also closed for maintenance since 2017; check current situation before visiting
- **What you see (when open):**
  - Emerald-colored alpine lake surrounded by dense forest
  - May–July phenomenon: "Fish Drunk in Azalea" (杜鹃醉鱼) — azalea petals fall on water, fish eat them and float to surface in a drunken state
  - UNESCO World Heritage component
  - Richer biodiversity than Shudu Lake

**Important note:** As of last confirmed reports, only Shudu Lake is fully accessible to tourists. Verify Bita Lake and Militang status before visiting.

## Park Route and Logistics

### Shuttle Bus System
- **Mandatory** — private vehicles not allowed inside the park
- Shuttle included in CNY 258 ticket
- Route runs clockwise: Entrance → Shudu Lake → Militang Pasture → Bita Lake → Exit
- Total road distance: ~69 km with shuttle
- Buses run continuously; board at designated stops

### Typical Visit Flow
1. **Entrance gate:** Purchase ticket, board shuttle
2. **Shudu Lake (1.5–2 hours):** Hike boardwalk or combine with boat
3. **Shuttle to Militang (currently closed):** View from bus window if accessible
4. **Shuttle to Bita Lake (2–3 hours if open):** Hike boardwalk or boat
5. **Shuttle to exit**

## Seasonal Guide

| Season | Conditions | Experience |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Spring (Apr–May)** | Cool, budding vegetation | Fresh alpine recovery; fewer tourists |
| **Summer (Jun–Aug)** | Warm days, rain possible | Wildflower blooms; lush green meadows; "Fish Drunk in Azalea" at Bita Lake (if open) |
| **Autumn (Sep–Nov)** | Clear skies, golden foliage | **Peak beauty** — blue sky, emerald water, golden leaves, yellow grassland |
| **Winter (Dec–Mar)** | Below 0 C, snow | Snowy fairyland; very cold; fewer visitors; some trails may close |

## Flora and Fauna

### Vegetation
- Primeval dark coniferous forests (fir, spruce, larch)
- Alpine meadows and wetlands
- Over 100 species of azaleas (杜鹃花)
- Wildflowers carpet meadows June–September

### Wildlife
- Rare double-lipped golden fish (endemic to Bita Lake)
- Wild boars, bears (rarely seen)
- Various bird species in wetland areas
- Grazing yak and horse herds on meadows

## Altitude and Health

- **Entire park is above 3,500 m** — altitude sickness precautions essential
- **Oxygen bottles:** Buy before entering the park — unavailable inside
- **Down jacket rental:** Available at park entrance if needed
- **Prevention tips:**
  - Walk slowly on boardwalks; don't rush
  - Stay hydrated; avoid alcohol the night before
  - Consider boat rides to reduce physical exertion
  - If symptoms worsen (severe headache, vomiting), return to shuttle immediately
- **Temperature:** Park is colder than Shangri-La city; weather changes rapidly at altitude

## What to Bring

- **Warm layers:** Temperature significantly lower than town even in summer
- **Rain gear:** Weather changes quickly at 3,500+ m
- **Comfortable walking shoes:** Boardwalks are flat but can be wet/slippery
- **Sun protection:** High altitude = intense UV; hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
- **Oxygen cans:** 1–2 per person (purchase in Shangri-La before visiting)
- **Water and snacks:** Limited food options inside the park
- **Camera:** Landscape photography paradise; bring wide-angle lens

## Practical Tips

- **Go early** — arrive at park opening for the best light and fewer crowds
- **Autumn is the best season** (Sep–Nov) for the most dramatic colors
- **Check current closure status** of Bita Lake and Militang before visiting
- **Budget a full morning** — even with only Shudu Lake open, the boardwalk and shuttle take 3–4 hours
- **Combine with:** Songzanlin Monastery (morning) + Potatso (afternoon), or dedicate a full day
- **Dress in layers** — you may start warm and get cold quickly at altitude
- **Save energy:** Take the boat across Shudu Lake one-way, walk back on boardwalk
