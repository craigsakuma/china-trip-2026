# Songzanlin Monastery (松赞林寺) — Detailed Guide, Shangri-La

**Source:** Composite — chinadiscovery.com, wanderinchina.com, travelchinaguide.com, chinaxiantour.com

## Quick Facts

- **Chinese name:** 噶丹·松赞林寺 (Gādān Sōngzànlín Sì) / also: 归化寺 (Guīhuà Sì)
- **Tibetan name:** Ganden Sumtseling Monastery
- **Nickname:** "Little Potala Palace" (小布达拉宫) — resembles Lhasa's Potala
- **Founded:** 1679 (completed 1681), by order of the 5th Dalai Lama
- **Sect:** Gelugpa (Yellow Hat) school of Tibetan Buddhism
- **Monk capacity:** 1,600+ monks for ceremonies
- **Location:** 5–8 km north of Shangri-La city center, on Foping Hill (佛屏山)
- **Altitude:** ~3,300 m
- **Entrance fee:** CNY 115 (includes sightseeing bus from tourist center)
- **Hours:** 08:00–18:00 daily (varies seasonally)
- **Time needed:** 2–3 hours (monastery); add 1 hour for sacred lake

## History

### Founding
- During the Ming Dynasty, a small Nyingma Sect (Red Hat) temple existed on the site
- In 1679, the Fifth Dalai Lama ordered construction of a grand Gelugpa monastery
- Named "Ganden Songzanlin" — linking it to Ganden Monastery in Tibet
- Completed in 1681; became the largest Tibetan Buddhist monastery in Yunnan Province

### Historical Milestones
- Renamed "Guihua Temple" (归化寺) during the Yongzheng reign (early 18th century)
- Flourished under the Seventh Dalai Lama during Emperor Qianlong's era
- Survived periods of destruction and rebuilding through centuries
- Remains an active monastery with resident monks today

## Architecture and Layout

### Approach
- 146 steep stone steps climb from the base of Foping Hill to the main halls
- Fortress-like exterior walls with golden roofs visible from across the valley
- Modeled after the Potala Palace in Lhasa in stepped, terraced design

### Main Halls

**Zhacang Hall (扎仓大殿)**
- One of two primary assembly halls at the center of the complex
- Used for major religious ceremonies and teachings

**Jikang Hall (吉康大殿)**
- Second primary assembly hall
- Together with Zhacang, forms the spiritual heart of the monastery

**Main Assembly Hall (大经堂)**
- Contains 108 imposing wooden pillars
- Frescoed walls depicting Buddhist narratives and deities
- Yak butter lamps burning continuously
- Gilded Shakyamuni Buddha statue (~8 m tall) at main altar
- Accommodates 1,600+ monks for chanting sessions
- Ornate altar sculptures and thangka paintings

### Eight Colleges (八大康参)
- Jaya, Duke, Dongwang, Rongba, Xiangcheng, Gro, Yangtang, and Jidi Khamstsen
- Surround the main halls; each serves different monastic functions
- Monks' living quarters in adjacent buildings

### Religious Artifacts
- Palm-leaf Buddhist scriptures (贝叶经)
- Thangka paintings (唐卡) — intricate Buddhist scroll paintings
- Yak butter sculptures created for festivals
- Bronze censers, prayer wheels, and ritual implements

## Sacred Lake: Lamuyangcuo (拉姆央措)

- **Also called:** "Soul of Heaven Fairy Lake" (天女之魂湖)
- **Location:** At the base of the hill, in front of the monastery
- **Circumambulation path:** 2,110 m (40 min–1 hour, clockwise)
- **What to see:** Monastery reflections in the water; prayer flags; local pilgrims
- **Photography spots:**
  1. Near Conggulong Village — monastery + lake panorama
  2. Center of the lake path — closest reflections
- **Best time:** Early morning for still-water reflections and sunrise light

## Visiting Etiquette

### Dress Code
- Cover shoulders and knees; no shorts, sleeveless tops, or revealing clothing
- Remove hats when entering halls and chapels
- Dark, modest colors preferred; avoid bright/flashy outfits

### Behavior
- Walk clockwise through the monastery (following Tibetan Buddhist tradition)
- Enter halls via the left side, exit via the right
- Do not point at Buddha statues with fingers — use an open palm gesture
- Speak quietly; no shouting or loud conversations
- No smoking inside the monastery grounds
- Do not touch religious objects, thangkas, or butter sculptures

### Photography
- Exterior photography freely permitted
- **Photography is forbidden inside the chapels and halls**
- Ask permission before photographing monks or pilgrims
- No flash photography anywhere inside buildings

## Festival Calendar

### Gedong Festival (格冬节)
- **When:** December 26–29 (Tibetan calendar; typically January/February in Western calendar)
- **What:** Largest annual religious festival at Songzanlin
- **Highlights:**
  - Masked dances (跳神) representing horses, cattle, sheep, crows, and yaks
  - Mysterious ritual performances by monks
  - Yak butter lamp displays
  - Devout pilgrims from across the Tibetan Plateau
- **Experience:** Rare insight into living Tibetan Buddhist traditions; highly recommended if timing aligns

### Butter Lamp Festival
- Elaborate yak butter sculptures displayed and lit
- Usually coincides with Tibetan New Year (Losar)

## Getting There

- **From Shangri-La Old Town (Dukezong):** ~5 km north
- **Taxi:** 15–20 minutes; CNY 15–20
- **Bus No. 3:** From downtown to monastery station (~40 min), then 2 km walk
- **Bicycle:** Possible but uphill; ~30 min
- **By car:** Direct to tourist center parking lot
- **Note:** Sightseeing bus from tourist center to monastery entrance is included in ticket

| Season | Conditions | Notes |

| **Spring (Mar–May)** | Mild, flowers beginning | Good visiting weather |
| **Summer (Jun–Aug)** | Warm, rainy season | Clouds and rain; monastery in mist |
| **Autumn (Sep–Nov)** | Clear skies, golden light | Best overall; coincides with harvest |
| **Winter (Dec–Feb)** | Cold, possible snow | Festival season (Gedong); dramatic atmosphere |

## Practical Tips

- **Allow 2–3 hours** for a thorough visit including the sacred lake walk
- **Wear comfortable shoes** — 146 steps plus uneven surfaces
- **Dress warmly** — 3,300 m altitude means cool temperatures even in summer; bring a jacket
- **Altitude awareness:** Take the steps slowly; rest if short of breath
- **Sunrise photography:** Arrive at 07:00 for golden light on the golden roofs (best from Conggulong Village)
- **Sunset photography:** ~20:00 in summer; monastery silhouetted against western sky
- **Combine with:** Napa Lake (纳帕海, 8 km away) and Dukezong Old Town
- **Guided tours:** Available through tour operators; helpful for understanding Buddhist iconography
- **Respect:** This is an active place of worship — treat it as a religious site, not just a tourist attraction
