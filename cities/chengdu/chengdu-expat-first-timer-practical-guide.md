# Chengdu First-Timer Practical Guide

**Source:** Chengdu-Expat.com (chengdu-expat.com/chengdu-travel-guide-useful-information/) with supplementary detail from China Discovery and The China Travel. Practical logistics for first-time visitors covering transport, payments, language, health, and daily life.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Capital of Sichuan Province (四川省), southwestern China. Elevation ~500 m.
- **Population:** ~21 million (metro area). China's 4th largest city by GDP.
- **Language:** Mandarin Chinese plus Sichuanese dialect (四川话). English is rare outside international hotels.
- **Currency:** Chinese Yuan (CNY/RMB). ¥1 ≈ $0.14 USD (2026).
- **Time zone:** China Standard Time (UTC+8). No daylight saving.
- **Typical visit:** 2-3 days for city sights; 5-7 days including day trips (Leshan, Emei, Dujiangyan).
- **Best for:** Food lovers, panda enthusiasts, history/culture travelers, photographers.

## Getting to Chengdu

### Airports
- **Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU):** Primary international airport, opened 2021. ~50 km from city center. Metro Line 18 connects to downtown (~50 min). Taxi ¥150-200.
- **Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU):** Older airport, still handles some domestic and international flights. Metro Lines 10/19 to downtown (~40 min). Taxi ¥50-80.
- **Which to fly into:** Check your specific flight. TFU handles most new routes; CTU still operates many domestic connections.

### Trains
Chengdu is a major high-speed rail hub. Key connections:
- Chongqing: 1.5-2 hours (frequent daily)
- Xi'an: 3-4 hours
- Kunming: 5-6 hours
- Beijing: 7-8 hours
- Shanghai: 8-10 hours

Main stations: **Chengdu East** (成都东站, most high-speed trains) and **Chengdu South** (成都南站). Book via 12306 app or Trip.com.

## Getting Around Chengdu

### Metro (地铁)
Extensive subway network with 12+ lines covering all major attractions. Clean, efficient, bilingual signage (Chinese/English). Runs ~06:30-23:00 daily. Fares ¥2-10 per trip. Buy tickets at station vending machines (accept cash, Alipay, WeChat Pay) or get a **Transportation Union Card** (交通联合卡) valid across multiple Chinese cities.

### Key Metro Connections
- Panda Base: Line 3 to Panda Avenue → Panda Bus D025
- Kuanzhai Alley: Line 4, Kuanzhai Alley Station
- Jinli/Wuhou Shrine: Line 3, Gaoshengqiao Station
- Chunxi Road/Taikoo Li: Line 2 or 3, Chunxi Road Station
- People's Park: Line 2, People's Park Station

### Taxis & Ride-Hailing
- **Didi** (滴滴): Chinese Uber. English version available. Reliable and cheap (¥15-40 for most urban trips).
- **Taxis:** Abundant. Base fare ~¥8. Drivers rarely speak English — have your destination written in Chinese.
- **Traffic:** Chengdu has significant congestion during rush hours (07:30-09:30, 17:00-19:00). Metro is faster.

### Cycling
Shared bikes (Meituan, Hello) available throughout the city. Scan to unlock with Alipay/WeChat. Flat terrain makes cycling practical. ¥1-2 per trip.

## Payment & Money

### Mobile Payment (Essential)
China runs almost entirely on mobile payments. **Alipay** and **WeChat Pay** are accepted everywhere — restaurants, metro, taxis, street vendors, temples, even beggars. Set up at least one before arrival:
- **Alipay:** International version accepts foreign credit cards. Download and link a Visa/Mastercard before your trip.
- **WeChat Pay:** Requires Chinese bank account OR can be activated with a foreign card through Tour Pass feature.

### Cash
Carry some small bills (¥10, ¥20, ¥50) as backup. A few small street vendors, rural areas near day trips, and older establishments still prefer cash. ATMs widely available (ICBC, Bank of China accept foreign cards).

### Credit Cards
Major international hotels and upscale restaurants accept Visa/Mastercard. Most everyday establishments do not. Do not rely on credit cards alone.

## Communication & Internet

### SIM Cards
Purchase a Chinese SIM card at the airport or any China Mobile/Unicom/Telecom store. Requires passport. Data plans are cheap (¥50-100/month for generous data). This gives you a Chinese phone number needed for many apps.

### VPN
China blocks Google, Gmail, WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, Twitter/X, YouTube, and most Western social media. Download and configure a VPN before arriving. Popular options: ExpressVPN, Astrill, NordVPN. VPN reliability varies — have a backup.

### Essential Apps
- **Alipay / WeChat:** Payment
- **Didi:** Ride-hailing
- **Baidu Maps or Amap (高德地图):** Navigation (Google Maps works poorly in China)
- **Pleco:** Chinese-English dictionary with OCR camera translation
- **Trip.com (携程):** Train/flight/hotel booking
- **12306:** Official China railway ticket app
- **Translate apps:** Google Translate (works offline with downloaded Chinese pack), Apple Translate

## Where to Stay

### Best Neighborhoods for Tourists
- **Chunxi Road / Taikoo Li (春熙路/太古里):** Central, walkable, metro-connected. Shopping, dining, nightlife. Modern hotels.
- **Kuanzhai Alley area (宽窄巷子):** Historic charm, teahouses, close to People's Park. Boutique hotels and hostels.
- **Anshun Bridge / Jiuyanqiao (安顺桥/九眼桥):** River views, nightlife, bar street. Mid-range to upscale hotels.
- **Near Wuhou Shrine / Jinli (武侯祠/锦里):** Cultural district, night market access. Budget to mid-range.

### Budget
- Hostels: ¥50-150/night
- Mid-range hotels: ¥300-600/night
- Upscale: ¥800-2,000/night
- International chains (Marriott, Hilton, IHG) are well-represented.

## Weather & What to Pack (October)

### October Conditions (Our Trip Window)
- **Temperature:** 15-22°C (59-72°F) daytime, 12-16°C (54-61°F) nighttime.
- **Rain:** Occasional drizzle. Chengdu is one of China's cloudiest cities (250-300 overcast days/year).
- **Humidity:** Moderate. Less humid than summer.
- **Sunlight:** Limited. The joke is that Chengdu dogs bark at the sun because they see it so rarely.

### Packing List
- Light layers (jacket + t-shirt system). No heavy winter coat needed in October.
- Compact umbrella or rain jacket (essential).
- Comfortable walking shoes (you will walk a lot, especially at panda base and day trips).
- Portable battery pack (phone is your wallet, map, translator, and camera).
- Sunglasses (for occasional sunny breaks and Emei Mountain).
- Motion sickness medication (if doing Emei Mountain shuttle bus — very winding roads).

## Health & Safety

### Air Quality
Chengdu has moderate air pollution. Winter months (Nov-Feb) are worst — PM2.5 can exceed 150. October is usually acceptable. Check the **Smart Air app** or US Consulate Chengdu air quality readings. Bring KN95 masks if sensitive.

### Water
Do not drink tap water. Bottled water is cheap and everywhere (¥2-3). Hotels provide free bottled water and/or electric kettles.

### Hospitals
Several hospitals offer English-speaking services for foreigners. Global Doctor Chengdu Clinic and Chengdu First People's Hospital international department are reliable options.

### Food Safety
Street food is generally safe — look for busy stalls with high turnover. Spice tolerance is the real health concern for visitors. Build up gradually and have digestive remedies on hand.

### Safety
Chengdu is extremely safe for tourists. Violent crime against foreigners is virtually unheard of. Standard precautions: watch for pickpockets in crowded tourist areas (Chunxi Road, Jinli), keep valuables secure. Scam risk is low compared to other major Chinese cities.

## Visa & Registration

### 240-Hour Visa-Free Transit
Travelers from 54 countries (including US, Canada, UK, Australia, most of EU) can enter Chengdu visa-free for up to 240 hours (10 days) when transiting to a third country. The clock starts at midnight the day after arrival. Valid at both Tianfu (TFU) and Shuangliu (CTU) airports. Travelers can now visit 10 additional Sichuan cities beyond Chengdu: Leshan, Ya'an, Deyang, Suining, Meishan, Ziyang, Neijiang, Zigong, Yibin, and Luzhou.

### Police Registration
Foreigners must register at the local police station within 24 hours of arrival. Hotels do this automatically. If staying at an Airbnb or private residence, you must register in person at the nearest police station with your passport and host's ID.

## Practical Tips

- **Pace:** Chengdu's culture is famously relaxed. Locals spend hours in teahouses. Embrace the pace — do not try to rush through everything.
- **Tipping:** Not customary in China. No need to tip at restaurants, taxis, or hotels.
- **Plugs:** China uses Type A (US-style flat prongs) and Type I (Australian-style angled). Most modern hotels have universal outlets. Bring a universal adapter to be safe.
- **Laundry:** Most hotels offer same-day laundry service. Budget hotels may have coin-operated machines.
- **Photography:** Chinese people are generally comfortable with photography in public spaces. Ask permission before photographing individuals up close.
