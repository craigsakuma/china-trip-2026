# Chengdu, China: Giant Panda Breeding Research Base & Budget Backpacker Guide

**Source:** Dariece Swift (goatsontheroad.com), long-running couple travel blog, lived in China. Published July 2012, updated December 2022. Budget-focused, first-hand experience.

## Quick Facts

- **Province:** Sichuan (四川)
- **Getting there:** 25-hour train from Guilin; most people fly but budget travelers take the train
- **Train classes:** "Hard seater" (90-degree angle, minimal padding) and "hard sleeper" (3-bunk system, like India's SL class but cleaner)
- **Language:** Hardly any English spoken outside hostels; have directions written in Mandarin by hostel staff

## Giant Panda Breeding Research Base (成都大熊猫繁育研究基地)

The authors' main reason for visiting Chengdu. Located just outside the city, reachable by 2 local buses in about an hour from the city center.

**The experience:**
- Calls itself a Wildlife Reserve, not a zoo. Authors found it "a pretty good reserve, not too zoo-like, but it could have been a bit better for the bears"
- Enclosures have trees for climbing, grass, and bamboo structures for lounging. Could be larger but are not terribly small
- Pandas are bred here but cannot be reintroduced into the wild (teeth not strong enough for wild bamboo, reluctant to mate)

**Highlights:**
- Group of 7 adolescent pandas eating and lounging "as if they'd just finished off a turkey dinner"
- A rambunctious cub who tumbled down hills, jumped on his mom, and awkwardly climbed a tree to reach his napping brother
- Red Pandas (小熊猫) — look more like raccoons than bears, equally cute; fewer than 1,000 wild pandas remain

**Practical details:**
- Skip the hostel tour; go independently by local bus (2 buses, ~1 hour)
- Allow a few hours for the full visit

## Wenshu Temple (文殊院)

Large, well-preserved Buddhist temple reachable by subway. Massive complex with parks, turtles, frogs, monasteries, monks, and metal urns burning incense. Great place to roam for a while. Surrounding backstreets have red lanterns, old Chinese architecture, and shops.

## Tea House Culture

Traditional Sichuanese afternoon activity. The authors had green tea in a local tea house. An unusual local service is offered: a man with a metal wire, a feathered paintbrush, and a headlamp performs ear cleanings. Authors opted out ("didn't seem sanitary or safe").

## Sichuan Hot Pot (四川火锅)

Famous spicy Sichuanese cuisine. Tables have built-in burners; a boiling vat of spicy oil bubbles at your table. You choose skewers from a cooler room — beef, pork, chicken, broccoli, cauliflower, sprouts, pumpkin, and more. "A pretty fun and interactive way of eating."

- **Cuisine style:** Very spicy, full of chillies
- **How it works:** Select skewers from cooler room, cook them in the bubbling spicy oil at your table

## Accommodation

**Sim's Cozy Guesthouse** — "Quite possibly the best hostel we've ever stayed in." Amenities include:
- Bar and restaurant serving amazing Sichuanese food (authors ate almost every meal there)
- 2 garden areas with tables and chairs
- Games room with pool table and ping pong table
- Travel desk with comprehensive information
- Staff speak great English and are knowledgeable about the city and country

## Getting Around Chengdu

- **From train station:** Local bus ~30 minutes, then transfer to another bus. Language barrier makes navigation difficult
- **Within city:** Subway system connects major sights (Wenshu Temple, hot pot restaurants)
- **Tip:** Have hostel staff write directions in Mandarin to show bus drivers/taxi drivers

## Practical Tips

- **Language:** "There is hardly any English spoken in China, literally next to zero most of the time." Hostel staff are the exception — leverage them heavily
- **Train travel:** Hard sleeper is preferable to hard seater for long journeys. Top bunks have least headroom and are closest to loud intercoms. Chinese passengers smoke between carriages and smoke wafts through
- **Budget strategy:** Eat at the hostel restaurant if the food is good (Sim's Cozy was excellent); skip organized tours and go independently to the Panda Base
- **Cultural notes:** Be prepared for doorless public toilets at some train stations; children's split pants and public urination are common and unremarked-upon by locals
- **Onward travel:** Overnight train to Xi'an (next logical stop on a south-to-north itinerary)
