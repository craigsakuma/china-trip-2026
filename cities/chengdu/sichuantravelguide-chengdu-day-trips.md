# Chengdu Day Trips — Leshan Giant Buddha, Mount Emei & Dujiangyan

**Source:** Sichuan Travel Guide (sichuantravelguide.com/leshan/) with supplementary detail from China Discovery and China Odyssey Tours. Covers the three major UNESCO World Heritage day trips from Chengdu.

## Quick Facts

- **Three UNESCO sites** within day-trip range of Chengdu: Leshan Giant Buddha, Mount Emei, Dujiangyan-Qingcheng Mountain.
- **Leshan:** 130 km south, ~45 min by high-speed rail or ~2 hours by car.
- **Mount Emei:** 150 km south, ~1.5 hours by high-speed rail. Often combined with Leshan.
- **Dujiangyan:** 60 km northwest, ~30 min by high-speed rail or ~1 hour by car.
- **Trip duration:** Leshan alone = half day. Leshan + Emei = 2 days. Dujiangyan + Qingcheng Mountain = full day.

## Leshan Giant Buddha (乐山大佛)

### Overview
The world's tallest pre-modern stone Buddha statue. A 71-meter (233 ft) Maitreya Buddha carved into the cliff face of Lingyun Mountain at the confluence of three rivers: the Min, Dadu, and Qingyi. Construction began in 713 AD (Tang Dynasty) and took 90 years to complete. UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1996.

### Dimensions
- Height: 71 meters (head to toe)
- Head width: 10 meters
- Ear length: 7 meters
- Nose length: 5.6 meters
- Each foot can seat 100 people
- A sophisticated hidden drainage system of channels in the hair, collar, chest, and behind the ears prevents water damage — an engineering marvel for its era.

### Visiting Options

**Walking Route (2-3 hours):** Descend the Nine-Turn Stairway (九曲栈道) carved into the cliff beside the Buddha to reach its feet. Steep and narrow — single-file in places. The stairway can have 1-2 hour queues on busy days. You see the Buddha from multiple angles: head level, mid-torso, and feet. This is the immersive, up-close experience.

**Boat Tour (1 hour):** View the Buddha from the river. Best for photography — you get the full-body frontal perspective that's impossible from the walking trail. No queuing for stairs. Economy boats lack air conditioning; comfort boats have it. Boats depart regularly from the dock.

**Combination:** Do the walking route first (morning, before queues build), then take the boat for the panoramic photo afterward.

### Getting There from Chengdu

**High-Speed Train (recommended):**
- Chengdu East Station → Leshan Station. Chengmianle Intercity Rail — ~45 minutes, frequent departures.
- From Leshan Station: bus or taxi (20-30 min) to the scenic area.

**Long-Distance Bus:**
- From Xinnanmen Bus Station or Shiyang Bus Station. ~2 hours.

**Private Car/Driver:**
- ~2 hours from downtown Chengdu or Shuangliu Airport. Convenient if combining with Mount Emei same day.

### Best Time to Visit
- Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) for mild weather and fewer crowds.
- **Avoid October Golden Week (Oct 1-7)** — extreme crowding, multi-hour queues for the stairway.
- October 10+ (our trip window) should be post-holiday and manageable.
- Mornings before 10:00 have shorter queues.

## Mount Emei (峨眉山)

### Overview
One of China's Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains, rising to 3,099 meters at the Golden Summit (金顶). Home to over 30 Buddhist temples spanning 2,000+ years of religious history. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Known for its biodiversity: 3,200+ plant species, Tibetan macaques, and subtropical-to-alpine climate zones within a single mountain.

### Key Attractions

**Golden Summit (金顶, 3,077 m)**
The highest accessible point. Features the Huazang Temple (华藏寺) and a 48-meter gold-plated Puxian Bodhisattva statue. On clear days, views extend to the snow peaks of western Sichuan. Famous for four natural phenomena: sunrise, sea of clouds, Buddha's Halo (rainbow ring shadow), and holy lamp (mysterious lights). Reached by cable car from Jieyin Palace.

**Wannian Temple (万年寺)**
One of Emei's most important temples, housing a 62-ton bronze Puxian Bodhisattva statue cast in 980 AD. Cable car access from Wannian parking area.

**Monkey Zone (猴区)**
Wild Tibetan macaques along the path between Qingyin Pavilion and Hongchun Ping. They are aggressive and will snatch food, bags, and cameras. Keep belongings secured and do not feed them (despite vendors selling "monkey food").

**Baoguo Temple (报国寺)**
The base-area temple and traditional starting point for the mountain pilgrimage. Houses a large porcelain Buddha.

### Logistics

**Getting There:** High-speed train from Chengdu East to Emeishan Station (~1.5 hours). Then taxi or local bus to the mountain gate.

**Route to Golden Summit:**
1. Take a shuttle bus from the scenic area entrance to Leidongping (雷洞坪) — about 2 hours by winding mountain road.
2. Hike from Leidongping to Jieyin Palace (接引殿) — ~30 minutes uphill walk.
3. Take cable car from Jieyin Palace to Golden Summit — ~5 minutes, ¥65 up / ¥55 down.

**Tickets:** Mountain entrance ¥160 (peak) / ¥110 (low season). Shuttle bus ¥90 round-trip.

**Duration:** Full day for Golden Summit route. 2-3 days for a thorough exploration including hiking between temples.

### Combining Leshan + Emei
- **Day 1:** Morning train to Leshan. Visit Giant Buddha (walking + boat). Afternoon train to Emeishan (30 min). Stay overnight at Emeishan town.
- **Day 2:** Golden Summit visit. Return to Chengdu by evening train.
- This is the most popular 2-day itinerary from Chengdu.

## Dujiangyan Irrigation System (都江堰)

### Overview
A 2,200-year-old irrigation system built in 256 BC by Li Bing (李冰) during the Qin Dynasty. Still in operation today — it irrigates over 5,300 square kilometers of farmland in the Chengdu Plain. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Often called the "ancestor of world water conservancy culture." The system works entirely by gravity — no dams, no pumps.

### Key Components
- **Fish Mouth Levee (鱼嘴):** V-shaped divider that splits the Min River into inner and outer channels.
- **Flying Sand Weir (飞沙堰):** Overflow dam that regulates water volume and flushes sediment.
- **Bottle Neck Channel (宝瓶口):** The final controlled entry into the irrigation network.

### Visiting
- **Tickets:** ¥80 (includes scenic area and Erwang Temple).
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours for the main scenic area.
- **Getting there:** High-speed rail from Chengdu to Dujiangyan Station (~30 minutes). Then bus or taxi to the scenic area.

### Qingcheng Mountain (青城山)
Often combined with Dujiangyan as a full-day trip. One of the birthplaces of Taoism. Front Mountain has Taoist temples and manicured paths. Back Mountain is wilder, with waterfalls and forest hiking. Front Mountain entrance: ¥80. Cable car: ¥60 round-trip. Half day for Front Mountain; full day for Back Mountain.

## Practical Tips

- **October timing:** Mid-October (our trip window) is ideal for all three sites — post-Golden Week, pleasant autumn weather, fall colors beginning on Mount Emei.
- **Altitude on Emei:** Golden Summit is 3,077 m. Mild altitude effects possible. Bring a jacket — temperatures drop 10-15°C from the base.
- **Leshan queues:** Visit the Nine-Turn Stairway before 10:00 AM or consider the boat-first approach if queues are already long.
- **Dujiangyan pandas:** The Dujiangyan Panda Base (中华大熊猫苑) is nearby and less crowded than the main Chengdu base. Can be combined for a panda + irrigation day trip.
- **Food near Leshan:** Local specialty is Leshan sweet-skin duck (甜皮鸭) — roast duck with a caramelized sweet-savory glaze. Available from street vendors near the scenic area.
- **Train booking:** Book high-speed rail tickets via Trip.com or 12306 app at least 2-3 days ahead. Trains fill up.
