# Chengdu Sichuan Food Guide — UNESCO City of Gastronomy

**Source:** China Discovery (chinadiscovery.com/sichuan/chengdu/food.html) with supplementary detail from The Food Ranger (thefoodranger.com). Comprehensive restaurant-level food guide to Chengdu's Sichuan cuisine scene.

## Quick Facts

- **UNESCO Status:** Chengdu designated a UNESCO City of Gastronomy in 2010 — first city in Asia to receive the honor.
- **Flavor Profile:** Sichuan cuisine (川菜) centers on málà (麻辣) — numbing from Sichuan peppercorns (花椒) plus heat from dried chilies. Seven flavors: hot, numbing, fresh, fragrant, savory, tender, crisp.
- **Key Ingredients:** Pixian chili bean paste (郫县豆瓣), Hanyuan peppercorns (汉源花椒), fermented black beans, rapeseed oil, garlic, ginger.
- **Budget:** Street snacks ¥10-30/person; sit-down meals ¥50-100/person; hotpot ¥80-120/person.
- **Spice Warning:** Even "not spicy" dishes often contain chili. Servers may resist removing spice — "it won't be tasty!" is a common reply.

## Hot Pot & Communal Dishes (火锅类)

### Chengdu Hot Pot (成都火锅)
The soul of Chengdu hotpot is its broth — pure beef tallow simmered with Pixian chili bean paste, fermented black beans, and Hanyuan peppercorns until the red oil rolls like lava. Order yuan yang pot (鸳鸯锅, half-spicy/half-mild) if spice-averse. **XiaoLongKan** (小龙坎), No. 15 Zhonglieci East Street, Jinjiang District — ¥110/person, 10:00-22:00.

### Maocai (冒菜) — Solo Hot Pot
Single-serving hot pot where the waiter cooks your selections and serves them in a bowl. No need for a dipping sauce station. Great for solo travelers. **FuRongShuXia** — ¥30/person.

### Chuanchuan Xiang (串串香) — Skewer Hot Pot
Ingredients strung on bamboo skewers, dipped into a shared communal pot. Pay by counting sticks. **MaoJiaoHuoLa** (冒椒火辣), #33 Kuixinglou Street, Qingyang District — ¥80/person, 11:30-14:00 & 17:00-22:00.

### Boboji (钵钵鸡) — Cold Pot Skewers
Cold version of chuanchuan: pre-cooked skewers sit in seasoned chili oil. A refreshing summer specialty. **Yepopo Boboji** (叶婆婆钵钵鸡), 1-19 Jianshe Lane, Chenghua District — ¥30/person.

### Fish Cold Pot (冷锅鱼) and Tofu Hot Pot (豆花火锅)
Fish cold pot features fresh river fish in room-temperature spicy broth — **SanZhiEr** (三只耳), 26 Fangcao Street, Hi-tech Zone, ¥70/person. Tofu hot pot is the mild-spice option — **LuZhouSuanCaiDouHua** near Sichuan University, ¥60/person.

## Signature Sichuan Dishes (经典川菜)

### Mapo Tofu (麻婆豆腐)
Silken tofu cubes simmered with Pixian chili bean paste, minced beef, and Hanyuan peppercorns. Invented in Chengdu in the 1860s. **ChenMaPo Tofu** (陈麻婆豆腐), 197 Xiyulong Street, Qingyang District — ¥30/person, 11:30-14:30 & 17:30-21:00. The original restaurant.

### Kung Pao Chicken (宫保鸡丁)
Diced chicken stir-fried with peanuts, dried chilies, and Sichuan peppercorn. Actually not particularly spicy — more savory-sweet. **HongXing Restaurant**, No. 7-9 Section 3 Jianshe North Road, Chenghua District — ¥100/person.

### Twice Cooked Pork (回锅肉)
Pork belly boiled then sliced and stir-fried with leek sprouts and Pixian bean paste. The essential Sichuan home-style dish. **MaWangZi Restaurant**, No. 1 Dongfurshi Street, Jinjiang District — ¥100/person.

### Sliced Beef & Offal in Chili Oil (夫妻肺片)
Cold dish of sliced beef, heart, tongue, and tripe in chili oil with peanuts and sesame. Name means "husband and wife lung slices" though no lung is used. **FuQiFeiPian Restaurant**, 2/F No. 23 Zongfu Road, Jinjiang District — ¥50/person, 10:30-21:30.

### Poached Fish in Chili Oil (水煮鱼)
Carp fillet poached in a sea of chili oil with bean sprouts. The oil is not for drinking — it cooks the fish. **QinShanZhai**, 247 Wuhouci Street, Wuhou District — ¥100/person.

## Noodles & Dumplings (面食饺子)

### Dan Dan Noodles (担担面)
Thin wheat noodles in chili oil, sesame paste, Sichuan peppercorn, minced pork, and preserved vegetables. Bright red color. Named after the carrying pole (担担) of street vendors. **Chunyangguan** is legendary for dan dan noodles and chili oil wontons. Also: **HuangChengBa Snack Restaurant**, No. 259-5 Wuhouci Street — ¥50/person, 09:30-21:30.

### Sweet Noodles (甜水面)
Thick chewy noodles in a salty-sweet sauce with chili oil and crushed peanut powder. Unique to Chengdu. **Dongzikou Zhang's Restaurant** — ¥20/person, 08:30-19:00.

### Feichangfen (肥肠粉) — Intestine Noodle Soup
Sweet potato glass noodles in rich pork intestine broth. Sounds intimidating but the flavor is earthy and deeply savory. **Bai's Feichangfen**, #35 Chengwu Building, Qingshiqiao North Street, Jinjiang District — ¥25/person, 08:30-22:00.

### Red Oil Dumplings (红油水饺)
Boiled dumplings swimming in chili oil with garlic and scallions. **ZhongShuiJiao**, No. 2-2 Wuhouci Street — ¥30/person, 07:00-21:00.

### Chaoshou (老麻抄手) — Sichuan Wontons
Similar to wontons but with thinner, silkier skin. Peppercorn numbness is the defining feature. **LongChaoShou**, #63 Chengshou Street, Jinjiang District (Chunxi Road branch) — ¥30/person, 09:00-22:00.

## Snacks & Desserts (小吃甜点)

### Iced Jelly (冰粉)
The essential spice-relief dessert. Translucent jelly topped with brown sugar syrup, peanuts, sesame, raisins, and sometimes rose petals. Order this alongside any spicy meal. **TianXinXianSheng**, No. 65 Xiadong Street, Jinjiang District — ¥20/person, 12:00-22:30.

### Egg Pancake (蛋烘糕)
Palm-sized crispy pancake with sweet or savory fillings. A classic Chengdu street snack. **He's DanHongGao**, Inside South Gate, No. 12 College Road, Wuhou District — ¥15/person, 11:00-13:00 & 15:00-20:00.

### Fried Sugar Dough Balls (糖油果子)
Deep-fried glutinous rice balls coated in brown sugar — crispy outside, chewy inside, bright golden color. Found at street stalls citywide.

### Spicy Rabbit Head (麻辣兔头)
Chengdu's most adventurous snack. Braised rabbit skulls split open and slathered in chili oil. Locals eat them casually with beer. **ShuangLiu Lao Ma Rabbit Head**, 22 Jinsi Street, Qingyang District — ¥50/person, 10:30-22:30.

## Key Food Neighborhoods

- **Yulin Road (玉林路):** Authentic local vibe, away from tourist crowds. Dan dan noodles, hotpot, small family restaurants.
- **Kuanzhai Alley (宽窄巷子):** Historic courtyards with street food stalls and teahouses. Tourist-oriented but photogenic.
- **Jinli Old Street (锦里):** Red-lantern-lit walking street near Wuhou Shrine. Sichuan snacks, cold rice pudding, spicy kebabs.
- **Jianshe Road Night Market (建设路夜市):** Near University of Electronic Science. Cheapest street food — spicy chicken feet, roast pig brain, baked hooves.
- **Dongzikou (东子口):** Noodle and snack specialty area. Sweet noodles, heartbroken jelly, dan dan noodles.

## Practical Tips

- **Vegetarian options:** Hotpot format naturally accommodates vegetarians (tofu, mushrooms, greens). Ask for "su de" (素的). Mapo tofu can be made with mushroom instead of beef at some restaurants.
- **Ordering spice level:** Wēi là (微辣) = mild spicy; zhōng là (中辣) = medium; tè là (特辣) = extreme.
- **Spice relief:** Order iced jelly (冰粉), sour plum juice (酸梅汤), or peanut milk alongside meals.
- **Payment:** Most restaurants accept WeChat Pay and Alipay. Small street stalls may be cash-only.
- **Peak hours:** Lunch 11:30-13:30, dinner 17:30-21:00. Arrive early or expect queues at popular spots.
