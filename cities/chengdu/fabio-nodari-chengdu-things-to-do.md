# Top Things to Do in Chengdu — Travel Guide

**Source:** Fabio Nodari (fabionodariphoto.com), travel photographer based in China. Published Jan 24, 2025. Updated Feb 8, 2026. All first-hand experience.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Capital of Sichuan Province (四川省), southwestern China.
- **Population:** ~14 million. Ancient history dating back thousands of years, now a massive tech hub and supposedly China's fashion capital.
- **Known for:** Giant pandas, Sichuan hot pot, Sichuan opera, historic temples, Tea Horse Road heritage.
- **Best time:** Spring and Autumn. Chengdu gets 250–300 foggy/cloudy/rainy days per year. Summer is hot and rainy. Winter not extremely cold but grey and foggy.
- **Typical stay:** 2–3 days for the city; longer if including day trips.
- **Where to stay:** Anshun Bridge area for best location. Hotel with river and bridge view recommended.

## Anshun Bridge (安顺桥)

One of Chengdu's most recognizable landmarks, first built during the Qing Dynasty. Rebuilt after flood destruction but retains traditional Chinese architectural style. At night the bridge is illuminated and its reflection on the Jin River is very photogenic. Surrounding area has shops, teahouses, and restaurants with river views. Best visited in the evening when lights are on and the riverfront feels relaxed.

## Kuanzhai Alley (宽窄巷子)

Three alleys — Kuan (Wide), Zhai (Narrow), and Jing — dating back several centuries. Gray brick walls and wooden doors with modern cafés, bars, and restaurants inside. A couple of hours is enough to walk through. Right in the city center, easy to reach. Mix of history and everyday life.

## People's Park (人民公园)

Built in 1911, covers 112,639 square meters. Gardens, a small lake, and a century-old teahouse: Heming Teahouse (鹤鸣茶馆). Great place to observe Chinese culture — old people singing, dancing, playing mahjong. City center location.

## Daci Temple (古大圣慈寺)

Historic Buddhist temple with an alleged 1,600-year history, rebuilt many times. Located next to Sino-Ocean Taikoo Li in the heart of Chengdu. A rare peaceful Buddhist sanctuary amid the city's skyscrapers — interesting contrast of ancient architecture against modern backdrop.

## Wuhou Shrine (武侯祠)

One of Chengdu's most important historical sites. Dedicated to Zhuge Liang, the brilliant strategist of the Shu Kingdom during the Three Kingdoms Period (220–280 AD) — remembered as politician, military mind, writer, engineer, and inventor. Also contains the Temple of Liu Bei (founding emperor of Shu) and his mausoleum. Ancient cypress trees, traditional halls, statues. Calm, park-like atmosphere. Booking a guide is worth it — the Three Kingdoms history is complex and a guide helps appreciate the significance.

## Jinsha Museum (金沙遗址博物馆)

Archaeological site accidentally discovered in 2001. Artifacts date to ~1000 BC. Exhibition halls with ivory (elephant tusks), jade jewels, bronze weapons, gold objects, and carved stones. Takes about 2 hours to visit. The site covers ~5 sq km. Subway Line 2 to Yipintianxia Station (一品天下站), Exit B, walk 1 km north to east gate. China's major 21st-century archaeological discovery.

## New Century Global Center (新世纪环球中心)

One of the world's largest buildings, completed 2013. Self-contained town with 2,000+ hotel rooms, indoor beach area over 1 km long, offices, conference rooms, university complex, two commercial centers, IMAX cinema, a "Mediterranean village," a pirate ship, and an Olympic-size skating rink. Twice the size of the previous record holder in Dubai. Subway Line 1 to Jincheng Square Station (锦城广场站).

## Sichuan Hot Pot and Food

Sichuan food is famous for insane amounts of chili peppers in almost every dish. The Sichuan hot pot is one of the best in China (if you like spicy). Challenging for those who don't eat spicy — even when asked, waiters often reply "it's not tasty!" and add chili anyway. For the adventurous: pig's brain hot pot. Chengdu's slower pace of life means people sit in teahouses for hours, play mahjong in parks, and take time with meals.

## Shu Feng Ya Yun Sichuan Opera (蜀风雅韵川剧)

Best venue in Chengdu for Sichuan opera. Performances include singing, marionettes, Chinese shadows, and acting. English introduction to each act, so non-Chinese speakers can follow. An important part of Sichuan's cultural heritage alongside hot pot and pandas.

## Giant Pandas: Chengdu Center vs Wolong (卧龙大熊猫自然保护区)

**Chengdu Panda Center:** Easy to reach by subway, but very crowded in summer. Some pandas moved indoors in summer. Best time: before or after summer, mornings when pandas are more active.

**Wolong Breeding Center:** About 2 hours from Chengdu. Much smaller and less crowded. Better for photos — nice mountain and forest background. Worth it only if time isn't a problem and/or you plan to explore Western Sichuan. Located en route to the mountains.

China's panda protection program has been so successful that since 2016, pandas are no longer endangered.

## Dujiangyan Zhongshuge Library (都江堰中书阁)

Part of the Zhongshuge bookstore chain, known for beautiful, creatively designed spaces. Located about 1.5 hours from Chengdu in Dujiangyan city. Combines modern architecture with traditional elements, but like most similar libraries in China, most books are fake — only good for Instagram photos. Worth visiting only if en route to Wolong Panda Center; otherwise a waste of time given the distance.

## Day Trip: Dujiangyan (都江堰) and Qingcheng Mountain (青城山)

**Dujiangyan:** Ancient irrigation system originally constructed around 256 BC by Qin governor Li Bing, still in use today. Instead of building a dam (which would block military vessels), Li Bing constructed an artificial levee to divert spring meltwater flow and cut a channel through Mount Yulei to direct excess water onto the dry Chengdu Plain. Several temples on site.

**Qingcheng Mountain (青城山):** One of China's most famous Taoist mountains, called "the most peaceful and secluded mountain under heaven" in Chinese poetry. Hike up (not difficult, mostly steps) and take a cable car. Taoist temples and pagodas along the way. Taoism emphasizes the relativity of truth and the good life, teaches self-reliance (unlike Buddhism's focus on alleviating suffering).

## Day Trip: Leshan Giant Buddha (乐山大佛)

HSR from Chengdu East Station to Leshan — about 1 hour. Short bus or taxi from station to entrance. Trains sell out quickly on weekends/holidays — book in advance.

The statue: 71 meters tall, carved into red sandstone cliffs where the Minjiang, Dadu, and Qingyi rivers meet. Largest stone Buddha in the world. Construction began in 8th century (Tang Dynasty), took ~90 years. Represents Maitreya (Buddha of the future). UNESCO-listed site with temples, pagodas, and pavilions. Narrow stairway carved into cliff descends from head to feet. Boat ride on river gives best complete view.

Plan at least half a day. Visit weekdays, go early morning. Very crowded during Chinese holidays.

## Shangli Old Town (上里古镇)

Small historic village on Chengdu's outskirts. Cobblestone streets, traditional wooden houses with tiled roofs, old stone bridges, waterways. Not overly commercialized — quieter and more authentic look at rural Sichuan life. Teahouses, local crafts, street food stalls, family-run restaurants. Works as a day trip or stop on the way back from Western Sichuan.

## Practical Tips

- **VPN:** Most Western social media and websites are blocked. Download a working VPN before arriving.
- **Weather:** Check forecasts carefully. Spring and autumn are best. Summer heat is "pretty unbearable."
- **Day trips:** Chengdu is the perfect base for road trips across the Tibetan Plateau. Consider Western Sichuan, Xining to Yushu, or Chongqing (2-hour train ride).
- **eSIM:** Recommended for maintaining connectivity.
- **Travel insurance:** Recommended for any China trip.
