# Xi'an Travel Guide: First-Time Visitor Essentials & Itinerary

**Source:** China Footprints — chinafootprints.com/xian-travel-guide-first-time-visitor-essentials-itinerary/

## Why Xi'an (西安) — Ancient Capital of 13 Dynasties

Xi'an, formerly Chang'an (长安, "Eternal Peace"), served as the capital of 13 Chinese dynasties and was the eastern terminus of the Silk Road. The city witnessed the rise and fall of the Zhou, Qin, Han, and Tang empires. Today it blends ancient heritage — city walls, pagodas, mosques — with a thriving modern food scene and creative art districts.

## Suggested 4-5 Day Itinerary

### Day 1 — City Culture and Street Food
- **Muslim Quarter (回民街):** Stroll the food streets, try roujiamo and biangbiang noodles
- **Bell Tower (钟楼) and Drum Tower (鼓楼):** Iconic Ming Dynasty towers at the city center
- **Xi'an City Wall (城墙):** Walk or cycle the 14 km circumference
- **Yongxingfang (永兴坊):** Alternative snack street east of the wall, less touristy than Muslim Quarter

### Day 2 — History and Heritage
- **Shaanxi History Museum (陕西历史博物馆):** One of China's top four museums. Free entry but book online in advance — tickets go fast
- **Small Wild Goose Pagoda (小雁塔):** 8th-century Tang pagoda in a peaceful temple complex
- **Big Wild Goose Pagoda (大雁塔):** Former Buddhist scripture repository built in 652 AD. Evening musical fountain show on the north square
- **Tang Paradise Night Street (大唐不夜城):** Tang Dynasty-themed pedestrian street with performers, light shows, and costumed visitors. Xi'an's "City That Never Sleeps"

### Day 3 — Imperial Sites (Half-Day Trip East)
- **Huaqing Palace (华清宫):** Imperial bathing palace with hot springs and Tang Dynasty history
- **"Song of Everlasting Sorrow" (长恨歌):** Evening outdoor performance at Huaqing Palace — one of Xi'an's best cultural experiences
- **Terracotta Army (兵马俑):** The world-famous warrior pits. Visit in the afternoon when morning tour groups have departed

### Day 4 — Art Districts and Hidden Xi'an
- **Banpo International Art Village (半坡国际艺术区):** Contemporary art galleries in converted industrial spaces
- **Old Steel Factory (老钢厂):** Creative hub with cafes, galleries, and design studios
- **Dahua 1935 (大华1935):** Converted cotton mill now housing theaters, restaurants, and cultural events

### Day 5 — Mountain Day Trip
- **Mount Huashan (华山):** One of China's Five Sacred Mountains, 120 km east. Take the high-speed train from Xi'an North Station (30 minutes, ~100 RMB). 53 daily trains with the last return at 21:48.

## Best Time to Visit

**Spring (March-May):** Warm and dry. Cherry blossoms bloom at temples and universities. Ideal.

**Autumn (September-November):** Cool and pleasant. Golden ginkgo trees and fall foliage. Equally ideal.

**Summer (June-August):** Temperatures reach 40C (104F). Avoid midday outdoor activities. Manageable if you plan around the heat.

**Winter (December-February):** Cold and windy with occasional snow. Lower hotel rates and ticket prices. Fewest crowds.

## Transportation Within Xi'an

**Metro:** 11 lines covering most attractions. Fares 2-8 RMB. Use Xi'an Metro Card or Alipay QR code. The metro is usually the fastest way between major sites — traffic jams are common on roads.

**Tourist Buses:**
- **Tourist Bus 8 (Route 610):** Connects major in-city attractions
- **Tourist Bus 5 (Route 306):** Reaches the Terracotta Army from Xi'an Railway Station. Use only official gray buses — ignore touts

**Bikes:** Public bike-sharing systems available via app (Hellobike, Meituan).

**Didi ride-hailing:** Widely used. Works through Alipay.

## Getting to Xi'an

**By air:** Xi'an Xianyang International Airport (XIY). Metro Line 14 connects to the city (~1 hour). Taxi ~100+ RMB.

**By high-speed train:** Xi'an North Station handles most bullet trains. Connected to metro Lines 2 and 14. Trains from Beijing (4.5 hours), Shanghai (6 hours), Chengdu (3 hours).

**Driving note:** License-plate restrictions apply weekdays — specific plate-ending numbers banned each day. Parking near historical sites is limited.

## Where to Stay

**Bell and Drum Tower area (钟鼓楼周边):** Best for first-timers. Walking distance to Muslim Quarter, city wall, and metro. Strong sense of history and local life inside the old walls.

**Big Wild Goose Pagoda area (大雁塔周边):** Good for families. Near the Shaanxi History Museum and Tang Paradise. More modern hotel options.

**Budget:** Winter offers lowest hotel rates. Budget hostels from ~100 RMB/night; mid-range hotels 300-600 RMB/night.

## Practical Tips

- **Guided tours:** Hiring a guide (~100 RMB) at the Terracotta Warriors is highly recommended for understanding the historical context
- **Morning strategy:** Visit Huaqing Palace first, then Terracotta Warriors in the afternoon to avoid the worst morning crowds
- **Museum reservations:** Shaanxi History Museum requires online booking — free tickets sell out quickly
- **Payment:** Alipay and WeChat Pay accepted everywhere. Carry some cash for small street food vendors
- **Language:** English is limited outside hotels and major attractions. Download offline Chinese translation
- **Water:** Tap water is not drinkable. Bottled water available everywhere for 2-3 RMB
