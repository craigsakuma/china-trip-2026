# Xi'an Ancient City Wall (西安城墙): Cycling, History & Visitor Guide

**Source:** China Highlights — chinahighlights.com/xian/attraction/ancient-city-wall.htm

## Historical Overview — From Tang Dynasty to Ming Fortress

Xi'an's city wall is the largest and best-preserved ancient city wall in China. Its origins trace to the Tang Dynasty (618-907), when Chang'an was the world's most populous city. The current fortification was built in 1370 during the Ming Dynasty under Emperor Zhu Yuanzhang, who followed his advisor Zhu Sheng's counsel: "Build high walls, store abundant provisions, and take your time in proclaiming yourself emperor." The city's name changed from Chang'an (长安, "Eternal Peace") to Xi'an (西安, "Peace in the West").

## Dimensions and Architecture

| Measurement | Value |
|------------|-------|
| Circumference | 14 km (8.7 miles) |
| Height | 12 meters (39 feet) |
| Width at top | 12-14 meters (wide enough for cycling) |
| Width at base | 15-18 meters |
| Watchtowers | Every ~120 meters ("horse faces" / 马面) |
| Moat | Surrounds the entire perimeter |

The wall features watchtowers approximately every 120 meters — called "horse faces" (马面) — providing vantage points for defenders and photographers alike. A moat encircles the entire structure. Parapets line both the inner and outer edges, with crenellations on the outer side for archers.

## The Four Main Gates (四大城门)

### South Gate — Yongning Gate (永宁门)
The grandest and most popular entrance. Features a complete defensive complex: drawbridge, sluice gate, arrow tower, and main tower. Faces Xi'an's central urban axis — a classic photo spot, especially at night when illuminated. Open until 10 PM. Most crowded but most accessible.

### East Gate (东门)
Fewer tourists than South Gate. Well-preserved architecture ideal for photography. Good starting point for cycling.

### West Gate (西门)
Similar advantages to East Gate — less crowded, good for cycling departure. Quieter atmosphere.

### North Gate (北门)
Closes earlier at 6 PM. Fewest visitors. Good for a quieter experience.

### Hanguang Gate (含光门)
The only gate with elevator access for wheelchair users. Adjacent to the Hanguang Gate Relic Museum, which displays the original Tang Dynasty Yinguangmen gate ruins — the oldest surviving section of the wall.

## Cycling the City Wall (骑行城墙)

The most popular activity. The flat, wide top of the wall provides a smooth cycling surface with panoramic views of both ancient and modern Xi'an.

**Duration:** A complete circuit takes approximately 1.5 hours by bike at a leisurely pace. Walking the full circuit takes 3-4 hours.

**Bike rental:** Available at all four main gates. Single bikes ~45 RMB/3 hours; tandem bikes ~90 RMB/3 hours. Deposit required.

**Best gates for cycling:** West, North, or East gates are recommended starting points — fewer tourists mean less congestion on the first stretch. South Gate is the busiest.

**Scenic highlights along the route:** Views alternate between the old city (temples, traditional rooftops) on the inside and modern Xi'an (skyscrapers, traffic) on the outside. Local residents practice tai chi and square dancing at the wall's base — visible from above.

## Walking Options

**South Gate to Hanguang Gate:** The recommended walking route heads westward through Zhuque Gate toward Hanguang Gate Relic Museum — traversing the oldest section of the wall. Takes approximately 30 minutes. Suitable for elderly visitors and children.

**Minor South Gate area:** A hotspot for social media content creators, with local snack vendors in the mornings.

## Best Times to Visit

**Spring / Autumn:** Optimal at ~25C all day. Spring Festival (Chinese New Year) features a spectacular Lantern Festival at South Gate with kilometers of illuminated lanterns. Try tanghulu (糖葫芦, sugar-coated hawthorn) from vendors.

**Summer:** Visit 8:00-10:30 AM before heat peaks (~35C by midday). No shade on top of the wall.

**Winter:** Afternoons (2:00-5:00 PM) when temperatures reach ~7C. Atmospheric with fewer visitors.

**Night visits:** South Gate stays open until 10 PM — the wall and towers are dramatically lit. Evening cycling is popular among locals.

## Getting to the City Wall

**Metro:** Line 2 serves both South Gate (Yongningmen Station) and North Gate directly. Most convenient option.

**Walking:** From Bell Tower, the South Gate is a 10-minute walk south along South Street.

**Taxi/Didi:** Convenient for East and West gates, which lack direct metro access.

## Nearby Attractions

- **Bell Tower (钟楼) and Drum Tower (鼓楼):** 10-minute walk north from South Gate, marking the center of old Xi'an
- **Muslim Quarter (回民街):** Just northwest of the Drum Tower
- **Shuncheng Alley (顺城巷):** Atmospheric lane running along the base of the wall inside the old city — cafes, boutiques, street art
- **Hanguang Gate Relic Museum (含光门遗址博物馆):** Free entry, displaying original Tang Dynasty wall foundations

## Practical Tips

- Bring water and sunscreen — no shade on top of the wall
- Rent bikes early in the day to avoid midday heat in summer
- Return bikes to any gate, not necessarily the starting point
- The wall surface is paved but slightly uneven in places — not ideal for rollerblades
- Sunset from the wall is spectacular — time your visit accordingly
- During Spring Festival, the South Gate lantern display is one of Xi'an's top seasonal events
