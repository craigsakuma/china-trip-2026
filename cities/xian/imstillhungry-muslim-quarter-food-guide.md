# 50 Must-Eat Street Food Dishes of Xi'an's Muslim Quarter (回民街)

**Source:** I'm Still Hungry — imstillhungry.net/xian-street-food-muslim-street-must-eat/

## The Muslim Quarter (回民街 Hui Min Jie) — Overview

Xi'an's Muslim Quarter is the city's premier street food destination, located near the Bell Tower. The area represents centuries of culinary tradition from the Hui Muslim community, descendants of Silk Road traders who settled in Chang'an over 1,000 years ago. Nearly 300 varieties of food are sold across its maze of streets and alleys. It takes only three words spoken to your taxi driver: "Hui Min Jie."

## Top Savory Dishes with Chinese Names and Prices

### Yang Rou Pao Mo (羊肉泡馍) — Lamb Soup with Torn Bread
Xi'an's signature dish. A bowl of rich lamb broth with hand-torn flatbread pieces, bean vermicelli, and tender lamb. Diners tear their own bread into tiny pieces, the smaller the pieces the better the texture. Comes with pickled garlic and chili sauce. Price: 30-55 RMB.

### Rou Jia Mo (肉夹馍) — Chinese Hamburger
The world's oldest hamburger concept, invented circa 200 BC. Stewed spiced meat stuffed into a crispy baked flatbread bun. Pork version is the most authentic (found outside the Muslim Quarter); beef and lamb versions dominate Hui Min Jie. Price: 5-8 RMB.

### Liang Pi (凉皮) — Cold Rice Noodles
Cold rice flour noodles dressed with vinegar, chili oil, and cucumber strips. Variants include ma jiang liang pi (麻酱凉皮, sesame paste version) and mian pi (面皮, wider wheat noodles). Price: 10-15 RMB.

### Biang Biang Mian (biangbiang面) — Belt Noodles
Ultra-wide, belt-like hand-pulled noodles dressed with chili oil and meat. Named after the famously complex 58-stroke Chinese character (biang), one of the most intricate characters in existence. Price: 20-35 RMB.

### Kao Rou Chuan (烤肉串) — Cumin Lamb Skewers
Charcoal-grilled meat skewers seasoned with cumin and chili. Insist on charcoal-grilled (not electric) for authentic smoky flavor. Price: around 20 RMB for 20 small skewers.

### Guan Tang Bao (灌汤包) — Soup Dumplings
Large soup-filled dumplings with paper-thin skin. The technique: bite a small hole, sip the hot broth, then eat the dumpling with vinegar. Price: around 20 RMB for 8 pieces.

### Shui Jian Bao (水煎包) — Pan-Fried Buns
Steamed-then-fried buns with crispy bottoms and juicy pork filling. Best found at breakfast time stalls. Price: 2 RMB each (3 for 5 RMB).

## Noodle Varieties (面食)

### He Luo (饸饹) — Buckwheat Noodles
Chinese soba-style noodles served hot in bone broth with vinegar and sesame. Price: 20-35 RMB.

### Sao Zi Mian (臊子面) — Minced Meat Noodles
Distinctly Xi'an specialty with high vinegar content, thickened broth, and spiced minced meat. Price: 15-25 RMB.

### Shou Gong Cha Cha Mian (手工搓搓面) — Hand-Rubbed Noodles
Springy hand-rolled noodles with chili and vinegar. More common outside the Muslim Quarter. Price: 20-25 RMB.

### Dan Dan Mian (担担面) — Sichuan Spicy Noodles
Adopted from Sichuan, thin noodles in spicy sesame-peanut sauce. Price: around 20 RMB.

## Soups and Stews

### Hu La Tang (胡辣汤) — Pepper Hot Soup
Thick, numbing pepper soup with cabbage, meatballs, and tofu. A classic Xi'an breakfast. Price: 8-15 RMB.

### Fen Tang Yang Xue (粉汤羊血) — Rice Noodles with Sheep Blood
Rice vermicelli in rich broth with cubes of congealed sheep blood. Adventurous but deeply flavorful. Price: around 20 RMB.

### Suan Tang Jiao Zi (酸汤饺子) — Sour Soup Dumplings
Dumplings swimming in tangy, vinegary broth. Price: around 10 RMB for 20 pieces.

## Practical Eating Tips

**Explore side streets:** Avoid eating from stalls on the main Hui Min Jie strip. Prices are higher and quality often lower. Duck into parallel alleys for better food, cheaper prices, and no crowd-bumping.

**Follow the lines:** The longest queues indicate the best stalls. Worth the wait.

**Freshness phrase:** Say "wo yao xin xian de" (我要新鲜的) to request freshly prepared food rather than pre-cooked items sitting under heat lamps.

**Best timing:** Late afternoon through evening (5 PM - 10 PM) for peak atmosphere and stall variety. Early morning for breakfast specialties like hu la tang and shui jian bao.

**No pork on Muslim Street:** The entire Muslim Quarter is halal, so no pork is available. For pork roujiamo, visit stalls outside the quarter.

**Vegetarian options:** Cai jia mo (菜夹馍, vegetable flatbread), jiu cai he zi (chive pockets), various tofu dishes, and noodle dishes can be ordered without meat.

## Cultural Context — The Hui Muslim Community (回族)

The Hui people are one of China's 56 recognized ethnic groups. Xi'an's Hui community descends from Arab, Persian, and Central Asian traders who traveled the Silk Road to Chang'an during the Tang Dynasty (618-907 CE). Over 1,300 years of integration created a unique culinary tradition blending Central Asian spices (cumin, chili, sesame) with Chinese cooking techniques. The Great Mosque of Xi'an (化觉巷清真大寺), built in 742 AD, remains an active place of worship and is the largest mosque in China.
