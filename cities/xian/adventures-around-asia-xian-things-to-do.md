# 10 Things to Do in Xi'an (西安)

**Source:** Richelle (adventuresaroundasia.com), lived in China for six years, speaks Chinese. Deep local knowledge — prefers Xi'an to Shanghai, recommends minimum three-day stay.

## Quick Facts

- **City:** Xi'an, Shaanxi Province — China's first capital under Qin Shi Huang
- **Known for:** Terracotta Warriors, city wall cycling, Muslim Quarter street food
- **Best timing:** Avoid July–August peak season

## HuiMinJie Muslim Market (回民街)

Vibrant evening market with stall upon stall of fresh food, spices, snacks, drinks, and souvenirs. Vendors sell traditional crafts and paintings created with finger-dipping techniques. Best visited before sunset for photography, then stay for dinner. The evening atmosphere features blue-lit trees and is ideal for wandering.

## Great Mosque of Xi'an (西安大清真寺)

China's largest mosque outside Xinjiang, spanning 12,000 square meters. Beautiful courtyards and pavilions blending Chinese and Islamic architecture.

- **Admission:** 15–25 RMB ($2–4 USD)
- **Timing:** Late afternoon, then walk to the nearby Muslim Market

## Xi'an City Wall (西安城墙)

Perfectly preserved Ming Dynasty wall surrounding downtown — one of the best-preserved ancient city walls in China. Bike rental available on top of the wall for a full circuit.

- **Single bike:** 45 RMB ($6 USD)
- **Tandem bike:** 90 RMB ($13 USD)
- **Deposit:** 200 RMB ($30 USD)
- **Duration:** 1–2 hours to complete the loop

## Drum Tower (鼓楼) & Bell Tower (钟楼)

Historic timekeeping structures in the central Xi'an square. Visit both afternoon and evening for different atmospheres. Evening brings locals out for dancing, hacky sack, and singing opera in the surrounding plaza.

## TangBo Art Museum — Calligraphy Classes

Museum tour (~45 minutes) plus hands-on calligraphy instruction. Classes cover Chinese culture and history alongside character writing. Learn the famously complex "biang" character used for local biangbiang noodles.

- **Contact:** tbart9@163.com for private/group pricing

## Hua Shan Mountain (华山) — Day Trip

Billed as the "Most Dangerous Hike in the World." Features wooden planks nailed to cliffsides with a flimsy harness. Includes a hair-raising cable car ride up. The plank walk section traverses cliff-edge wooden boards with thousand-foot drops below. Not for the faint of heart.

## Shaanxi History Museum (陕西历史博物馆)

Chronicles Xi'an's role as China's first capital. Tang Dynasty exhibits are particularly extensive.

- **Admission:** 20 RMB ($3 USD)
- **Closed:** Mondays
- **Tip:** Avoid July–August peak season crowds

## Wild Goose Pagoda (大雁塔)

Tang Dynasty structure (618–907) originally built to house Buddhist scriptures and materials. Climbable interior with city views from the top. The adjacent plaza comes alive at night with dancing grannies, kite flying, and occasional fireworks. Nearby Haidilao hotpot restaurant for post-visit dinner.

## Terracotta Warriors (兵马俑)

Archaeological site one hour outside Xi'an. Life-size pottery soldiers protecting Emperor Qin Shi Huang's tomb — every single one is individually different. Active excavation still ongoing, with archaeologists reassembling broken pieces in view. Three pits to explore, including reconstructed soldiers and photo opportunities.

## Practical Tips

- **Minimum stay:** 3 days to see everything properly
- **Food tours:** Lost Plate for guided tuk-tuk food crawls
- **Best strategy:** Muslim Market in evening, museums and wall during day
- **Roujiamo tip:** Hole-in-the-wall shops are better than tourist-area vendors
- **Hua Shan:** Full day trip — cable car up, plank walk is optional
