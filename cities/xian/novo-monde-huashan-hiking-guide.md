# Hiking Mount Huashan (华山): Complete Trail Guide

**Source:** Novo-monde — novo-monde.com/en/hiking-mount-hua-in-china-huashan/

## Overview — One of China's Five Great Mountains (五岳)

Mount Hua (华山, Huàshān) is one of China's Five Sacred Mountains and a 5A-rated scenic spot. Located 120 km east of Xi'an in Shaanxi Province, it is famous for its sheer granite cliffs, chain-assisted staircases, and the terrifying Cliffside Plank Walk. The mountain has five interconnected peaks, with the highest (South Peak) reaching 2,150 meters.

## The Five Peaks (五峰)

| Peak | Chinese | Elevation | Known For |
|------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| North Peak (北峰) | Bei Feng | 1,615 m | Cable car arrival; lowest of the five |
| Central Peak (中峰) | Zhong Feng | ~1,600 m | Junction point between peaks |
| East Peak (东峰) | Dong Feng | ~2,090 m | Sunrise viewing; cliff-edge hotel |
| South Peak (南峰) | Nan Feng | 2,150 m | Highest point; Plank Walk access |
| West Peak (西峰) | Xi Feng | ~2,080 m | Sunset views; cable car station |

## Hiking Routes

### Full Circuit on Foot (Most Challenging)
- **Distance:** 13.2 km
- **Duration:** 8.5 hours
- **Elevation gain:** 1,500 m
- **Route:** North Peak -> Central Peak -> East Peak -> South Peak -> West Peak -> descend to North Peak
- Requires strong fitness and early start (ideally before dawn)

### Cable Car + Peak Loop (Recommended for Most Visitors)
- **Distance:** 4.3 km hiking
- **Duration:** 4-5 hours
- **Elevation gain:** 730 m
- **Route:** West cable car up -> peak loop (West -> South -> East -> Central -> North) -> North cable car down
- Most practical option combining mountain views with manageable exertion

### Soldiers' Path Ascent to North Peak (Historic Trail)
- **Distance:** 2.6 km with 4,000 stair steps
- **Slopes:** Up to 80 degrees in places
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours up
- The ancient pilgrim route, steep and relentless. Alternative bypass paths exist for those with vertigo.

## Key Trail Sections and Challenges

### Black Dragon Ridge (苍龙岭) — North to Central Peak
Narrow knife-edge ridge with steep carved steps and chain handrails. Dramatic exposure on both sides. An alternative easier pathway bypasses the ridge for nervous hikers.

### Sky Ladder (天梯) — Central to East Peak
A near-vertical wall climb using small carved footholds and chain assists. Heart-pounding but short. A normal stairway alternative is available.

### Cliffside Plank Walk (长空栈道) — Near South Peak
The most famous thrill on Huashan: 100 meters of narrow wooden planks bolted to a sheer cliff face with 2,000 meters of void below. Harness rental required (30 RMB). **Restrictions:** Ages 15-55 only, minimum height 1.6 meters. Expect 1+ hour waits during peak season. The plank walk is an out-and-back — you return the same way.

## Getting to Huashan from Xi'an (西安)

**High-speed train (recommended):** From Xi'an North Station (metro Lines 2, 4, or 14), bullet trains depart from ~7:00 AM throughout the day. Journey takes approximately 30 minutes, tickets ~100 RMB. Free shuttle buses from Huashan North Station to the visitor center (20 minutes). Return trains run until ~10:30 PM.

**Regular bus:** Over 2 hours from Xi'an. Cheaper but much slower.

**Private car/driver:** Flexible timing, ~2 hours drive.

## Accommodation on the Mountain

Hotels exist at all peaks except South Peak. **East Peak Hotel** is the most popular for sunrise viewing — approximately $200 for double rooms, $25 for dorm beds. Conditions are spartan (basic beds, limited hot water, no heating). Book well in advance for weekends and holidays.

**Town base option:** Stay in Huayin town at the mountain's base for better accommodation and still make an early morning summit attempt.

## Essential Gear and Preparation

- **Footwear:** Proper hiking boots essential — many accidents caused by inappropriate shoes (loafers, heels, slippery-soled sneakers)
- **Gloves:** Lightweight work gloves for gripping chains (can buy at base for a few RMB)
- **Hiking poles:** Useful for descent sections
- **Water and food:** Bring adequate supplies — prices on the mountain are inflated 3-5x
- **Rain gear:** Weather changes rapidly at altitude
- **Headlamp:** If attempting overnight hike or pre-dawn start for sunrise

## Sunrise Viewing from East Peak (东峰观日出)

East Peak is the traditional sunrise viewing spot. Options: (1) Stay overnight at East Peak Hotel and walk out at dawn, (2) Begin hiking up the Soldiers' Path at midnight with a headlamp for a ~4 AM summit arrival, or (3) Take the first West cable car and fast-hike to East Peak. Sunrise times vary seasonally — check in advance.

## Safety Notes

- The Cliffside Plank Walk and Hawk Flipping Over (鹞子翻身) sections are optional — alternative routes exist
- Most trails have chain handrails on steep sections
- Primary hazards: crowding during peak periods, wet conditions making stone steps slippery, and altitude-related fatigue
- Stretch thoroughly after descending to avoid days of severe leg soreness
