# Xizhou Ancient Town & Surrounding Villages (喜洲古镇) — Dali, Yunnan

**Source:** China Discovery — chinadiscovery.com/yunnan/dali/xizhou-village.html

## Quick Facts

- **Chinese name:** 喜洲古镇 (Xǐzhōu Gǔzhèn)
- **Location:** 18 km north of Dali Old Town, between Cangshan (west) and Erhai Lake (east)
- **Altitude:** ~1,900 m
- **Entrance fee:** Free (individual historic houses may charge)
- **Opening hours:** 08:00–18:00
- **Best months:** March–October
- **Time needed:** Half day minimum; full day to include Zhoucheng
- **Getting there:** Bus ~30 min (CNY 7), taxi ~30 min (CNY 40), bicycle ~2 hours via lakeside path

## Bai Architecture (白族建筑)

Xizhou is called the "Museum of Bai Residence Architecture" (白族民居建筑博物馆) with over 150 well-preserved Ming and Republic-era Bai family compounds.

### Architectural Styles
- **Three Houses, One Screen Wall (三坊一照壁):** Three buildings arranged around a courtyard with a decorative screen wall facing the main hall — the most common Bai layout
- **Four Houses, Five Courtyards (四合五天井):** Four buildings enclosing a central courtyard with four smaller corner courtyards — wealthier families
- **Key features:** Painted screen walls bearing auspicious phrases like "清白世家" (Qingbai Shijia — "Innocent Family"), carved wooden doors, tiled roofs with upturned eaves

### Must-Visit Compounds
- **Yan Family Compound (严家大院):** Largest and best-preserved; hosts Three-Course Tea ceremony; CNY 60 entry includes tea
- **Yang Family Compound (杨家大院):** Fine example of Bai merchant architecture
- **Yang Shiyun's Seven-Feet Book Building (杨士云七尺书楼):** Historic scholarly residence
- **Zhao Tingjun Compound (赵廷俊大院):** Republic-era mansion blending Bai and Western styles
- **Mansion of General Yin (尹辅成府):** Military history exhibits

## Sifang Street & Morning Market (四方街与早市)

### Sifang Street (四方街)
- Central square marked by the stone archway "Wenmingfang" (文明坊)
- Surrounded by shops, restaurants, Baba stalls
- Hub of village life; gathering point for locals and visitors

### Morning Market (早市)
- **Time:** ~07:00–10:00 daily
- **What:** Fresh produce, Bai spices, handmade goods, traditional snacks
- **Experience:** Primarily a local market — vegetables, fruits, tofu, herbs
- **Tip:** Arrive early (before 08:00) for authentic atmosphere before tour groups

## Three-Course Tea Ceremony (三道茶)

The defining Bai cultural ritual, performed at Xizhou's historic compounds:

1. **Bitter Tea (苦茶):** Roasted green tea — symbolizes youth's hardship
2. **Sweet Tea (甜茶):** Tea with walnut, sesame, brown sugar, cheese — symbolizes prosperity
3. **Aftertaste Tea (回味茶):** Tea with Sichuan pepper, ginger, honey — symbolizes wisdom

- **Where:** Yan Family Compound (best-known venue), Baocheng Mansion
- **Cost:** CNY 30–60 (often included with compound entry)
- **Duration:** ~30 minutes; accompanied by Bai song and dance performance
- **Philosophy:** "Life is first bitter, then sweet, then worth savoring" (先苦后甜再回味)

## Xizhou Baba (喜洲粑粑)

- **What:** Layered wheat flatbread baked in charcoal oven between two heated plates
- **Savory version:** Minced pork, scallions, Sichuan pepper
- **Sweet version:** Rose jam (玫瑰酱), brown sugar, sesame
- **Where:** Multiple stalls on Sifang Street; look for ones with active ovens
- **Cost:** CNY 5–15
- **Tip:** Eat immediately — best when crispy and hot

## Zhoucheng Village (周城村) — Tie-Dye Capital

Located 7 km north of Xizhou (10-minute drive), Zhoucheng is the designated "Hometown of Bai Tie-Dye Art" (白族扎染艺术之乡).

### Tie-Dye Tradition (扎染)
- **UNESCO status:** Listed as national intangible cultural heritage in 2006
- **Technique:** Cotton cloth folded, tied with string in patterns, then dipped in plant-based indigo dye
- **Colors:** Primarily indigo blue and white; some modern variations with other natural dyes
- **Motifs:** Butterflies, flowers, geometric patterns, Bai cultural symbols

### Workshops and Shopping
- **Hands-on workshops:** Several family workshops offer 1–2 hour tie-dye classes (CNY 50–100)
- **What you make:** Small fabric piece (handkerchief, scarf) to take home
- **Shopping:** Tablecloths, wall hangings, scarves, clothing at village shops
- **Prices:** Handkerchiefs CNY 10–30; tablecloths CNY 100–300; large wall hangings CNY 200+

### Zhoucheng Village Highlights
- Larger and more authentically Bai than Xizhou
- Butterfly Spring (蝴蝶泉) nearby — scenic pool with legendary love story
- Ancient banyan tree at village center
- Less touristy than Xizhou despite being a tie-dye destination

## Other Nearby Villages

### Taoyuan Village (桃源村)
- South of Xizhou, lakeside location
- Excellent Erhai Lake views and sunset photography
- Small, quiet alternative to Shuanglang

### Wase Village (挖色镇)
- East shore of Erhai Lake
- Traditional fishing village with Qing-era gate towers
- Authentic local markets

## Cultural Experiences in Xizhou

- **Bai cooking class:** Some guesthouses arrange traditional cooking workshops
- **Calligraphy and painting:** Art studios in historic compounds
- **Photography:** The "three houses, one screen wall" compounds are supremely photogenic in morning light
- **Cycling:** Flat lakeside roads connect Xizhou to Dali Old Town and Zhoucheng

## Accommodation

- **The Linden Centre (喜林苑):** Restored Bai mansion turned boutique hotel; cultural immersion programs
- **Sky Valley Heritage Hotel:** Traditional architecture with modern comforts
- **Budget guesthouses:** Multiple family-run options in the village; CNY 100–300/night

## Practical Tips

- **Half-day minimum:** Morning market + Yan Family Compound + Sifang Street + Baba
- **Full day:** Add Zhoucheng tie-dye workshop + Butterfly Spring
- **Combine with:** Erhai Lake cycling (Xizhou is on the west-shore route)
- **Photography:** Best light is early morning (08:00–10:00) for architecture; golden hour for Erhai views
- **Respect:** Ask before photographing Bai villagers; some elderly women prefer not to be photographed
