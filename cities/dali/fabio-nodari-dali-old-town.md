# Dali Old Town — Complete Travel Guide

**Source:** Fabio Nodari (fabionodariphoto.com), travel photographer based in China. Published Oct 21, 2025. Updated Jan 19, 2026. All first-hand experience.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Yunnan Province (云南省), southwestern China. Ancient capital of the Nanzhao kingdom. On the Tea Horse Road (South Silk Road).
- **Elevation:** ~2,000 meters. Mild climate year-round. Protected by Erhai Lake on one side, Changshan Mountains (~4,000m peaks) on the other.
- **Ethnic group:** Most important center for the Bai (白) minority — famous for silver, marble, and white houses with painted exterior walls.
- **Best time:** Spring (Mar–May) and Autumn (Sep–Nov). Summer is rainy season. Winter dry but cold, especially overcast days.
- **Getting there:** HSR from Kunming (~2 hours). Small airport ~1 hour from Old Town. Long-distance buses available but not recommended.

## Dali Old Town (大理古城)

One of the few old towns in China still surrounded by walls. More authentic than Lijiang because many families still live here. Walk the walls for panoramic views of the lake on one side and mountains on the other. Two main streets are crowded with shops, but wander the side alleys for genuine local atmosphere. Stay inside the old town for best experience.

**Restaurant recommendation:** 梅子井酒家(两百年古院店) — over 200 years old, located inside a beautiful Bai house. Great for evening dining.

**Honest assessment:** Dali Old Town has become heavily commercialized. If expecting a quiet untouched village, you may be disappointed. But manage expectations and it still has much to offer, especially as a base for exploring surrounding area.

## Three Pagodas (三塔)

Complex of temples about 1.5 km from the Old Town (walk or taxi). Several temples — at least 2 hours to see everything. The three pagodas are different from most Chinese religious buildings because they are truly ancient (survived the Cultural Revolution). Don't miss the small pond on the right side of the pagodas for reflection photos. Go early morning as soon as it opens, take electric bus to top, walk down to avoid tour groups.

## Bai Craftsmen and Batik Workshops

Bai women create elaborate batik fabric using needle and thread (different from Guizhou's Miao minority who use wax). Stitched areas resist dye; removing stitches reveals the design against the colored background. Each piece requires days of patient work. Silk artisans create paintings that are true works of art — one piece was priced at €13,000 and took 13 months to complete. Visit workshops to watch artisans work.

## Cangshan Mountain (苍山)

Rises directly behind Old Town. Cable car options: Gantong Cable Car (感通索道) goes to Jade Belt Road — scenic trail with great views of town and Erhai Lake, relatively easy walking. Zhonghe Cable Car (中和索道) reaches higher peaks with less crowded trails for more adventurous hiking.

## Erhai Lake (洱海)

Central to the region's identity. Cycle lakeside roads stopping at small Bai villages. Boat rides available. Each section has its own character: Caicun/Xizhou area has traditional houses and fields to the shoreline; western side near Shuanglang is more developed with boutique hotels and cafés built over the water. The combination of Cangshan and Erhai is what makes Dali special — mountains and open water within easy reach.

## Xizhou (喜洲)

Charming ancient town near Erhai Lake, 18 km north of Dali Old Town. More authentic and laid back than Dali. Famous for Xizhou Baba (喜洲粑粑) — round flatbread baked in clay ovens. Savory version: minced pork, scallions, spices. Sweet version: brown sugar or rose jam. Sold on almost every corner. Quiet streets and traditional courtyards give a glimpse of pre-tourism Dali.

## Shaxi (沙溪)

Halfway between Dali and Lijiang. One of the best-preserved old towns along the Tea Horse Road. Retained original charm without heavy commercialization (though changing). Cobbled streets, traditional Bai and Yi architecture, quiet courtyards, family-run shops. Once an important mule caravan stop for tea, salt, and horse trade. Friday Market: villagers from surrounding countryside sell produce and handicrafts — centuries-old tradition. Spend at least one night to enjoy early morning/evening atmosphere after day-trippers leave. Old Town is small — can visit in a few hours if time is limited.

## Local Markets

Not all village markets open every day. Ask your hotel for accurate schedules. For a local guide to find off-the-beaten-path markets, contact a local agency.

## Practical Tips

- **VPN:** Essential — all social media and Google (including Gmail) blocked without one.
- **eSIM:** Recommended for connectivity.
- **Travel insurance:** Recommended.
- **Manage expectations:** Use Dali as a base to explore surrounding villages (Xizhou, Shaxi, Bai villages) for the most rewarding experience.
- **Onward travel:** Combine with Lijiang, Shaxi, and Shangri-La for a full Yunnan itinerary.
