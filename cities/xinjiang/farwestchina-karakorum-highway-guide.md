# Karakorum Highway — China Section (中巴公路) Complete Guide

**Source:** Far West China (farwestchina.com, 2024 update) by Josh Summers, long-term Xinjiang resident. Detailed route guide covering every stop from Kashgar to Tashkurgan with permits, transport options, costs, and photography spots.

## Quick Facts

- **Route:** Kashgar (喀什) → Tashkurgan (塔什库尔干), ~300 km one-way on China side.
- **Full highway:** 1,300+ km connecting Pakistan and China — highest paved road on earth, topping out at 4,693 meters.
- **Season:** Officially open May 1 – December 31. Best: June through early September.
- **Duration:** Minimum 3 days for comprehensive viewing; 1–2 days possible for Karakul Lake only.
- **Three ethnic groups along route:** Kyrgyz (柯尔克孜族), Uyghur (维吾尔族), Tajik (塔吉克族).
- **Key rule:** Foreigners cannot spend the night anywhere between Kashgar and Tashkurgan — only at endpoints.

## Key Stops (South from Kashgar)

### Opal (乌帕尔) — ~1 Hour from Kashgar

Rest stop with restaurants serving Uyghur polo and laghman. Home to the tomb of Mahmud al-Kashgari (11th-century Uyghur scholar who compiled the first Turkic dictionary).

### Red Mountain / Oytagh Canyon (奥依塔克)

Red-tinted mountains ideal for photography. Oytagh Glacier Park has been closed since 2014. The color contrast against blue sky makes this a great photo stop.

### White Sand Lake (白沙湖)

Water reservoir with striking white sand dunes. Kyrgyz traders sell local trinkets. Some yurt accommodation reportedly available — verify current policies.

### Karakul Lake (卡拉库里湖) — Crown Jewel

- Surrounded by snowcapped peaks with **Muztagh Ata (慕士塔格峰, "Father of Ice Mountains")** towering above at 7,546 meters.
- Best views at sunrise and sunset (glacier reflections in still water).
- Overnight yurt stays forbidden since 2017; meals available from lakeside vendors.
- Emerald to turquoise water depending on light conditions.

### Subash Pass (苏巴什达坂) — 4,100 meters (13,400 feet)

Highest point on the Chinese side. Security checkpoint — passport required. Watch for altitude sickness symptoms. Platform viewpoint with panoramic mountain vistas.

### Taheman Grasslands (塔合曼湿地)

Mountain scenery with a winding river through green grasslands. Excellent photography and cycling opportunities. Less visited than Karakul.

### Tashkurgan (塔什库尔干) — Final Major Town

- **Stone Fortress (石头城):** 2,000-year-old ruins that protected Silk Road caravans. Atmospheric at sunset.
- **Golden Grasslands (金草滩):** Wooden pathways through wetlands with mountain backdrop. Beautiful morning light.
- **Buzkashi:** Traditional Tajik horseback goat-carcass game, occasionally viewable.
- Budget half a day minimum for exploring the town.

### Khunjerab Pass (红其拉甫口岸) — Pakistan Border

Last checkpoint before Pakistan. Pakistani visa required to cross. Photo opportunities at the border marker.

## Transportation Options

### Travel Agency (Recommended)

Easiest option. Arranges permits, customized itineraries, local activities, and treks from Karakul to Kyrgyz villages. Cost: several thousand RMB at minimum. Recommended: **Old Road Tours** (Kashgar-based).

### Private Driver

Hire through hostels or bus station (客运站). Flexible scheduling and stop selection. Cost: **400–800 RMB** depending on vehicle and negotiation. May or may not include driver's housing/meals.

### Public Bus

- **Departs Kashgar:** 9:30 AM daily from city bus station.
- **Arrives Tashkurgan:** ~6 hours later.
- **Return buses:** 8:00 AM and 3:00 PM from Tashkurgan.
- **Price:** 51 RMB one way.
- **Limitation:** No guaranteed stops at scenic areas — the bus may bypass Karakul Lake entirely.

## Accommodation

**Tashkurgan only** (foreigners cannot stay between Kashgar and Tashkurgan):
- **K2 Youth Hostel:** 4/8-bed dorms and private twin rooms, pool table, bar. Budget option.
- **Crown Inn Hotel:** Comfortable rooms with exceptional breakfast including real coffee. Mid-range.

**Camping:** Technically illegal for foreigners but sometimes tolerated if discreet. Limited flat grassy areas. Karakul Lake camping permissions are difficult and expensive to obtain.

## Permits & Documentation

- **Border Travel Permit:** Increasingly required for Tashkurgan visits. Travel agencies can arrange this in Kashgar.
- **Muztagata Mountain trekking:** Requires special permit from Kashgar + environmental fee.
- **Police checkpoints:** Expect passport registration at multiple points along the route (~5 minutes each).
- **Critical:** Permit requirements change frequently — verify current rules before travel.

## Weather & Safety

- Best time: June through early September (warmer, but evenings remain cold even in summer).
- Avoid July–August rainy season peak — heavy rains cause mudslides, highway washouts, and traffic delays.
- Road hazards: Falling rocks, mudslides, low visibility in rain. Government responds quickly with hazard markers.
- Travel from Kashgar to Tashkurgan is "quite safe" per long-term resident assessment.
- Altitude sickness possible at Subash Pass (4,100m) — hydrate, move slowly.

## Photography Highlights

1. Karakul Lake at sunrise/sunset — glacier reflections in still water.
2. Red Mountains of Oytagh Canyon — dramatic color contrast.
3. White Sand Lake — teal water against white dunes.
4. Taheman Grasslands — winding river through green valleys.
5. Subash Pass platform — 360-degree mountain panorama.
6. Muztagh Ata — visible throughout much of the route.

## Practical Tips

- Pack warm layers even in summer — temperatures drop significantly at altitude and after sunset.
- Bring snacks and water — limited food options between Kashgar and Tashkurgan.
- Sunscreen and sunglasses essential at high altitude.
- Download offline maps — phone service is intermittent along the route.
- Carry your original passport at all times.
- The 2017 new highway improved conditions significantly but weather delays still possible.
