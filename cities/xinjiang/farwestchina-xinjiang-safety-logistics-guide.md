# Xinjiang Safety & Logistics — Practical Guide for Foreign Travelers

**Source:** Far West China (farwestchina.com, updated 2025) by Josh Summers, American who lived in Xinjiang for 10+ years + Eventurus Asia (eventurusasia.com, 2025) + China Dragon Travel (chinadragontravel.com, 2025). Real-world safety assessment, permit information, and logistics from long-term residents and recent travelers.

## Quick Facts

- **Safety verdict:** "Xinjiang is safe for tourists." Over the past decade, no reports of tourists being directly or indirectly harmed in any incident.
- **Visa:** Standard China visa sufficient for most of Xinjiang (no special Xinjiang permit needed, unlike Tibet).
- **New visa-free policies (2024–2025):** 54-country transit visa-free stays up to 10 days; France, Germany, Italy, Spain, and others get 30-day visa-free; US/UK/Canada get 6-day visa-free.
- **Best time:** April–October. July–August hottest and most crowded.
- **Primary gateway:** Urumqi Diwopu International Airport.

## Security Environment

### What to Expect

Heavy military and police presence — significantly more visible than other Chinese regions. Metal detectors and bag inspections at airports, malls, transportation hubs, and even some markets. Multiple passport checks throughout any travel day.

### How to Handle It

- Carry your **original passport** at all times, especially in southern Xinjiang. Keep a physical copy in a separate bag as backup.
- Arrive earlier than standard for flights and trains due to enhanced security screening.
- **Never photograph** military, police, or security installations — cameras may be confiscated and memory cards wiped.
- Knives and souvenirs like Uyghur knives may be confiscated at checkpoints — store purchases in your hotel room rather than carrying them.
- Security is more relaxed than previous years but checkpoints remain frequent.

### Traveler Testimonials

Multiple independent travelers (solo female, family with young children, group of 12 Americans) all report feeling safe and welcomed throughout Xinjiang.

## Permits & Restricted Areas

### No Permit Needed

Most of Xinjiang is open with a standard China visa: Urumqi, Turpan, Kashgar city, Hotan, Kuqa, Sayram Lake, Kanas Lake.

### Border Travel Permit Required

Areas near international borders require a Border Travel Permit (边境通行证):
- Tashkurgan and Karakul Lake (Pamir Plateau)
- Khunjerab Pass (Pakistan border)
- Baihaba Village (near Kazakhstan border)
- Horgos Port area

**How to obtain:** Book through a travel agency (easiest), or apply at the Self-Driving and Camping Office near Kashgar airport (fill in English form, wait potentially several hours).

### Other Restricted Areas

- Duku Highway (独库公路): Some sections restricted for foreigners.
- Hejing County, Luobu Village, Bayinbuluke: Check current policies.
- Access rules can change without notice — always verify before traveling.

## Hotels & Registration

- Hotels must register foreign guests with local police (standard across China).
- Not all hotels in smaller cities accept foreigners — confirm acceptance when booking.
- In remote areas (Kashgar Old Town, Hemu, Bayinbuluke), confirm overseas traveler acceptance before arrival.
- Booking.com and Trip.com work. Ctrip has the widest Chinese hotel selection.

## Transportation

### Between Cities

- **Flights:** Urumqi to Kashgar ~2 hours. Multiple daily flights. Also connects to Hotan, Kuqa, Kanas.
- **Train:** High-speed Lanzhou → Urumqi (stops in Turpan). Overnight trains Urumqi → Kashgar.
- **Bus:** Comprehensive network but long distances (Xinjiang is massive — think Texas-sized).
- **Hire a car with driver:** Recommended for southern Xinjiang and remote routes. Licensed drivers essential.

### Within Cities

Taxis, Didi (ride-hailing), and public buses work well in Urumqi and Kashgar.

## Money & Payment

- **Alipay and WeChat Pay** dominate — even small fruit stalls in bazaars take mobile payments.
- **Cash (CNY)** still accepted and recommended for remote Pamir areas.
- International credit cards rarely accepted outside major hotels.
- Carry enough cash before leaving major cities.

## Communication

- **VPN essential:** WhatsApp, Google, Instagram, Facebook all blocked in China. Download a VPN before arrival.
- **SIM card:** Chinese SIM from China Mobile or China Unicom. Foreign SIMs may not work in Xinjiang.
- **eSIM:** Increasingly available for China. Check compatibility.
- **Language:** Mandarin in tourist areas. Uyghur/Kazakh in rural areas. Very limited English anywhere.

## Cultural Etiquette

- **Pork:** Most ethnic minorities in Xinjiang don't eat pork. Never bring pork products into restaurants.
- **Dress:** Modest clothing recommended in cultural areas like Kashgar — shoulders and knees covered.
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing locals, especially women.
- **Mosques:** Remove shoes before entering. Women should cover their heads.
- **Hospitality:** Tea is offered automatically and continuously — refusing is impolite.

## Time Zone Quirk

Xinjiang officially uses Beijing time (UTC+8) but operates on unofficial "Xinjiang time" (UTC+6). Restaurants open late, markets start mid-morning. When locals say "10 AM" they usually mean Xinjiang time (noon Beijing time). Always clarify which time someone means.

## Practical Tips

- **Flexibility is key:** Delays from security checks, road conditions, and permit issues are normal. Build buffer days into your itinerary.
- **Travel insurance:** Get comprehensive coverage — medical facilities in remote areas are basic.
- **Altitude:** Karakul Lake and Tashkurgan sit above 3,500 meters. Acclimatize and hydrate.
- **Distances:** Xinjiang covers 1.66 million km² — larger than most European countries. Plan accordingly.
- **Souvenir knives:** Beautiful Uyghur knives available everywhere but will likely be confiscated at airport security. Ship via courier if you want to keep them.
- The common-sense safety advice: "Use common sense and be flexible."
