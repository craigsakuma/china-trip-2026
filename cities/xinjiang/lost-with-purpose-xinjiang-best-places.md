# 12 Best Places to Visit in Xinjiang (新疆)

**Source:** Alex Reynolds (lostwithpurpose.com), solo female traveler who lived in China for a year. First-hand experience from 22+ days backpacking across Xinjiang's cities, deserts, and mountain highways.

---

## Quick Facts

- **Region:** Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region (新疆维吾尔自治区) — China's largest province
- **Peoples:** Uyghur, Han Chinese, Tajik, Mongol, Kazakh, Kyrgyz
- **Landscapes:** Deserts, grasslands, alpine lakes, 7,000m+ peaks, Silk Road oases
- **Key challenge:** Many hotels refuse foreign guests; some areas require permits or are off-limits
- **Essential:** VPN required for internet access throughout China
- **Safety:** Generally safe; widespread government surveillance infrastructure

---

## 1. Urumqi (乌鲁木齐)

Capital city, 3.5 million people. Claims title of farthest city from any ocean coast. Modern, Han Chinese-styled, limited historic interest.

**Worth seeing:** Xinjiang Autonomous Region Museum; Geographic Center of Asia monument (20 km southwest of city).

---

## 2. Turpan (吐鲁番)

Low-altitude Uyghur city near the Turpan Depression — 157m below sea level. Famous for vineyards and ancient Silk Road ruins.

**Sights:**
- Yarkhoto / Jiaohe ruins (交河故城) — ancient cliff-top city
- Karakhoja / Gaochang (高昌故城) — Tang Dynasty ruins
- Karez Wells (坎儿井) — ancient underground irrigation system
- Emin Minaret (额敏塔) — 18th-century Islamic tower

**Stay:**
- Budget: **Dap Youth Hostel** — accepts foreigners, vine-covered courtyard, ~50 RMB dorm
- Mid-range: **Silk Road Lodges** — English-speaking staff, grape field views

---

## 3. Kashgar (喀什)

Remote Uyghur settlement at the crossroads of the Silk Road, surrounded by desert and mountains. The most Central Asian-feeling city in China.

**Sights:**
- Reconstructed Old Town (老城) — winding lanes, Uyghur architecture
- Afaq Khoja Mausoleum (阿帕克霍加墓)
- Tomb of Yusuf Khas Hajib
- Kashgar Grand Bazaar (喀什大巴扎) — livestock, textiles, crafts

**Stay:** **Kashgar Pamir Youth Hostel** — accepts foreigners, near Id Kah Mosque (艾提尕尔清真寺). ~140 RMB double.

---

## 4. Tashkurgan (塔什库尔干)

Highland town at 3,100m on the Karakoram Highway (KKH). Last settlement before the Pakistan border. Home to Pamiri Tajiks.

**Sights:**
- Views of Kongur Tagh (公格尔峰, 7,649m) and Muztagh Ata (慕士塔格峰, 7,509m)
- Karakul Lake (卡拉库勒湖) — stunning alpine lake en route

**Stay:** **Crown Inn Tashkurgan** — mid-range, English-speaking staff.

**Note:** Travel beyond Tashkurgan requires organized tour permits. Exception: Pakistan visa holders transiting to the border.

---

## 5. Taklamakan Desert (塔克拉玛干沙漠)

One of Earth's largest sandy deserts. Name reportedly translates as "place you come in, but never go out."

**Access points:**
- Makit N39, Karsu, Turdi Aji Agricultural Garden (edges)
- Cross via Aksu–Khotan road or Luntai–Niya–Qiemo road

**Activities:** Camel rides, ATV excursions at edge points.

**Warning:** Full desert crossing requires serious equipment, experience, and preparation. Not casual.

---

## 6. Khotan (和田)

Southern oasis city, "Jade City." Less touristy than Kashgar with more living Uyghur traditions intact.

**Sights:**
- Khotan Museum
- Colorful bazaar — more authentic than Kashgar's
- White Jade River (白玉河) — locals hunt for jade stones
- Tomb of Imam Asim
- Day trip: Melikawat ruins (买力克阿瓦提古城)

---

## 7. Duku Highway (独库公路)

500+ km mountain road through the Tianshan (天山) connecting Kuqa (库车, south) to Dushanzi (独山子, north). One of China's most spectacular drives.

**Highlights along the route:**
- Grand Canyons of Tianshan (天山大峡谷)
- Alpine lakes
- Bayin Buluk (巴音布鲁克) grassland plateau — Mongolian yurts, Nine Bends of the Kaidu River
- Majestic alpine canyons

**Transport:** No public transport. Hire a private car or join an organized tour. Yurt stays possible along the route; some hotels may restrict foreigners.

---

## 8. Sayram Lake (赛里木湖)

"Easternmost teardrop of the Atlantic" — near the Kazakhstan border in Yili (伊犁) county. Surrounded by grasslands and nomadic yurts. One of Asia's most beautiful lakes.

**Access note:** Proximity to international border means regulations change frequently. Foreigners may need travel permits.

---

## 9. Bogda Shan Mountains (博格达山)

Eastern Tianshan sub-range between Urumqi and Turpan. Mt. Bogda (博格达峰) reaches 5,445m.

**Highlight:** Tianchi Lake (天池, "Heavenly Lake") — glacial lake in a national park with valleys, grasslands, forests. Popular hiking trails; accessible day trip from Urumqi.

---

## 10. Hami (哈密)

Easternmost Xinjiang city, near Mongolia and Gansu borders. Famous for hami melons (哈密瓜).

**Sights:**
- Hami Kings Tomb (哈密王陵) — 19th century
- Hami Museum
- Ghost City (魔鬼城) — wind-eroded rock formations resembling ruins

---

## 11. Kanas Lake (喀纳斯湖)

Remote northern Xinjiang in the Altai Mountains (阿勒泰山), near the Kazakhstan/Russia/Mongolia tri-border. Coniferous and deciduous forests, four-season destination.

**Getting there:** Travel to Buerqin (布尔津) by bus, train, or plane. Then join a tour or apply for permits for independent travel.

---

## 12. K2 / Qogir (乔戈里峰)

Second-highest peak globally at 8,611m, on the Pakistan border. Extreme conditions.

**What's possible:** Trek to the northern base camp (accessible route) with an organized tour from Kashgar. Multi-week trek requiring good physical condition and full permits.

---

## Practical Tips

- **Hotel refusals:** Many hotels in Xinjiang will not accept foreign guests. Hostels listed above are confirmed foreigner-friendly.
- **Permits:** Several areas (especially border zones and areas beyond Tashkurgan) require travel permits or organized tours. Check current regulations before going.
- **VPN:** Essential for accessing Google, WhatsApp, and Western internet services throughout China.
- **Surveillance:** Extensive security checkpoints and surveillance cameras throughout Xinjiang. Carry your passport at all times.
- **Ethical awareness:** The author notes serious Chinese government repression of Uyghur Muslims, including internment camps, forced sterilizations, and forced indoctrination. Visitor spending may indirectly support these policies. Consider this when planning travel.
- **Transport:** Distances are enormous. Budget significant time for overland travel between cities.
- **Duku Highway season:** Only open roughly June–October due to snow.
