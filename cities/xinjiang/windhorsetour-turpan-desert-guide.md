# Turpan (吐鲁番) — Silk Road Oasis & Desert Guide

**Source:** Windhorse Tour (windhorsetour.com) + China Discovery (chinadiscovery.com) + Eventurus Asia (eventurusasia.com). Comprehensive guide to Turpan's Silk Road ruins, desert landscapes, and ancient engineering marvels.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Eastern Xinjiang, ~180 km southeast of Urumqi (~2 hours by car/train).
- **Elevation:** 30 meters below sea level (Ayding Lake at -155 meters — one of Earth's lowest points).
- **Climate:** Hottest place in inland China. Summer temperatures exceed 40°C (104°F). Called the "Land of Fire."
- **Best time:** April–June and September–October for mild weather. Avoid July–August extreme heat.
- **Nickname:** "The hottest oasis on earth."
- **Recommended duration:** 2 days to cover all major sites.

## Historical Sites

### Jiaohe Ancient City (交河故城) — UNESCO World Heritage

- **Age:** Established ~200 BC, ruined in the 14th century.
- **Significance:** World's largest, oldest, and best-preserved earthen architectural city. Crucial Silk Road junction.
- **Layout:** Leaf-shaped plateau with preserved earthen structures, Buddhist monasteries, and underground temples carved directly into earth.
- **Location:** 11 km west of Turpan downtown.
- **Tip:** Visit in late afternoon to avoid heat and catch sunset light on the ruins. Bring water and sun protection.

### Gaochang Ruins (高昌故城)

Largest ancient city ruins in the Western Regions, spanning the 1st–14th centuries. Walls, temples, palaces, and residential zones spread across an extensive area. Exploration options include walking or **donkey cart rides** through the ruins.

### Bezeklik Thousand Buddha Caves (柏孜克里克千佛洞)

Cave complex dating from ~400–1,300 years ago. Frescoes and murals depicting Buddha's life reflect Indian, Chinese, and Central Asian Buddhist traditions. Evidence of the Silk Road's cultural mixing.

### Astana Tombs (阿斯塔那古墓群)

Part of Gaochang's ancient graveyard. Approximately 1,000 tombs containing epigraphs, frescoes, mummies, and artwork from lost civilizations. Provides tangible evidence of daily life on the ancient Silk Road.

### Tuyugou Valley (吐峪沟)

Features grottos with vivid paintings and carvings depicting people of various races engaged in music, rituals, and Buddhist activities. Sand dunes available for climbing and photography. Tall canyon trees provide shade.

## Natural Landmarks

### Flaming Mountains (火焰山)

Reddish-orange hills stretching 100+ km east-west, maximum width 10 km. Heat wave mirages make the hills appear to be on fire. Famous from the classic novel *Journey to the West* (西游记). Best visited during sunrise or sunset for vibrant orange and red hues. Featured in Chinese literature and mythology for centuries.

### Grape Valley (葡萄沟)

Turpan is "the hometown of grapes in China." The valley within the Flaming Mountains offers:
- Grape tasting (seedless varieties, intensely sweet due to temperature extremes)
- Fresh juices and local wines
- Traditional raisin-drying houses (晾房) — unique architectural structures
- **Grape Festival:** August 18–28 with fireworks, dancing, Uyghur weddings, folk performances, and vineyard tours.

### Ayding Lake (艾丁湖)

~155 meters below sea level — one of Earth's lowest points after the Dead Sea. Now a dried salt flat with surreal lunar-like landscape. Photography highlight.

## Engineering Marvel — Karez Water System (坎儿井)

Underground irrigation network developed over 2,000 years ago. System of wells, underground channels, and surface reservoirs efficiently transports snowmelt from the Tianshan Mountains to Turpan's farms. One of China's three great ancient engineering projects (alongside the Great Wall and Grand Canal). **Karez Museum** provides detailed explanation with walking tours through actual channels.

## Local Food

- **Big Plate Chicken (大盘鸡):** Chicken and potatoes in spicy sauce, shared communal-style.
- **Polo (手抓饭):** Mutton, carrots, onions, and rice pilaf eaten with hands.
- **Roasted dumplings (烤饺子):** Crispy fried crescent pastries with lamb filling.
- **Kebabs (烤肉串):** Cumin-spiced lamb on charcoal.
- **Grapes and raisins:** Turpan's famous seedless varieties — buy directly from farms.

## Getting There

- **From Urumqi:** High-speed train (~1 hour), bus (~2 hours), or drive (~2 hours).
- **By air:** Urumqi Diwopu International Airport is the nearest major airport, then transfer.
- **Local transport:** Buses, taxis, rented bicycles, hired cars. Most sites spread out — a hired car for 1–2 days is the most efficient.

## Practical Tips

- Carry massive amounts of water — dehydration is a real risk in summer.
- Sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses are non-negotiable even in spring/autumn.
- Sites are spread across ~30 km — don't try to walk between them.
- Jiaohe and Gaochang are the two must-sees if time is limited.
- Turpan makes an excellent 1–2 day side trip from Urumqi before heading west to Kashgar.
- Combined Turpan + Urumqi covers the eastern Xinjiang highlights in 3–4 days.
