# Kashgar (喀什) — Silk Road City Guide

**Source:** Non Stop Travelling (nonstoptravelling.com, Sep 2024) + Eventurus Asia (eventurusasia.com, 2025). First-hand accounts of visiting Kashgar's reconstructed old town, Sunday livestock market, mosques, and Uyghur food culture.

## Quick Facts

- **Location:** Southwestern Xinjiang (新疆), one of China's westernmost cities. ~1,500 km from Urumqi.
- **History:** 2,000+ year Silk Road trading hub connecting China, Central Asia, Middle East, India, and Europe.
- **Ethnic character:** Predominantly Uyghur — feels more Central Asian than Chinese. Turkic language, Islamic architecture, bazaar culture.
- **Best time:** April–October. July–August hottest and most crowded.
- **Getting there:** Direct flights from Urumqi (~2 hours), Beijing, Shanghai, Xi'an. Overnight train from Urumqi (~20 hours). High-speed rail connection planned/expanding.

## Old Town (喀什古城)

Reconstructed old city with alleyways of beige, straw, and mud-covered houses decorated with vintage rugs, plates, and flower pots. Some travelers note a "Disney-like" aesthetic to the rebuilt areas, but the narrow back lanes remain more authentic. Many traditional houses feature inner courtyards with plants and seating areas.

The reconstruction maintained the traditional mud-brick house style while modernizing infrastructure. Wander the back alleys away from the main tourist routes for a more genuine experience.

## Id Kah Mosque (艾提尕尔清真寺)

Located in the central square. Largest mosque in China. Daily camel parades feature Bactrian two-hump camels — historically used along the Silk Road. The mosque dates to 1442 and can hold 10,000 worshippers. Yellow-tiled facade is an iconic Kashgar landmark.

## Sunday Livestock Market (牲畜巴扎)

Operating for thousands of years, this massive weekly market draws hundreds of animal trucks. While it operates daily, Sundays are by far the busiest.

**What you'll see:**
- Hundreds of sheep and goats (including fat-tailed breeds weighing 30+ kg per tail)
- Cattle sections — navigate carefully
- Horses, foals, and yaks
- Traders assessing livestock using scales in metal baskets
- Food stalls selling melons by the slice and fresh pomegranate juice

The market is loud, dusty, and absolutely fascinating. Photography generally welcomed.

## Afaq Khoja Mausoleum (阿帕克霍加麻扎)

Stunning Islamic mausoleum with beautiful mosaics and tiled domes, reminiscent of Uzbekistan's architecture. The holiest Muslim site in Xinjiang, containing the tombs of five generations of the same family. Built in 1640.

## Grand Bazaar (大巴扎)

As of September 2024, the most famous Grand Bazaar has been relocated and is under construction. Check current status before visiting. Traditional crafts include copperware, knives (note: may be confiscated at security checkpoints), carpets, dried fruits, and spices.

## Food & Drink

- **Polo (抓饭):** Rice pilaf with carrots, lamb, and oil — the Uyghur staple.
- **Laghman (拌面):** Hand-pulled noodles with stir-fried vegetables and meat.
- **Lamb kebabs (烤肉串):** Cumin and pepper seasoning, grilled over charcoal.
- **Samsa (烤包子):** Lamb-and-onion filled baked pastries from a tandoor oven.
- **Fresh melon and pomegranate juice:** Widely available at markets.
- **Century-old teahouse:** Evening visit recommended (~7 PM) for local Uyghur dancing and traditional tea service.

## Day Trips from Kashgar

- **Karakul Lake (卡拉库里湖):** Emerald glacial lake with Muztagh Ata views. ~3.5 hours drive.
- **Tashkurgan (塔什库尔干):** Tajik town with Stone Fortress. ~5–6 hours by bus.
- **Full Karakorum Highway:** 2–3 day excursion. See separate KKH guide.

## Practical Tips

- **Passport:** Carry your original at all times — required for hotels, markets, gas stations, and security checkpoints.
- **Permits:** Standard China visa sufficient for Kashgar. Pamir Plateau/Tashkurgan may require a Border Travel Permit — arrange through a local agency or at the Self-Driving and Camping Office near Kashgar airport.
- **Payment:** Alipay and WeChat Pay dominate; even small fruit stalls take mobile payments. Carry cash for remote areas.
- **Time zone:** Xinjiang unofficially operates 2 hours behind Beijing time. Locals use "Xinjiang time" — restaurants may not open until 10 AM Beijing time.
- **Dress code:** Modest clothing recommended in cultural areas (shoulders and knees covered).
- **Security:** Checkpoints are frequent but routine. Plan extra time for airport and train arrivals.
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing people. Never photograph military/police.
- **Cultural note:** Most ethnic minorities in Xinjiang don't eat pork. Avoid mentioning pork or bringing pork products into restaurants.
