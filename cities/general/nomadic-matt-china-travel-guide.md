# China Travel Guide

**Source:** Nomadic Matt (nomadicmatt.com), one of the most popular travel blogs, budget-focused, regularly updated. Updated November 13, 2025.

---

## Top Destinations

### Hong Kong (香港)
Bustling metropolis combining high-rise buildings with traditional street markets and temples. Large expat population, world-class shopping, fantastic nightlife, one of the best food scenes in the world. Also has beautiful wilderness, hiking, and beaches beyond the dense urban core. Author considers it a place where "east meets west." Recommend at least 3 days.

### Shanghai (上海)
One of China's largest and most visited cities. "Like visiting the future" -- fast trains, neon lights, efficient organization, cosmopolitan vibe. Old City and YuYuan Gardens for historical China. Nanjing Road for shopping. Amazing sushi and cocktail scenes. Wild nightlife.

### Beijing (北京)
Center of China for centuries. 21 million people. GIGANTIC -- spend at least 4 days. Key sites: Tian'anmen Square, the Forbidden City (175+ acres, UNESCO World Heritage, imperial palace 1420-1912 CE, 16M visitors/year), Temple of Heaven, Summer Palace, Great Wall. Pollution has improved significantly in recent years. "You have to visit to understand modern China."

### Xi'an (西安)
Ancient capital. Home to famed Terracotta Army (2,000+ years old). Author's favorite place in China. Walk or bike the city wall, visit ancient pagodas/temples in Old City, explore architecture and cuisine of Muslim quarters. Mount Hua for hiking. Great cafes, cute bars, incredible food scene.

### Chengdu (成都)
Panda Research Base -- get there early to beat crowds. Pandas relax, eat, and sleep (that's all they do). Still worth seeing.

### Guilin / Li River (桂林 / 漓江)
Li River: 272 miles long, dozens of places to explore. Best photography: Xiaolong, Laozhai Hill, karst mountains. Karst mountains on back of 20 yuan banknote. Boat trips, bicycle rental (~20 CNY half day).

### Macau (澳门)
"Las Vegas of Asia." Portuguese colony for 300+ years -- interesting mix of Chinese and Portuguese culture. Special Administrative Region with autonomy from mainland. World's highest bungee jump (233m). Good food and architecture. If not gambling, one night is enough.

### Harbin (哈尔滨)
Harbin International Ice and Snow Sculpture Festival: every January, world's largest ice/snow festival, ~20M visitors/year. Lasts ~1 month. Over 200,000 cubic meters of ice used. New theme each year.

### Dunhuang (敦煌) -- Mogao Grottos
Also called Thousand Buddha Caves. Largest, best-preserved Buddhist art in the world -- first cave carved 366 AD. Almost 500 individual temples. Major Silk Road stop.

### Tibet (西藏)
"Roof of the World." Snowy mountains, exotic customs, Buddhism. Need special permit to visit. Avoid discussing Dalai Lama or politics -- region annexed by China in 1950s. Very sensitive subject for both sides.

## Tian'anmen Square (天安门广场)
Hard to grasp the sheer size until you stand in the middle. Includes: Tiananmen Tower, Great Hall of the People, People's Heroes Monument, National Museum, Mao Zedong's mausoleum. Photos allowed in square but not in mausoleum.

## The Great Wall (长城)
Stretches over 21,000 km. Built over centuries against Mongolian armies (never successfully). Both busy tourist sections and secluded ruins (can camp beside it). Most popular section near Beijing, 2 hours by public bus or guided tour.

## The Silk Road (丝绸之路)
Dating back 2,000+ years. Total length over 3,800 km (2,400 mi), half within China. No "official" road -- trace traditional route as far as you like. Must-see stops: Mogao Caves (Dunhuang), ancient city of Turpan, Rainbow Mountains near Zhangye.

## Huashan Plank Walk (华山)
Narrow wooden planks bolted to steep cliff. ~120 km east of Xi'an. Only ~100 meters long but takes time due to fear/crowds. Two safety cords attached. Unrivalled panoramic views.

## Food and Cuisine

China has four main cooking styles: Cantonese, Beijing, Shanghai, and Sichuan. Regional breakdown:
- **Sichuan / Hunan (Central):** Spicy food, hot pot
- **Northern China:** Salty items -- dried meats, pickled vegetables (fresh produce less common)
- **Beijing, Hong Kong, Shanghai:** Everything available
- **Street food:** Delicious but can cause stomach upset; be prepared for adjustment period

Tip: Eating alone, ask for "xiao pan" (小盘) -- small portions at 70% of size and price.

## Transportation

### Within Cities
- **Buses:** Most popular, 2-3 CNY per ride
- **Subway/metro:** Extensive systems in major cities, under 6 CNY per ride. Beijing Airport Express: 25 CNY
- **Walking:** Great but check air quality daily; consider mask on bad days
- **Car rental:** Not possible -- China doesn't recognize International Driving Permit

### Intercity: Buses
Cheaper than trains for distances under 8-10 hours.
- Beijing to Anshan: 9 hours, ~205 CNY (vs. 400 CNY by train)
- Beijing to Tianjin: 2 hours, ~40 CNY (vs. 90 CNY high-speed train)

### Intercity: Trains
Multiple speed tiers:

**Beijing to Shanghai:**
| Train | Time | Price (CNY) |
|-------|------|-------------|
| G train (high-speed) | 4.5-5.5 hrs | 550-650 (2nd), 915-1,000 (1st), 1,850-2,300 (VIP) |
| D/T trains (mid) | 12-14 hrs | 300-550 |
| Non-high-speed | 19 hrs | 175 (hard seat), 305 (hard sleeper), 470 (soft sleeper) |

**Shanghai to Xi'an:**
| Train | Time | Price (CNY) |
|-------|------|-------------|
| G train (fastest) | 5.5 hrs | ~710 (2nd class) |
| K train (slow) | 21.5 hrs | ~400 (hard sleeper) |

Sleeper train tips: Lower bunk is cheaper (closer to noise). Top bunks more expensive but little headroom; you may not be able to sit up. Top bunks offer more privacy.

### Flights
Domestic carriers: Air China, China Eastern, China Southern, Southwest Airlines.
- Beijing to Shanghai round-trip: from 600 CNY (2 hrs one-way)
- Beijing to Hong Kong one-way: from 650 CNY (~4 hrs)
- Xi'an to Shanghai one-way: from 500 CNY (~2 hrs)
- Beijing to Taipei one-way: ~1,000 CNY (~3 hrs)

### Hitchhiking
Not common. Thumb gesture not understood. Need sign in Mandarin saying "hitchhiking." Otherwise taxis will stop for you.

## Safety

China is quite safe for travelers. Robbery, kidnapping etc. very unlikely -- strictly controlled country.

**Key concerns:**
- **Food sanitation:** Not the most stringent. Use hand sanitizer before eating. Restaurants should appear clean. Street food may cause upset initially
- **Price inflation:** Tourists get ripped off at markets. Ask hostel staff for price estimates. Barter hard at local markets (commercial shops are fine)
- **Scams:** Very common. Number one scam: "student" asks to practice English, takes you to cafe, you get stuck with inflated bill. Politely decline English practice requests
- **Traffic:** Pedestrians do NOT have right of way. Follow locals when crossing
- **Drugs:** Extremely harsh penalties including hard labor and death. Police may extort bribes. Avoid all drugs
- **Politics:** Do not discuss, especially Tibet and Hong Kong. Information censored, conversations monitored online

## When to Visit

China's climate varies drastically by region.

| Season | Conditions | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | Peak season, 30C+ (87-92F), humid | Hottest, most crowded, worst air quality, inflated prices |
| Sep-Oct | ~20C (68F), pleasant | Great time BUT dodge Golden Week (early Oct) |
| Winter | Varies hugely | HK still pleasant (20C/68F). Great Wall may close. Tibet drops to -13C (9F) overnight |

**Golden Week (early October):** Entire country is off. Prices rise, transport booked weeks ahead, cities gridlocked. Avoid or at least avoid big cities.

## Money-Saving Tips

1. **Sleeper trains** -- saves a night's accommodation
2. **Ask for "xiao pan"** -- small portions, 70% price when eating alone
3. **Hard seats** -- cheapest train option, not actually uncomfortable (just regular seats)
4. **Free walking tours** -- available in Beijing, Shanghai, Hong Kong, Xi'an and others. Tip your guide
5. **Avoid Golden Week** -- prices spike, everything packed
6. **Stick to public transport** -- bus/subway gets you anywhere cheaply
7. **Bring filtered water bottle** -- tap water not potable, saves buying bottled water daily (recommends LifeStraw)

## Activities and Classes

Calligraphy classes, cooking classes, tea ceremonies available in all major cities. Some last an hour, some multiple days. Most options in Beijing, Shanghai, and Hong Kong. Book through GetYourGuide, Klook, or Trip.com.

## Practical Tips

- Sanitation: hand sanitizer, check restaurant cleanliness, brace for street food adjustment
- Walking tours: Google "free walking tour in [city]" to find companies
- Sleeper trains: buy a few days in advance for lower bunk savings; some stations have foreigner ticket offices
- Water: bring reusable filtered bottle (tap not potable)
- Air quality: check every morning; wear mask on bad days
- Politics: avoid all political discussion, especially about Tibet and Hong Kong
- Traffic: always look both ways, follow locals crossing streets
- Scams: decline "English practice" invitations from strangers
- Travel insurance: strongly recommended
