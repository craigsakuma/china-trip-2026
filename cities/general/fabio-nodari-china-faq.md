# China Travel FAQ — Essential Guide for First-Timers

**Source:** Fabio Nodari (fabionodariphoto.com), travel photographer living in China for many years. Published Oct 24, 2025. Updated Jan 19, 2026. Answers to the most common questions from first-time visitors.

## Booking Hotels, Trains, and Flights

**Trip.com** is the only good English option. Booking.com is more expensive with fewer options. Some hotels refuse foreigners (claiming a "special license" — not true). Train tickets available on the official China Railway website/app, but requires in-person account validation at a train station. Train tickets go on sale 15 days before departure. Fixed prices — no benefit to booking early. Free cancellation up to 48 hours before.

## Do I Need a Physical Train Ticket?

No. Since October 1, 2025, you can't even print tickets anymore. Your passport is your ticket — it's linked to your booking. Just scan your passport before boarding.

## Which Apps Do I Need?

- **WeChat (微信):** Essential. Real-time messaging, payments (link credit card), built-in translation. May need an existing user to scan your QR code for activation.
- **Alipay (支付宝):** Online payments, works with most foreign debit/credit cards. Also: subway/bus/bike rental, text translation.
- **Didi:** Taxi app in English (Chinese Uber). Also available as mini-app inside WeChat and Alipay.
- **Trip.com:** Book hotels, trains, flights. Best English option. Cannot book public buses online (foreigners must buy in person at bus station).
- **Maps.me or Apple Maps:** Google Maps is blocked. AMAP (Chinese Google Maps) now available in English.

## Is English Commonly Spoken?

No, especially outside big cities like Shanghai. Use the Alipay translator mini-app for communication. Microsoft Translator works offline. Pleco is best for Chinese characters and pronunciation.

## Can I Use Cash?

Technically yes, but cash has virtually disappeared. Everyone uses WeChat or Alipay. The 100 RMB bill (highest value) causes constant change problems. ATM withdrawals: Bank of China works best with foreign cards.

## SIM Card Options

- **eSIM (Holafly):** No VPN needed but no Chinese phone number (needed for some app features and bike-sharing).
- **Physical SIM:** Buy at China Unicom, China Telecom, or China Mobile store — bring passport. Requires VPN but cheaper/faster internet, and you get a phone number. Can also pre-order for airport/hotel delivery.

## How to Use the Internet

Almost every foreign app/website is blocked without a VPN. **Download before traveling.** ExpressVPN doesn't work anymore in China.

**Working VPNs:**
- **Astrill:** Most popular in China, works well.
- **Let's VPN:** One of the cheapest, working properly.
- **Mullvad:** Privacy-focused (no-logs policy), cheap, works well.
- **Shadowsocks:** Advanced encryption protocol, cheap, tech-savvy users only.

## When Is the Best Time to Visit?

**Worst times (avoid at all costs):** Chinese New Year, Labor Holiday (first week of May), National Holiday (first week of October).

**General:** Winters cold — especially south of the Yangtze where there's no heating system. Northern China is cold but all indoor spaces are heated. Summers: hot and humid everywhere. Exceptions: Tibetan Plateau (pleasant temperatures, some rain), Xinjiang (extremely hot but no rain). Spring and autumn are generally best.

## Do I Need a Travel Agency?

For most people and destinations: no. Exceptions: remote places like Dulongjiang or Western Sichuan where it's very difficult to get around without a driver. Rule of thumb: if easily reachable by public transportation, you'll also find plenty of tourists (and can manage DIY).

## Are Scams Common?

Much less common than the rest of Asia, thanks to ubiquitous cameras. Follow common-sense rules. Most common scam: taxi drivers asking for more money than the actual fare. Use Didi to avoid this entirely.

## Can I Fly a Drone?

Yes, but since January 1, 2024, all drones must be registered with Civil Aviation. Max altitude: 120 meters. Banned entirely in Beijing. In Shanghai: must register each flight with police via Alipay mini app. Registration process is complicated for foreigners (Chinese number needed for authentication) — use a specialized registration service.

## Practical Tips Summary

- Always carry your passport.
- Contact your bank before leaving to prevent card blocks.
- Currency exchange only at Bank of China.
- No tipping in restaurants.
- Chinese number gestures differ from Western (6 and 10 especially).
- If food touches the table, don't pick it up.
- Tea is served instead of water in restaurants.
