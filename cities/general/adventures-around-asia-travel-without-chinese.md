# How to Travel China Without Speaking Chinese

**Source:** Richelle (adventuresaroundasia.com), lived in China for six years, speaks Chinese. Practical strategies tested by non-Chinese-speaking travelers she guided.

## Hotel Business Cards

Keep multiple cards from your accommodation. They display the address in both romanized text and Chinese characters. Show them to taxi drivers — drivers rarely recognize English place names but can read Chinese addresses instantly.

## Save Chinese Addresses Offline

Before arriving, grab the Chinese-character address from your booking confirmation email. Save as screenshots or email drafts accessible without internet. You may not have wifi at the airport on arrival.

## Use Subways Over Taxis

Subways in Chinese cities list all stops in English and are easy to navigate without language skills. Buses and trains also work for getting around without luggage. Saves money and avoids the taxi communication problem entirely.

## Picture Menus at Restaurants

"Any decently nice restaurant in China tends to have picture menus." They show exactly what you're ordering, eliminating guesswork about unfamiliar dishes. Look for the photo menu before sitting down.

## Point-and-Order Technique

Enter a restaurant, look at what other diners are eating, point to an appealing dish, hold up one finger, point to yourself. This cross-cultural sign language works remarkably well and is not considered rude.

## Street Food Confidence

Street food is safe because you watch the entire preparation process. Point at what looks good and order. Author notes never getting sick from street vendors — only from a Western restaurant.

## Calculator Haggling at Markets

Markets have calculators available at every stall. Use them to negotiate prices by showing numbers back and forth rather than attempting price pronunciation.

## Research History Before Visiting

Study destinations before arriving. English signage at temples and historical sites is often poorly translated or entirely absent. Reading up in advance makes the visit far more meaningful.

## Book Tours Through Your Hostel/Hotel

Reserve day trips through your accommodation for convenient access to distant attractions (like the Terracotta Warriors or Great Wall sections) without navigating multiple buses and stations independently.

## Ask Staff for Everything

Hostel and hotel receptionists are your best resource. They can write destinations on paper in Chinese, recommend local restaurants, provide maps, give directions, and sometimes make phone calls on your behalf.

## Download Pleco (Essential App)

Pleco is an offline Chinese dictionary app. Translates English to Chinese with both pinyin (pronunciation) and characters. Also recognizes handwritten characters — draw an unknown character with your finger and it identifies it. **Works without internet.**

## Practical Tips

- **Layer your strategies:** Business card + Pleco + picture menus covers 90% of situations
- **Screenshots save you:** Save addresses, maps, and Chinese text as phone screenshots before losing wifi
- **Subways are universal:** English signage in every major Chinese city's metro system
- **Hotels are allies:** Front desk staff expect to help foreign guests navigate — use them freely
- **Street food is safe:** Visible preparation = transparency; crowded stalls = freshness
- **Don't stress:** Millions of non-Chinese-speaking tourists navigate China annually. Gestures, phone apps, and patience work
