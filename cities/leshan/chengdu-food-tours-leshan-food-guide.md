# Leshan Food Guide: Sichuan's Underrated Culinary Capital

**Source:** Chengdu Food Tours (chengdufoodtours.com), specialist food tour operator. Supplemented with data from ChinaDiscovery, PATA, VisitOurChina, and SichuanTravelGuide.

## Quick Facts

- **Reputation:** "This is why you go to Leshan — hint: it's not the Buddha"
- **Cuisine family:** Sichuan (Upper River style — least spicy of the three Sichuan sub-regions)
- **Specialty:** Street food capital; Leshan is to Sichuan food what Chengdu wishes it was
- **Key food street:** Zhanggongqiao (张公桥), the #1 food street — locals and visitors alike
- **Notable:** Boboji (cold skewers) originated here; Qiaojiao Beef is on the intangible cultural heritage list
- **Budget:** Street food meals from 10-30 RMB; restaurant meals 30-80 RMB

## Signature Dishes

### Qiaojiao Beef (跷脚牛肉) — Leshan's #1 Dish
- **Description:** Rich, delicate savory slow-cooked beef broth made from "pretty much all parts of the cow"
- **How it works:** Order by bowl; select cuts including tripe, tendon, stomach, tongue, or regular beef
- **Served with:** Spicy chili dipping sauce on the side
- **Variants:** Hot pot-style versions also available for groups
- **Status:** Listed on Leshan's second batch of intangible cultural heritage
- **Unique:** One of the rare Sichuan dishes that is NOT spicy — broth is savory and herbal
- **Where:** Widely available; best at Zhanggongqiao food street

### Boboji / Bobo Chicken (钵钵鸡) — Born in Leshan
- **Description:** Cold skewers of meat and vegetables soaked in spicy, numbing chile oil broth
- **History:** Over 100 years old; originated in Leshan
- **How it works:** Pre-cooked skewers served cold; diners select from display and pay per stick
- **Ingredients:** Deboned chicken, lotus root, potatoes, cauliflower, wood ear mushrooms, tofu
- **Flavor:** Spicy-fresh from native chicken stock base with complex seasoning blend
- **Difference from Chuanchuan (串串):** Boboji is cold and pre-cooked; chuanchuan is hot pot style

### Fenzheng Beef (粉蒸牛肉) — Steamed Rice-Coated Beef
- **Description:** Beef pieces coated in ground rice and spices, steamed in bamboo baskets
- **Texture:** "Uber-tender" from the steaming process
- **Topped with:** Garlic, cilantro, and Sichuan peppercorn
- **Presentation:** Served in bamboo steamer baskets

### Sweet Skin Duck (甜皮鸭) — Leshan's Famous Duck
- **Description:** Crispy-skinned duck glazed with malt sugar; also called "duck in brine"
- **Texture:** Crackling sweet exterior with juicy, savory meat
- **Unique to Leshan:** One of the city's most iconic takeaway foods
- **How to eat:** Chopped and sold by weight; eaten with hands

## Street Foods & Snacks

### Kabing (卡饼) — Stuffed Steamed Buns
- Steamed beef (or pig intestines) in soft steamed buns
- Classic Leshan street breakfast

### Doufunao (豆腐脑) — Tofu Brain
- Silky tofu topped with chile oil, peanuts, crispy noodles, and beef
- Rich, spicy, comforting — Leshan's version is distinct from other regions

### Baobing (薄饼) — Savory Crepes
- Thin crepes with julienned vegetables and peanut sauce
- Light, fresh street snack

### Xiba Tofu (西坝豆腐)
- From ancient Xiba Town, 20+ km from Leshan
- Tofu-making tradition since the Wanli Period of Ming Dynasty (1570s)
- Multiple preparations: fried, braised, cold, in soup
- Worth a dedicated trip for tofu lovers

### Blood Pudding
- Pig blood served with chile oil, garlic, and soy nuts
- Rich in iron; a Sichuan comfort food staple

### Malatang (麻辣烫)
- Sichuan spicy numbing hot pot bowl — single-serving version
- Select ingredients from display; cooked to order in spicy broth

### Qianwei Dried Tofu (犍为豆腐干 / 咔咔儿)
- Crispy deep-fried tofu squares from nearby Qianwei County
- Stuffed with various fillings; addictive crunchy snack

### Ye'erba (叶儿粑)
- Glutinous rice cakes wrapped in leaves with sweet or savory fillings
- Traditional Sichuan snack; widely available

### Shaomai (烧麦)
- Open-topped dumplings with pork and seasoning
- Leshan style is juicier than northern Chinese versions

## Where to Eat

### Zhanggongqiao Food Street (张公桥美食街)
- **Leshan's #1 food destination** — not just for tourists; locals eat here daily
- Concentrated strip of restaurants and street food stalls
- Qiaojiao Beef, Boboji, and Sweet Skin Duck all readily available
- Best visited for lunch or dinner; some stalls open for breakfast

### Night Markets
- Street food vendors set up along riverfront areas in the evening
- Grilled skewers (烧烤), cold noodles, fresh fruit
- More casual and cheaper than restaurant dining

### Near the Giant Buddha
- Tourist-oriented restaurants near the scenic area
- Higher prices, lower quality — eat in town before/after visiting

## Leshan vs. Chengdu Food

- Leshan is Upper River Sichuan food — **less spicy** than Chongqing or eastern Sichuan
- Many dishes that Chengdu is famous for (Boboji, malatang) actually originated in Leshan
- Leshan's food scene is more concentrated, authentic, and cheaper than Chengdu's
- Food-focused travelers should allocate at least one full meal session to Zhanggongqiao
- "This is why you go to Leshan" — the food rivals or exceeds the Buddha as a reason to visit

---

## Practical Tips

- Leshan is an excellent food day trip from Chengdu even without visiting the Buddha
- Zhanggongqiao is the only food street you need; it has everything
- Prices are significantly cheaper than Chengdu — street food from 5-15 RMB per item
- Most stalls accept WeChat Pay and Alipay; bring small cash as backup
- Point-and-order works at most stalls; limited English
- Boboji and Sweet Skin Duck make excellent takeaway/train snacks
- Leshan food is at its best for lunch — many stalls sell out by early afternoon
- If visiting Xiba for tofu, combine with a river town walk along the Min River
