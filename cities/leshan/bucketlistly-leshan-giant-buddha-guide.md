# Exploring Leshan Giant Buddha: A Complete Travel Guide

**Source:** Pete R. (bucketlistly.blog), solo travel and photography blogger. Published 2025. First-hand experience.

The Leshan Giant Buddha (乐山大佛) is a towering 71-meter stone statue carved directly into a cliff face where the Min River (岷江), Qingyi River (青衣江), and Dadu River (大渡河) converge. Beyond the iconic Buddha, Leshan offers a window into Sichuan's spiritual heritage, ancient engineering, and slow-paced riverside life. This is a perfect day trip from Chengdu.

## Quick Facts

- **Height:** 71 meters — world's tallest pre-modern stone Buddha
- **Construction:** Started 713 AD, completed 803 AD (Tang Dynasty)
- **UNESCO:** World Heritage Site since 1996
- **Builder:** Initiated by monk Haitong (海通) to calm the turbulent river waters
- **Location:** Confluence of three rivers in Leshan, Sichuan
- **Day trip from Chengdu:** ~25 min by high-speed train
- **Time needed:** 3–4 hours for the scenic area; half day with transport
- **Entrance ticket:** ~80 CNY per person (scenic area)
- **Hours:** Apr–Oct: 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM; Nov–Mar: 8:30 AM – 5:30 PM

## Getting to Leshan from Chengdu

### By High-Speed Train (recommended)
Departs from Chengdu East, Chengdu South, or Chengdu stations. Only about **25 minutes** — fast, cheap, convenient.

### By Bus (slower option)
Direct buses from Chengdu's Xinnanmen Bus Station to Leshan, but slower and less convenient (~2 hours).

### From Leshan Station to the Buddha
From Leshan Railway Station to the Leshan Giant Buddha Scenic Area, take a Didi or taxi — about **20 minutes**.

## Visiting the Giant Buddha Scenic Area

### Entry Points & Tickets

- **North Gate (recommended):** Most tourists enter here. Parking is convenient. Ticket: ~80 CNY per person
- **Oriental Buddha Park East Gate:** Combined ticket for Oriental Buddha Park + Giant Buddha: ~160 CNY. The park is a lush green area with rock-carved Buddha statues across the mountains — an optional add-on before the main Buddha
- **Skip the park?** Take a taxi directly to the North Gate for 80 CNY entry and head straight to the main event

### Walking Route from North Gate

Starting at the North Gate, you'll walk along a shady path. First landmark: a large stone with the character "佛" (Buddha) carved by Su Dongpo (苏东坡), the famous Song dynasty writer — a popular photo spot for calligraphy fans.

Continue through the Entrance Gate along a well-paved rocky footpath. Buddhist terms are carved on the left-side red sandstone walls. After walking 10+ minutes, you arrive at **Lingyun Temple** (凌云寺/大佛寺), a beautiful ancient Buddhist temple built in the early Tang Dynasty.

### Nine Bends Plank Road (九曲栈道)

The cliffside stairway descending alongside the Giant Buddha down to its feet. Carved during the Tang Dynasty, it has **278 stone steps**, with the widest section 1.45 m and the narrowest just 0.6 m, dropping about 70 m vertically. Parts are steep — good shoes and a reasonable fitness level required. This is the most dramatic way to experience the Buddha's massive scale up close.

**Crowd tip:** This stairway gets very congested. Arrive early morning to avoid the worst queues.

### At the Buddha's Feet

Standing at the base, you get the full sense of the 71-meter statue looming above. Note the sophisticated **drainage system** incorporated into the carving — drainage pipes carved into various places on the body to carry away rainwater and reduce weathering. Ingenious Tang-dynasty engineering that has preserved the statue for over 1,200 years.

## River Boat Cruise

An alternative (or complement) to the walking tour — see the full Buddha from the river.

- **Departure:** Baxiandong Sightseeing Cruise Wharf (八仙洞观光码头), about 1 km / 15-min walk from the North Entrance Gate
- **Duration:** ~30 minutes with a photo stop in front of the Giant Buddha
- **Cost:** ~80 CNY per person
- **Hours:** 7:30 AM – 6:30 PM
- **Tickets:** Cannot be booked online — buy at the wharf on arrival. Payment: cash (CNY), Alipay, or WeChat Pay

**Author's take:** The boat gives you the iconic front-facing photo of the Buddha that you can't get from the walking trail. Worth doing if time allows.

## Other Attractions in the Scenic Area

### Lingyun Temple (凌云寺)
Ancient Buddhist temple dating to the Tang Dynasty, located near the top of the Buddha. Well-preserved with multiple halls. No extra ticket needed.

### Wuyou Temple (乌尤寺)
Situated on the eastern side, reached by crossing Haoshang Bridge. Peaceful and contemplative atmosphere surrounded by lush gardens. No extra ticket beyond the scenic area entrance.

### Mahao Cliff Tombs (麻浩崖墓)
A cluster of 544 numbered cliff tombs spanning approximately 200 meters, dating to the Han dynasty. Located along the Lingyun Plank Road route. Fascinating glimpse of ancient burial practices.

### Oriental Buddha Park (东方佛都)
Lush green park with beautiful rock-carved Buddha statues scattered across the mountains. Separate ticket or combined entry from the East Gate (~160 CNY total). Nice if you have extra time but optional.

## Practical Tips

- **Recommended approach:** High-speed train from Chengdu + Didi to North Gate → walk through scenic area → boat cruise → Didi back to station. Comfortably fills a half day
- **Accommodation:** Stay in Chengdu — Leshan is best as a day trip, only 25 minutes away by train
- **Footwear:** Sturdy walking shoes are essential — the Nine Bends Plank Road is steep and can be slippery
- **Time of day:** Arrive as early as possible to beat crowds on the stairway
- **Seasons:** The scenic area is open year-round; spring and autumn have the best weather
- **Hydration:** Bring water — limited vendors inside the scenic area
- **Photography:** The boat cruise provides the best full-frontal view; the stairway gives dramatic close-up perspective
- **Combine with:** Many travelers pair Leshan with the Chengdu Panda Base for a full Chengdu area experience
