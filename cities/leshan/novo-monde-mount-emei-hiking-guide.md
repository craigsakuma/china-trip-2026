# Mount Emei Hiking Guide: Sacred Buddhist Mountain to Golden Summit

**Source:** Novo-Monde (novo-monde.com), French-Swiss adventure travel blog with detailed Mount Emei first-hand hiking account. Supplemented with data from ChinaDiscovery and Taste of Everywhere.

## Quick Facts

- **Name:** Mount Emei / Emeishan (峨眉山)
- **Elevation:** 3,099 m (10,167 ft) at Golden Summit
- **Status:** UNESCO World Heritage Site (1996), one of China's 4 Sacred Buddhist Mountains
- **Trail distance:** ~48 km total; 60,000+ steps
- **Elevation gain:** ~5,315 m cumulative (both directions)
- **Duration:** 2-3 days hiking; 1 day with cable car/bus
- **Entry fee:** 160 RMB (high season Jan 16-Dec 14); 110 RMB (low season Dec 15-Jan 15)
- **Ticket validity:** 4 days
- **Location:** ~160 km from Chengdu; 50 km from Leshan Giant Buddha
- **Best time:** March-May, September-November

---

## Two Main Hiking Routes

### Left Flank (Recommended Ascent)
- **Distance:** 13 km
- **Elevation gain:** 2,600 m
- Passes through pristine forests with waterfalls
- More scenic; less developed; fewer tourists
- More physically demanding but rewarding

### Right Flank
- **Distance:** 10 km
- **Elevation gain:** 1,900 m
- Near bus stops and more facilities
- Less scenic than left route
- Easier but less memorable

**Common strategy:** Ascend left flank, descend right flank (or reverse)

## Day-by-Day Itinerary (2-3 Days)

### Day 1: Baoguo Temple to Mid-Mountain
- Start at Baoguo Temple (报国寺) at the mountain base
- Hike through lush subtropical forest
- Pass through Qingyin Pavilion (清音阁) — **MONKEY WARNING ZONE**
- Continue to overnight temple: Xianfeng Temple or Xixiangchi Temple
- **Distance:** ~20 km; 8-10 hours hiking

### Day 2: Mid-Mountain to Golden Summit
- Continue ascending through cloud forests
- Pass Taiziping (太子坪) — last temple, 45 minutes from summit
- Arrive at **Golden Summit (金顶/Jinding)** at 3,079 m
- View the 48-meter golden Samantabhadra (Puxian) statue with 10 faces
- **Optional:** Stay overnight at summit for sunrise
- **Distance:** ~15 km; 6-8 hours hiking

### Day 3: Descent
- Descend via alternative route (right flank if you ascended left)
- Or take cable car down from Jinding to Leidongping, then bus to base
- **Distance:** Variable; 4-8 hours depending on route

## Temple Accommodation on the Mountain

| Temple | Cost | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| Xianfeng Temple (仙峰寺) | 50 RMB/person | Basic but memorable experience |
| Chu Monastery | 40 RMB/person | Excellent mountain views |
| Xixiangchi Temple (洗象池) | ~50 RMB/person | Convenient mid-mountain stop |
| Taiziping (太子坪) | ~60 RMB/person | Last before summit; 45 min from top |

- Stay at monasteries rather than small hostels — better maintained and atmospheric
- "Almost every monastery has guesthouse services with beds or rooms"
- Bedding provided but bring layers for cold nights at altitude
- No hot showers at most mountain temples

---

## Golden Summit (金顶) — 3,079m

- **Samantabhadra Statue (普贤菩萨):** "Immense golden statue representing Puxian with 10 faces"
- 48 meters tall; visible for miles; the mountain's spiritual culmination
- **Sunrise:** Spectacular from the summit; requires overnight stay
- **Brocade spectre:** Rare atmospheric phenomenon — your shadow projected onto clouds with rainbow halo
- **Sea of clouds:** Common in mornings; entire valleys filled with white cloud
- Summit has a hotel, restaurant, and observation platforms

## Cable Car & Bus Options (Non-Hiking)

| Segment | Cost | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Bus: Baoguo → Leidongping | 90 RMB round trip | Operates 6 AM-6 PM |
| Cable car: Leidongping → Jinding | 65 RMB up / 55 RMB down | ~20 minutes each way |
| Total transport + entry | ~370 RMB | For non-hikers |

- Leidongping parking lot is the bus terminus; 2.5 hours hiking or cable car to summit
- Buses and cable cars operate approximately 6 AM - 6 PM (verify current schedules)
- Cable car cannot reach summit for sunrise (too early) — hiking required

## Monkey Warning (Qingyin Pavilion Area)

- Macaques near Qingyin Pavilion are aggressive and accustomed to tourists
- "Keep everything well inside your backpack (glasses, phone, camera, food...)"
- Do not display food — monkeys will snatch bags and pockets
- Use hiking sticks to discourage approach
- **Distraction trick:** "Throw it in the trash; they'll rush to rummage through it"
- Higher elevation monkeys are peaceful and avoid humans
- Monkey encounters are entertaining from a safe distance; dangerous up close

---

## Food & Water on the Mountain

- Small vendors every 3-5 km along trails
- "All materials in the middle area are transported by manpower. It takes 4 hours to go up the mountain" — explains inflated prices
- Restaurants near temples; Xixiangchi restaurant gets positive reviews
- **Water:** Carry 3+ liters; spring refill points along trails
- Filtering water bottle (Lifestraw or similar) recommended
- Instant noodles and basic snacks available at all stops

## Getting to Mount Emei

### From Chengdu
| Mode | Duration | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| High-speed train | ~1 hour | ~110 RMB |
| Regular train | ~2 hours | ~76 RMB |
| Bus | ~3 hours | ~51 RMB |

- From Emeishan Railway Station: Bus 5A or 12 to Baoguo village (~40 minutes)

### From Leshan
- High-speed train: 15 minutes, 11-17 RMB
- Bus: ~1 hour
- Only one station apart on the rail line

## What to Pack

- **Footwear:** Waterproof hiking boots with good grip (stairs are often damp/mossy)
- **Clothing:** Multiple layers — subtropical at base (20-30C), cold at summit (0-10C)
- **Rain gear:** Waterproof jacket essential; rain possible any season
- **Sun protection:** Sunglasses, sunscreen, hat
- **Water:** 3+ liters per day; filtering bottle recommended
- **Food:** Snacks for energy; trail vendors are expensive
- **Hiking poles:** Useful for steep stairs and monkey defense
- **Headlamp:** For pre-dawn summit push or early morning temple departure
- **Light daypack:** Leave large bags at base accommodation

---

## Difficulty & Physical Demands

- Requires "good physical condition" and stamina for sustained stair climbing
- 60,000+ steps create significant muscle soreness (especially quads on descent)
- Altitude gain from base (~500m) to summit (3,079m) is substantial
- Trails are well-marked and in good condition; simple shops every 3-5 km
- "Walking stairs are usually damp in each season" — care needed on moss
- Non-hikers can use bus + cable car combination for summit access

---

## Practical Tips

- Mount Emei and Leshan Giant Buddha are a natural 2-3 day combination
- Book Chengdu accommodation for before/after; base yourself at Baoguo village during the hike
- Weekdays dramatically reduce crowds compared to weekends
- **Avoid:** July-August (heat, rain, crowds), October Golden Week
- Temple stays are the quintessential Emeishan experience — do not skip
- Sunrise from the summit is one of Sichuan's most spectacular experiences
- The hike is demanding but well-supported with infrastructure
- Carry cash — mountain vendors and temples may not accept mobile payment
