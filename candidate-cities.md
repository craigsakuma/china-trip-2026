# Candidate Cities — China Trip 2026

22 destinations beyond Beijing and Hong Kong, selected to match our traveler profile: authentic experiences, hiking, great food, art/history, and cultural immersion.

---

## 1. Guilin & Yangshuo — Guangxi

Iconic karst landscape with the real magic in the surrounding countryside. Yangshuo offers a laid-back, immersive base with cycling routes through rice paddies and limestone towers. Mid-October brings comfortable temps (18-25°C) and spectacular Li River scenery.

- **Highlights:** Bamboo raft on quieter Li River sections (skip the large tourist cruise), cycling/hiking the Yulong River valley, Guilin rice noodle cooking class
- **Best for:** Scenery, hiking, food, cycling
- **Suggested days:** 3-4

---

## 2. Chengdu — Sichuan

Arguably China's best food city. Thriving teahouse culture, Sichuan opera, contemporary art scene, and surrounding mountains for day hikes. October weather is mild and atmospheric.

- **Highlights:** Neighborhood food crawl in Yulin Lu area (mapo tofu, dan dan noodles, hotpot), day hike on Mount Qingcheng (birthplace of Taoism), Sichuan Museum and East Music Park art galleries
- **Best for:** Food (world-class), culture, hiking, art
- **Suggested days:** 3-4

---

## 3. Dali — Yunnan

Sits at 2,000m beside Erhai Lake with Cangshan Mountains as backdrop. Strong Bai ethnic minority culture that's genuinely alive — tie-dye traditions, distinct architecture, and unique cuisine. October is one of the best months: clear skies and comfortable temps.

- **Highlights:** Cangshan Mountain trail hikes with lake views, e-bike loop around Erhai Lake stopping at Bai villages, Shaping Monday market (genuine local Bai market)
- **Best for:** Ethnic minority culture, scenery, hiking, cycling, food
- **Suggested days:** 3

---

## 4. Lijiang & Tiger Leaping Gorge — Yunnan

Gateway to Tiger Leaping Gorge, one of the world's deepest and most dramatic river canyons. The two-day high trail hike is a bucket-list trek. Naxi minority culture in the area is fascinating with the right guide.

- **Highlights:** Two-day high trail hike through Tiger Leaping Gorge (overnight at mid-trail guesthouse), Baisha village and murals (quieter/more authentic than Lijiang Old Town), Naxi ancient music evening
- **Best for:** Hiking (world-class), scenery, ethnic minority culture
- **Suggested days:** 3-4

---

## 5. Kunming — Yunnan

Often just a transit hub, but deserves time on its own. "Spring City" climate, excellent Yunnan cuisine (one of China's most diverse and underrated food traditions), and a crossroads of multiple ethnic cultures.

- **Highlights:** Crossing-the-bridge noodles and wild mushroom hotpot (October is still mushroom season), Stone Forest (UNESCO) day trip, Yunnan Provincial Museum
- **Best for:** Food, culture, Yunnan transit hub
- **Suggested days:** 1-2

---

## 6. Zhangjiajie — Hunan

Towering sandstone pillar formations that inspired the *Avatar* floating mountains. October brings excellent visibility and early autumn colors. The park is vast enough to escape crowds, and surrounding Tujia minority villages offer cultural encounters.

- **Highlights:** Multi-day hiking in the less-visited Yangjiajie section, Tianmen Mountain cliffside walkway, Tujia minority village visit
- **Best for:** Hiking, dramatic scenery, photography
- **Suggested days:** 3

---

## 7. Fenghuang Ancient Town — Hunan

Stunningly preserved riverside town with stilted wooden buildings over the Tuo River. Retains genuine Miao and Tujia minority life in surrounding villages. Pairs well with Zhangjiajie for a combined Hunan itinerary.

- **Highlights:** Dawn walk through the old town before crowds, half-day trip to nearby Miao village (silver jewelry-making, indigo dyeing, sour fish soup), illuminated night views from across the river
- **Best for:** Ethnic minority culture, photography, food, history
- **Suggested days:** 1-2

---

## 8. Leshan & Emeishan — Sichuan

Mount Emei is one of China's four sacred Buddhist mountains with multi-day hiking through mist-shrouded forests past ancient temples. The Giant Buddha at Leshan (71m, world's largest stone Buddha) is genuinely impressive. October brings fog and golden autumn foliage.

- **Highlights:** Two-day hike up Mount Emei with monastery overnight near Golden Summit for sunrise, Leshan Giant Buddha by boat and cliff-side stairways, Leshan's famous local dishes
- **Best for:** Hiking, Buddhist history, food, scenery
- **Suggested days:** 2-3

---

## 9. Guizhou (Kaili / Xijiang / Zhaoxing)

One of China's least-visited provinces and arguably the best for authentic ethnic minority cultural immersion. Southeastern region has dozens of Miao and Dong villages with extraordinary architecture and living traditions. October may coincide with harvest festivals. The food (sour soups, fermented chili, rice wine) is distinctive and outstanding.

- **Highlights:** Xijiang (largest Miao village) plus smaller villages like Langde and Basha, Zhaoxing Dong village with wind-and-rain bridges and drum towers, communal "long table banquet"
- **Best for:** Cultural immersion (top-tier), ethnic minority heritage, food, off-the-beaten-path
- **Suggested days:** 4-5

---

## 10. Chongqing

A sprawling vertical city built on mountain ridges at the Yangtze/Jialing confluence. Birthplace of Sichuan-style hotpot at its most intense. Gritty, authentic energy unlike the polished east coast megacities.

- **Highlights:** Hotpot crawl through back-alley joints in Jiulongpo district, wartime-era stilted hillside neighborhoods, day trip to Dazu Rock Carvings (UNESCO, 9th-13th century Buddhist/Taoist/Confucian cliff carvings)
- **Best for:** Food (hotpot capital), history, gritty urban culture
- **Suggested days:** 2-3

---

## 11. Pingyao — Shanxi

One of the best-preserved ancient walled cities in China (UNESCO). Ming and Qing dynasty architecture is largely original, and local life continues within the walls. Was China's financial capital in the 19th century. October brings crisp autumn weather and far fewer tourists.

- **Highlights:** Walk the complete 6km city walls, explore Rishengchang Exchange House (China's first bank), eat Pingyao beef, hand-pulled noodles, and Shanxi vinegar dishes
- **Best for:** History, architecture, food, culture
- **Suggested days:** 2

---

## 12. Datong — Shanxi

Underrated historical powerhouse. The Yungang Grottoes (51,000 Buddhist statues carved into cliffs in the 5th century) rival Dunhuang with a fraction of the visitors. October weather is dry and clear, ideal for outdoor sites.

- **Highlights:** Yungang Grottoes (UNESCO) with statues up to 17m tall, Hanging Monastery (1,500-year-old temple built into a sheer cliff), Datong's famous knife-cut noodles
- **Best for:** History (Buddhist art), architecture, food
- **Suggested days:** 2

---

## 13. Luoyang — Henan

Capital of 13 Chinese dynasties, home to the Longmen Grottoes (UNESCO). Has the first Buddhist temple in China (White Horse Temple, founded 68 AD), Sui-Tang dynasty ruins, and martial arts connections. October has clear skies and moderate temps.

- **Highlights:** Longmen Grottoes with a guide who can explain Tang Dynasty artistic evolution across 2,300+ caves, Luoyang Museum, the famous water banquet (shui xi, multi-course soup-based feast)
- **Best for:** Ancient history, Buddhist art, food
- **Suggested days:** 2

---

## 14. Huangshan (Yellow Mountain) — Anhui

The mountain that inspired centuries of Chinese landscape painting — twisted pines, granite peaks, seas of cloud. October is peak autumn with stunning foliage and high probability of the famous "sea of clouds." Surrounding Huizhou villages (Hongcun, Xidi) are UNESCO-listed gems.

- **Highlights:** Two-day hike with summit overnight for sunrise (take the less-traveled West Sea Grand Canyon trail), Hongcun village (900-year-old Huizhou architecture), Huizhou cuisine (stinky mandarin fish, hairy tofu)
- **Best for:** Hiking (iconic), scenery, traditional architecture, food
- **Suggested days:** 3

---

## 15. Xiamen & Gulangyu Island — Fujian

Relaxed coastal city with excellent seafood and a mix of Southern Fujianese culture and colonial architecture. Gulangyu Island (UNESCO) is car-free with winding lanes. Nearby Tulou (massive circular Hakka earthen fortresses) make a great day trip. October is warm and dry.

- **Highlights:** Gulangyu Island backstreets, day trip to Fujian Tulou (UNESCO, Hakka earthen roundhouses), Xiamen seafood and street food (oyster omelets, satay noodles, shacha hotpot)
- **Best for:** Food (seafood/street food), architecture, culture, coastal scenery
- **Suggested days:** 3

---

## 16. Suzhou — Jiangsu

Nine UNESCO-listed classical gardens representing the highest achievements of Chinese landscape design. Thriving silk and embroidery tradition, ancient canal neighborhoods, and superb Jiangnan cuisine. October gardens are at their most beautiful.

- **Highlights:** 2-3 gardens with specialist guide (Humble Administrator's Garden + Master of Nets Garden, go early morning), Pingjiang Road canal district with silk workshops and teahouses, refined Suzhou cuisine (osmanthus blooms in October)
- **Best for:** Gardens/art, history, refined food, culture
- **Suggested days:** 2

---

## 17. Hangzhou — Zhejiang

Celebrated for over a thousand years as one of China's most beautiful cities. Beyond West Lake: exceptional tea culture (origin of Longjing/Dragon Well tea), outstanding museums, and hill walks. October is outstanding — osmanthus trees bloom and scent the entire city.

- **Highlights:** Hike the hills around Longjing Village visiting family tea farms, China National Tea Museum and Zhejiang Provincial Museum, Hangzhou cuisine (Dongpo pork, beggar's chicken) at local joints not tourist restaurants
- **Best for:** Food, tea culture, gentle hiking, scenery, art/history
- **Suggested days:** 2-3

---

## 18. Lhasa — Tibet

One of the most culturally profound destinations in the world. The Potala Palace, Jokhang Temple, and Barkhor pilgrim circuit go far beyond sightseeing. October is dry, clear, and less crowded. Requires a Tibet Travel Permit (arranged through licensed agency) and mandatory guide.

- **Highlights:** Barkhor kora pilgrim circuit around Jokhang Temple, Sera Monastery monk debates, day trip to Namtso or Yamdrok Lake for high-altitude scenery
- **Best for:** Cultural immersion (unparalleled), spirituality, history, scenery
- **Suggested days:** 3-4 (include acclimatization day)
- **Note:** Tibet Travel Permit required, must arrange 2-3 weeks in advance

---

## 19. Shangri-La (Zhongdian) — Yunnan

Sits at 3,200m on the Tibetan Plateau's edge. Tibetan Buddhist culture in a more accessible setting than Lhasa (no permit required). October brings golden larches, blue skies, and crisp mountain air around the massive Songzanlin Monastery.

- **Highlights:** Songzanlin Monastery ("Little Potala") with Tibetan guide, hiking in Pudacuo National Park (old-growth forests, alpine lakes, autumn color), Tibetan hotpot and yak butter tea
- **Best for:** Tibetan culture, hiking, scenery, off-the-beaten-path
- **Suggested days:** 2-3

---

## 20. Quanzhou — Fujian

One of China's most historically significant and least-touristed cities. Was the world's largest port in the Song/Yuan dynasties (Marco Polo called it one of Earth's two greatest ports). UNESCO status since 2021. Ancient mosques, Hindu temples, Nestorian Christian ruins, and a Manichaean temple — all within walking distance. Outstanding Fujianese coastal food scene.

- **Highlights:** Old city walk covering Kaiyuan Temple, Qingjing Mosque (one of China's oldest), the only surviving Manichaean temple in the world; Maritime Museum; street food (meat dumplings, fish ball soup, oyster omelets)
- **Best for:** History (deeply unique), food, cultural immersion, off-the-beaten-path
- **Suggested days:** 2

---

## 21. Shanghai — Shanghai Municipality

Shanghai is China's most cosmopolitan metropolis, and at first glance may seem like an odd fit for travelers seeking authentic cultural immersion. However, beneath the glitzy surface lies a city with deep roots: centuries-old water towns on its outskirts, a thriving local food scene beyond xiaolongbao, and some of China's best museums and art spaces. Mid-October weather is excellent (mid-60s to low 70s°F, low rainfall). It rewards those who dig deeper but requires more effort to find the authentic layer compared to cities like Chengdu or Dali, and it lacks serious hiking.

- **Highlights:** Jinze Water Town (quiet, genuinely lived-in, almost no foreign tourists — skip the over-touristed Zhouzhuang), Old City backstreet food deep-dive through longtang lane houses and wet markets, M50 Art District (working artist studios in converted textile mills) and Power Station of Art (free contemporary art museum)
- **Best for:** Food, art, modern history, architecture, urban cultural contrast
- **Suggested days:** 2-3 (best as a gateway city at trip start/end)
- **Why you might skip it:** Authentic side requires deliberate seeking-out. City's dominant character is modern and commercial. No hiking. Most expensive city in mainland China. Time may be better allocated to places where authentic experiences are the default.

---

## 22. Xi'an — Shaanxi Province

Xi'an is one of the strongest candidates in all of China for travelers who prioritize history and food. As the ancient capital of thirteen dynasties and the eastern terminus of the Silk Road, the depth of history is unmatched — 3,000+ years of layers. Unlike Shanghai, the authentic character is impossible to miss, from the intact Ming Dynasty city walls to the vibrant Muslim Quarter where Hui culture has thrived for a millennium. Mid-October weather is ideal (55-68°F, crisp and dry). The food scene is distinct from the rest of China — heavy on hand-pulled noodles, lamb, and bread-based dishes reflecting its Silk Road heritage.

- **Highlights:** Terracotta Warriors combined with Huaqing Hot Springs (world history + light hiking), Muslim Quarter deep food exploration beyond the tourist drag (yangrou paomo, biang biang noodles, persimmon cakes), cycling/walking the 13.7km Ming Dynasty city wall loop and Beilin (Forest of Steles) Museum with 2,000 years of calligraphy
- **Best for:** Ancient history, food (unique regional cuisine), cultural immersion, Silk Road heritage, calligraphy/art
- **Suggested days:** 2-3 (optional 3rd day for Huashan sacred mountain hike — world-class but strenuous)
- **Why you might skip it:** Geographic outlier if itinerary focuses on southern China (requires internal flight). Terracotta Warriors site is a mass-tourism destination with large crowds. City outside the walls feels like a typical large Chinese city.
